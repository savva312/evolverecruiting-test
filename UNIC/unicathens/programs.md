# UNIC Athens: Programmes Catalogue (December 20, 2025)

Source: UNIC Athens published programme pages and Tuition & Fees (Academic Year 2025–2026) as of December 20, 2025.

## Campus snapshot
- **Scope:** 6 schools delivering **9 accredited undergraduate programmes**; accreditation reported via the Hellenic Authority for Higher Education (HAHE).
- **Planned (not yet offered):** MBA and PhD in Medical Sciences targeted for 2026 accreditation/launch.
- **ECTS structures:** Seven programmes at **4 years / 240 ECTS**, Pharmacy at **5 years / 300 ECTS**, Medicine at **6 years / 360 ECTS (12 semesters × 30 ECTS)**.

## Programme profiles (condensed from official pages)

### Accounting (BSc, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** English. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition (60 ECTS/year):** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** Professionally oriented accounting degree covering financial reporting, audit, taxation, management accounting, and tech-enabled accounting practice (IT/AI tools, accounting information systems). Emphasises international developments and governance/compliance themes.
- **Learning highlights:** Prepare and analyse financial statements (including group accounts); apply management accounting for planning and control; apply financial management techniques; explain legal/tax principles; execute audit engagements within regulatory frameworks.
- **Structure:** 240 ECTS bachelor path with semester breakdown published; graduation requires completion of all programme requirements and 240 ECTS.

### Business Administration (BBA, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** English. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** Multidisciplinary business degree combining core business foundations with concentration pathways (e.g., entrepreneurship/innovation, finance/economics, management/HR). Framed for multicultural environments and emphasises critical thinking, communication, and creativity.
- **Learning highlights:** Apply business knowledge to real-world decisions; work effectively in multicultural teams; identify ethical and sustainable leadership traits; use analytical tools for business information; communicate effectively in writing/presentations.
- **Structure:** Academic Path separates common core and concentration content to reach 240 ECTS.

### Marketing Management (BBA, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** English. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** Focused on modern marketing strategy connecting brands with stakeholders, blending creativity and analytics. Covers digital/social media, marketing research and analytics, CSR/sustainable marketing, international marketing, neuromarketing, and AI/big data impacts.
- **Learning highlights:** Integrate marketing principles into organisational decisions; evaluate environments and convert insights into marketing choices; design research and insight-generation; plan digital/social media strategies; formulate marketing plans across the 4Ps; explain AI/big data impacts on marketing.
- **Data-quality note:** Programme page shows BBA vs BA wording; canonical award treated as **BBA**.

### Psychology (BSc, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** Greek. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** Introduces psychology as a scientific discipline with emphasis on research methods, analysis, and applying psychological principles to personal, social, and organisational issues. Stresses ethical reasoning and sociocultural awareness.
- **Learning highlights:** Familiarity with key concepts/perspectives; apply research methods (design, analysis, interpretation); use scientific inquiry and critical/creative thinking; apply principles to real-world contexts; act ethically and communicate effectively.
- **Data-quality note:** Page shows “Greek” and “English and Greek”; tuition table/PDF list **Greek** as canonical language.

### Law (LLB, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** Greek. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** First degree in legal studies with professional orientation toward practice/consulting/licensure. Emphasises legal reasoning, problem solving, and research across public law, civil and criminal law, commercial/company law, and EU/international dimensions.
- **Learning highlights:** Identify and evaluate legal concepts and specialised fields; extract facts and apply rules; develop structured legal arguments; provide responsible legal opinions; draft legal documents; conduct independent legal research.
- **Professional note:** Page states graduates are prepared to obtain licences as **Lawyer, Notary, and Bailiff in Greece**.

### Pharmacy (MPharm Integrated Master, 5 years, 300 ECTS)
- **Language:** Greek. **Mode:** Full-time (page also shows part-time; inconsistency noted). **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000 (applied each of 5 years).
- **Profile:** Educates pharmacy scientists for evidence-based, person-centred pharmaceutical care, including interprofessional practice and public health contributions. Covers pharmaceutical sciences, therapeutics, medication safety, research competence, and ethical/technological considerations (including AI tools).
- **Learning highlights:** Critically evaluate pharmacy literature; apply knowledge across core disciplines and clinical practice; solve therapeutic problems; conduct pharmaceutical/clinical research and analyse data; collaborate in healthcare teams; apply medication safety standards and report adverse reactions.

### Computer Science (BSc, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** English. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** Computing foundation oriented to contemporary practice with AI influence. Emphasises designing/evaluating computing solutions with trade-offs, scalability, security, and responsible practice. Includes core systems, software engineering, data management, AI/ML, cybersecurity/privacy, and specialisation electives (AI/ML, cybersecurity/privacy, IoT, VR/game development) plus a two-part final-year project.
- **Learning highlights:** Design and evaluate computing solutions; set specs for complex problems; design/maintain intelligent systems (AI, IoT, ML); address social/legal/ethical considerations including responsible AI; manage software projects; identify/mitigate privacy and cybersecurity risks.

### Data Science (BSc, 4 years, 240 ECTS)
- **Language:** English. **Mode:** Full-time or Part-time. **Tuition:** EU €15,480; non-EU €18,000.
- **Profile:** Interdisciplinary degree integrating computer science, statistics, and mathematics for data-driven problem solving. Covers data acquisition/cleaning/management, statistical modelling/inference, machine learning/AI/data mining, big-data computing (parallel/distributed), visualisation/communication, privacy/security/ethics, and project-based application (including dedicated project and two-part final year project).
- **Learning highlights:** Apply data science methodologies to real-world problems; use ML/AI and scalable computing for big data; implement algorithms in high-level languages; clean/transform/query/manage data; visualise data and communicate results; assess privacy/security and act with professional responsibility.
- **Data-quality note:** Page shows BSc Data Science vs BSc Computer Science variants; canonical award treated as **BSc in Data Science**.

### Doctor of Medicine (MD, 6 years, 360 ECTS)
- **Language:** English. **Mode:** Full-time. **Tuition:** EU €27,000; non-EU €28,980 (50% charged per semester).
- **Profile:** Six-year medical degree (12 semesters × 30 ECTS) with integrated basic and clinical sciences, small-group teaching, community orientation, and longitudinal learning streams. Curriculum split between **Health and Disease Years (1–3)** and **Transitional + Clinical Years (4–6)** with increasing clinical immersion, a research project, clinical specialties, and a clinical elective.
- **Learning highlights:** Explain normal human structure/function; explain mechanisms of disease and therapeutics; describe pharmacology of common medications; apply research methods and appraise literature; communicate effectively and compassionately; apply ethical practice (confidentiality, autonomy, consent, safeguarding) and reflect on bias/professionalism.
- **Fees:** See Medical School fee table above (administrative fee €1,500 before enrolment; malpractice insurance €300–€700/year; optional health insurance €180/year, etc.).
- **Accreditation statement:** Programme page includes an explicit accreditation/quality assurance statement.
