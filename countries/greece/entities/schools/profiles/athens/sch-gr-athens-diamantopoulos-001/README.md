Diamantopoulos Schools is a long-running private K–12 institution in Aigaleo (West Athens) operating since 1957 with Greek national curriculum through Gymnasium and Lyceum. Limited public information on international placements means early outreach should focus on relationship-building with school leadership, mapping counseling practices, and gauging which families consider cross-border university options.

**Top opportunities:**

- Greek-language outreach and scholarship framing can position UNIC as an affordable, accredited alternative to UK/EU routes for families with moderate budgets.
- Longstanding school (founded 1957) with K–12 continuity offers the chance to build multi-year pipelines if counseling engagement is secured.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Fees and counselor contacts are not published, creating access friction and unclear affordability without direct conversations.
- International pathway data is absent; student aspirations may be domestically oriented unless counselors champion alternatives.
- Limited digital footprint may slow responsiveness; in-person or phone outreach likely required.
