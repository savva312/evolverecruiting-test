**One-paragraph summary:**
Athens College (operated by the Hellenic-American Educational Foundation) is a flagship private nonprofit in Psychiko that runs parallel Greek Lyceum and IB Diploma pathways. Premium IB fees (~€13.5k–€14k captured for 2023–2024) and longstanding international university progression make it a top Athens/Attica prospect for families able to afford €10k–€27k annual tuition. The school emphasizes bilingual Greek-English learning, selective admissions, and a large alumni network; outreach must respect its structured counseling processes and competitive destination set.

**Top opportunities:**
- IB DP cohort with high English readiness aligns to UNIC CS/data, business, and health sciences programs.
- Premium tuition and nonprofit governance suggest households can meet UNIC and UNIC Athens affordability ranges; scholarships can be framed as value enhancers rather than price relief.
- Strong alumni and U.S./UK-facing counseling culture enables joint sessions on EU mobility, visas, and professional outcomes.

**Top risks or constraints:**
- Admissions and counseling offices are gatekeepers; cold outreach may be filtered without references or clear value.
- Competition from UK, Netherlands, U.S., and Greek/European medicine pathways; messaging must differentiate accreditation, cost, and residency outcomes.
- IB and Panhellenic exam periods compress spring visit windows; scheduling must avoid May–June.
