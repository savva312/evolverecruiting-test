## 1. Executive summary

Doukas School (Εκπαιδευτήρια Δούκα) is a large private K–12 school in Marousi (151, Mesogion Str., Maroussi 15126, Athens), operating international pathways alongside the Greek Lyceum. Publicly verifiable evidence confirms the school is an IB World School (IB school code **001346**) offering the **IB Diploma Programme** with **May examinations**, with the IB listing also showing the school’s address and DP subject registrations (as currently listed in IB systems). (Source: `https://www.ibo.org/en/school/001346`, accessed 2025-12-21). ([ibo.org](https://www.ibo.org/en/school/001346?utm_source=openai)) The school also markets **International A-Levels**, a **BTEC Foundation** route, and a **US Dual Diploma Program** under its “International Programs” portfolio. (Source: `https://www.doukas.edu.gr/en/`, accessed 2025-12-21). ([doukas.edu.gr](https://www.doukas.edu.gr/en/)) A key strength for university recruitment partnerships is its dedicated **University Placement Center**, which explicitly advertises admissions-test preparation (including **UCAT, BMAT, LNAT, HPAT, CCVX**), **SAT preparation/examination**, and interview support. (Source: `https://www.doukas.edu.gr/en/international-programs/university-placement-center/`, accessed 2025-12-21). ([doukas.edu.gr](https://www.doukas.edu.gr/en/international-programs/university-placement-center/))

On fees, Doukas’ admissions workflow publicly references a **€50 evaluation fee** for applicant participation, deducted from tuition if admitted, but does not publish full tuition amounts on that page. (Source: `https://www.doukas.edu.gr/eggrafes/`, accessed 2025-12-21). ### 8.3 Mitigation plan (recommended operating approach for UNIC)

**1) Verification workflow (minimum viable due diligence)**
- Step 1: Confirm the correct counterpart office for each data item: admissions for fees, counseling for outcomes, academic office for calendar. Use the generic contacts from the official contact page to route requests. ([doukas.edu.gr](https://www.doukas.edu.gr/en/contact/?utm_source=openai))  
- Step 2: Request documents in a re-usable format (PDF preferred; XLSX acceptable for fee schedules).  
- Step 3: Store each artifact with a year label (for example, “Fees 2025–2026,” “Destinations Class of 2024”) and note the retrieval date for auditability.  
- Step 4: Where claims are marketing-style (97% admission, 100% admission “each year”), ask for denominators and year tags before using the figures in external-facing collateral.

**2) Messaging safeguards (relationship-first, evidence-led)**
- Frame requests as standard counselor-to-university collaboration: “to align student advising, scholarships, and application timelines.”  
- Avoid any request for student-level data; ask only for aggregated, cohort-level outcomes and anonymized destinations lists.

**3) Timing safeguards**
- Avoid high-intensity event asks in May – June due to IB and Panhellenic exam concentration; prioritize October – February. ([ibo.org](https://www.ibo.org/en/school/001346?utm_source=openai))

**4) Governance safeguards post-Cognita partnership**
- When proposing MoUs, fairs, or branded scholarships, ask early about internal approval routing and whether a Cognita-level review is required. ([cognita.com](https://www.cognita.com/news-and-views/cognita-welcomes-first-school-in-greece-through-partnership-with-doukas-school/?utm_source=openai))
