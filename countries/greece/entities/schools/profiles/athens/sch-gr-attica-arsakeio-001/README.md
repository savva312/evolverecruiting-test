**One-paragraph summary:**
Arsakeio-Tositseia Schools operate long-standing private Lyceum campuses in Attica, anchored in Psychiko, with published Lyceum tuition of about €6,500 for 2025–2026. The network serves co-ed students across multiple levels; upper-secondary instruction follows the Greek national curriculum. Families are fee-paying but not in the highest premium tier, so international study propensity needs to be qualified during counselor conversations. The school’s brand recognition and multi-campus footprint make it a credible Tier 3 outreach target for UNIC, with affordability aligned to many non-Medicine programs and selective Medicine prospects after screening.

**Top opportunities:**
- Published Lyceum tuition (~€6.5k) verifies ability-to-pay above public-school baseline and supports UNIC Nicosia/Athens affordability messaging.
- Multi-campus Attica network provides a broader parent/student base and potential for repeated counselor or info-session touchpoints.
- Heritage brand (Filiki Etairia) with strong local trust can lend credibility to co-branded events once relationships are built.

**Top risks or constraints:**
- Greek Lyceum (no IB/AP) means outbound pathways may be less standardized; requires counseling partnerships to identify internationally oriented students.
- Fee level sits below premium IB schools, so Medicine affordability is case-by-case and needs early financial framing.
- Contacts and calendars vary by campus; reception routing required to reach the Lyceum leadership/counseling lead.
