**One-paragraph summary:**  
American Farm School’s General High School is a non-profit private Lyceum in the Thessaloniki metro area that blends STEM-forward Greek Lyceum academics with hands-on experiential learning at its Educational Farm and an optional Study USA pathway that layers College Prep English, SAT/IELTS preparation, and U.S. application coaching. Boarding and day students from across Greece participate in residential life, clubs, and athletics, creating a national intake with demonstrated international aspirations, but tuition levels are not published and require confirmation during outreach.

**Top opportunities:**  
- Study USA program embeds English-medium coursework, U.S. application guidance, and SAT/IELTS prep, aligning with UNIC’s international programs.  
- Residential model pulls students from multiple Greek regions, expanding reach beyond Thessaloniki day families.  
- STEM and experiential learning brand (Educational Farm, science workshops, robotics/entrepreneurship clubs) supports positioning for UNIC Medicine, Health Sciences, CS/AI, and sustainability narratives.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Tuition not published; affordability must be validated directly despite premium residential signals.  
- Agricultural/experiential identity may channel some students toward agriscience paths unless UNIC offers clear adjacent options.  
- Competes for internationally oriented students with Pinewood, Anatolia, and other Thessaloniki premium schools; requires differentiated messaging and scholarship clarity.
