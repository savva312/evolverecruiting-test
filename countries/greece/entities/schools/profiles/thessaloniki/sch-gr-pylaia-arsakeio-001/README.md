**One-paragraph summary:**  
Arsakeio Schools Thessaloniki runs a long-established private Greek Lyceum in the Pylaia-Chortiatis corridor of the Thessaloniki metro. Families pay published Lyceum tuition (~€5,355 for 2025–2026), indicating willingness to invest in private education though below the highest-fee metro schools. The school follows the national curriculum and markets a broad academic program with strong language offerings; outbound pathways typically include Greek universities and selective EU destinations. This makes Arsakeio a solid coverage target for UNIC, with screening for English readiness and ability-to-pay for premium programs.

**Top opportunities:**  
- Engage fee-paying families in Thessaloniki metro that value private schooling and language proficiency.  
- Position UNIC Business, CS, and Health Sciences (non-Medicine) as cost-comparable to local private options.  
- Explore group visits and counselor briefings via the Arsakeio network brand recognition across Greece.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Tuition band is mid-tier; Medicine affordability requires case-by-case financial screening.  
- National curriculum orientation may bias students toward Panhellenic exams and Greek universities unless early international advising is offered.
