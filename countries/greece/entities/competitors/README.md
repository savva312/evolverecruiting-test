# Competitors

Track universities and private providers recruiting Greek students, especially those overlapping with UNIC and UNIC Athens programs. Summaries should note positioning, pricing, admission requirements, and evidence of on-the-ground activity.

- `profiles/` – individual competitor write-ups with links to sources and `last_verified` dates.

## Scope

- Focus on private universities, transnational campuses, and foreign degree franchises marketing English-taught programs to Greek students.
- Prioritize competitors with strong overlap to UNIC health, business, computing, and law offerings; reference [UNIC Nicosia and Athens programs](../../../../UNIC/README.md) instead of duplicating program details.

## Current priorities

1. Cyprus-based private universities (e.g., European University Cyprus, UCLan Cyprus, Frederick) competing for health, business, and computing against [UNIC Athens programs](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/programs.md).
2. Greek private colleges with foreign accreditations (e.g., Deree/ACG, CITY College) attracting English-taught undergraduate students in Athens/Thessaloniki.
3. Near-shore medical options popular with Greek families (Cyprus, Central/Eastern Europe) that compete directly with [UNIC Nicosia’s medical portfolio](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/programs.md).
4. Major Greek public universities adding English-taught tracks (e.g., AUTh English MD) that blend public-brand credibility with paid English delivery.
