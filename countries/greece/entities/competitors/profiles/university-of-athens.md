# National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (University of Athens)

- **Last verified:** 2026-12-21
- **Website:** https://en.uoa.gr/

## Positioning
- Greece’s flagship public university with strong brand equity and the widest program catalogue in Athens; attracts high-achieving domestic students via national exams and remains the default “prestige” pathway for families who want students to stay in Greece.
- Delivers most undergraduate programs in Greek at no tuition for EU/EEA students, reinforcing affordability and public mission. Select English-taught degrees (Medicine; BA in Archaeology/History/Literature of Ancient Greece) target international demand and fee-paying segments while leveraging Athens’ appeal.
- Promotes research reputation and clinical affiliations in Athens hospitals, making its English-taught Medicine program a status alternative to Cyprus/other near-shore options.

## Pricing and scholarships
- Greek-taught undergraduate programs: **No tuition for EU students** at public universities; non-EU students typically pay around **€1,000 per year** depending on program/institution, keeping public options highly price-competitive.【https://www.studyineurope.eu/study-in-greece/tuition-fees】
- English-taught Medicine (6-year): tuition set at **€17,000 per year for 2026–2027**, with a **€5,000 non-refundable deposit** that counts toward fees; remaining **€12,000** split across September and January installments.【https://medicen.uoa.gr/tuition-fees/】
- English-taught BA in Archaeology/History/Literature of Ancient Greece: **Estimated tuition €6,000 per year** per official admissions page.【https://baag.uoa.gr/cyprus_branch/admissions_tuition】

## Recruitment signals in Greece
- Reliance on the national Panhellenic exam system for Greek-taught seats drives high selectivity and keeps domestic demand strong without heavy marketing spend.
- Free tuition for EU students sharply undercuts private colleges and foreign franchises; non-EU fees remain modest, enabling appeal to Balkan/EU-adjacent students seeking a low-cost public option in Athens.
- Fee-paying English programs indicate a growing internationalization push; the Medicine program’s structured deposits and installment plan show readiness to capture international demand quickly.

## Overlap with UNIC / UNIC Athens
- Competes with [UNIC Athens](../../../../../UNIC/unicathens/programs.md) for academically strong Greek/EU students who prefer to remain in Athens and pay little or no tuition for Greek-taught programs.
- English-taught Medicine directly overlaps with [UNIC Nicosia’s medical portfolio](../../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/programs.md) on international med students; BA Archaeology overlaps with humanities offerings and international liberal arts demand targeted by UNIC Athens.
- NKUA’s public prestige and zero-tuition baseline increase the hurdle for UNIC Athens in price-sensitive segments; UNIC must differentiate via English-medium access, industry links, and applied/tech programs.
