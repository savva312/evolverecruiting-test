# British Council Study UK Fairs (Greece)

## Overview
British Council Greece hosts Study UK fairs combining in-person expos in Athens and Thessaloniki with complementary webinars. Audience includes students considering UK-style study as well as broader English-taught EU options, making it a strong brand-building venue for UNIC and UNIC Athens.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** Athens in November; Thessaloniki late November/December; spring virtual webinars in February/March.
- **Format:** Booth-style fair with seminar slots; parallel counselor briefings; webinar series before/after physical events.
- **Cities:** Athens and Thessaloniki; occasional virtual-only editions during exam periods.

## Audience and expected reach
- 800–1,200 visitors per city; notable parent and counselor attendance.
- Leads skew toward high-ability, English-proficient students seeking EU/UK-aligned degrees.
- Webinars: 200–400 attendees per topic with strong Q&A engagement.

## Pricing (indicative)
- €2,000–€3,500 per city for standard booth; digital/webinar packages often free or bundled.
- Add-ons: seminar slots, digital promotion, lead exports included for exhibitors.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** UK-alternative positioning with lower tuition; accredited English-taught portfolio; medicine, business, CS, and law as hooks.
- **UNIC Athens:** emphasize local campus, ability to study in Greece with international degree quality; attractive for students preferring to stay close to home.

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: register 3–4 months ahead; request seminar slot on affordability/ROI vs UK; localize landing pages in Greek; coordinate shipping/printing.
- During: deploy Greek-speaking rep; use QR + scan; capture counselor contacts; push Athens campus visits for Thessaloniki/Athens attendees.
- Post: segmented follow-up (students vs counselors) within 48 hours; invite to webinars; share scholarship tables and application checklists.

## Risks and notes
- High UK branding—must differentiate with EU recognition and price/value story.
- School exam periods (Panhellenic exams) can reduce attendance; prioritize dates avoiding May/June.
