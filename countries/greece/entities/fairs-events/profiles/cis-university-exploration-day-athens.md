# CIS University Exploration Day (Athens)

## Overview
Council of International Schools (CIS) and its European Admission Counselors (EAC) network run university exploration days across IB/A-level schools. The Athens stop gathers high-performing students and counselors from international/independent schools, with structured school visits and counselor networking.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** February/March (spring tour) and September/October (fall tour).
- **Format:** On-campus mini-fairs + presentations during school hours; dedicated counselor session; some years include evening public session.
- **City:** Athens (rotating host schools such as ACS Athens, St. Catherine's British School, Campion School).

## Audience and expected reach
- 150–300 students per city across participating schools; smaller but influential counselor cohort (20–50).
- High English fluency; strong IB/A-level academic profile; families receptive to EU options.

## Pricing (indicative)
- Approximately €1,500–€2,000 per city stop plus CIS membership; travel/logistics extra.
- Bundling multiple cities in a CIS tour reduces per-stop cost.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** EU English-taught degrees with strong ROI; showcase medicine, business, CS, and law; scholarships for IB achievers.
- **UNIC Athens:** emphasize local campus access, internships with Greek/European firms, and ability to remain in Greece.

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: secure CIS/EAC membership eligibility; request Athens stop; schedule counseling sessions and class talks; prepare school-specific data sheets.
- During: focus on counselor relationships; collect student interests; promote campus visits/open days; offer fee waivers for on-site applicants.
- Post: send tailored follow-up to each school within 48 hours; share counselor toolkit (program brochures, deadlines, contacts); invite to virtual open house.

## Risks and notes
- Slots limited to member institutions; early commitment needed.
- School timetables and exam windows may constrain student turnout; coordinate timing with hosts.
