# Ministry of Migration and Asylum (Υπουργείο Μετανάστευσης και Ασύλου)

- **Mandate:** Implements Greece’s immigration code, sets policy for entry visas and residence permits, and oversees regional migration offices that issue study-related permits to third-country nationals.
- **Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens:** Determines visa/residence pathways for non-EU/EEA students enrolling at UNIC Athens; guidance also informs Greek-resident third-country nationals considering UNIC Nicosia on whether they can travel or return without jeopardizing status in Greece.
- **Key processes and timelines:** Student applicants typically secure a national (D-type) visa via Greek consulates with proof of admission, financial means, and insurance; after arrival they must apply for a residence permit for studies at the local migration office (application usually required within 90 days of entry or before visa expiry). Processing times vary by prefecture, so early appointment booking is advised.
- **Contact points:** Policy and forms through https://migration.gov.gr/en/; local competent authorities list and booking links published on the same portal; consular pre-visa steps handled by Greek embassies/consulates (Ministry of Foreign Affairs).
- **Sources:** Ministry of Migration and Asylum portal (https://migration.gov.gr/en/) and Greek consular visa guidance (https://www.mfa.gr/en/visas/).
