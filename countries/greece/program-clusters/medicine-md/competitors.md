# Competitors — Medicine / MD

Use this list to track options Greek students compare to UNIC and UNIC Athens. Keep tuition and entry requirements refreshed each cycle.

## Local competitors (Greece)
- **National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA) — Medical School (Athens):** Public prestige; Greek-language instruction with heavy Panhellenic reliance. *Position vs UNIC:* UNIC offers English delivery and clearer clinical rotations without Greek-language dependency.
- **Aristotle University of Thessaloniki — English MD:** Public brand with ~60 seats; higher selectivity and Greek-language exposure in hospitals. *Position vs UNIC:* Highlight seat scarcity and language load; UNIC provides larger international cohort and blended Cyprus/Greece placements.
- **University of Crete — Faculty of Medicine (Heraklion):** Greek-language public option; strong research faculty. *Position vs UNIC:* Emphasize English-medium continuity and mobility for electives at UNIC.
- **University of Patras — School of Medicine:** Domestic public program with limited English modules. *Position vs UNIC:* Contrast with UNIC’s full-English instruction and support for outward rotations.
- **Private franchise colleges (e.g., Metropolitan College partnerships):** Offer UK-linked health sciences but not full MD; marketed as alternative pathways. *Position vs UNIC:* UNIC provides full medical degree with EU recognition rather than feeder diplomas.

## Regional/EU alternatives
- **Charles University (Czechia):** Entrance-exam driven; higher living costs in Prague. *Position vs UNIC:* UNIC avoids Czech language requirements in clinical years and offers Greece-based campus proximity.
- **Semmelweis University (Hungary):** High brand value; rigorous entrance exams and €17k+ tuition. *Position vs UNIC:* Competitive pricing and English rotations in Cyprus/Greece; smaller cohorts for support.
- **Medical University of Sofia / Varna (Bulgaria):** Lower tuition; entrance exams and local-language clinical exposure. *Position vs UNIC:* English-only rotations and Athens location reduce language and relocation friction.
- **European University Cyprus (EUC) — MD:** English program in Nicosia with growing intake. *Position vs UNIC:* UNIC can differentiate with Athens option plus broader clinical rotation map.
- **UCLan Cyprus / UK offshore MD pathways:** Market UK brand with Cyprus base. *Position vs UNIC:* Stress integrated support, scholarship levers, and medical faculty depth at UNIC.

## Positioning reminders vs UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Lead with English-medium delivery end-to-end, structured clinical placements, and mobility between Cyprus and Greece.
- Counter lower-cost CEE publics by emphasizing support services, USMLE/PLAB prep, and modern simulation facilities.
- For domestic publics, focus on avoiding Panhellenic dependence and Greek-language clinical barriers.
