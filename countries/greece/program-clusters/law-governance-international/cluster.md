# Law, Governance, and International cluster

## Overview
Greek students pursue law through domestic publics, UK LLBs delivered via private colleges, and EU programs with IR/governance tracks. UNIC and UNIC Athens must offer clear recognition pathways, English delivery, and strong ties to EU law and international relations careers.

## Demand signals in Greece
- Law faculties in Athens/Thessaloniki/Komotini are over-subscribed; many students seek English LLB/LLM options without Panhellenic entry.
- Private colleges offer University of London or UK franchise LLBs; recognition and internship clarity can be weak.
- Growing interest in EU law, maritime law, and IR tied to shipping and regional diplomacy roles.

## UNIC/UNIC Athens positioning
- English-taught LLB/LLM with EU law focus and links to moot courts, legal clinics, and shipping/maritime partners.
- Athens campus proximity reduces relocation costs; Cyprus rotations add EU institutional exposure.
- Structured guidance for Greek Bar/recognition steps compared to franchise ambiguity.
