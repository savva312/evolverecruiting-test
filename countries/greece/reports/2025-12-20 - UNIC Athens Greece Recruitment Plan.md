# UNIC Athens Greece Recruitment Plan

## 1) Executive summary

UNIC Athens can win domestic Greek students by combining proximity, English delivery, and clear recognition/licensure proof while keeping net price predictable. The goal for Fall 2026 is a defensible first-year intake rather than volume bets.

Success markers for Fall 2026 (working targets)
- **Starts:** 120–150 non-medical plus **20–30 Medicine** with disciplined scholarship governance.
- **Net price clarity:** publish a transparent merit grid that lands strong profiles in the **10–25%** range for non-medical; selective Medicine fee credits tied to early decision.
- **Coverage:** consistent counselor/agent touchpoints across **Athens/Attica, Thessaloniki, Patras, and Crete** with 48-hour pre-check SLAs.

Why UNIC Athens wins in Greece
- English-taught degrees with EU recognition and predictable admissions (no numerus fixus).
- Athens location reduces relocation friction for Greek students; clear living-cost guidance keeps comparisons against UK/Netherlands realistic.
- Program portfolio includes high-demand clusters (business, CS/data, Medicine) with employability framing and internship pathways in Greece/Cyprus/EU.

## 2) Portfolio, pricing, and recognition (Athens reference)

Use these anchors in Greece-facing materials; refresh with official sources each intake.

- **Programs (English, Athens):** Accounting, Business Administration, Marketing, Computer Science, Data Science, Medicine (MD). Psychology/Law/Pharmacy run in Greek—keep that explicit.
- **Tuition (EU students):** Non-medical €15,480/year; Medicine €27,000/year. Semester fees and medical insurance charged separately per Medical School policy.
- **Payment options:** 10-month plan at 0% interest for non-medical; semester instalments for Medicine. 4% early-payment discount per semester when paid by add/drop.
- **Cost of attendance (Athens south suburbs):** Low/Mid/High scenarios roughly €10.6k/€14k/€17.5k per year for housing and living; localise for Attica vs regional students.
- **Recognition:** Programs accredited via CYQAA and HAHE; degrees awarded by University of Nicosia. Emphasise professional pathways for Medicine and regulated fields with country-specific notes (Greece, Cyprus, EU).

## 3) Market context and demand signals

- Competitive set: Greek public universities (low tuition, strong brand), UK/Netherlands/Germany for English-taught study, and Cyprus as a nearby alternative. Parents scrutinise net price and employability.
- City concentration: Attica dominates volume; Thessaloniki and Patras have strong science/engineering schools and private college ecosystems; Crete (Heraklion/Chania) hosts IB/private schools with outbound intent.
- Decision drivers: predictable admissions, transparent net price, proof of recognition/licensure, Erasmus/placement opportunities, and campus life in English.

## 4) Priority segments and personas

- **English-medium and IB students (Athens/Thessaloniki):** High English proficiency; compare against UK/Netherlands. Needs: scholarship clarity, internship evidence, and Erasmus links.
- **STEM/CS aspirants (Attica + Patras):** Interested in CS/data/cybersecurity; value industry projects and placement partners. Needs: employability narrative and lab/innovation access.
- **Medicine candidates (nationwide, agent-influenced):** Seek structured clinical training close to home. Needs: admissions calendar, interview prep, licensure roadmap (Greece/Cyprus/EU), and transparent clinical placements.
- **Working-professional upskillers (Athens metro):** Looking for career switches or advancement in business/data. Needs: flexible scheduling, micro-credentials, and employer-facing outcomes.

## 5) Channel and partner plan

Schools
- Target IB and bilingual schools in **Athens, Thessaloniki, Patras, Heraklion/Chania**; use visit templates in `go-to-market/schools-playbook/`.
- Offer masterclasses (CS/data, entrepreneurship) and Medicine Q&A; capture opt-ins via QR/UTM and return pre-checks within 48 hours.
- Translate key assets (COA, recognition/licensure one-pager, merit grid) into Greek for parents.

Agents and fairs
- Engage Greece-based agencies and counseling hubs (e.g., StudyCare, EducationUSA events for credibility) with clear SLAs on lead quality and documentation.
- Use local fair circuits (EducationUSA/US-embassy events, Study in Greece expos, QS/WEF stops in Athens/Thessaloniki) and wrap school visits around those dates.

Digital
- Channels: Meta + Google Search as primary; start with Greek/English split **70/30** for undergrad. Guardrails from `data/marketing/channel-benchmarks.csv` when available.
- Assets: Greek-language landing pages, recognition/licensure FAQs, COA table, and merit calculator; enforce UTM schema by city/partner.

Offerholder and yield
- Publish Greek-language checklists for documents, payment plans, housing, and recognition. Run monthly offer-holder webinars (general + Medicine) with Greek Q&A.
- Maintain weekly interview slots (Medicine) and 48-hour pre-checks during February–April; time deposit pushes around Panhellenic/IB calendars.
