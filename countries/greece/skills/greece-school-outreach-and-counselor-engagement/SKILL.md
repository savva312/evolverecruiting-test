# Greece addendum: school outreach & counselor engagement

Use `/skills/school-outreach-and-counselor-engagement/SKILL.md` for the standard workflow. Apply these Greece-specific rules to keep visits and logging consistent.

## Data sources and logging
- Pull school and counselor targets from `countries/greece/entities/schools/` and related CSVs under `countries/greece/data/entities/`.
- Track interactions in the country data model (`countries/greece/skills/greece-data-model-csvs-and-profiles/`) before syncing to CRM tools.
- Tag visits by metro area (Athens, Thessaloniki, Patras, Crete, regional) to plan routing and follow-up.

## Language and etiquette
- Default to Greek-language outreach and leave-behinds; share English versions for IB or international schools.
- Avoid scheduling in-person visits near Panhellenic exam periods (typically June) or during school breaks; propose virtual briefings instead.
- Viber, phone, and email are common; confirm WhatsApp adoption before logging it as a primary channel.

## Logistics and approvals
- Some public lyceums expect advance approval from school leadership; send agenda and presenter names in Greek.
- Bring concise UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning in Greek, plus translated application steps for counselors to share with families.

## Consent and privacy
- Treat all outreach under GDPR; capture explicit opt-in (source + timestamp) for any counselor or student contact data.
- Limit notes to professional contact fields and interaction history; avoid storing national ID numbers or grades.

## Follow-up cadence
- Send Greek-language recaps within 48 hours of visits/fairs with promised links and application checklists.
- Time application workshops around intake deadlines but avoid heavy pushes during August holidays.
