# Greece addendum: events and fairs

Follow `/skills/events-and-fairs/SKILL.md` for the core workflow. Use this addendum when planning or staffing fairs in Greece.

## Organizer norms and assets
- Expect bilingual (Greek/English) materials; default booth signage to Greek with clear UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning.
- Athens and Thessaloniki fairs may have limited power/Wi‑Fi; bring backup printed sign-up sheets and QR codes.
- School career days often require pre-approved visitor lists—submit IDs/names in advance where requested.

## Lead capture and consent
- Apply GDPR standards: show a short consent statement on tablets/paper forms and log opt-in source/date.
- Collect only necessary fields (name, role, email/phone, city, program interest); avoid birth dates or grades.
- When using organizer badge scans, verify their consent language before importing data into the country data model.

## Staffing and follow-up
- Staff at least one Greek-speaking rep at each booth; have an English-speaking backup for international programs.
- Send Greek-language recaps within 48 hours with promised links; include English attachments for IB/international school leads.
- Log leads in the country data model with event name/date, city, and campus preference (Nicosia vs Athens) before launching sequences.
- Route hot leads to near-term webinars or campus briefings and tag whether interest is for Nicosia or Athens.
