# Outbound mobility — Greece

Last updated: 2025-12-20  
Key sources: UNESCO UIS 2022 outbound mobility dataset; HESA Student 2022/23 open data; DAAD/DESTATIS Wissenschaft weltoffen 2024; CYSTAT Education Statistics 2023; Erasmus+ 2022 annual report.

## 2022 outbound snapshot (UNESCO UIS)
- **Internationally mobile tertiary students:** ~**33,000** Greek-origin students (UIS 2022), implying an outbound mobility rate around **7–8%** of total tertiary enrolment. Source: [UIS Education Data Explorer](https://uis.unesco.org/).
- **Top destinations (UIS 2022):** **United Kingdom (~12k), Germany (~6–7k), Italy (~5k), Bulgaria (~3k), Netherlands (~2.5k), United States (~1k)**. Cyprus also hosts a large Greek cohort but is captured separately in national stats rather than UIS.
- **Cyprus gravity:** CYSTAT’s **Education Statistics 2022/23** show **Greek nationals as the largest non-Cypriot group in RoC universities (≈40% of foreign students)**, reinforcing a strong cultural/linguistic pathway for UNIC Nicosia.
- **Erasmus+ credit mobility:** The 2022 Erasmus+ annual report lists **~16k outbound higher-education mobilities** (studies + traineeships) from Greece, supplying a steady pipeline of students with short-term international experience.

## Implications for UNIC / UNIC Athens
- **Athens positioning:** Greece already exports students at scale, so UNIC Athens must compete with UK/Germany/Italy offers; lean on English-taught delivery, work-integrated learning, and affordability vs UK/Netherlands.
- **Cyprus positioning:** Use the established Greece→Cyprus flow to pitch UNIC Nicosia as a familiar, lower-risk alternative to the UK; emphasize Greek language services and DOATAP-recognized degrees for postgraduate/public-sector eligibility.
- **Program focus:** Prioritize business/management, health/psychology (with clear licensure pathways), ICT/data, and hospitality—fields popular in UK/DE/IT destination data.
- **Channel focus:** Agencies and school counselors with Erasmus+ experience are comfortable handling documentation; pair them with clarity on visa proof-of-funds for Cyprus and no-visa needs for Athens.
