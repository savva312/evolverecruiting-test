# Recognition and licensure

Capture recognition requirements and professional licensure considerations for Greek students studying with UNIC or UNIC Athens. Flag regulated fields (e.g., law, health) and credential evaluation steps.

## Academic recognition (degrees and credits)
- **DOATAP (Hellenic NARIC)** handles academic recognition/equivalence of foreign HE degrees; graduates often need DOATAP decisions for public-sector roles or postgraduate admission in Greece. See regulator extract in [`../data/entities/government_regulators.csv`](../data/entities/government_regulators.csv) (DOATAP row; source: [doatap.gr](https://www.doatap.gr/en/)).
- **Credit transfer:** For students returning to Greek universities, ECTS transcripts from UNIC are generally portable, but receiving institutions decide on recognition; Erasmus+ precedents help if syllabi and learning outcomes are mapped.
- **Private college linkages:** UK-validated private colleges operating in Greece use NARIC/ENIC recognitions from the validating country; UNIC should emphasise its own ENIC acceptance history to ease concerns.

## Professional recognition (regulated professions)
- **SAEP (Council for the Recognition of Professional Qualifications)** implements **EU Directive 2005/36/EC** for professional rights (engineering, architecture, health, psychology). Graduates may need compensatory measures (aptitude test/adaptation period) when seeking Greek licensure. Reference: SAEP portal in [`../data/entities/government_regulators.csv`](../data/entities/government_regulators.csv).
- **Health programmes:** Medical, nursing, physiotherapy, and pharmacy roles require Greek-language competence and registration with sector councils after DOATAP/SAEP steps; clinical placement evidence and internship duration should be documented early.
- **Law:** Foreign LL.B. holders seeking practice in Greece typically complete **Bar training (nomikos askoumenos)** and pass **Bar exams**; non-Greek curricula can trigger course-by-course equivalence checks.
- **Psychology/education:** Greek licensure often needs modules in Greek law/ethics and supervised practice logged; SAEP decisions may impose additional supervised hours.

## Practical implications for UNIC vs UNIC Athens
- Provide **step-by-step licensing outlines** by field (health, engineering, law, psychology) with evidence of past DOATAP/SAEP outcomes where available.
- Maintain **official-language documentation** (Greek translations of diplomas/transcripts, stamped syllabi) to speed DOATAP or SAEP files.
- Set **student expectations** on timelines: SAEP targets ~3 months after complete files; DOATAP decisions can take longer—advise early dossier assembly.
- For Athens delivery, clarify whether programmes are **Cyprus-awarded** (DOATAP route) or **Greek-awarded** (domestic pathway) to prevent confusion on recognition routes.
