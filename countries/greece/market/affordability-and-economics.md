# Affordability and economics

## Affordability signals for Greek students
- **Tuition anchors are low** because public universities charge **€0–€500/year for EU undergraduates** and master’s fees cluster around **€1,000–€6,000** (EACEA National Student Fee and Support Systems 2024/25 — [Eurydice](https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/publications/national-student-fee-and-support-systems-europe-2024-2025)). Students compare private offers against this baseline, so value proof is essential.
- **Private college reference points:** English-taught programmes validated by UK/EU partners in Athens/Thessaloniki often list **€6,000–€9,000/year** tuition (e.g., Deree/American College of Greece [2024/25 fee grid](https://www.acg.edu/admissions/tuition-and-financial-assistance/) and CITY College/University of York Europe Campus [2024 fees](https://york.citycollege.eu/frontend/articles.php?cid=243&p=479)), setting the ceiling most families cite when assessing overseas degrees.
- **Living-cost sensitivity:** Numbeo Jan 2025 data show **Athens monthly costs ≈€800–€900 before rent** ([Numbeo Athens](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Athens)) and **€1,150–€1,300 with a shared city flat**; **Nicosia ≈€850–€950 before rent** ([Numbeo Nicosia](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Nicosia)) and **€1,200–€1,350 with shared rent**, keeping both below UK/Netherlands but above Eastern Europe.
- **Proof-of-funds friction:** Students targeting Cyprus worry about D-visa/residence proof-of-funds; predictable instalments and early-payment discounts help families document liquidity without large lump sums.
- **Erasmus familiarity:** Erasmus+ grant bands for Greece-origin students (2024/25 call) sit around **€450–€600/month** for Group 1/2 hosts ([Programme Guide 2025](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/resources-and-tools/programme-guide)), so families are comfortable treating a semester abroad as a cost offset.

## Financing norms
- **Family-funded first:** Most outbound Greek students rely on **parent savings and remittances**; there is no broad state-backed loan scheme for overseas study (OECD [Education at a Glance 2024](https://www.oecd.org/education/education-at-a-glance/) country note).
- **Bank products are consumer-style:** Major banks (Alpha Bank, Piraeus) market unsecured education/consumer loans with **7–11% variable APR**, guarantor requirements, and 5–8 year terms ([Alpha Bank education loan](https://www.alpha.gr/en/retail/loans/personal-loans/education-loan); [Piraeus \"Express\" personal loan](https://www.piraeusbank.gr/en/idiwtes/daneismoi/katanalwtika-daneia/express-personal-loan))—making staged tuition invoicing important for affordability.
- **Scholarships stack, but expectations are moderate:** Merit bands of **10–30%** plus sibling/early-payment discounts mirror what private colleges publish domestically; full-ride expectations are rare outside need-based IKY (State Scholarships Foundation) schemes.
- **Work constraints:** Students assume limited term-time work rights in Cyprus (20 hrs/wk in-service sectors) and modest campus work in Greece, so budgeting leans on upfront affordability rather than earnings.

## Positioning implications for UNIC vs UNIC Athens
- **Price parity and transparency:** Publish EU-rate tuition with a **clear 10–30% merit grid** and pre-approved early-payment options to keep proof-of-funds manageable.
- **Living-cost messaging:** Lead with a **total monthly cost band (€1,150–€1,350)** for Athens/Nicosia versus UK/Netherlands peers; back it with Numbeo or embassy estimates to counter perceptions of “big-city inflation.”
- **Payment cadence:** Offer **semester or monthly instalments** aligned to Greek family cashflow (summer savings, remittances) and accept common payment rails (SEPA, cards) to avoid FX fees.
- **Scholarship stacking rules:** Mirror domestic private-college practice (merit + sibling + early payment, with caps) and communicate caps early to avoid last-minute attrition.
