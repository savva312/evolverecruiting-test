# Greece education system

## Governance and overall structure
- **Centralized control:** The Ministry of Education, Religious Affairs and Sports (MoERAS) sets curriculum, staffing, and assessment policy across public and private schools. The National Organization for the Certification of Qualifications & Vocational Guidance (EOPPEP) oversees post-secondary VET certification, and the Hellenic Authority for Higher Education (HAHE) quality-assures universities. (Source: MoERAS overview; Eurydice – Greece country profile)
- **Compulsory education:** Ages **4–15** (1 year pre-primary + 6 years primary + 3 years lower secondary/Gymnasio). Students typically enter primary at age 6 and Gymnasio at 12. (Source: Eurydice – Greece)
- **Upper secondary split:** After Gymnasio, learners choose **General Lykeio** (academic) or **Vocational Lykeio (EPAL)**; private schools may also offer International Baccalaureate or other foreign curricula but the public pathway is Lykeio/EPAL.

## Stages, grades, and typical ages
- **Pre-primary (Nipiagogeio):** 1 year compulsory at age 4–5; focus on readiness and socialization.
- **Primary (Dimotiko, Grades 1–6, ages ~6–12):** National curriculum with descriptive assessment in early grades; numerically graded 1–10 in upper primary.
- **Lower secondary (Gymnasio, Grades 7–9, ages ~12–15):** General curriculum; grading scale **0–20**, pass ≥10; successful completion earns the **Gymnasio certificate** required for Lykeio/EPAL entry.
- **Upper secondary (General Lykeio, Grades 10–12, ages ~15–18):** Orientation groups start in Grade 11 (Humanities; Sciences/Health; Economics & Computer Science); Grade 12 deepens specialization and prepares for Panhellenic exams. Graduates receive the **General Lykeio Apolytirio**.
- **Upper secondary (Vocational Lykeio / EPAL, Grades 10–12):** Mix of general education and technical streams (e.g., health, ICT, engineering, maritime). Three-year cycle yields a **Vocational Lykeio Apolytirio** plus a specialty diploma; an optional **4th “apprenticeship year”** (Metalykeio) combines paid work-based learning with lab practice and leads to an **EQF Level 5 qualification** after passing EOPPEP certification.

## Credentials and progression
- **Apolytirio** (school-leaving certificate) is awarded by both General Lykeio and EPAL upon passing school-based assessments; it certifies secondary completion and carries a grade on a 0–20 scale.
- **EPAL specialty diploma** documents technical field; graduates may either pursue the apprenticeship year, sit certification exams for EQF 5, or take Panhellenic exams on the EPAL track.
- **Post-secondary non-tertiary VET (IEK):** Public and private **Institutes of Vocational Training (IEK)** offer 2-year programs (EQF 5 after certification), admitting Gymnasio or Lykeio graduates depending on field.
- **Higher education:** Public universities and the Hellenic Open University are free of tuition for Greek/EU students and are accessed primarily via Panhellenic results; private colleges operate via foreign university franchising, granting non-public Greek titles but EU-recognized professional rights in regulated fields through directive-based recognition.

## National examinations and assessment
- **School-based exams:** Internal exams contribute to Apolytirio grades in both General Lykeio and EPAL; promotion/pass requires a weighted average ≥10/20.
- **Panhellenic Examinations (Panellinies):** National standardized exams in Grade 12 used for university admission. General Lykeio students sit four written subjects aligned to their orientation group. Scores combine with the Apolytirio average (currently weighted via “barytites” coefficients) to produce admission points (mória).
- **EPAL Panhellenic track:** EPAL candidates take four subjects (two general, two specialty) with a dedicated quota (~5–10% annually) for universities and technological institutions; they may alternatively enter higher VET (IEK) without Panhellenic scores.
- **Apallaktikes examinations:** Make-up/resit exams (usually late August–September) for Lykeio/EPAL students who failed specific subjects; needed to secure the Apolytirio but **do not** replace Panhellenic exams for university admission.

## Grading scales
- **Primary (upper grades):** 1–10, pass typically ≥5.
- **Gymnasio & Lykeio (general/vocational):** 0–20 scale; **≥10** required for promotion and Apolytirio issuance.
- **Higher education:** 0–10 scale; **5** is the minimum pass for modules and degree conferral.

## University and tertiary entry routes
- **Main route:** Panhellenic exams + Apolytirio grade; students rank programs nationwide, and places are allocated through centralized clearing using admission points.
- **Repeat/transfer options:** A **“10% route”** lets recent graduates reuse prior-year Panhellenic scores without re-examination for a small quota; limited transfer rights exist based on social/academic criteria set by MoERAS.
- **EPAL progression:** EPAL graduates can (a) use the EPAL Panhellenic quota to enter universities/technological schools, (b) pursue the apprenticeship year and sit EOPPEP certification for EQF 5, or (c) enroll in IEK programs.
- **Special categories:** Separate quotas for students with disabilities, athletes, members of the Hellenic diaspora, and music/arts schools may adjust required scores but still rely on Panhellenic subject performance.
