# Greece exams and academic calendar (recruiting view)

Sourced rundown of Greece’s secondary exit/tertiary entrance exams and the school calendar for the next 12–24 months. Focuses on what matters for UNIC and UNIC Athens outreach, intake phasing, and offer timing.

## Panhellenic examinations (main pathway)

| Track | Administering body | Purpose & components | Timing (latest confirmed pattern) | Notes for UNIC/UNIC Athens |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Geniko Lykeio (ΓΕΛ) Panhellenic exams | Ministry of Education (ΥΠΑΙΘ) via National Exams Org (ΕΟΕ) | 4 core subjects by orientation group (Humanities, Sciences/Health, Economics/IT). Score: 0–20 per subject; weighted nationwide ranking used for Greek public HE admission. | Written papers early–mid June; special subjects (drawing, music) mid–late June; results early July; preference form (Μηχανογραφικό) submission July; limited September special session for documented absences. Schedule mirrors 2024 circular issued 27 Mar 2024. | UNIC/UNIC Athens can accept Apolytirio (school leaving cert) + Panhellenic marks as proof of rigor; position June as blackout and July as conversion window once results land. |
| EPAL (vocational lyceum) Panhellenic exams | ΥΠΑΙΘ / ΕΟΕ | 4 subjects (Greek, Math, 2 specialization). Scored similarly; separate quotas for TEIs/AEIs in Greece. | Start typically a few days before ΓΕΛ (early June); completes by late June; results early July. 2024 bulletin (27 Mar 2024) had EPAL starting 4 June. | Small but relevant for applied programs (IT, engineering tech). Treat June as blackout; outreach resumes after results. |

### Registrations and paperwork
- **Application–Declaration** (δήλωση) for Panhellenic participation submitted at schools **late Feb–late Mar** (2024 deadline 29 Mar per ΥΠΑΙΘ press note). Late changes rarely accepted.
- **Subject combination selection** finalized with the declaration; impacts which universities are eligible.
- **Apolytirio exams** (school-based) run mid–late May before Panhellenic; necessary for the leaving certificate even if targeting foreign universities.

### Alternative / internationally benchmarked routes
- **IB Diploma** (offered in select private/international schools in Athens/Thessaloniki): exams **first three weeks of May**; results **early July**. UNIC/UNIC Athens accept standard IB thresholds; good pipeline for English-taught programs.
- **GCE A-Levels** (British schools/centers): main sittings **May–June** with results **mid–August**; October/January resits limited. Suitable for students already abroad-facing.
- **SAT/ACT + APs**: SAT offered **7x/year** (Aug, Oct, Nov, Dec, Mar, May, Jun) by College Board centers; score availability ~2 weeks post-test. Useful for US-bound applicants considering Cyprus/Greece as alternatives.
- **French Baccalauréat / German Abitur**: niche but appear in international schools; timelines align with May–July results—plan individualized follow-up.

## Academic calendar and month-by-month recruiting map (2025 focus, with 2026 expected to mirror)

The Greek school year runs **11 September–30 June**, with teaching for Lyceum ending **mid–May** and exam periods through June. Below is a recruiting-oriented calendar; exact Panhellenic dates are confirmed annually by ΥΠΑΙΘ but follow stable month windows.

| Month (2025) | Key school/university events | Recruiting implications |
| --- | --- | --- |
| January | Classes resume after **24 Dec–7 Jan** Christmas/New Year break. Mock exams in many Lyceia; counseling meetings for Panhellenic declarations. | Re-engage counselors; seed UNIC/UNIC Athens options before declarations lock. Avoid first week back. |
| February | Teaching ongoing; **Clean Monday** holiday (3 Mar 2025 week). Schools prep declaration forms. | Digital nurture; counselor briefings; light school visits outside holiday week. |
| March | **Panhellenic application-declaration window** (late Feb–late Mar per 2024 circular). National holiday **25 Mar**. | Support subject/track fit messaging; avoid 25 Mar events. Capture leads before declarations close. |
| April | Teaching continues until Orthodox Easter break (**14–27 Apr 2025** window around 20 Apr Easter). | Treat Easter fortnight as blackout. Push pre-break webinars; collect interest for May follow-up. |
| May | Classes end for Lyceia **mid-May**; **school-based Apolytirio exams** mid–late May. Labor Day **1 May**. | Minimal school access; focus on digital campaigns and direct student follow-up. Prep post-results conversion assets. |
| June | **Panhellenic written exams**: EPAL early June; ΓΕΛ early–mid June; special subjects mid–late June. | Blackout for school visits. Keep light-touch digital; schedule webinars for late June graduates. |
| July | **Results published early July**; **preference form (Μηχανογραφικό)** submission July; IB results early July; some A-Level AS results. | High-intent conversion window: offer turnaround and scholarship packaging. Target students who fall short of Greek HE choices. |
| August | A-Level results **mid-August**; SAT (Aug) session; many schools on break. | Run “second-chance” campaigns; highlight September/October start dates and visa timelines. |
| September | New school year starts **11 Sep**; orientation weeks. **Special Panhellenic session** (for candidates excused from June) often held early September. | Restart counselor outreach; onboard new Grade 11/12 leads; position autumn intakes. Monitor special-session outcomes for late deciders. |
| October | Normal teaching; **28 Oct Ohi Day** closure. | Resume school visits except around 28 Oct. Launch fall open days. |
| November | Teaching steady; **17 Nov Polytechnic anniversary** events/closures in some schools. | Keep visits early/late month; continue digital nurture. |
| December | Classes until **23 Dec**; **Christmas break 24 Dec–7 Jan**. | Pre-break reminders; collect inquiries for January follow-up. |

## Outreach and offer timing guidance
- Treat **mid-May through late June** as high-prep/blackout for Panhellenic candidates; avoid heavy outreach and prioritize asynchronous content.
- **Early July** is critical: mobilize fast-offer workflows and scholarship guidance for students recalibrating after results.
- **IB/A-Level** candidates often finalize destinations **July–August**; align merit aid messaging with their result release weeks.
- Keep **Easter and Panhellenic weeks** free of in-person events; schedule counselor roundtables in **September–October** and **January–February** when access is strongest.
- Highlight UNIC/UNIC Athens flexibility on English proficiency and alternative credentials (IB/A-Levels/SAT) in March–April while Panhellenic choices are being set.
