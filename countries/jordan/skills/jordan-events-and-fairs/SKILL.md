# Jordan addendum: events and fairs

Follow `/skills/events-and-fairs/SKILL.md` for the core workflow. Apply these Jordan-specific considerations.

## Organizer norms and assets
- Prioritize Amman fairs and school career days; confirm booth power/Wi‑Fi and carry offline sign-up sheets as backup.
- Provide Arabic-first signage and flyers; keep English versions available for international programs and clearly distinguish UNIC Nicosia vs UNIC Athens positioning.

## Lead capture and consent
- Display a short consent statement (Arabic/English) on tablets or paper forms; log opt-in source and date.
- Collect minimal fields: name, role, school, email/phone/WhatsApp (if consented), city, and program interest. Avoid capturing national IDs or grades.
- When using organizer badge scans, confirm the consent language before importing data into `countries/jordan/data/`.

## Staffing and follow-up
- Staff at least one Arabic-speaking representative; ensure gender-appropriate staffing for single-gender schools where required.
- Send Arabic recaps within 48 hours, tagging the event and city; include English attachments for international tracks.
- Log leads in the country data model with event name/date, city, and campus interest (Nicosia vs Athens) before launching sequences.
- Route hot leads to near-term webinars timed after Tawjihi results; tag event names and campus preference in follow-up sequences.
