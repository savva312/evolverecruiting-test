# Housing and arrival

- **Last updated:** 2026-04-12
- **Owner:** Housing Lead (campus) + Admissions Ops; Support: Country Recruiter Jordan

## Assumptions (visa/residence)

**Guardrail:** This section is for operational planning only and is not legal advice. Always tell students and parents that only the relevant embassy/consulate or immigration authority can confirm visa/entry and residence requirements.

- Most Jordanian and other non-EU passport holders will need a **visa or entry permit before travel** plus a **residence-permit/registration step after arrival**, with different rules for Cyprus (UNIC Nicosia) and Greece (UNIC Athens). Do not promise that anyone can “arrive on a visit stamp and fix status later”.
- EU/EEA/Swiss passport holders living in Jordan usually enter Cyprus and Greece without a visa but must **register residence** for stays beyond three months; housing proof and enrolment letters are central for these steps.
- Housing and arrival timelines must work **backwards from the student’s immigration route**: acceptance + deposit → visa/entry permit (where required) → residence/registration after arrival. Avoid recommending travel before students have followed the authority’s instructions.
- Insurance: students must have health insurance that meets the requirements of the destination country (public system, private policy, or university-arranged cover); do not describe specific policies as “sufficient” without checking against current rules.
- If assumptions change after a recency check, update this doc, checklists, and webinar slides within 24 hours.

## Housing playbook

- **UNIC Nicosia:** University-managed dorms + vetted private flats. Send housing offer within 48h of deposit; hold room 10 days pending contract signature.
- **UNIC Athens:** Private housing partners + student hubs; provide neighborhood guide (Ampelokipi, Pangrati, Neos Kosmos) with price bands and commute times.
- **Process owners:** Housing Lead issues contracts; Finance confirms deposit; Recruiter keeps students warm with bilingual FAQs.

## Travel windows

- **Fall intake:** Arrivals recommended 10–14 days before classes; Medicine arrivals 14–21 days to finish medical checks.
- **Spring intake:** Arrivals 7–10 days prior; schedule campus SIM/bank booth for first weekend.
- Avoid booking until housing allocation + acceptance letter issued; share cancellation-friendly airline options.

## Pre-departure checklist (Jordanian offerholders)

- Passport/ID valid 6+ months; copies uploaded to portal.
- Deposit paid + receipt saved (owner: Admissions Ops).
- Housing contract signed; first rent/security paid (owner: Housing Lead).
- Insurance proof (EHIC or private) uploaded; if not, opt into university policy.
- Flights booked inside recommended window; arrival form submitted with flight details (owner: student; verified by Recruiter).
- Academic docs translated if needed (diploma, transcripts); originals packed.
- Join campus WhatsApp/Discord; RSVP to airport pickup (if offered).

## Arrival-week tasks

- **Day 0–1:** Airport pickup or taxi instructions; check-in to housing; Wi-Fi + SIM guidance.
- **Day 2–3:** Campus ID issuance; tuition payment confirmation; schedule orientation.
- **Day 4–7:** Residence registration (EU): proof of address, bank statement, enrollment letter; book appointment slots via Housing desk. Non-EU: finalize biometric/permit steps per consulate checklist.
- **Week 2:** Health insurance validation; open bank account (if needed); attend club fair; register with local embassy (optional).

## Residence/permit workflows (high-level, always confirm locally)

- **Cyprus (Nicosia):**
  - EU/EEA/Swiss students: in most cases no entry visa is required, but they must apply for a residence certificate (“yellow slip”) using passport/ID, enrolment letter, lease, photos, and proof of financial means within the timeframe set by the Civil Registry and Migration Department.
  - Non-EU students (including most Jordanian passport holders): arrive with the appropriate student visa/entry permit and then complete temporary residence-permit steps (biometrics, medical checks, etc.) as instructed by the Migration Department and UNIC visa team.
- **Greece (Athens):**
  - EU/EEA/Swiss students: can usually enter without a visa but must register residence with the local authority for stays beyond three months, providing lease, enrolment, and insurance proof.
  - Non-EU students (including most Jordanian passport holders): must enter on a national visa for studies (Type D) and then apply for a residence permit within the deadlines given by the competent authority (for example, the Decentralised Administration of Attica).
- Add an “Assumption check” note on each confirmation email to verify current municipal requirements and link to the latest visa/residence doc for the student’s campus.

## Risks and mitigations

- **Late housing allocation:** keep 10% buffer rooms; publish weekly availability dashboard; redirect to vetted private listings if under 14 days to arrival.
- **Non-EU visa delays:** identify non-EU residents at offer stage; fast-track document issuance within 48h; provide consulate booking script.
- **Athens vs Nicosia confusion:** include campus tag on every email subject; distinct arrival forms; separate WhatsApp groups per campus.
