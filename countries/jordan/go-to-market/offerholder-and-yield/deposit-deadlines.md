# Deposit deadlines

- **Last updated:** 2026-04-12
- **Owners:** Admissions Ops (UNIC Nicosia); Admissions Ops (UNIC Athens) — accountable; Country Recruiter Jordan — support

## Jordan-specific principles

- Align to Jordanian secondary exams (May–June) and diploma release (mid–July); allow families ~2 weeks post-results to lock seats.
- Medicine/limited seats require faster deposits to signal scarcity; business/CS allow slightly longer.
- Deposit confirms eligibility to issue final acceptance/visa support; outstanding documents pause visa kits.

## January/spring intake cadence

- Shorten by ~3–5 days because students already graduated.
- Target Day 10 deadline for all programs; single escalation on Day 14 before seat release on Day 17.

## Visa/arrival dependencies (Jordan cohorts) — do not assume “visa-free”

**Compliance note:** This section is guidance for planning only, not legal advice. Requirements can change by passport, residence status, intake, and embassy/consulate. Always ask students to confirm their route with the relevant embassy/consulate or immigration authority before travel or booking non-refundable services.

### Branch 1 — Jordanian citizens (default non-EU passport)

**UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus)**
- Jordanian passport holders are generally treated as visa-required third-country nationals for Cyprus. For full-time studies, they typically need a **student visa or entry permit before travel**, then must apply for a **temporary residence permit** after arrival.
- Deposit timing must protect: issuing the acceptance letter and any visa-support documents (for example, proof of paid tuition deposit and, where requested, housing confirmation) early enough to meet embassy/consulate document deadlines and appointment dates.

Official sources (last_verified: 2026-04-12):
- EU Immigration Portal — Student in Cyprus (entry visa/entry permit plus residence permit after arrival): https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-cyprus_en
- Cyprus Civil Registry and Migration Department — Students (entry and residence conditions for third-country-national students): https://www.gov.cy/mip-md/en/documents/students/
- Cyprus MFA — Embassy of the Republic of Cyprus in the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan (contact point for Jordan-based applicants): https://www.gov.cy/mfa/en/jordan/

**UNIC Athens (Greece)**
- Jordanian passport holders are generally visa-required for Greece. For degree studies, they usually need to apply for a **national visa for studies (Type D)** at the competent Greek embassy/consulate (for example, the Embassy of Greece in Amman) and then apply for a **residence permit for studies** after arrival.
- Deposit timing must protect: issuing the unconditional acceptance/enrolment letter and any required fee-payment receipts in time to support the national visa application and residence-permit steps.

Official sources (last_verified: 2026-04-12):
- EU Immigration Portal — Student in Greece (long-stay visa and residence permit for studies): https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-greece_en
- Greece — residence permits for studies (Law 4251/2014, studies at higher education institutions): https://apdattikis.gov.gr/en/process/6-1-1-studies-at-higher-education-institutions-article-33-law-4251-14-issuance/
- Greece in the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan — Visas (Embassy of Greece in Amman, visa section): https://www.mfa.gr/missionsabroad/en/jordan-en/visas

### Branch 2 — EU/EEA/Swiss citizens resident in Jordan

**UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus)**
- EU/EEA/Swiss citizens do not generally need an entry visa for Cyprus but must **register their residence** and obtain a residence document when staying longer than three months. Proof of enrolment and health insurance are core requirements.
- Deposit timing must protect: issuing enrolment/acceptance confirmation quickly so students can complete registration soon after arrival.

**UNIC Athens (Greece)**
- Similarly, EU/EEA/Swiss citizens can usually enter Greece without a visa but must **register their residence** with local authorities for stays over three months, again using proof of enrolment and insurance.
- Deposit timing must protect: issuing the necessary university documents early enough to avoid registration delays.

Official EU guidance (last_verified: 2026-04-12):
- Residence formalities for EU citizens in Cyprus (registration when staying longer than three months): see current “Living in another EU country — residence rights” guidance from the European Commission.
- Residence formalities for EU citizens in Greece: same EU-level guidance; always cross-check with local authorities in the student’s destination city.

### Branch 3 — Other non-EU residents in Jordan (non-Jordanian passports)

Treat these as **passport-driven third-country cases**. Do not assume visa-free entry, even for short stays.

**UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus)**
- Many non-EU passports require a visa or entry permit before travel plus a temporary residence permit after arrival for studies. Requirements differ by nationality and by the Cyprus embassy/consulate processing the case.
- Deposit timing must protect: issuing acceptance and (when requested) proof of tuition payment and housing confirmation early enough for the student’s specific embassy/consulate checklist and appointment date.

**UNIC Athens (Greece)**
- For most non-EU passports, long-term study normally requires a national visa for studies (Type D) before travel and a residence-permit application soon after arrival.
- Deposit timing must protect: issuance of unconditional acceptance/enrolment documentation and payment receipts needed for the visa and residence-permit process.

Official starting points (last_verified: 2026-04-12):
- EU Immigration Portal — Student in Cyprus: https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-cyprus_en
- EU Immigration Portal — Student in Greece: https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-greece_en

**Reminder:** For all branches, the **embassy/consulate or immigration authority handling the case is the final authority** on visa/entry and residence rules, required documents, and processing times. UNIC cannot guarantee visa outcomes or appointment availability, and deposit extensions must not be promised as a way to “solve” immigration constraints.

## Escalation rules and channels

- **Risk triggers:** no payment proof by Day 14 (Day 10 Medicine) or missed WhatsApp read for 72h.
- **Tier 1:** Country Recruiter call/WhatsApp + parent call.
- **Tier 2:** Admissions Manager email from official inbox + SMS.
- **Tier 3:** Director note warning of seat release; copy agent (if applicable).
- Track all touches in CRM with timestamps; only Director can approve extensions beyond seat release window.
