# Exams and academic calendar

Jordan’s secondary exit pathways centre on the national Tawjihi plus a growing set of international options (IB, A-Levels, SAT). Recruiters need to map exam windows to outreach and offer timing, especially around June–August results and fall university admissions.

## Tawjihi (General Secondary Education Certificate)
- **Administering body:** Ministry of Education (MoE); grading on 0–100 scale with stream-specific weightings. Streams include **scientific**, **literary/arts**, and vocational tracks (industrial, IT/commercial/hotel, agriculture, health). Students apply to universities via the Unified Admission portal using Tawjihi averages. *Source: Jordan MoE Tawjihi regulations and 2024 session circulars.*
- **Sessions and timing:** MoE typically runs a **main session mid-June to early July** with results in **mid-July**; a **supplementary/resit session in early August** with results later in August. Registration commonly opens Nov–Jan with a late-spring top-up window. *Source: MoE announcements for 2023–2024 cycles; 2025/26 dates pending — timelines below extrapolate from this pattern and should be refreshed once MoE posts the official calendar.*
- **Format:** Written exams across 12th-grade subjects with practical components for vocational streams. Students may resit up to 4–5 subjects to improve averages.
- **Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens:** Tawjihi scores drive public/private university admission and scholarship eligibility. Results release in July/August is the decisive moment to convert offer-holders and late applicants; resit outcomes in August can trigger late deferrals or seat shifts.

## International alternatives (common in private/international schools)
- **IB Diploma Programme:** May and November exam sessions administered by the IB; **results released ~6 July (May session) and early January (Nov session)**. Two-year programme; CAS/EE/Internal Assessments run throughout Grade 12. *Source: IB Organisation global exam calendar.*
- **GCE A-Levels / IGCSE (Cambridge & Pearson Edexcel via British Council Jordan):** Major written sessions **May/June** (results mid-August) and **October/November** (results mid-January). Coursework deadlines typically February (for May/June) and September (for Oct/Nov). *Source: British Council Jordan exam timetables and Cambridge/Edexcel board calendars.*
- **SAT (digital):** College Board monthly windows commonly offered **Aug, Oct, Nov, Dec, Mar, May, Jun** at Jordan test centres; registration closes ~1 month prior, score release ~2 weeks after. *Source: College Board global SAT calendar.*
- **Advanced Placement (AP):** Limited footprint; main exams **first half of May** with scores in July.

## Outreach and timing implications
- **Peak conversion moments:** Tawjihi results (mid-Jul) and resits (Aug) drive sudden eligibility shifts; have **rapid-offer and scholarship decision scripts** ready for high achievers and borderline resit candidates.
- **Counsellor engagement windows:** Early **Sep–Nov** (before midterms) and **Feb–Mar** (start of second semester) are the least congested periods for school visits; avoid deep Ramadan/Eid weeks and June exam windows.
- **International-school cycle:** IB/A-Level cohorts need predicted-grade letters by **Oct–Nov** (for early applications) and **Feb–Mar** (for regular); align with **IB IA/A-Level coursework** pressure points to keep counsellor goodwill.
- **Testing support:** Offer SAT prep/webinars aligned to **Aug/Oct/Dec/Mar/May** sittings; for Tawjihi students, emphasise **foundation/bridging** options timed to **Aug/Sept enrolment**.
- **Offer and deposit cadence:** Issue **conditional offers** on predicted Tawjihi/IB/A-Level grades by **Mar–Apr**; shift to **firm offers** immediately after **mid-Jul** Tawjihi results and **mid-Aug** A-Level/IGCSE results; keep a **late-August intake pathway** for resit candidates.
