# Affordability and economics

## Affordability signals for Jordanian students
- **Segmented ability to pay:** West Amman private/IB/A-Level schools show the clearest ability to pay €10k–€27k/year (medicine-capable); Irbid/Aqaba families are more price-sensitive and often seek scholarships or part-time work options.
- **Scholarship norms:** Families look for transparent **10–30% merit bands** and smaller stackable perks (family/early payment). Full-ride expectations are uncommon outside need-based NGO schemes.
- **Currency and budgeting:** The **JOD-USD peg keeps exchange-rate risk low**, but families want tuition quoted in EUR with JOD equivalents for planning. Predictable installment plans reduce anxiety around proof-of-funds for Cyprus/Greece visas.
- **Cost-of-living framing:** Athens is generally perceived as slightly cheaper than Nicosia and materially below UK/Netherlands tiers; highlight realistic monthly budgets and housing options.
- **Mobility offsets:** Erasmus+ stipends and part-time campus work (where permitted) are treated as secondary offsets, not primary funding.

## Financing norms
- **State mechanisms are domestic-focused:** MoHESR/loan schemes largely fund public/local study; overseas degrees are typically **self-funded via family savings/remittances** (Gulf employment is common) or employer sponsorship for postgrads.
- **Bank financing exists but needs guardrails:** Major banks (e.g., Arab Bank, Housing Bank, Cairo Amman Bank) market personal/education loans; families prefer short tenors and clear repayment calendars. Co-signers are standard.
- **Stacking clarity matters:** Parents value the ability to combine **merit + sibling + early-payment** incentives; publish caps and renewal criteria to avoid perceived bait-and-switch.
- **Payment cadence:** Installments aligned to salary/remittance cycles (quarterly/semester) and deposit deferrals that respect Tawjihi release dates reduce churn after offer.

## Positioning implications for UNIC vs UNIC Athens
- Lead with **transparent scholarship grids** in Arabic and English, showing JOD/EUR side-by-side. Pre-approve **early-payment discounts** that directly lower visa proof-of-funds.
- For high-fee programs (medicine/health), anchor conversations in **Tier 1 West Amman schools and King’s Academy** where fee tolerance is proven; pair with financing options for postgrad upskillers.
- Keep **Athens vs Nicosia** messaging consistent on price/recognition; use Athens when cost-of-living is a blocker, and Nicosia when students want a more established campus footprint or distance-learning add-ons.
