# Jordan education system overview

## Governance and system map
- **Ministry of Education (MoE)** oversees pre-primary through secondary (K–12), including curriculum, school licensing, and the **General Secondary Education Certificate Examination (Tawjihi)**.[^moe]
- **Ministry of Higher Education & Scientific Research (MoHESR)** and its **Unified Admission Unit** handle public university intake, set minimum Tawjihi averages, and regulate credit transfer/equivalency for private universities and foreign credentials.[^mohesr]
- School age bands: *KG1–2* (ages 4–5, expanding to universal coverage), *Basic education grades 1–10* (compulsory, ages ~6–15), and *Upper secondary grades 11–12* (academic or vocational, ages ~16–18).[^education_jordan]

## Structure and tracks
- **Basic education (G1–10):** Comprehensive national curriculum with Arabic-medium instruction and mandatory English. Students sit school-based assessments; completion is required to progress.[^education_jordan]
- **Upper secondary (G11–12):** Two-year program culminating in Tawjihi. Students select a **track at the end of G10**:
  - **Academic streams:** Scientific (math/physics/biology/chemistry), Literary/Humanities (Arabic, English, history, civics, geography), and emerging ICT/business variants.
  - **Vocational/technical streams:** Industrial, agricultural, hospitality, home economics, health, and newer applied/BTEC-aligned options run with sector councils; includes substantial practical hours and work-based learning.
- **International/private pathways:** IB Diploma Programme (IBDP), Cambridge/Edexcel IGCSE-AS/A Levels, American high school + SAT/AP, French Baccalauréat, and other international curricula operate in licensed private schools; MoE provides equivalency rules for Tawjihi parity.[^equiv]

## Credentials and assessments
- **Tawjihi (General Secondary Education Certificate):**
  - National high-stakes exam administered across G11–12 subjects; students earn a **composite score out of 100** based on weighted subject marks.[^tawjihi]
  - Pass threshold commonly **50%** in each subject with stream-specific weighting; cumulative average determines university eligibility and seat allocation.
  - Grade bands typically used by schools/universities: **Excellent (90–100), Very Good (80–89), Good (70–79), Fair (60–69), Pass (50–59), Fail (<50).**
  - Retakes permitted to improve the cumulative average; results remain valid for unified admission in the year issued, with private universities often accepting prior-year scores.
- **Vocational certificate:** Graduates of vocational tracks sit the **vocational Tawjihi**; successful candidates may enter labor-market programs or take the **Comprehensive Exam (al-shamel)** via Al-Balqa Applied University to bridge into applied bachelor programs.[^vocational]
- **International alternatives:** Universities accept accredited equivalents (IBDP, A Levels, AP/SAT, French Bac, Abitur, etc.) once certified by MoE for equivalency. Subject minima (e.g., HL sciences for Medicine, A-level math/physics for Engineering) mirror Tawjihi prerequisites.

## Grading and progression
- **School grading:** Most public and private schools use a **0–100 numeric scale**; **50%** is the standard pass mark in basic and secondary education.[^tawjihi]
- **Progression points:** G10 completion → track selection; successful Tawjihi/vocational certificate → eligibility for higher education (university, community college, or applied diplomas).

## Higher-education entry mechanics
- **Unified Admission (public universities):**
  - Seats allocated centrally by MoHESR using Tawjihi averages plus applicant preferences.
  - **Minimum Tawjihi averages (recent practice):** ≈**85–90% Medicine**, **80% Dentistry/Pharmacy/Engineering**, **75% Nursing/Health Allied**, **65% most STEM/Business programs**, **60–65% Humanities/Sharia/Law**. Cut-offs adjust annually based on demand and seat supply.[^mohesr]
- **Private universities:** Set their own thresholds within MoHESR limits (often **≈60%+** for most majors; higher for health/engineering) and may admit on earlier Tawjihi sittings or recognized international credentials.
- **Vocational to degree pathways:** Vocational Tawjihi + Comprehensive Exam enables entry to applied bachelor tracks; otherwise graduates access community colleges, TVET diplomas, or regulated apprenticeship schemes.
- **International credential evaluation:** Applicants submit MoE equivalency; universities convert IB (typically ≥24 with required HL subjects), A-levels (usually 2–3 A-levels including prerequisites), or U.S. high school + SAT/AP subject tests to match Tawjihi minima and subject coverage.

## Implications for UNIC recruiting
- Anchor messaging to **clear Tawjihi minima** and provide equivalency tables for IB/A-level/AP to de-risk admissibility.
- Highlight **vocational bridging options** (community college + credit transfer) for applicants from vocational Tawjihi or Comprehensive Exam backgrounds.
- Emphasize **English-medium readiness** (10 years of mandatory English instruction) but plan for IELTS/TOEFL support, especially for vocational graduates.
