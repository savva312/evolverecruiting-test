# Medicine / MD competitors (Jordan)

Last updated: 2025-12-22

This list prioritizes Medicine/MD options that Jordanian applicants commonly compare against UNIC Nicosia and UNIC Athens, with an emphasis on English-taught pathways. It is designed for counselling (not as a legal or licensure guide).

## Counselling guardrails (use every time)
- **Always click sources:** admissions tests, entry requirements, and fees change by intake and applicant category.
- **Tuition:** only quote a number when you can point to a published fee source for the same intake/track (otherwise use “published on request”).
- **Clinical language:** “English-taught” often still requires the local language for patient contact in clinical years—verify on the program site.
- **Recognition/licensure:** confirm with the destination regulator and Jordan’s relevant authorities; do not assume portability.

## Snapshot (Jordan decision patterns)
- **Jordan-local anchors:** public medical schools are the cost/competition reference point.
- **Regional alternatives:** Cyprus, Lebanon, and Georgia show up frequently because travel and family support are easier.
- **EU/CEE English tracks:** Bulgaria/Romania/Hungary/Czechia/Poland are common due to established English tracks and admissions pathways.

## Practical positioning reminders (Jordan)
- For families comparing **Jordan public** vs **international**, anchor on *seat competitiveness* and *clinical mobility* (not only tuition).
- For families comparing **regional** (Cyprus/Lebanon/Georgia), emphasize *travel convenience + student support*, and re-check *licensure recognition* early.
- For families comparing **CEE/EU**, proactively set expectations about *local-language clinical years* and administrative timelines (visa/residency, documentation, and clinical placement rules).
