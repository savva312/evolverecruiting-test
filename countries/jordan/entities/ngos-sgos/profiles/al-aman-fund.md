# Al Aman Fund for the Future of Orphans

## Overview
- Non-profit founded in 2003 by HM Queen Rania to support orphaned youth transitioning to independence.
- Provides comprehensive support: university/vocational scholarships, living stipends, and life-skills coaching.
- Relevance: sizable pipeline of motivated students who need funding, housing, and psychosocial support to succeed in higher education.

## Programs and reach
- **University and college scholarships** — covers tuition/fees and provides monthly stipends for living expenses until graduation.
- **Vocational training sponsorships** — short-cycle programs with employability coaching.
- **Case management and counseling** — social workers track academic progress and wellbeing; partners with employers for internships.
- Reach: national with concentration in Amman/Zarqa; has served 3,000+ beneficiaries since inception across higher-ed and vocational tracks.

## Target students
- Orphaned youth aged 18–24 pursuing diplomas, bachelor’s degrees, or vocational programs.
- Students needing wraparound support (housing, stipends, counseling) to persist through graduation.
