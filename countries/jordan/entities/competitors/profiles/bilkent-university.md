# Bilkent University (Ankara, Turkey)

- **last_verified:** 2025-12-20  
- **sources:**  
  - Bilkent University tuition fees, 2025–2026 (950,000 TL annual fee for new YKS admits; semester instalment schedules by cohort; 50% scholarship halves tuition). https://w3.bilkent.edu.tr/bilkent/tuition-fees/  
  - University overview and research focus (private non-profit research university in Ankara; founded 1984; consistently top-ranked in Turkey). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilkent_University

## Snapshot and target segments
- English-medium, research-led private university with strong placement in Turkish rankings; perceived as elite for engineering, computer science, business, and international relations.
- Attracts academically strong Middle East students seeking English instruction and proximity to home; competitive alternative for Jordanian STEM/business applicants comparing to Cyprus and EU options.

## Pricing and aid signals
- 2025–2026 published tuition for new domestic YKS entrants: **950,000 TL** per academic year (includes 10% VAT); semester instalment paths available for continuing cohorts.  
- Bilkent advertises **50% scholarships** that reduce tuition to half of the list price for eligible students; higher scholarship tiers exist but require separate merit assessment.  
- Payment can be annual (before fall) or split by semester; instalment options differ by admission year (e.g., 2023–2025 cohorts have lower semester amounts).

## Positioning vs UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Overlaps with UNIC/UNIC Athens in Computer Science, Data Science, Business, and Law (see [UNIC Athens programmes](../../../../UNIC/UNIC%20Athens/programs.md)). Competes on brand/research strength and Turkey-based networks; UNIC counters with EU degree recognition and Mediterranean campus experience.  
- Bilkent’s merit scholarships create a price anchor for high-GPA applicants; UNIC may need to highlight EU mobility pathways and clinical/business ties for differentiation.
