# Nigeria agent compliance playbook (UNIC Nicosia + UNIC Athens)

**Purpose:** protect students, protect UNIC, and keep Nigeria agent partnerships audit-ready.  
**Applies to:** any Nigeria-based education agent/counselor/partner (and any sub-agents) sourcing for **UNIC Nicosia** and/or **UNIC Athens**.  
**Non-legal note:** this is an operational SOP, not legal advice. When in doubt, escalate to UNIC Legal/Compliance.

> **Repo rule (UNIC internal):** do not store any student PII in this repository (no names, IDs, documents, emails, phone numbers). Use UNIC systems for case files and secure storage.

---

## 0) Non-negotiables (instant stop + escalation)

If any of the following occurs, **stop onboarding / freeze new referrals immediately** and escalate to Enrollment + Legal/Compliance:

1) **Fraudulent or altered documents**
   - Any suspected forged/altered academic results, bank statements, identity documents, or visa documents.
   - Any instruction (by agent staff or sub-agents) to “edit,” “enhance,” or “re-create” documents.

2) **Misrepresentation**
   - “Guaranteed visa,” “guaranteed work rights,” “guaranteed scholarship,” “guaranteed recognition/licensure,” or any claim not present in approved UNIC materials.
   - Any “fast track” or “special arrangement” claims with embassies, ministries, or professional bodies.

3) **Undisclosed sub-agents / lead provenance opacity**
   - Any use of sub-agents without prior written disclosure + UNIC approval + flow-down obligations.
   - Leads that cannot be traced to a lawful, permission-based capture source.

4) **Data privacy violations**
   - Lead transfer without verifiable consent (timestamp + capture method + text shown).
   - Sensitive documents shared via open WhatsApp threads, personal emails, or public links.
   - Any suspected data breach, loss, or unauthorized access not reported immediately.

5) **Corruption / bribery / off-book payments**
   - Any request for “facilitation payments,” gifts to decision-makers, or unethical inducements.

6) **Tuition/commission integrity breaches**
   - Cashback schemes, deposit refunds funded by commission, or tuition discounting outside official UNIC offers.

## 1) Due diligence checklist (before contracting)

Use this checklist to decide **Pass / Conditional Pass / Fail** for each prospective Nigeria agent partner. Document outcomes in the partner record (not in this repo).

### 1.1 Gate A — Business legitimacy (KYB)

**Pass criteria (must meet all):**
- **Legal registration verified:** entity is registered and details (name, address, directors) are consistent across documents.
- **Authorized signatory verified:** signatory authority is documented.
- **Bank account alignment:** beneficiary name matches contracting entity; no third-party payment instructions.
- **Physical presence:** verifiable office address and operational contact channels.
- **Reputation baseline:** no unresolved major fraud/ethics complaints from credible sources.

**Fail criteria (any one = Fail):**
- Refusal to provide verifiable registration/ownership information.
- Request to pay commissions to personal accounts, third parties, or a different legal entity.
- Evidence of prior fraud, bribery, or repeated partner terminations for integrity reasons.

**Evidence to collect/verify (minimum):**
- Corporate registration documents (or registry extract) + registered address.
- Ownership/beneficial owner declaration (review in secure storage; do not store copies in-repo).
- Signatory authority proof (e.g., company resolution/POA if applicable).
- Bank confirmation letter on letterhead showing account name + number.
- Website + official domains + social handles used for recruitment.
- Two university/partner references (with institutional emails) or equivalent proof of track record.

### 1.2 Gate B — Student consent handling (lead transfer readiness)

**Pass criteria (must meet all):**
- **Consent is explicit + informed:** consent text names UNIC (and campus where possible: Nicosia vs Athens).
- **Consent is traceable:** timestamp, channel (form/event/CRM), and exact text shown can be reproduced.
- **Consent is retrievable:** agent can provide proof within **5 business days** for an audit sample.
- **Consent is withdrawable:** agent honors opt-out and can suppress further contact.

**Fail criteria (any one = Fail):**
- “We don’t keep consent records” / “consent is implied” / “students gave verbal consent only.”
- Leads sourced from bought lists, scraped contacts, or unverifiable lead brokers.

**Evidence to collect/verify (minimum):**
- Screenshots/exports of consent capture flow (checkbox + text) + audit log fields captured.
- List of capture channels used (website forms, events, webinars, school visits, referrals) and how consent is recorded for each.

### 1.3 Gate C — Data handling & security (minimum operational controls)

**Pass criteria (must meet all):**
- **Data minimization:** agent collects only necessary fields at lead stage (see Section 2).
- **Secure transfer:** agent uses UNIC-approved channels (portal/secure upload) and avoids public links.
- **Access control:** defined staff access, password hygiene, device security, and revocation on staff exit.
- **Retention & deletion:** time-bound retention schedule + deletion process exists.
- **Incident readiness:** agent commits to notifying UNIC within **24 hours** of any suspected breach/loss.

**Fail criteria (any one = Fail):**
- WhatsApp/email is the primary method for collecting/storing sensitive documents.
- Agent refuses to describe storage location, access controls, or retention/deletion practices.

**Evidence to collect/verify (minimum):**
- Privacy notice or data handling statement shown to prospects.
- Description of systems used (CRM, file storage) + access model (roles).
- Short retention schedule (lead vs applicant records) + deletion workflow.
- Named data/security owner and escalation contact.

### 1.4 Gate D — Marketing claims & brand guardrails

**Pass criteria (must meet all):**
- Agent commits to using **approved UNIC messaging and assets** only.
- Agent discloses all channels used (social pages, websites, ad accounts, events) and agrees to audits.
- Agent has an internal review/approval workflow for claims (who signs off).

**Fail criteria (any one = Fail):**
- Existing marketing contains prohibited claims (guaranteed visa/scholarship/work rights/licensure).
- Refusal to provide a current inventory of marketing assets/channels.

**Evidence to collect/verify (minimum):**
- Links/screenshots of current landing pages, social profiles, ad library, webinar decks, and counselor scripts.
- Written acknowledgment of UNIC “claims rules” (Section 3).

### 1.5 Gate E — Sub-agent disclosure & control (if applicable)

**Pass criteria (must meet all):**
- Agent discloses any sub-agents **before** they are used (names, locations, channels, estimated volume share).
- Agent agrees to **flow-down** UNIC compliance rules to sub-agents and accepts full accountability.
- Leads can be traced to the sub-agent ID + channel (no anonymous “network” leads).

**Fail criteria (any one = Fail):**
- “We can’t disclose our network” or any multi-layer chain without transparency.
- Refusal to accept audit rights for sub-agent activity.

**Evidence to collect/verify (minimum):**
- Sub-agent roster (company names; no personal data) + geographies + channels.
- Written agreement to pre-approval and audit (see Section 5).

### 1.6 Onboarding gate (decision + sign-off)

- **Minimum bar:** all Gates A–D must be **Pass**. Gate E is required if sub-agents exist.
- **Conditional pass:** allowed only with a written remediation plan (owner + deadline + evidence). Until closed, treat as **probation** with increased monitoring.
- **Fail:** do not contract; do not accept leads; do not share application links.

**Required contract clauses (operational requirements):**
- Audit rights (including sub-agents), record-keeping and evidence timelines
- Marketing approval + prohibited claims + brand usage rules
- Data processing/security obligations + breach notification timeline
- Sub-agent pre-approval + flow-down obligations
- Anti-bribery/anti-corruption and integrity clauses
- Commission payment hygiene (payee/bank account alignment; no third-party reroutes)

## 2) Data handling & consent rules (Nigeria agents recruiting into the EU)

UNIC operates in the EU; Nigeria partners must meet **GDPR-level** expectations when collecting and transferring student data to UNIC.

### 2.1 Allowed “lead-stage” fields (data minimization)
Agents may collect/share only what is necessary to support inquiry and admissions steps, typically:
- Full name
- Email and phone (with opt-in)
- City + state + country of residence
- Intended program/level/campus (Nicosia vs Athens) + preferred intake
- Current school/university + graduation year (as needed for advising)
- Source category (school visit, event, referral, digital, sub-agent)

### 2.2 Prohibited until UNIC requests via a secure application workflow
Agents must not collect/transmit/store outside UNIC’s secure processes:
- Passport/ID scans or ID numbers
- Date of birth (unless required at formal application stage via UNIC process)
- Bank statements or detailed financial documents (unless UNIC explicitly requests through secure workflow)
- Health data, disability information, religion, ethnicity, political opinions
- Criminal records
- Payment card data

### 2.3 Consent requirements (lead transfer must be permission-based)
Consent must be:
1. **Explicit:** a clear opt-in; no pre-ticked boxes.
2. **Named:** UNIC must be named; include campus where possible (UNIC Nicosia / UNIC Athens).
3. **Informed:** what data is shared, why, and how to withdraw.
4. **Traceable:** timestamp + capture channel + exact text shown.
5. **Retrievable:** proof must be provided within **5 business days** on request (redacted as needed).

### 2.4 Secure transfer expectations
- Allowed: UNIC CRM/partner portal, secure upload links provided by UNIC, or other UNIC-approved encrypted methods.
- Forbidden: public Google Drive links, open WhatsApp threads for sensitive docs, personal email accounts, shared passwords.

### 2.5 Retention and deletion (operational defaults)
- Marketing leads without an application: retain no longer than **12 months** without re-consent.
- Once UNIC confirms receipt of an application: delete redundant local copies within **30 days** (unless required for invoicing, then retain minimal invoice support only).
- Maintain a simple deletion log (date, record type, staff initials) in the agent’s internal system.

### 2.6 Incident reporting
- Notify UNIC within **24 hours** of discovering any suspected data breach, loss, or unauthorized access.
- Provide: what happened, data categories (not individual names), approximate record count, containment steps taken, and what support is needed.

### 3.2 High-risk claims (cheat sheet)

Forbidden (examples):
- “Guaranteed visa” / “fast-track visa” / “no interview needed”
- “Guaranteed scholarship” / “automatic discount if you apply with us”
- “Guaranteed job” / “guaranteed salary” / “work rights guaranteed”

- “Career services support outcomes; employment is not guaranteed.”
- “Recognition/licensure depends on the relevant Nigerian competent authority and program pathway.”

## 4) Document integrity expectations (Nigeria-specific risk area)

**Rule:** agents may advise students on requirements and completeness, but must **never** alter, recreate, or “improve” documents.

Operational requirements:
- If a document looks inconsistent or suspicious, **pause** and escalate to UNIC for verification guidance.
- Keep a clear lead/application trail: capture source, counseling notes, and who handled the case (in UNIC systems).
- UNIC may request original-source verification (issuing institution checks, additional evidence, or originals). Agents must cooperate promptly.

---

## 5) Sub-agent controls (risk containment)

1) **Pre-approval only:** no sub-agent can represent UNIC until disclosed and approved in writing.  
2) **Flow-down obligations:** sub-agents must follow the same rules on consent, data security, claims, and integrity.  
3) **Lead traceability:** every lead must include a sub-agent identifier + capture channel; missing metadata = reject lead.  
4) **Audit parity:** sub-agents are subject to the same monitoring cadence and can be suspended independently.  
5) **Accountability:** the primary agent is fully accountable for sub-agent conduct and remediation costs arising from misconduct.

## 6) Monitoring cadence + required reporting (non-PII)

### 6.1 Cadence (minimum)

| Cadence | UNIC does | Agent provides (non-PII) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Monthly** | Performance + quality review; spot-check claims on live channels | Monthly partner report (Section 6.2) + updated marketing inventory (links) + sub-agent changes (if any) |
| **Quarterly** | Compliance audit + sampling; secret-shopper checks of counseling claims | Consent-proof sample (e.g., 10 leads; redacted / via secure channel), complaints log (counts + categories), document integrity flags summary |
| **Annually** | Re-certification; contract renewal decision | Updated KYB docs if changed, re-attestation to non-negotiables, updated fee sheet and channel list |

### 6.2 Required monthly reporting fields (must be aggregated; no student PII)

Report must cover each active intake and include:
- Reporting period (month) + intakes covered
- Campus interest split (Nicosia vs Athens) and primary program clusters (binned)
- Funnel counts: **leads → applications → offers → deposits → enrollments**
- Visa outcomes (counts): approved / refused / pending / not started (where known)
- Deferrals and withdrawals (counts) + reason codes (standard categories)
- Lead provenance mix (percent of leads): school/counselor, fairs/events, digital, referrals, sub-agent
- Consent exceptions (count): leads submitted without retrievable consent proof (should be **0**)
- Sub-agent volume share (percent) + any roster changes (company names only)
- Complaints (count) + categories (pricing, pressure selling, misinformation, data handling, other)
- Data incidents (count) + whether UNIC was notified within 24h (yes/no)
- Document integrity flags (count): suspected issues raised, outcomes (cleared/confirmed/unknown)
- Marketing inventory changes: new landing pages, ads, webinars (links only)
- Student-facing fees: confirmation unchanged (yes/no) + if changed, high-level summary

## 7) Red flags (triage list)

Escalate and consider an immediate referral freeze if you observe:
- Repeated “visa/work rights guaranteed” statements (calls, WhatsApp scripts, webinar decks, ads)
- Any mention of document “editing,” “templates,” or “agents can fix it”
- Refusal or inability to produce consent proof on demand
- High refusal rates with no reasonable explanation + poor documentation quality
- Sudden volume spikes from opaque sources (lead brokers, “database,” undisclosed sub-agent networks)
- Student complaints about undisclosed fees, pressure tactics, cashback promises, or misinformation
- Unapproved UNIC-branded domains/pages or paid ads with misleading copy
- Payment anomalies (third-party accounts, split invoices, requests to reroute payments)

## 8) Suspension & offboarding process

### 8.1 Triggers

**Immediate suspension (freeze referrals same day):**
- Any suspected document fraud or instruction to falsify/alter documents
- Bribery/corruption allegations or “facilitation payment” requests
- Material misrepresentation (visa/work rights/recognition guarantees)
- Data breach or major privacy violation (especially sensitive docs shared insecurely)
- Undisclosed sub-agent activity

**Probation (30–90 day remediation window; increased monitoring):**
- Repeated minor claims violations (copy errors) after warnings
- Poor application quality (missing/inconsistent docs) beyond agreed thresholds
- Late reporting or repeated inability to provide audit evidence

### 8.2 Steps (operational workflow)
1) **Freeze** new referrals and pause marketing using UNIC claims within **24 hours** of trigger.  
2) **Open a compliance case** in UNIC’s tracker (date, allegation, evidence list, owner, severity).  
3) **Contain**: take down/disable non-compliant ads/pages; secure any exposed links; stop sub-agent activity pending review.  
4) **Investigate** (5–10 business days): request explanations and evidence via secure channels; validate lead provenance and consent where relevant.  
5) **Decide outcome**:
   - *Reinstate* (no breach found)  
   - *Reinstate with conditions* (remediation plan + probation for 2 quarters)  
   - *Terminate* (material breach / repeat offenses)
6) **Protect students**: communicate accurate next steps to impacted applicants and ensure continuity of advising through UNIC channels.  
7) **Data return/deletion**: require written confirmation of deletion/return of UNIC prospect data per Section 2.  
8) **Commercial close-out**: hold commissions tied to disputed cases until resolution per contract; do not process exceptions without Finance + Legal approval.

## 9) What we will not do (UNIC standard)

- We will not accept, encourage, or ignore fraudulent or altered documents.
- We will not pay or reimburse bribes, facilitation payments, or “off-book” inducements.
- We will not allow agents to promise visas, work rights, scholarships, housing, jobs, or licensure outcomes.
- We will not accept leads without verifiable consent or with unclear provenance (bought/scraped lists).
- We will not allow undisclosed sub-agent chains or “confidential networks.”
- We will not tolerate cashback schemes, unofficial discounts, or payment reroutes to third parties.
