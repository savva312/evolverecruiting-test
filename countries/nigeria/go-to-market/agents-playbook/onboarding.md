# Nigeria agent onboarding SOP (operational v1)

This SOP is written for the UNIC enrollment/partner manager responsible for **Nigeria**. It is designed to onboard an education agent end-to-end — **screen → KYC → contract → train → launch → 30/60/90-day pilot → scale/stop** — with clear owners, checklists, and decision rules.

**Inputs to use (Nigeria)**
- Target roster: [`countries/nigeria/entities/agents/feeder-candidates.md`](../../entities/agents/feeder-candidates.md)
- Agent profiles: [`countries/nigeria/entities/agents/profiles/`](../../entities/agents/profiles/)
- Nigeria program cluster playbooks (positioning + competitor context):
  - [`medicine-md`](../../program-clusters/medicine-md/nigeria-playbook.md)
  - [`cs-data-ai-cyber`](../../program-clusters/cs-data-ai-cyber/nigeria-playbook.md)
  - [`business-finance-accounting-marketing`](../../program-clusters/business-finance-accounting-marketing/nigeria-playbook.md)
- Campus “single source of truth” (use for training and fact-checking):
  - UNIC Nicosia: [`UNIC/unicnicosia/programs`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/programs/README.md), [`admissions`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/admissions/README.md), [`visas`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/visas/README.md), [`scholarships`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/scholarships/README.md)
  - UNIC Athens: [`UNIC/unicathens/programs`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/programs/README.md), [`admissions`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/admissions/README.md), [`visas`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/visas/README.md), [`scholarships`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/scholarships/README.md)

**Non-negotiables (Nigeria)**
- **Document integrity (zero tolerance):** no forged/altered documents, “statement packaging”, impersonation, or misrepresentation — stop work triggers below.
- **No guaranteed outcomes:** no “guaranteed admission”, “guaranteed scholarship”, or “guaranteed visa/residence” claims.
- **Scholarship/price integrity:** agents may only use **UNIC-approved** tuition/scholarship materials for the student’s program/campus/intake (no invented discounts).
- **Data protection:** agent must capture **explicit consent** to share student data with UNIC (EU-based processing) and follow applicable privacy rules (Nigeria + EU). Minimize data; use secure intake channels.
- **Campus clarity:** agent must not blur **UNIC Nicosia** vs **UNIC Athens** (program availability, admissions, visas, fees, and scholarships can differ).

## Operating model (owners + cadence)

**Core owners (UNIC)**
- **Nigeria Partner/Agent Manager:** partner selection, onboarding execution, weekly cadence, performance management, co-marketing coordination.
- **Admissions Lead — UNIC Nicosia:** admissions requirements, file quality, offer turnaround, conversion coaching for Nicosia.
- **Admissions Lead — UNIC Athens:** admissions requirements, file quality, offer turnaround, conversion coaching for Athens.
- **Admissions Ops / CRM:** lead intake standards, attribution/duplicate rules, reporting, partner code setup.
- **Marketing:** asset pack, approvals, landing pages/UTMs, brand compliance, event/webinar support.
- **Finance:** commission setup, payment terms, invoice requirements, refund/chargeback rules.
- **Legal/Compliance:** contracting, data protection terms, anti-fraud/anti-bribery, prohibited claims, termination.

**Working cadence**
- **Week 0–2 (setup):** screening + KYC + contract + training + go-live checklist.
- **Weeks 3–12 (pilot):** weekly 30-minute pipeline call + monthly performance snapshot.
- **Day 90:** scale/fix/stop decision.
- **Quarterly:** QBR and renewal/tier decisions.

## Stage 0 — Pre-qualification (1–2 days)

### Eligibility criteria (Nigeria-ready)
Agent is eligible for onboarding only if they can demonstrate:
- A **registered business** operating in Nigeria (and/or regional HQ), with stable operations and a public presence.
- A **track record** of higher-ed placements (not only short-stay travel).
- Clear counseling model (in-house counselors vs sub-agents) and willingness to disclose any sub-agent network.
- Willingness to operate under UNIC guardrails: integrity, no outcome guarantees, no unapproved scholarship claims, campus clarity.
- Capability to run **events/webinars** and to report pipeline activity weekly.
- Capability to follow privacy requirements and provide evidence of student consent for data sharing.

### Pre-qualification checklist (Agent Manager)
- Select candidate from [`feeder-candidates.md`](../../entities/agents/feeder-candidates.md); read their profile in [`profiles/`](../../entities/agents/profiles/).
- Confirm campus/program fit and how they will position UNIC vs UK/Canada alternatives (without overpromising work/immigration).
- Ask for a **90-day pilot commitment**: weekly cadence + minimum reporting + willingness to retrain quickly.
- Screen for red flags:
  - asks for “rebates/cashback” to students, hidden fees, or cash incentives
  - pushes “visa certainty” promises
  - refuses consent evidence / privacy terms
  - promotes unethical services (ghost-writing, document “assistance”, bank statement sourcing)

**Go / No-go (rule of thumb)**
- **Go** if they accept a 90-day pilot, agree to compliance guardrails, and can show credible lead sources.
- **No-go** if they request unethical practices, refuse transparency (sub-agents/fees), or cannot commit to reporting discipline.

## Stage 1 — KYC + due diligence (3–7 days)

**Goal:** validate operational readiness and reduce compliance/reputation risk before contracting.

### A) KYC checklist (collect before contract)
Request **company-level** information only (do not store personal IDs in shared docs).
- Legal entity name and registration details (e.g., CAC registration evidence), tax identifiers (if applicable).
- Office address(es) + operating cities; website and official social channels.
- Ownership/beneficial owner disclosure (company-level statement; handled by Legal/Compliance).
- Bank account details for payments (handled by Finance via secure channel).
- Counselor model: in-house vs sub-agents; if sub-agents exist, request **entity list** and governance approach.
- Reference checks: 1–2 higher-ed partners who can confirm performance and payment integrity (or equivalent).
- Student fee transparency: whether they charge students, and how fees are disclosed (must be transparent).

### B) Compliance and integrity screening (must pass)
**Data protection readiness**
- Agent can explain how they capture **explicit consent** for sharing student data with UNIC and how consent evidence is stored.
- Agent agrees to data minimization and to use UNIC-approved intake channels for sensitive data/documents.

**Marketing/claims control**
- Agent agrees to use only UNIC-approved program/tuition/scholarship assets and to seek approval before co-branded ads/landing pages.
- Agent agrees they will not claim guaranteed visas, work rights, or scholarships.

**Document integrity controls (Nigeria-specific risk point)**
- Agent confirms they will not procure/alter documents (academic or financial) and will not coach students to misrepresent finances.
- Agent agrees to “stop and escalate” if document authenticity is in doubt (see stop-work triggers).
- Pilot audit agreement: UNIC may request random sampling of application files to review document quality patterns.

**Sub-agent governance**
- If the partner uses sub-agents: require written sub-agent policy, disclosure, and agreement that only trained sub-agents can represent UNIC.

### C) Risk rating and onboarding track (internal)
Assign a simple risk rating to set depth of onboarding:
- **Low risk:** Tier 1 profile, strong references, clear consent flows, no sub-agents → standard onboarding.
- **Medium risk:** mixed track record, sub-agents, aggressive marketing style → enhanced training + tighter pilot KPIs.
- **High risk:** refusal to disclose sub-agents/fees, unethical “services”, repeated misrepresentation → **do not contract**.

**Output:** KYC pack complete + risk rating recorded + decision to proceed to contracting.

## Stage 2 — Contracting + setup (5–10 days)

**Goal:** signed agreement + partner operational setup so leads can be routed, tracked, and serviced.

### Contracting steps (step-by-step)
1. **Send contract pack** (Legal/Compliance owner) including:
   - commission and payment terms, refund/chargeback handling
   - scope (campuses/programs/territory), sub-agent rules, marketing approvals
   - integrity + prohibited practices clause (no fraud, no bribery, no outcome guarantees)
   - data protection / consent requirements and secure handling requirements
2. **Commercial alignment call** (Agent Manager + Finance): commission bands, payout schedule, and performance expectations for the 90-day pilot.
3. **Compliance walkthrough** (Legal/Compliance): prohibited claims, document integrity expectations, escalation/termination.
4. **Signature + onboarding confirmation**: no training access until agreement is signed (or until Legal approves pre-contract training for Tier 1 only).
5. **Finance setup**:
   - capture invoice entity details and payment method (secure channel only)
   - confirm required invoice fields and payout triggers
6. **CRM/partner code setup** (Ops):
   - create partner record + unique **agent code**
   - define attribution and duplicate logic (see “Lead routing”)
   - set access method (portal, intake form, or controlled shared channel)
7. **Communication channels** (Agent Manager):
   - add partner to official update cadence (monthly partner bulletin; training refresh invites)
   - define escalation inboxes/roles (no personal WhatsApp numbers as “official routing”)

**Output:** signed contract + partner code + intake path ready.

## Stage 3 — Training + certification (5–10 days)

**Goal:** agent team can counsel accurately and submit clean leads/applications without compliance risk.

### Training agenda (recommended minimum)
Run as 3 short sessions plus a certification check (record attendance; re-run quarterly).

**Session 1 (60–90 min): UNIC overview + campus clarity**
- UNIC structure: Nicosia vs Athens; what differs and why it matters.
- How to present two-campus options without “bundling” or mixing requirements.
- Program portfolio overview (use campus sources of truth):
  - Nicosia programs: [`UNIC/unicnicosia/programs`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/programs/README.md)
  - Athens programs: [`UNIC/unicathens/programs`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/programs/README.md)

**Session 2 (60–90 min): Admissions + document integrity**
- Admissions basics: required documents and common failure points (use campus admissions pages).
- Nigeria-specific integrity risks: altered transcripts/results, unverifiable references, financial document manipulation.
- What support is allowed vs not allowed:
  - allowed: explaining requirements, helping students collect genuine documents, checklisting
  - not allowed: writing essays/SOPs for students, falsifying/altering documents, “proof-of-funds engineering”

**Session 3 (60–90 min): Visas, scholarships, and realistic expectation setting**
- High-level visa pathways and timelines (no guarantees; use campus visa pages as the baseline):
  - Nicosia visas: [`UNIC/unicnicosia/visas`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/visas/README.md)
  - Athens visas: [`UNIC/unicathens/visas`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/visas/README.md)
- Scholarship positioning for Nigeria (price sensitivity, transparency, written confirmation only):
  - Nicosia scholarships: [`UNIC/unicnicosia/scholarships`](../../../../UNIC/unicnicosia/scholarships/README.md)
  - Athens scholarships: [`UNIC/unicathens/scholarships`](../../../../UNIC/unicathens/scholarships/README.md)
- Nigeria program-cluster messaging refresh (use playbooks to align on objections and competitor set).

**Certification check (15–20 min)**
- Quick quiz + “mock lead submission” to confirm:
  - campus clarity
  - qualified lead definition
  - prohibited claims and stop-work triggers
  - required lead fields and intake process

**Output:** agent team certified + first sample leads accepted.

### Asset pack checklist (what the agent receives)
**UNIC provides (latest approved versions only)**

- Program sheets (per campus) + admissions requirements checklist.

**Agent must not**

## Lead routing (required), qualified lead definition, and minimum data fields

### Lead routing workflow (agent → UNIC)
1. **Agent counsels student** and captures explicit consent for sharing data with UNIC.
2. **Agent submits lead** via UNIC-approved intake path using the required fields.
3. **UNIC acknowledges within 1 business day** (or per SLA) and assigns a counselor-of-record.
4. **Duplicates are resolved** using the attribution rules below; agent is notified if a lead is rejected or already exists.
5. **Status updates**: agent receives stage updates on a regular cadence (weekly pilot call + monthly snapshot).

### Definition of a qualified lead (Nigeria, v1)
A lead is “qualified” only if the student has:
- a clear **campus interest** (Athens/Nicosia/unsure) and **program cluster interest**
- a target **intake window** (month/term)
- an eligibility baseline (education level + readiness for English/entry requirements)
- a realistic funding approach (budget band or funding source category)
- provided **explicit consent** for data sharing with UNIC

### Required minimum lead fields (non-PII)
Use this list for the **minimum** fields required for routing and reporting without personal identifiers.

| Field | Required | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `agent_name` | Yes | Agency/company name (not an individual) |
| `agent_lead_id` | Yes | Unique ID created by the agent (stable across resubmits) |
| `consent_captured` | Yes | `yes/no` |
| `consent_timestamp` | Yes | ISO format `YYYY-MM-DDThh:mm:ss±hh:mm` |
| `consent_source` | Yes | e.g., website form, webinar registration, signed consent |
| `current_city_state` | Yes | e.g., `Lagos`, `Abuja`, `Port Harcourt` (no street address) |
| `citizenship_category` | Yes | e.g., `Nigerian`, `dual`, `other` (do not collect passport numbers in reporting) |
| `intended_intake` | Yes | e.g., `2026-09` |
| `campus_interest` | Yes | `ATHENS`, `NICOSIA`, or `UNSURE` |
| `program_cluster_interest` | Yes | e.g., `medicine-md`, `cs-data-ai-cyber`, `business-finance-accounting-marketing` |
| `education_level` | Yes | e.g., secondary, bachelor, master |
| `graduation_year` | Yes | Year only |
| `english_readiness` | Yes | e.g., `needs_test`, `has_test`, `native/intl_school` |
| `budget_band` | No | Use ranges; do not collect detailed bank data in reports |
| `funding_source_category` | No | e.g., family, scholarship-seeking, sponsor, mixed |
| `document_readiness` | No | e.g., `ready`, `in_progress`, `missing_key_docs` |
| `notes` | No | Constraints, objections, next steps (no sensitive personal data) |

### PII fields (capture only in secure intake/CRM)
Personal identifiers and documents (name, email/phone, passport details, bank statements, academic records) should be collected **only** through the approved secure intake path and with consent. Do not send sensitive documents via uncontrolled channels.

### Attribution and duplicate rules (simple default)
- Leads must include `agent_lead_id` and `agent_name` to be attributed.
- If the student already exists in UNIC systems, Ops decides attribution based on:
  - who first submitted a complete lead with consent evidence
  - the most recent meaningful counseling activity (tie-breaker)
- Leads missing required fields can be rejected until corrected (no attribution until accepted).

## Stage 5 — 30/60/90-day pilot plan (KPIs + decision rules)

This pilot is designed to validate the partner quickly using **quality + integrity + pace**, not just raw lead volume.

### Day 0–30: “Proof of process”
**Outputs**
- Training completed; certification passed.
- Intake path working; sample leads accepted.
- 1 joint webinar/event completed (or scheduled if intake timing requires).

**KPIs (minimum)**
- **Data completeness:** ≥90% of submitted leads include all required minimum fields.
- **Speed:** median lead submission within 48 hours of first counseling interaction.
- **Qualified leads:** ≥20 qualified leads (definition above) with campus split recorded.
- **Integrity:** zero incidents of misrepresentation, unapproved discount claims, or document integrity concerns.

### Day 31–60: “Proof of applications”
**Outputs**
- Consistent weekly pipeline cadence.
- First complete applications submitted (where intake timing allows).

**KPIs (minimum)**
- **Qualified → Application:** ≥25% of qualified leads reach application submission (adjust for intake seasonality).
- **Application quality:** ≥80% “complete on first review” (no major missing documents).
- **Expectation management:** track top objections and ensure agent messaging matches UNIC-approved facts.

### Day 61–90: “Proof of enrollments”
**Outputs**
- Offer management and yield plan executed (offers → deposits).
- Joint next-quarter plan built (events, targets, program focus).

**KPIs (targets to calibrate by intake timeline)**
- **Applications → Offers:** tracked by program cluster; escalate bottlenecks to admissions.
- **Offers → Deposit:** set a joint target by intake; treat as shared KPI (agent + UNIC).
- **Compliance:** zero high-severity incidents; timely corrective actions for any minor issues.

### Decision rules (Day 90)
- **Scale** if KPIs are met and quality is strong: increase joint events, deepen program focus, consider tier upgrade.
- **Fix** if volume is good but quality/pace is weak: retrain, tighten SLAs, reduce scope temporarily.
- **Stop** if any integrity/compliance breach occurs or if repeated low-quality submissions persist after retraining.

## Escalations and “stop work” triggers (Nigeria)

### Escalation paths (role-based)
- **Admissions issues:** route to Admissions Lead (campus-specific) + Admissions Ops for SLA or document questions.
- **Visa/timeline expectations:** route to campus visa guidance owner (do not improvise).
- **Scholarship/discount questions:** route to campus scholarship policy owner; require written confirmation before quoting.
- **Brand/co-marketing approvals:** route to Marketing; suspend any live ads until approved.
- **Compliance/integrity concerns:** route immediately to Legal/Compliance; pause processing while investigated.

### Stop work triggers (immediate pause + escalation)
Stop accepting new leads and pause active cases for the partner if any of the below occur:
- Suspected **document fraud** or attempted submission of altered documents (academic, financial, identity).
- Any offer of “statement sourcing/packaging”, impersonation services, or bribery.
- Misrepresentation of UNIC affiliation (unauthorized logos, fake letters, unapproved claims).
- Guaranteed admission/visa/scholarship claims or pressure to “make it happen”.
- Refusal to disclose sub-agents or repeated use of untrained sub-agents representing UNIC.
- Evidence of unethical counseling (ghost-writing, coercion, hidden student fees tied to visa outcomes).

**Stop-work procedure**
1. Agent Manager pauses partner activity and records the incident summary (no sensitive personal data in shared docs).
2. Legal/Compliance reviews and decides: corrective action, probation, or termination per contract.
3. Marketing disables any co-branded campaigns immediately until cleared.
