# Nigeria addendum: events and fairs

Use `/skills/events-and-fairs/SKILL.md` for the main workflow. Apply these Nigeria-specific practices.

## Organizer norms and setup
- Anchor planning around Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt fairs plus school career days; confirm booth power and Wi‑Fi and carry offline backups (printed QR codes and paper forms) because connectivity is inconsistent.
- Many fairs permit WhatsApp follow-up; obtain written opt-in at the booth before messaging.

## Lead capture and consent
- Show a short NDPR-aligned consent statement on tablets and paper forms; store source and timestamp for each contact.
- Collect minimal fields: name, role, email/phone/WhatsApp (if consented), city, program interest. Avoid capturing passport numbers or birth dates.
- When using organizer badge scans, confirm what consent language was shown before importing data into `countries/nigeria/data/`.

## Staffing and assets
- Staff at least one Nigeria-based rep who can address WAEC/NECO timelines and visa basics; keep claims fact-checked and avoid promising fee waivers on the spot.
- Carry concise comparisons of UNIC vs UNIC Athens and scholarship summaries approved by admissions.

## Post-event follow-up
- Send recap emails/WhatsApp summaries within 48 hours, tagging event and city; include links to webinars or application workshops scheduled for the relevant city cluster.
- Log leads in the country data model with event name/date, city cluster, and campus interest (Nicosia vs Athens) before launching sequences.
- Prioritize hot leads before exam periods; during exams switch to low-touch nurture until counselors are free, and keep follow-ups aligned to the campus preference captured.
