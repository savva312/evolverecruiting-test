# Nigeria addendum: school outreach & counselor engagement

Use `/skills/school-outreach-and-counselor-engagement/SKILL.md` for the global workflow. Apply these Nigeria-specific details for targeting, consent, and follow-up.

## Data sources and tagging
- Pull school/counselor targets from `countries/nigeria/entities/schools/` and related CSVs in `countries/nigeria/data/entities/`.
- Tag contacts by city cluster (Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Kano, regional) and curriculum type (WAEC/NECO vs international) for routing and sequencing.
- Log interactions in the country data model (`countries/nigeria/skills/nigeria-data-model-csvs-and-profiles/`) before syncing elsewhere.

## Language and channel norms
- English is acceptable; some counselors prefer WhatsApp voice notes for quick coordination. Always capture opt-in before messaging on WhatsApp.
- Avoid high-volume outreach during WAEC/NECO exam windows and UTME counseling periods; propose virtual briefings once results are released.

## Visit logistics
- Confirm access rules for federal vs private schools; some require ministry or proprietor approval letters before visits.
- Carry short leave-behinds that separate Nicosia vs Athens options and summarize visa/tuition at a high level without promising discounts.

## Consent and data protection
- Treat contact collection under NDPR principles: obtain explicit permission, record the source/time, and avoid storing sensitive personal data (no passport or national ID details in notes).
- Limit logs to professional contact info, program interests, and interaction history; anonymize student stories unless consented.

## Follow-up cadence
- Send recap emails/WhatsApp summaries within 48 hours, with links to application checklists and upcoming webinars.
- Route hot counselors to city-specific workshops (e.g., Lagos/Abuja/Port Harcourt) and tag event names in the log for reporting.
