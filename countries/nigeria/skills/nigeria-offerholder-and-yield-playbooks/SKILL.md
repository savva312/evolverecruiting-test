# Nigeria addendum: offerholder & yield playbooks

Use `/skills/offerholder-and-yield-playbooks/SKILL.md` for cadences, data capture, and objection handling. Apply these Nigeria-specific points.

## Channel and language norms
- WhatsApp is the fastest follow-up channel; capture opt-in explicitly and log it. Use email and phone for formal updates and document requests.
- Keep templates in English; simplify language for parents/guardians when needed and provide short voice-note summaries if requested.

## Timing anchors
- Expect slower responses during WAEC/NECO/UTME seasons; shift to lighter nudges and resume heavier cadences once results are out.
- Start visa-prep checklists early (bank statements, sponsorship letters, passport validity). Avoid promising expedited appointments; direct students to official VFS/embassy guidance.

## Compliance and documentation
- Do not store passports, birth certificates, or bank documents in the repo. Log only application IDs, contact details, and checklist status.
- Capture consent for any messaging groups (WhatsApp/Telegram) with source + timestamp and owner responsible for moderation.

## Objection handling to localize
- Cost/FX: keep pricing in EUR and avoid guaranteeing NGN equivalents; note the conversion date when estimates are requested.
- Recognition/visa: reference the Nigeria recognition and licensure skill (`countries/nigeria/skills/nigeria-recognition-and-licensure/`) before addressing licensing or employability claims.
