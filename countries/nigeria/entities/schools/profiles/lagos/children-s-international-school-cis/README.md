**One-paragraph summary:**  
CIS is a 2003-founded premium British school in Lekki Phase 1 that combines a selective Senior School (Years 7–11) with the on-site Chadsworth Sixth Form College (Years 12–13). It delivers 15 subjects across 11 departments at KS3, 28 Pearson Edexcel IGCSE options at KS4, and bespoke A-Level tracks aimed at “top universities in the UK, USA, Canada, Europe and other parts of the world,” backed by small classes and individualized counseling. Boarding runs from Year 6 through Year 13 (weekly or full) on the same secure campus, signaling affluent day and boarding cohorts aligned to UNIC Nicosia and UNIC Athens pipelines.

**Top opportunities:**  
- Chadsworth’s bespoke counseling, internships, and small A-Level cohorts explicitly target high-ranked overseas universities, creating a ready-made Medicine, Business, and CS/Data/AI audience.  
- Boarding (weekly/full) from Year 6–13 inside Lekki Phase 1 offers access to out-of-state and expatriate families who can prioritize overseas study and are receptive to Cyprus-based degrees.  
- Admissions calendar publishes fixed Year 7 tests (November/January) and Sixth Form scholarship papers (March), giving recruiters predictable touchpoints for counselor briefings and post-test conversion pushes.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Tuition remains “fees on request”; affordability confirmation requires direct dialogue with admissions before quoting cost comparisons.  
- Academically selective, often oversubscribed year groups mean cold outreach without counselor sponsorship may stall.  
- Limited published outcome data; need verified placement lists to prove fit for Medicine/healthcare regulators.

## 10. Logistics for Visits and Events

- Visit policy: Coordinate via admissions@cislagos.org with preferred dates ≥3 weeks out; government-issued ID required at Lekki Phase 1 gate. 
- Best time windows: Late Jan–Feb (post-January entrance tests), mid-April before exam lockdown, early September before new cohort orientation. Avoid November entrance test dates, March scholarship testing, and May–June external exams. 
- Visitor check-in process: Advance approval, security check at gate, escorted to reception; sign-in/out required. 
- Campus access notes: Urban campus with on-site boarding; parking limited, consider drop-off. Boarding house has separate access rules; coordinate with Head of Boarding. 
- AV/room setup: Sixth Form seminar rooms equipped with projector/HDMI; confirm tech support beforehand. 
- Typical audience sizes: Sixth Form cohorts ~30–60 students; counselor briefings 5–10 staff; parent evenings by invitation.
