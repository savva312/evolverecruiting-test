# University of the People (UoPeople)

- **Location:** Online, US-accredited.
- **Website:** https://www.uopeople.edu/
- **Model:** Tuition-free online degrees with low administrative/assessment fees; offers scholarships for students needing fee waivers. ([Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_the_People))
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-20
- **Sources:** Tuition-free model and assessment-fee structure from UoPeople’s Wikipedia entry and linked fee page. ([Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_the_People); [Fees page](https://www.uopeople.edu/tuition-free/processing-fees/))

## Positioning
- Markets itself as a “tuition-free” university aimed at students priced out of traditional institutions, highlighting social mobility and accessibility. ([Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_the_People))
- Offers fully online **business**, **computer science**, **health science**, and **education** programs that overlap with UNIC online/at-scale offerings.

## Program overlaps with UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Business administration (associate–MBA), computer science (associate–MS), health science, and education degrees mirror UNIC’s pathways for cost-sensitive Nigerians seeking employability credentials.
- 100% online delivery and asynchronous pacing compete with UNIC’s online courses for working professionals and second-degree seekers.

## Pricing and scholarships
- No tuition; students pay per-course assessment fees (e.g., undergraduate course assessment at low hundreds of USD) with scholarships available for those unable to pay. ([Fees page](https://www.uopeople.edu/tuition-free/processing-fees/))
- Absence of housing/visa costs dramatically undercuts total cost of attendance versus UNIC campuses.

## Relevance to Nigerian students
- Strong fit for students seeking internationally recognized credentials without relocation costs; threatens UNIC’s price-sensitive segments.
- Lacks in-person labs and networking; UNIC can differentiate with experiential learning, EU mobility, and hybrid study.

- Benchmark total credential cost (assessment fees × courses) against UNIC online tuition to position value narratives credibly.
