# Federal Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA) — Legalisation

Mandate: Manages Nigeria’s foreign relations and consular services, including legalising Nigerian public documents for use abroad.

Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens: Many destination embassies and universities require Nigerian academic certificates to be legalised by MFA after prior authentication by the issuing body (e.g., Ministry of Education or exam board). Students headed to Cyprus/Greece often need this stamp before visa or enrolment.

Key processes and timelines:
- Legalisation is initiated online through MFA’s paperless portal (`https://mfa.1gov.ng/selfservice`) before in-person submission; applicants upload documents, pay fees, and book appointments.
- MFA typically requires documents to be authenticated first by the issuing ministry/authority (e.g., Ministry of Education for transcripts, WAEC for WASSCE). Build this into applicant checklists.
- Processing times are not published; plan several working days after the scheduled appointment for stamping/collection and leave buffer for peak periods (assumption, based on typical consular workflows).
