# National Universities Commission (NUC)

Mandate: Federal regulator for university education; licenses universities, accredits academic programmes, and sets quality assurance standards.

Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens: Admission and transfer evaluations must confirm that Nigerian degrees come from NUC-approved institutions with accredited programmes. Partner outreach should map to NUC-recognized private and state universities to avoid recognition risk.

Key processes and timelines:
- Accreditation status is published on NUC’s portal (e.g., undergraduate accreditation results); use these lists to validate institution/program status before making offers or marketing claims.
- Institutions and graduates can request clarification letters on programme or institutional status via NUC; timelines are not published, so budget multiple weeks for responses and follow-ups through official channels (assumption).
- NUC periodically publishes warnings against unapproved universities; screening Nigerian applicants against those notices reduces fraud risk.
