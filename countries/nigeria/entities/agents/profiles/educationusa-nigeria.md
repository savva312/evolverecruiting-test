# EducationUSA Nigeria (Public Advising Network)

- **Website**: [https://ng.usembassy.gov/education/educationusa/](https://ng.usembassy.gov/education/educationusa/)  
- **Ownership/leadership**: Run by the U.S. Department of State via American Corners and advising centers. Not commission-based.  
- **Offices & reach**: Abuja and Lagos American Centers plus satellite corners across Nigeria; strong digital webinars reaching national audience.  
- **Registrations & associations**: Government program; advisers often hold NACAC/NAFSA training. No CAC registration needed.  
- **Destinations & product mix**: Exclusively U.S. degree pathways; focuses on advising ethics and financing guidance.  
- **UNIC vs UNIC Athens fit**: While U.S.-only, counselors influence decision sets; engage for credible third-party webinars positioning UNIC/UNIC Athens as EU alternatives when cost or visa odds reduce U.S. feasibility.  
- **Recruitment volume signals**: High advising throughput (thousands annually) though not all convert; potential referral partner, not commission agent.  
- **Visa coaching & compliance**: Adheres to ethical advising; no application writing; can reinforce compliance messaging.  
- **Commission/payment terms**: None; collaborations must remain informational and non-incentivized.  
- **Student fees & scholarships**: Advising mostly free; emphasizes merit/need-based options. Provide transparent scholarship data if doing info sessions.  
- **Sub-agent model**: None.  
- **Risks & compliance notes**: Low risk; ensure any collaboration respects U.S. government neutrality and avoids perception of endorsement.  
- **Evidence & sources**: EducationUSA Nigeria webpages and webinar schedules (accessed 2025-12-20).  
- **Last validated**: 2025-12-20
