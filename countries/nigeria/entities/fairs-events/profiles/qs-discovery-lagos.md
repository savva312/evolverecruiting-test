# QS Discovery / MBA & Grad School Tour – Lagos

## Overview
QS runs a combined MBA and postgraduate discovery event in Lagos, bringing pre-qualified candidates interested in international business, STEM, and professional master’s programs. QS marketing includes profile matching and appointment booking that improves lead quality for paid exhibitors.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** October–November (post-summer testing window); occasionally a spring stop if demand is high.
- **City/format:** In-person, Lagos hotel/venue; QS also offers post-event virtual follow-ups.
- **Format:** Booths plus presentation slots; QS app supports scheduled 1:1 chats.

## Audience and expected reach
- 500–800 registrants; majority postgraduate with 1–5 years of work experience, GMAT/GRE/IELTS-ready.
- Strong interest in MBA, business analytics, computer science, cybersecurity, and finance.

## Pricing (indicative)
- USD 2.8k–3.8k per city based on QS 2024–25 Africa rate bands; add-ons for speaking slots and lead nurture tools.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Highlight MBA/fintech/cybersecurity strengths, affordable tuition vs UK/Canada, and online-to-on-campus flexibility where available.
- **UNIC Athens:** Stress Athens business hub angle, Schengen access, and EU career pathways.

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: request full delegate list criteria; push QS to target STEM/business segments; book a 20–30 minute talk if available.
- During: prioritize booked appointments; scan every interaction via QS app; schedule short interviews for high-GPA/high-test candidates.
- Post: follow up within 24 hours with program-specific emails; invite to GMAT/GRE-light pathways webinar; route hot leads to admissions.

## Risks and notes
- Costs are higher than agent fairs; ROI depends on postgraduate conversion discipline.
- Traffic can skew MBA—bring clear messaging for MSc programs.
