# EducationUSA College & Career Fairs – Lagos and Abuja

## Overview
Flagship U.S.-leaning fairs run by EducationUSA advising centers under the U.S. State Department. Attract academically strong students, counselors, and parents seeking accredited international options; open to select non-U.S. institutions that fit English-medium and mobility pathways.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** Late August to early September (pre-fall U.S. intake), with occasional spring virtual briefings.
- **Cities:** Lagos and Abuja; virtual prep webinars sometimes precede the physical fairs.
- **Format:** Standard tables with lead cards/scanners; EducationUSA may curate info sessions.

## Audience and expected reach
- 800–1,200 attendees across both cities; high English proficiency and strong counselor turnout.
- Interests: U.S.-style programs, scholarships, credit transfer, and visa reliability.

## Pricing (indicative)
- Often free or <USD 500 table fee; priority given to accredited institutions. Travel and shipping are the main costs.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Emphasize English-medium instruction, accreditation, transfer/articulation pathways, and affordability versus U.S. campuses.
- **UNIC Athens:** Position as EU destination with Schengen mobility and proximity; highlight business/CS/psychology programs taught in English.

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: apply early with clear U.S.-relevant messaging; prepare counselor briefing pack (programs, scholarships, visa guidance, credit transfer options); request any speaking slots.
- During: collect counselor contacts separately; invite students to near-term webinars; log inquiries about U.S. pathways for tailored follow-up.
- Post: 24–48 hour follow-up by segment (undergrad vs grad); share counselor toolkit and propose school visits during same trip window.

## Risks and notes
- Space is limited; non-U.S. schools can be waitlisted if branding is too EU-focused.
- Lead capture may rely on paper forms—bring QR and tablets to reduce drop-off.
