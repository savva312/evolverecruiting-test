# Intake (Study World) Nigeria Fair – Lagos and Abuja

## Overview
In-country fair organized by Intake Education (formerly UKEAS). Focuses on UK, Ireland, and global study destinations with counselor support and school outreach. Intake can pre-qualify students and book school visits around the tour.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** February/March (spring) and September/October (fall).
- **Cities:** Lagos and Abuja; Port Harcourt added occasionally; virtual day sometimes bundled.
- **Format:** Booth/table with Intake-managed registrations; optional school visits and counselor roundtables.

## Audience and expected reach
- 1,500–2,500 visitors per tour across cities; strong undergrad pipeline plus taught master’s seekers.
- Leads often have IELTS/WAEC/IGCSE results and are actively applying through Intake counselors.

## Pricing (indicative)
- Often free or discounted for partner universities. Non-partner booths: ~USD 1k–2k per city based on prior UKEAS/Intake decks; confirm current Nigeria rates.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Emphasize English-medium degrees, value vs UK/Canada, and recognized professional accreditations; provide scholarship ranges Intake counselors can quote.
- **UNIC Athens:** Use proximity, Schengen travel, and EU business hub pitch; align with programs Intake already promotes (business, CS, design).

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: confirm partner status and fees; supply Intake counselors with updated program/scholarship sheets; schedule school visits on Lagos/Abuja legs.
- During: scan/QR every interaction; filter for intake and program interest; capture counselor feedback on objections.
- Post: send tailored follow-up within 24–48 hours; invite hot leads to campus-specific webinars; share conversion feedback with Intake team.

## Risks and notes
- Tour dates can shift around visa/strike disruptions—keep flexible travel buffer.
- Lead capture depends on Intake staff; align on data fields and consent language ahead of time.
