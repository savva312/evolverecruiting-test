# Maple Canada Education Fair – Lagos and Abuja

## Overview
Canada-focused expo run by Maple Education (Maple Canadian College). Draws families targeting Canadian universities and colleges, with seminars on study permits, settlement, and co-op programs. Good opportunity to position UNIC/UNIC Athens as EU alternatives for cost and visa resilience.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** October (aligned to September intake marketing) and March (ahead of fall intake planning).
- **Cities:** Lagos main event; Abuja satellite fair; occasional counselor briefings in both cities.
- **Format:** Booths plus short info sessions; Maple promotes via partner schools and alumni.

## Audience and expected reach
- 1,000–1,500 attendees per edition; heavy parent involvement and STEM/business interest.
- Leads skew toward Canada-first mindset; open to EU options if ROI/visa pathways are clear.

## Pricing (indicative)
- NGN 400k–700k per booth based on historical 2023–24 exhibitor ranges shared by agents; confirm current packages. Seminar slots typically bundled.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Compare tuition/living costs vs Canada; emphasize co-op/internship pathways, English-medium delivery, and post-study employment outcomes.
- **UNIC Athens:** Pitch proximity, Schengen travel, and EU business internships; highlight campus life in a major European capital.

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: request most recent exhibitor kit and seminar slot; prepare ROI one-pager comparing EU vs Canada; localize scholarship messaging for Nigerian families.
- During: capture family contact + program interest; run short sessions on visa/arrival timelines; collect counselor contacts from feeder schools.
- Post: follow up within 48 hours with EU vs Canada comparison deck and intake timeline; invite interested families to finance/visa webinars.

## Risks and notes
- Audience is Canada-centric—must address “why EU” early to avoid low conversion.
- Pricing and dates can shift with Canadian visa policy updates.
