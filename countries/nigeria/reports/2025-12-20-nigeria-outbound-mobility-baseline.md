## 2. Baseline market size and destination structure (global outbound, Nigeria)

### 2.1 What “outbound mobility” means (and why numbers disagree)

- **Preferred global definition (UNESCO UIS):** “Internationally mobile students” are those who physically cross an international border to study, where the destination differs from the country of origin. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/node/4789141?utm_source=openai))
- **How “country of origin” should be defined (UIS):** preferred basis is country of prior education (upper secondary qualification); if not feasible, use usual/permanent residence, and citizenship only as a last resort. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/node/4789141?utm_source=openai))
- **What is excluded in UIS mobility (important for market sizing):** distance learning and many forms of transnational delivery that do not require the student to cross a border are not counted as mobility. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/node/4789141?utm_source=openai))
- **Why totals vary across sources:** UIS notes that comprehensive enumeration can be challenging; origin definitions and criteria vary. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/node/4789139?utm_source=openai))

**Baseline rule for this report:** treat UNESCO UIS internationally mobile students (outbound, tertiary) as the global baseline concept; treat host-country statistics (UK/US/Canada) as destination-specific corroboration, not as a sum-to-total dataset.
