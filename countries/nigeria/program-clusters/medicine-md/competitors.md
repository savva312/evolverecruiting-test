# Medicine / MD competitors — Nigeria

Last updated: 2026-04-12

This list prioritises Medicine/MD options that Nigerian applicants commonly compare against UNIC Nicosia and UNIC Athens, with an emphasis on English‑taught or mobility‑friendly pathways. It is designed for counselling, not as a legal or licensure guide.

## Counselling guardrails (use every time)

- **Always click sources:** admissions rules and fees change by intake and applicant category.
- **Tuition:** only quote a number when you can point to a published fee source for the same intake/track; otherwise, say “see linked tuition page” and let families read the table themselves.
- **Clinical language:** “English‑taught” often still requires the local language for patient contact in clinical years—verify this on the programme site.
- **Recognition/licensure:** do not rely on marketing claims; confirm with destination regulators and Nigeria’s authorities. Always cross‑check against `countries/nigeria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`.

## Snapshot (Nigeria decision patterns)

- **Agents are powerful:** many Nigerian families first hear about CEE and Caribbean options through agencies that frame them as “easy entry” compared with UK schools.
- **Cyprus/Greece vs CEE vs Caribbean:** Cyprus and Greece appeal on culture, proximity, and perceived quality; CEE and Caribbean schools are often pitched as cheaper or more accessible for admissions.
- **Key trade‑offs:** families weigh tuition and living costs, admissions difficulty, perceived prestige, teaching quality, and how straightforward the licensure story sounds.

## Competitor entries

### Cyprus and Northern Cyprus

#### European University Cyprus (EUC) — Medicine (MD, 6 years) (Nicosia, Cyprus)

- **Location:** Nicosia, Cyprus.
- **Language of instruction:** English.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** secondary‑school credential with strong science profile (Biology plus a second lab science), plus an interview; EUC also advertises foundation options for students who do not meet direct‑entry criteria.
- **Tuition (published):** EUC tuition tables for 2022–2023 list the Doctor of Medicine (MD, 6 years) at roughly €21,000 per year; applicants must confirm the current figure on the official tuition‑fees page or downloadable price list.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** English‑taught MD in Cyprus with a straightforward admissions narrative and proximity to UNIC; often appears in agent shortlists alongside UNIC’s MD for families comparing Cyprus options.
- **Sources:** [Medicine (MD) programme – European University Cyprus](https://euc.ac.cy/en/programs/bachelor-medicine/), [Admissions FAQs – tuition and fees](https://euc.ac.cy/en/admissions/frequently-asked-questions-faqs/).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### Cyprus International University (CIU) — Human Medicine (6‑year programme) (Nicosia, Northern Cyprus)

- **Location:** Nicosia (Haspolat), Northern Cyprus.
- **Language of instruction:** English (medicine).
- **Admissions (high‑level):** high‑school completion with science background; CIU and its agents commonly highlight generous entrance scholarships for international students.
- **Tuition (published):** public fee tables show Human Medicine tuition in the mid‑€6,000s per year after standard 50% international‑student scholarships (list prices are higher; see CIU fee schedule for details).
- **Sources:** [CIU fees and scholarships](https://ciu-edu.com/fees-and-scholarships/).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### Near East University (NEU) — Faculty of Medicine (MD, 6 years) (Nicosia, Northern Cyprus)

- **Location:** Nicosia, Northern Cyprus.
- **Language of instruction:** English track available (local language required for full participation in some clinical settings).
- **Admissions (high‑level):** high‑school completion with required grades in sciences; selective merit‑based scholarships advertised through regional representatives.
- **Tuition (published):** recent fee tables and recruitment partners typically quote medicine tuition in the €11,600–€13,000 per‑year range before scholarships, with older material citing around €12,500 plus VAT or c.$18,250; counsellors must treat these as indicative only and confirm current fees.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** long‑standing international intake, aggressive agency marketing, and perceived affordability relative to some EU programmes.
- **Sources:** [Near East University – programme fees 2023–2024](https://aday.neu.edu.tr/2023-2024-academic-year-program-fees/?lang=en).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

### Greece

#### Aristotle University of Thessaloniki — Undergraduate Medical Studies (English programme) (Thessaloniki, Greece)

- **Location:** Thessaloniki, Greece.
- **Language of instruction:** English‑taught curriculum; Greek is still needed in clinical years for patient communication.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** competitive entry based on school‑leaver grades (or equivalent) in sciences plus English‑language evidence; the programme publishes detailed academic and documentation criteria.
- **Tuition (published):** the programme’s tuition‑fees page lists annual fees for the English‑language MD; counsellors should rely on the official table (commonly cited in the low‑€10,000s per year) and verify by intake.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** public‑university brand plus English delivery in Greece, often positioned as a mid‑price EU alternative to Cyprus or CEE.
- **Sources:** [Undergraduate Medical Studies – English Programme](https://aristotlemedical.edu.gr/undergraduate-studies/english-programme/), [Tuition and fees](https://aristotlemedical.edu.gr/admission-fees).

#### National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA) — English‑language Medical Degree (MD, 6 years) (Athens, Greece)

- **Location:** Athens, Greece.
- **Language of instruction:** English; Greek required over time for clinical practice.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** 6‑year undergraduate MD requiring strong science profile (e.g. A‑levels/IB or local equivalents) and proof of English proficiency.
- **Tuition (published):** NKUA’s English‑language MD documentation and official briefs state tuition of €13,000 per academic year; counsellors must check the latest NKUA admissions and tuition material for each intake.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** historic Greek public brand, clear English‑medium track, and relatively transparent fee point; used as a benchmark when comparing UNIC Athens or Cyprus options.
- **Sources:** [English‑language Medical Degree – NKUA](https://medicen.uoa.gr/), [Medical Degree English‑Program tuition PDF](https://studyingreece.edu.gr/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/Medical-Degree-English-Program-NKUA.pdf).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

### United Kingdom

#### University of Manchester — MBChB Medicine (Manchester, UK)

- **Location:** Manchester, United Kingdom.
- **Language of instruction:** English.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** highly competitive; requires strong A‑levels/IB (including Chemistry and usually Biology), UCAT, and multiple‑mini interviews.
- **Tuition (published):** fee tables for 2025 entry list international tuition at roughly £39,900 per year for pre‑clinical years and around £60,000+ per year for clinical years; fees increase periodically.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** prestige of a major UK medical school and perceived residency prospects in the NHS, despite very high total cost.
- **Sources:** [MBChB Medicine – University of Manchester](https://www.manchester.ac.uk/study/undergraduate/courses/2026/01428/mbchb-medicine/).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### University of Birmingham — Medicine and Surgery MBChB (Birmingham, UK)

- **Location:** Birmingham, United Kingdom.
- **Language of instruction:** English.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** A‑level/IB requirements with Chemistry plus one other lab science, UCAT, and interview; very selective for international applicants.
- **Tuition (published):** recent fee information and independent fee tables place international MBChB tuition slightly above £40,000 per year; always verify on the official Birmingham fees and funding page for the relevant intake.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** Russell Group reputation and strong NHS teaching‑hospital network; frequently appears on aspirational UK lists before families consider EU alternatives.
- **Sources:** [Medicine and Surgery MBChB – University of Birmingham](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/undergraduate/courses/med/medicine.aspx).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### St George’s, University of London — Medicine MBBS (London, UK)

- **Location:** London, United Kingdom.
- **Language of instruction:** English.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** standard UK route (A‑levels/IB in sciences, UCAT, interview) with strong emphasis on academic performance and professional values.
- **Tuition (published):** fee tables for 2025/26 list international tuition at roughly £44,700 per year over the five‑year course; fees are subject to annual increases.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** London location, specialist medical focus, and perceived pathway into NHS training; costs and living expenses are significantly higher than many EU options.
- **Sources:** [Medicine MBBS – St George’s, University of London](https://www.sgul.ac.uk/study/courses/medicine), [Medicine MBBS – City St George’s, University of London](https://www.citystgeorges.ac.uk/prospective-students/courses/undergraduate/medicine).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

### Central and Eastern Europe (CEE)

#### University of Debrecen — General Medicine (MD, 6 years) (Debrecen, Hungary)

- **Location:** Debrecen, Hungary.
- **Language of instruction:** English track.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** entrance exam in sciences plus interview; secondary‑school performance in Biology and Chemistry is important.
- **Tuition (published):** the university’s brochures and tuition‑fee tables list General Medicine tuition at around USD 16,900 per academic year (12‑semester programme).
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** long‑established English‑taught MD with a large international cohort and predictable fee structure, often marketed heavily by agencies.
- **Sources:** [General Medicine – University of Debrecen](https://edu.unideb.hu/p/medicine-general-medicine), [Tuition fees – University of Debrecen](https://edu.unideb.hu/p/tuition-fees).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### Medical University of Sofia — Medicine in English (Sofia, Bulgaria)

- **Location:** Sofia, Bulgaria.
- **Language of instruction:** English‑taught track.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** entrance exams in Biology and Chemistry (and English in some schemes); applicants must have completed secondary‑school science subjects.
- **Tuition (published):** official and partner fee notices for recent intakes quote tuition of €9,000 per year for Medicine in English.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** EU public‑university status with lower tuition than many Western European options; Bulgaria is a long‑standing CEE destination for international medicine students.
- **Sources:** [Admission of foreign citizens – Medical University of Sofia](https://mu-sofia.bg/en/admission/admissions-of-foreign-citizen/), [Tuition fees for Medicine in English – Sofia Medical University (FAQ)](https://www.interhecs.com/sv/news/study-at-sofia-medical-university-faq/).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### Carol Davila University of Medicine and Pharmacy — Medicine (MD, 6 years) (Bucharest, Romania)

- **Location:** Bucharest, Romania.
- **Language of instruction:** English and Romanian tracks (English‑taught pathway available for international students).
- **Admissions (high‑level):** competitive selection based on science grades, entrance testing, and language requirements as published each year.
- **Tuition (published):** recent university‑linked and specialist sources cite tuition of approximately €10,000 per year for the English‑language Medicine track; UMFCD and ministry documents also reference annual fees around €8,000–€10,000 depending on category.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** EU capital city with well‑known public medical faculty and relatively moderate tuition compared with UK or Cyprus; frequently appears in price‑sensitive agent shortlists.
- **Sources:** [UMFCD tuition‑fee table for international students 2025–2026 (PDF)](https://umfcd.ro/en/wp-content/uploads/2025/INTERNATIONAL_STUDENTS/ADMISSION_2025/DOCUMENTS/Taxe_UMFCD_2025-2026_studenti_internationali_EN.pdf), [Medicine at Carol Davila University – tuition summary](https://stinwo.ro/programmes/medicine-at-carol-davila-university/).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

### Caribbean

#### St George’s University (SGU) — School of Medicine (MD) (Grenada, Caribbean)

- **Location:** St. George’s, Grenada.
- **Language of instruction:** English.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** usually graduate‑entry; requires a bachelor’s degree with pre‑med sciences, MCAT (for many applicants), and holistic review.
- **Tuition (published):** official tuition schedules show high five‑figure USD tuition per year (detailed per term for basic and clinical sciences), making SGU one of the more expensive MD routes globally.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** perceived US‑aligned pathway with strong US residency outcomes; sometimes recommended by agents to families set on North American practice.
- **Sources:** [St George’s University – School of Medicine](https://www.sgu.edu/academic-programs/school-of-medicine/), [Tuition and fees – St George’s University](https://www.sgu.edu/tuition-and-fees/).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12

#### American University of the Caribbean (AUC) — School of Medicine (MD) (Sint Maarten, Caribbean)

- **Location:** Sint Maarten (Dutch Caribbean).
- **Language of instruction:** English.
- **Admissions (high‑level):** primarily graduate‑entry; requires a prior bachelor’s degree with core science prerequisites and English‑language evidence.
- **Tuition (published):** AUC cost‑of‑attendance and tuition pages publish per‑semester rates for Medical Sciences and Clinical Sciences; total MD tuition sits in the low‑to‑mid‑six‑figure USD range over four years.
- **Why Nigerian families choose it:** marketed as a US‑style curriculum with clinical rotations in the US and UK; sometimes surfaced when families are focused on US practice and willing to pay high tuition.
- **Sources:** [Tuition and financial aid – AUC School of Medicine](https://www.aucmed.edu/admissions/tuition-and-financial-aid), [Cost of attendance – AUC School of Medicine](https://www.aucmed.edu/media/12766/cost-of-attendance).
- **last_verified:** 2026-04-12
