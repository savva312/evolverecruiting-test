# Nigeria outbound mobility

**Last updated:** 2025-12-20  
**Source report:** [Nigeria Outbound Mobility Baseline (2025-12-20)](../reports/2025-12-20-nigeria-outbound-mobility-baseline.md)

## Decision snapshot (for UNIC and UNIC Athens)

- Nigeria is a large outbound tertiary market; a direct UNESCO UIS extract is still pending, so treat any global totals from secondary sources as provisional.
- Nigeria demand skews postgraduate: in the U.S. (Open Doors 2024/25) Nigerians are **53.2% graduate** and **20.1% on OPT**, supporting employability-focused master’s pathways.
- Anchor destinations with strong official series:
  - **United States:** 21,847 Nigerian students (2024/25, Open Doors).
  - **Canada:** 45,965 study permit holders (Dec 31, 2023, IRCC; 11,869 in 2019).
  - **United Kingdom:** 44,195 total Nigerian students and 32,945 new enrolments (2021/22, HESA via Universities UK).
- Greece vs RoC Cyprus (Eurostat, origin Nigeria):
  - **Enrolled mobile tertiary students (stock, ISCED 5–8):** RoC 295–377 (2020–2024 range); Greece 123 (2020) then 0/0/0 with 2024 missing.
  - **First residence permits for education (flow proxy):** RoC 122–154 (2020–2022) → 72 (2024); Greece 5–59 range (2020–2024).

## What the indicators imply

- **UNIC (RoC Cyprus):** Demonstrated Nigeria-linked footprint (hundreds enrolled; tens–low hundreds permits). Keep Nigeria as a priority market but tighten visa-file quality and monitor the 2022–2024 permit downshift.
- **UNIC Athens (Greece):** External signals are small/volatile; treat Nigeria as a market-building effort with differentiated value, strong documentation support, and targeted program bets.
- **Program focus:** Lead with taught master’s and employability outcomes; use computing/data, engineering, and business as high-probability clusters (context-based until Nigeria-by-field tables are added). Regulated tracks require feasibility checks (licensure, placements).

## Data caveats (use verbatim in downstream decks)

- **UIS extract pending:** global outbound totals and destination ranking need a direct UIS export; current totals are provisional.
- **Stock vs flow:** Eurostat enrolled students (`educ_uoe_mobs02`) ≠ permits (`migr_resfirst`); use stock for footprint, permits for momentum.
- **Origin definition variability:** UIS/UOE prefer prior education as origin; some national systems fall back to residence/citizenship.
- **Small-cell volatility:** Greece series shows 0s/missing; treat as “low and uncertain” rather than proof of absence until corroborated.
- **RoC vs TRNC:** Eurostat “Cyprus (CY)” = Republic of Cyprus only; TRNC volumes are not captured and need separate estimation.
