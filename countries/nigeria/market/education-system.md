# Nigeria education system overview

This guide summarizes how Nigerian students progress from basic education into tertiary study, with emphasis on credentials, grading, and timelines relevant to recruiting for UNIC/UNIC Athens.

## Governance and system structure
- **Federal Ministry of Education** sets national policy; **State Ministries of Education** run public schools.  
- **Universal Basic Education Commission (UBEC)** oversees the 9-year compulsory basic cycle (primary + lower secondary).  
- **Regulators/exam bodies:**  
  - **WAEC** administers the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE) for school candidates.  
  - **NECO** runs the Senior School Certificate Examination (SSCE) and Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE).  
  - **NABTEB** manages technical/vocational exams (NTC/NBC) for technical colleges.  
  - **JAMB** runs the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) for university/polytechnic admission.  
  - **NUC** (universities), **NBTE** (polytechnics/mono­technics), **NCCE** (colleges of education) accredit tertiary programs.

## Grade/age bands and pathways
- **Basic education (9 years, compulsory):** Primary 1–6 (ages ~6–11), then **Junior Secondary School (JSS 1–3)** (ages ~12–14). Students sit **BECE** (NECO) at end of JSS3 to stream into senior routes.  
- **Senior Secondary School (SSS 1–3, ages ~15–17):** Three-year upper secondary with elective clusters (Science/Tech, Arts/Social Science, Commercial). Culminates in **WASSCE (WAEC)** and/or **SSCE (NECO)** in SS3.  
- **Technical/Vocational route:** Post-BECE entry to technical colleges offering **National Technical/Business Certificates (NTC/NBC)** via NABTEB; graduates may progress to polytechnics or direct employment.  
- **Post-secondary bridges:**  
  - **A-Levels/IB/Foundations** offered by private schools or offshore providers; used for direct 200-level entry or foreign admissions.  
  - **Polytechnics/Monotechnics:** Admit with SSCE/NTC/NBC; award **National Diploma (ND)** after 2 years, then **Higher National Diploma (HND)** after industrial attachment and further 2 years.  
  - **Colleges of Education:** Three-year **Nigeria Certificate in Education (NCE)** feeding teaching careers or lateral university entry.

## Curriculum highlights
- **Languages:** English (medium of instruction after early primary) + one major Nigerian language.  
- **Core subjects:** English, Mathematics, one Nigerian language, Biology, Chemistry, Physics (for science), Civic Education, Economics/Government, plus electives (Literature, Further Math, ICT, Technical Drawing, Commerce, Accounting, etc.).  
- **Continuous assessment (CA):** Typically 30% of final secondary score; 70% from external WAEC/NECO exam papers.

## Exit exams and grading
- **WASSCE (WAEC school candidates):** Written Apr–Jun; results usually released Jul–Aug.  
  - **Grading scale:** A1 (75–100), B2 (70–74), B3 (65–69), C4 (60–64), C5 (55–59), C6 (50–54), D7 (45–49), E8 (40–44), F9 (<40).  
  - **University minimum:** Five credits (A1–C6) including English and Math; competitive programs expect B3+ in core subjects.  
- **NECO SSCE:** Held Jun–Jul; results ~Sep. Grading mirrors WAEC (A1–F9). Often used as second sitting or to combine results (“two sittings”).  
- **BECE (JSS3):** Graded A–F; used for senior school placement, not university admission.  
- **NABTEB NTC/NBC:** Graded A1–F9; recognized for technical progression and some university tech entries.  
- **Other credentials in market:** Cambridge International A-Levels/IGCSE, IB Diploma, and university foundation programs; transcripts and scratch-card verified WAEC/NECO results are standard for foreign applications.

## University entry (JAMB/UTME and alternatives)
- **UTME:** Computer-based exam (score 0–400) with four subjects aligned to intended course; English is compulsory. Registration typically Jan–Feb; exams Mar–May.  
  - **Cut-off policy:** JAMB sets a sector baseline (often 140–160); universities set higher departmental cut-offs (200–280 for competitive courses like Medicine, Law, Engineering).  
- **Post-UTME/Screening:** Many universities add their own test or aggregate WAEC/NECO + UTME scores; timelines Jul–Oct.  
- **Direct Entry (DE):** Allows 200-level entry for candidates with A-Levels, ND/HND, NCE, or JUPEB/other foundation diplomas; still processed through JAMB.  
- **Results combination:** Most universities accept **“two sittings”** of WAEC/NECO credits; some elite/private schools prefer single sitting.  
- **International admissions (e.g., UNIC/UNIC Athens):**  
  - Common package: WAEC/NECO scratch card printout + school transcript; English waivers often granted because language of instruction is English (confirm per program).  
  - Stronger candidates may present A-Levels/IB or ND/HND for advanced standing.

## Key timelines (typical)
- **SS3 teaching year:** Sep–Mar.  
- **WAEC school exam:** Apr–Jun; **results:** Jul–Aug.  
- **NECO SSCE:** Jun–Jul; **results:** Sep.  
- **UTME registration:** Jan–Feb; **exam:** Mar–May; **results:** within days; **post-UTME:** Jul–Oct.  
- **Admission offers/matriculation:** Aug–Nov for local universities; foreign intakes follow destination calendars (e.g., Sep/Jan).
