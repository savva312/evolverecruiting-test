# Serbia — recognition & licensure (UNIC Nicosia / UNIC Athens)

Last updated: 2025-12-22  
Sources last verified: 2025-12-22

> Disclaimer: This brief is informational for UNIC enrollment planning and does not constitute legal advice. Candidates must confirm requirements directly with Serbian authorities before taking action.

## Snapshot for counselors
- Academic recognition for employment is centralised under the Qualifications Agency / ENIC-NARIC Serbia (Belgrade). Decisions take up to 60 days once a file is complete, or eight days for degrees from universities listed in the latest QS/Times/Shanghai top-500 tables or for Serbian government scholarship recipients, provided all documentation is in order. (Sources: https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-diplomas ; https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/)
- Academic recognition for further study is handled by the receiving Serbian university (e.g., University of Belgrade, University of Kragujevac) and mirrors ENIC requirements but with university-specific fees and deadlines. (https://www.bg.edu.rs/recognition/ ; https://en.kg.ac.rs/foreign_qualifications.php)
- Professional licensure (medicine, nursing/allied health, psychology, law) requires academic recognition plus sector-specific steps with Serbian chambers/ministries. Licences typically run seven years and rely on continuing education logs; temporary licences for foreign professionals can be issued for up to 180 days per calendar year. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/License.aspx ; https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/serbia-licensing-requirements-professional-services)
- Always highlight the awarding campus in offers/contracts. Serbian authorities base recognition on the awarding country listed on the diploma, so spell out that UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus) and UNIC Athens (Greece) hold EU-recognised accreditation and provide those attestations with every recognition file.

## 1. How Serbia organises recognition

| Step | What happens | Notes for UNIC |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Academic recognition for employment | The Qualifications Agency (ENIC-NARIC Serbia) runs an e-portal for applications, followed by an in-person handover of originals in Belgrade. Standard decisions: ≤60 days; expedited cases: eight days when eligibility is met. (https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-diplomas ; https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/) | Build a CRM trigger so Serbian candidates collect apostilled documents, sworn translations, and CVs before leaving Cyprus/Greece. |
| Academic recognition for further study | Serbian universities review files for enrolment. Fees/examples: University of Belgrade charges 33,000 RSD + 14,420 RSD administrative fee and requires the process to start ≥15 days before faculty deadlines; University of Kragujevac charges 30,000–40,000 RSD plus a 13,210 RSD tax. (https://www.bg.edu.rs/recognition/ ; https://en.kg.ac.rs/foreign_qualifications.php) | Provide accreditation letters (Cyprus HQAA, Greece HQA) and curriculum outlines within each admissions pack. |
| Professional recognition/licensure | Competent ministries + chambers (medical, nursing, psychology, bar) issue licences. Most require Serbian language readiness, proof of employment, membership dues, and CME/CPD logs. Temporary licences allow short-term work (≤180 days/year) with an invitation letter and proof of home-country licensure. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/License.aspx ; https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/serbia-licensing-requirements-professional-services) | Maintain a shared repository of programme descriptions, clinical hour summaries, and good-standing letters so alumni can supply regulators quickly. |

## 2. Academic recognition (Qualifications Agency / ENIC-NARIC Serbia)

### 2.1 When Serbian students need it
- Employment in the Serbian public sector or any regulated profession.
- Temporary residence based on “talent” or employment categories.
- Supporting evidence for professional licences and, on request, for postgraduate admissions. (https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-qualifications)

### 2.2 Process & timelines
1. **Pre-application** – Gather scans of the diploma, Diploma Supplement/transcript, previous degrees, passport/ID, CV, proof of accreditation, and payment receipt. (https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/)
2. **E-request** – Submit via the Qualifications Agency portal; applicants must also enter data for prior qualifications when requesting Master/PhD recognition. (https://welcometoserbia.org/en/recognition-of-an-education-document/)
3. **Document handover** – Print the e-request and deliver originals + certified copies to the Agency in Belgrade (Majke Jevrosime 51) when scheduled. Decisions are collected in person. (https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-diplomas)
4. **Decision window** – Up to 60 days after a *complete* file. Fast track (eight days) applies to degrees from institutions listed in recognised global rankings or to Serbian state scholars. (https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/)
5. **Use of decision** – Provide notarised copies to employers/ministries/chambers; keep the original in secure storage.

### 2.3 Documents (prepare before graduation travel)

| Document | Why Serbia needs it | UNIC preparation tips |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Diploma + Diploma Supplement / transcript | Confirms qualification level, credits, grades. Originals must carry the issuing country’s official seal/legalisation (apostille or equivalent) before Serbia accepts the file. (https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-diplomas) | Provide degree packages with serial numbers and official signatures; pre-book legalization and apostille appointments in Cyprus/Greece. |
| Certified Serbian translations | Serbian authorities accept only translations by sworn court interpreters registered with the Serbian MFA; universities demand multiple certified copies. (https://www.bg.edu.rs/recognition/) | Keep a vetted list of Belgrade court interpreters and budget ~1,500 RSD per page (per Čučković Legal). |
| Programme accreditation proof & curricula | Universities compare outcomes/ECTS; some request curricula and proof of accreditation. (https://en.kg.ac.rs/foreign_qualifications.php) | Store Cyprus HQAA / Greek HQA certificates, Diploma Supplement templates, and curriculum maps in a shared drive. |
| Identity document, CV, prior-degree evidence | Needed for Master/PhD recognition and to verify applicant history. (https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/) | Issue a UNIC CV template capturing internships and clinical placements. |
| Proof of payment | Agency fee: 13,150 RSD; translation/apostille fees extra. (https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/) | Encourage payments via Serbian partner banks to avoid FX delays; upload receipts to the portal. |

### 2.4 Translation & legalisation rules
- Originals must carry the issuing-country authentication (apostille or equivalent) before the Agency accepts them; the Qualifications Agency stresses that documents have to be “certified with a stamp/seal by the competent authority” before submission. (https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-diplomas)
- Serbian universities explicitly require “two copies of a certified translation … translated by a sworn-in court interpreter in the Republic of Serbia.” (https://www.bg.edu.rs/recognition/)
- Even EU-format Diploma Supplements are safest when translated into Serbian to avoid rework at the counter. (Qualifications Agency practice shared on https://welcometoserbia.gov.rs/recognition-of-diplomas)

### 2.5 Fees & scheduling
- Qualifications Agency (professional recognition): 13,150 RSD, plus translation/apostille costs. (https://cuckoviclegal.com/professional-recognition-of-foreign-higher-education-diplomas-in-serbia/)
- University of Belgrade (academic recognition for enrolment): 33,000 RSD + 14,420 RSD republic administrative fee; applications must start ≥15 days before faculty deadlines (e.g., July 15, 2025 for 2025/26 intake). (https://www.bg.edu.rs/recognition/)
- University of Kragujevac: 30,000 RSD (Bachelor) / 40,000 RSD (Master/Doctoral) + 13,210 RSD administrative tax. (https://en.kg.ac.rs/foreign_qualifications.php)

### 2.6 Submission checkpoints & common blockers
- **Incomplete translations** – Without sworn Serbian translations, files are returned; maintain translator bookings before graduation.
- **Missing prior-degree proof** – Master-level recognition requires Bachelor credentials; remind students in integrated programmes.
- **Late submissions** – Universities will not accept files started inside the 15-day buffer; set CRM reminders for Serbian applicants mid-semester.
- **Portal access** – Applicants without Serbian eID may need to finalise submissions at the Agency desk; offer Serbian-speaking support staff.

## 4. Professional recognition & licensure (regulated programmes)

General flow:
1. **Academic recognition** (ENIC or Serbian university decision).
2. **Language and residency readiness** – regulators expect Serbian language proficiency and valid residence/work status.
3. **Chamber application** – includes membership dues, proof of employment/internship, clean criminal record, and evidence of passing the Serbian state/professional exam.
4. **Continuing education** – licences typically last seven years; e.g., physicians must log ≥10 CME points per licence year or sit a licence exam prior to renewal. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/Kme.aspx)

## 5. Program-specific callouts (last_verified: 2025-12-22)

### 5.1 Medicine / MD
- **Competent bodies**: Serbian Medical Chamber (Lekarska komora Srbije) via regional chambers + Ministry of Health. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/License.aspx)
- **Key requirements**:
  1. ENIC or Serbian university recognition of the MD diploma.
  2. Completion of Serbian internship/state exam or approval of an equivalent internship abroad; applicants must submit proof of internship, state exam, and specialisation certificates when applicable. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/Registration.aspx)
  3. Chamber membership (0.8% of net salary monthly; minimum 400 RSD) and licence fee payment. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/MembershipFee.aspx)
  4. CME compliance: physicians must submit CME certificates annually and log ≥10 points per licence year or sit a licence exam. (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/Kme.aspx)
- **Temporary licences**: Foreign doctors may obtain a temporary licence for up to 180 days/year with a Serbian invitation, proof of home-country licence, passport, and payments (6,000 RSD registration + 10,000 RSD licence). (https://www.lkv.org.rs/PagesUserEng/License.aspx ; https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/serbia-licensing-requirements-professional-services)
- **UNIC action items**: Provide Serbian-language “certificate of conformity” letters summarising curriculum, clinical rotations, and accreditation for UNIC MD programmes; collect good-standing letters before students leave Cyprus/Greece.

### 5.2 Nursing, physiotherapy & allied health
- **Competent body**: Komora medicinskih sestara i zdravstvenih tehničara Srbije (KMSTS). The 2025 Statute confirms the chamber issues, renews, and revokes licences and maintains the registry. (https://www.paragraf.rs/propisi/statut_komore_medicinskih_sestara_i_zdravstvenih_tehnicara_srbije.html)
- **Key requirements**:
  1. ENIC/Serbian university recognition of the nursing/allied health degree.
  2. Proof of completed internship and state exam for Serbian nationals returning home.
  3. Evidence of CME credits; members lacking the required points must pass a KMSTS licence exam before renewal. (https://uzrsnobelosrce.co.rs/izdavanje-licence-obnova-i-druga-dokumenta/)
  4. Active employment contract or invitation letter; KMSTS expects confirmation of employment when issuing licences.
- **Licence validity**: Licences are typically issued for seven years (initial nationwide rollout confirmed by VMA’s licence ceremony note). (https://www.vma.mod.gov.rs/sr-lat/vesti/svecana-dodela-licenci-za-rad-medicinskim-sestrama-i-tehnicarima-vma)
- **UNIC action items**: Package clinical hour logs and practicum attestations with Serbian translations so alumni can evidence hands-on training; coordinate with Serbian employers to provide onboarding letters.

### 5.3 Psychology / counselling
- **Legal framework**: The Law on Conditions for Performing Psychological Activity (Official Gazette RS 25/96 & 101/2005) limits psychological services to graduates of accredited psychology programmes who have completed a one-year internship and passed the state professional exam. (https://www.lexfacie.com/documents/view/564/zakon-o-uslovima-za-obavljanje-psiholoske-delatnosti-rs-1996-2005)
- **Oversight & reforms**: Draft amendments published in 2024 propose establishing a Psychology Chamber that would issue six-year licences; until adopted, oversight stays with the Ministry of Labour/social protection and its exam commissions. (https://www.paragraf.rs/dnevne-vesti/120624/120624-vest10.html)
- **Key requirements**:
  1. ENIC recognition of the psychology degree or Master’s.
  2. Proof of internship (minimum 12 months) and Serbian state exam completion. (https://www.epsihoterapija.com/polaganje-drzavnog-ispita)
  3. Compliance with existing law prohibiting unlicensed “psychological” services; the Psychologists’ Society is actively enforcing this, so alumni should avoid mislabelling counselling before licensure. (https://eklinika.telegraf.rs/vesti/171864-drustvo-psihologa-srbije-upozorava-ko-sme-da-drzi-psiholoska-savetovanja-edukacije-ili-psiholoske-radionice)
- **UNIC action items**: Maintain detailed practicum logs and, where needed, connect graduates with Serbian internship slots to complete the statutory year.

### 5.4 Law
- **Competent body**: Serbian Bar Association (Advokatska komora Srbije) under the Law on Legal Practice (Official Gazette RS 31/2011). (https://www.cplaw.rs/law-on-legal-practice/)
- **Key requirements**:
  1. ENIC recognition/equivalency of the law degree.
  2. Serbian bar exam. Foreign attorneys can register in **Register A** to advise on home-country or international law without taking the Serbian bar exam; to practise Serbian law they must pass both the Serbian bar exam and the attorney exam, then spend up to three years co-signing with a local attorney before independent practice (**Register B**). (https://www.cplaw.rs/law-on-legal-practice/ ; https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/serbia-licensing-requirements-professional-services)
  3. Professional indemnity insurance and chamber membership prior to licence issuance.
- **UNIC action items**: Provide curriculum summaries highlighting civil-law training and EU modules; partner with Serbian firms for trainee placements for alumni pursuing full licensure.
