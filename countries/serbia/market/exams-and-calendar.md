# Serbia – exams and academic calendar (2024–25 / 2025–26 outlook)

Document national exams (State Matura rollout status, current graduation exams, university entrance tests), key testing periods, and school/university calendar milestones. Note timing impacts on outreach, application windows, and deposit deadlines. Serbia’s State Matura is still transitioning from pilots to full implementation; universities continue to rely on faculty-specific entrance exams.

## Month-by-month view (current and next cycle)

- **September** – School year starts the **first working day of September** (2024: 2 Sep). Universities begin **early October**. Outreach: reintroduce UNIC to Grade 12 before faculty application prep accelerates.
- **October** – Regular classes and first assessment wave. Light recruitment window; schedule counselor briefings. IB/A-Level schools begin mock prep.
- **November** – **Armistice Day (11 Nov) break**; some schools add an extra day creating a long weekend. Limited outreach during that week; good time for webinars.
- **December** – Semester closes **late Dec (around 27–30 Dec)**. Winter break starts immediately after; avoid in-person activity from Christmas through New Year.
- **January** – Winter break continues into the **second/third week of Jan**; **St. Sava Day (27 Jan)** is a public school holiday. Use digital nurturing; restart school visits in late Jan once second semester begins.
- **February** – Career guidance events and mock graduation exams often occur. **State Matura/ graduation exam candidate lists finalized by mid–late Feb** (school-managed). Push subject selection and scholarship awareness before lists lock.
- **March** – Faculties publish **entrance-exam syllabi and registration instructions**. Target parent nights and counselor PD; encourage students to book English tests (IELTS/TOEFL) before May congestion.
- **April** – **Spring break aligns with Orthodox Easter (late Apr/early May, shifts annually)**; schools pause for ~5–7 days. Keep outreach virtual; avoid Holy Week. IB/Cambridge revision intensifies.
- **May** – **Graduation exams begin late May**; IB/Cambridge/AP written papers run **early–late May**. Faculties open **entrance exam applications mid May**, closing mid June. Outreach: switch to low-friction reminders and conditional offer pushes.
- **June** – **Graduation exams and defenses wrap by early June**; **(future) State Matura pilots/launch planned early–mid June**; **university entrance exams typically the last week of June**. Results for graduation exams released by mid June. Action: prepare document checks and fast-track offer issuance immediately after results.
- **July** – **First enrollment/acceptance rounds early July**, with **second rounds mid-July**. Strong month for conversion, deposits, and visa guidance.
- **August** – Some faculties run **make-up/late entrance sessions**; **State Matura retakes (once live) would fall late Aug/early Sep**. Re-engage students who missed July decisions; offer September intake options.
- **September (next cycle)** – **Third/late university enrollment early Sep**; new school year begins first working day of Sep; universities resume early Oct. Reset outreach to new Grade 12 and keep touchpoints with deferrals.

## Timing implications for UNIC/UNIC Athens

- **Application pacing:** Secure applications **before mid May** so candidates are free during graduation and entrance exam prep. Offer conditional letters pending graduation certificates and entrance results.
- **Results sprint:** Mobilize verification for **mid-June graduation results** and **early July university entrance outcomes**; aim to issue final offers within **5–7 days** of each release.
- **Entrance-exam blackout:** Avoid heavy in-person outreach **late May–end June**; shift to reassurance content, scholarship nudges, and remote Q&A.
- **International school track:** For IB/Cambridge schools, anchor outreach **Jan–Mar**, collect **predicted grades**, and pre-book **May** follow-ups right after exam periods.
- **Retention of uncertain cohorts:** Prepare contingency messaging for **State Matura rollout slippage** (potential deferral to 2026); keep both entrance-exam and Matura-based checklists ready for counselors.

## Assumptions and caveats

- Dates are based on the **2024–25 national school calendar order** and typical university schedules; exact days shift annually and are confirmed by Ministry decrees and faculty notices each spring.
- **State Matura timing is not yet fixed**; universities continue to run entrance exams and may do so in parallel during the first Matura years.
- Private/international schools may vary slightly on breaks but generally mirror national windows for holidays and exam pauses.
