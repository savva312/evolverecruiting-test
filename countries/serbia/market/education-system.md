# Education system

## Governance snapshot
- **Ministry of Education (MoE)** sets national curricula, licensing, and standards for all levels; local self-governments finance and organise pre-primary and primary schools while MoE directly oversees most secondary schools and higher education.
- **National Education Council** approves curricula/standards, and the **Institute for Education Quality and Evaluation** runs national testing (primary school leaving exam) and leads the rollout of the **State Matura**.
- Qualifications use a 1–5 grading scale and align to the National Qualifications Framework (NQFS), which is progressively mapped to the EQF.

## Structure and grade/age bands
| Stage | Ages | Grades | Notes and transition rules |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Preschool (compulsory preparatory programme) | 5–6 | pre‑school year | Half-day programme became compulsory in 2006/07; delivered in kindergartens before entry to primary. |
| Primary (osnovna škola) | 6/7–14/15 | 1–8 | Single-structure compulsory education. Years 1–4 use a class teacher model; years 5–8 switch to subject teachers. National final exam after grade 8 is used to rank students for secondary placement. |
| Upper secondary (srednje obrazovanje) | 14/15–18/19 | 1–4 (some VET 3-year) | Choice of **general/gymnasium** (4-year), **vocational/technical** (3- or 4-year) and **arts** schools. Students apply via a points system combining primary GPA + grade 8 final exam + school preferences. |
| Post-secondary VET | 18–19 | 1-year “master craftsman” or specialist courses | Optional progression for graduates of 3-year vocational programmes to access higher-skilled employment or applied HE. |
| Higher education | 18/19+ | Bachelor (3–4 years), applied studies (3 years), integrated master (5–6 years) | Universities and applied science/college institutions; current admission is university-run entrance exams + secondary record, shifting toward State Matura. |

### Curriculum streams and tracking
- **General/gymnasium**: 4-year programmes with common core (Serbian language, mathematics, sciences, social sciences, foreign languages) plus elective modules (e.g., maths/CS, social sciences, natural sciences, languages). Builds toward general/opšta matura and university entry.
- **Vocational/technical (VET)**: 4-year technical tracks (ICT, mechanical, health, economics, agriculture, etc.) mix general subjects with 30–40% vocational theory and practical training; lead to stručna matura and allow higher education entry. 3-year craft programmes focus on work-based learning and end with a final (non-matura) practical exam for labour-market entry; graduates can add a 1-year master craftsman/specialist year or take bridging courses for HE.
- **Arts**: 4-year specialised schools (music, ballet, visual arts) culminating in umetnička matura plus portfolio/performance components.

### Grading scale
- **1 (fail), 2 (sufficient), 3 (good), 4 (very good), 5 (excellent)**. Promotion requires at least “2” per subject; GPA from grades 6–8 drives secondary admission scoring, and secondary GPA contributes to higher-ed admission weighting.

## Credentials, exams, and transition points
- **Grade 8 final exam (završni ispit)**: Serbian (or mother tongue), mathematics, and a combined subject test. Results + GPA determine secondary placement.
- **School-leaving credentials**:
  - **Opšta matura (general matura)** for gymnasium students (Serbian/mother tongue, Mathematics, and an elective such as physics/history/foreign language). First national rollout is planned with cohorts graduating 2025/26; until then, schools issue a diploma plus school-level finals.
  - **Stručna matura (vocational matura)** for 4-year VET adds an occupational/practical exam to Serbian/mother tongue and either Mathematics or a professional theory subject.
  - **Umetnička matura** blends general tests with performance/portfolio exams for arts schools.
  - **Final/practical exam (završni ispit)** for 3-year VET confirms occupational competence; does not on its own grant direct university access.
- **University entrance** (current practice): completion of a 4-year secondary programme + university/faculty entrance exam (subject-specific) + weighted secondary GPA. Faculties may retain aptitude tests (e.g., arts, architecture, PE) even after State Matura.
- **University entrance (transition plan)**: State Matura results will become the primary admission instrument, with universities setting subject combinations and minimum scores; faculties can add aptitude tests where needed. Policy signals target replacing most institutional entrance exams once State Matura stabilises (earliest cohorts 2025/26).

## Implications for UNIC outreach
- **Track targeting**: Prioritise 4-year gymnasia and 4-year technical VET for direct HE progression; 3-year VET students may need bridging or a specialist year.
- **Messaging**: Highlight recognition of upcoming State Matura/stručna matura results plus flexibility for faculty-specific aptitude tests; clarify English proficiency expectations (foreign language instruction begins in grade 1 and a second foreign language typically by grade 5).
- **Calendar**: Primary final exam and secondary admissions occur in June; secondary graduation exams and university entrance tests typically run June–July, with State Matura expected in the same window once fully rolled out.
