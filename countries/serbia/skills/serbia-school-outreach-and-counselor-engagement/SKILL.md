# Serbia addendum: school outreach & counselor engagement

Use `/skills/school-outreach-and-counselor-engagement/SKILL.md` for the standard workflow. Apply these Serbia-specific notes when contacting schools and counselors.

## Data sources and routing
- Use school/counselor profiles under `countries/serbia/entities/schools/` and CSVs in `countries/serbia/data/entities/` as the starting list.
- Tag interactions by region (Belgrade, Novi Sad, Niš, Kragujevac, regional) and curriculum (national vs international) for prioritization.
- Log consent and touchpoints in the country data model (`countries/serbia/skills/serbia-data-model-csvs-and-profiles/`) before syncing elsewhere.

## Language and etiquette
- Default to Serbian for outreach and leave-behinds; include English versions for international schools.
- Avoid heavy outreach during Matura/final exam periods (late spring/early summer) and major holidays; propose virtual sessions when in-person slots are limited.
- Viber and email are common; confirm WhatsApp preference before using it for follow-ups.

## Visit logistics
- Some schools require advance approval from directors; share agenda and presenter names in Serbian.
- Bring concise UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning and admissions timelines; avoid speculative scholarship statements.

## Consent and data protection
- Apply Serbia’s data protection rules (GDPR-aligned): capture opt-in source/time, honor opt-outs, and avoid storing national ID numbers or grades in notes.
- Keep logs to professional contact info, program interests, and interaction history.

## Follow-up cadence
- Send Serbian-language recaps within 48 hours, tagging the visit or event name; include English attachments for international schools.
- Time workshops/webinars after exam results when counselors and families can focus on applications.
