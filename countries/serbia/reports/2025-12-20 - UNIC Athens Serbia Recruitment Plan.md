# UNIC Athens Serbia Recruitment Plan

## 1) Executive summary

UNIC Athens can grow a steady Serbia pipeline by pairing transparent pricing with proximity (Belgrade–Athens ~1h35m) and English delivery backed by clear recognition. The initial target is to prove a 2026 intake with disciplined execution rather than volume bets.

Success markers for Fall 2026
- Starters: **40–60 non-medical** plus **10–15 Medicine** with cost-per-start kept within scholarship + marketing caps.
- Net price clarity: public merit grid that lands strong Serbian profiles at **10–30%** tuition relief; selective Medicine fee credits for early decision.
- Coverage: consistent counselor and agent presence across **Belgrade, Novi Sad, Niš, and Kragujevac** with clear follow-up SLAs.

Why UNIC Athens wins in Serbia
- English-taught degrees with EU recognition and a predictable admissions path (no numerus fixus).
- Short travel, familiar culture, and transparent living costs versus Western Europe.
- Disciplined scholarship policy and early-decision offers that align to Serbia’s exam calendar.

## 2) Portfolio, pricing, and recognition (Athens reference)

These anchors should be used in Serbia-facing materials and refreshed with official sources each intake.

- **Programs (English, Athens):** Accounting, Business Administration, Marketing, Computer Science, Data Science, Medicine (MD). Psychology/Law/Pharmacy run in Greek in Athens—keep that clear.
- **Tuition (EU students):** Non-medical €15,480/year; Medicine €27,000/year. Semester fees and medical insurance charged separately per Medical School policy.
- **Payment options:** 10-month plan at 0% interest for non-medical; semester instalments for Medicine. 4% early-payment discount per semester applies when paid by add/drop.
- **Cost of attendance (Athens south suburbs):** Low/Mid/High scenarios roughly €10.6k/€14k/€17.5k per year for housing and living—use the COA tables in go-to-market materials.
- **Recognition:** Program accreditations via CYQAA and HAHE; degrees awarded by the University of Nicosia. For Serbia, academic recognition handled by **ENIC-NARIC Serbia (Foundation Tempus)**; professional recognition for Medicine follows Serbian Ministry of Health/Medical Chamber steps after academic recognition.

## 3) Market context and demand signals

- Outbound patterns: Germany, Austria, and Italy dominate; Hungary and Slovenia visible for STEM/health. Data extracts for Serbia from Eurostat/UIS are pending—treat current guidance as qualitative until the dataset is added to `countries/serbia/data/`.
- City concentration: international/IB schools and elite gymnasiums in **Belgrade** plus strong math/language schools in **Novi Sad, Niš, and Kragujevac** generate most English-proficient applicants.
- Decision drivers: predictable admissions, transparent net price, living-cost clarity, and licensure/recognition proof. Parents respond well to Serbian-language materials and early deposit incentives tied to exam calendars.

## 4) Priority segments and personas

- **STEM/CS students (Belgrade & Novi Sad):** High math proficiency, interested in CS/data/cybersecurity; compare against Germany/Austria/Netherlands. Needs: internship proof, scholarships in the 10–20% band, and visa simplicity.
- **Business/psychology seekers (Belgrade/Niš):** Strong English, mid-to-high income families; value proximity and English campus life. Needs: cost-of-attendance clarity and Erasmus+/placement stories.
- **Medicine candidates (nationwide, agent-driven):** Willing to travel for structured clinical training; compare against CEE faculties. Needs: entrance/interview calendar, licensure pathway for Serbia, and selective fee credits.

## 5) Channel and partner plan

Schools
- Target Tier A international/IB and STEM language schools in Belgrade first, then Novi Sad, Niš, Kragujevac. Use the visit templates in `go-to-market/schools-playbook/` with Serbian-language assets for parents.
- Offer masterclasses (CS/data; business entrepreneurship) and Medicine Q&A blocks; collect opt-ins via QR and deliver 48-hour pre-checks.

Agents and fairs
- Onboard **IntegralEdu Serbia** (World Education Fair tour), **Via Academica**, and **Future Option** with clear SLAs (lead quality, pre-check turnaround, scholarship governance).
- Use **World Education Fair**, **Education Beyond Borders Belgrade**, and **EducationUSA/Fulbright Belgrade** for visibility; wrap school visits around Belgrade/Novi Sad/Niš fair stops.

Digital
- Meta + Google Search as primary; Serbian/English split starting 60/40 for undergrad. Guardrails in `data/marketing/channel-benchmarks.csv`.
- Landing pages: Serbian copy, recognition/licensure one-pager, COA table, and merit grid calculator; UTMs per city/partner.

Offerholder and yield
- Publish Serbian-language checklists for documents, payment plans, housing, and recognition. Run monthly offer-holder webinars (general + Medicine) with Serbian Q&A.
- Keep 48-hour pre-checks and weekly interview slots during February–April; align deposit pushes before June exam windows.

## 6) 2026 execution calendar (Serbia-facing)

- **January:** Launch Serbian landing pages + calculator; publish merit grid; agent onboarding; counselor outreach begins for Belgrade/Novi Sad. Start digital always-on campaigns.
- **February–March:** School visits in Belgrade/Novi Sad/Niš; World Education Fair spring tour; Medicine webinar series and interviews; counselor breakfast in Belgrade.
- **April:** Early-bird deposit push (non-medical); Medicine early-decision fee credits communicated; light digital during exam prep weeks.
- **May–June:** Avoid State Matura/IB windows; focus on offer-holder support, housing, and visa/registration guidance; run parent webinars.
- **July–August:** Finalize visa/registration steps; publish Autumn fair schedule; retarget digital to late deciders.
- **September–October:** Autumn fairs (Belgrade/Novi Sad/Kragujevac); seed Spring 2027 interest; refresh scholarship grid for next cycle.
