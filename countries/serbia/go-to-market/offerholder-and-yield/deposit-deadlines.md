# Deposit deadlines

- **Last updated:** 2025-12-22  
- **Owners:** Admissions Ops (UNIC Nicosia); Admissions Ops (UNIC Athens) — accountable; Country Recruiter Serbia — support

## Serbia-specific principles

- Align to Serbian secondary exams (May–June) and diploma release (mid–July); allow families ~2 weeks post-results to lock seats.  
- Medicine/limited seats require faster deposits to signal scarcity; business/CS allow slightly longer.  
- Deposit confirms eligibility to issue final acceptance/visa support; outstanding documents pause visa kits.

## Standard fall intake timeline (September starts)

| Step | Timing from offer | Owner | Notes/CTAs |

## January/spring intake cadence

- Shorten by ~3–5 days because students already graduated.  
- Target Day 10 deadline for all programs; single escalation on Day 14 before seat release on Day 17.

## Immigration dependencies (Serbia) — do not assume “EU passport” or “visa-free”

**Prominent disclaimer:** Requirements change and differ by passport and intake. Always confirm with the relevant embassy/consulate and the campus visa team before advising students.

### Serbia-ready immigration branching (use this before quoting/negotiating extensions)

#### Branch 1 — Serbian citizens (default case)

**UNIC Athens (Greece)**  
- Study stays require a **visa for studies** issued by a Greek embassy/consulate after acceptance by a Greek education institution.  
- Supporting documents can include a **certificate of acceptance** and (where applicable) **receipt for payment of enrolment fees**.  
- **Deposit timing must protect:** issuing the unconditional acceptance/enrolment certificate (and fee-payment proof when required) **before** the student books/attends their visa appointment.

Official sources (last_verified: 2025-12-22):  
- https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-greece_en  
- https://apdattikis.gov.gr/en/process/6-1-1-studies-at-higher-education-institutions-article-33-law-4251-14-issuance/

**UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus)**  
- To enter and reside in Cyprus as a student, the applicant **must obtain a visa or an entry permit before travel**, and **must obtain a temporary residence permit after arrival**.  
- **Deposit timing must protect:** issuing the admission/acceptance documentation needed for the visa/entry-permit step (and any university visa-support pack) early enough to avoid intake risk.

Official sources (last_verified: 2025-12-22):  
- https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-cyprus_en

#### Branch 2 — EU/EEA/Swiss citizens resident in Serbia

**UNIC Athens (Greece)** and **UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus)**  
- EU citizens don’t use the third-country “student visa” pathway; instead, for stays beyond the first 3 months they register residence and obtain a **registration certificate**.  
- For students, registration documentation includes **proof of enrolment** and **proof of comprehensive health insurance**.  
- **Deposit timing must protect:** issuing proof of enrolment quickly (so students can register post-arrival on time) and ensuring they understand health insurance expectations.

Official sources (last_verified: 2025-12-22):  
- Greece: https://europa.eu/youreurope/citizens/residence/documents-formalities/registering-residence/greece/index_en.htm  
- Cyprus: https://europa.eu/youreurope/citizens/residence/documents-formalities/registering-residence/cyprus/index_en.htm

#### Branch 3 — Non-EU residents in Serbia (not Serbian citizens; not EU/EEA/Swiss)

Treat as **passport-driven third‑country cases**. Do not assume “visa-free” entry even for short stays.

**UNIC Athens (Greece)**  
- The studies pathway still requires applying for a **visa for studies** via a Greek embassy/consulate, and residence-permit steps after arrival.  
- **Deposit timing must protect:** issuing unconditional acceptance/enrolment documentation and fee-payment proof (when required) early enough to secure/keep consular appointment slots.

**UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus)**  
- The studies pathway requires a **visa or entry permit before travel** and a **temporary residence permit after arrival**.  
- **Deposit timing must protect:** issuing acceptance documentation needed for visa/entry-permit steps early, and preventing “last minute” deposits that create arrival risk.

Official sources (last_verified: 2025-12-22):  
- Greece: https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-greece_en  
- Greece (Type D + acceptance/fees evidence): https://apdattikis.gov.gr/en/process/6-1-1-studies-at-higher-education-institutions-article-33-law-4251-14-issuance/  
- Cyprus: https://home-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies/migration-and-asylum/eu-immigration-portal/student-cyprus_en

## Escalation rules and channels

- **Risk triggers:** no payment proof by Day 14 (Day 10 Medicine) or missed WhatsApp read for 72h.  
- **Tier 1:** Country Recruiter call/WhatsApp + parent call.  
- **Tier 2:** Admissions Manager email from official inbox + SMS.  
- **Tier 3:** Director note warning of seat release; copy agent (if applicable).  
- Track all touches in CRM with timestamps; only Director can approve extensions beyond seat release window.
