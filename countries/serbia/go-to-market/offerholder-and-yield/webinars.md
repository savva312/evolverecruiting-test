## Segmentation (Serbia cohorts)

- **Default segment:** Serbian citizens applying on a Serbian passport (non-EU/EEA) for UNIC Nicosia or UNIC Athens.
- **Exceptions to handle explicitly:** EU/EEA/Swiss passports (including dual nationals), non-Serbian passports living in Serbia, and Serbian citizens applying from a third country (place of legal residence can affect where/how they apply).
- **Operating rule:** If the student’s passport/residence situation is not the default, route them to 1:1 document triage instead of giving a “one-size-fits-all” answer live.

## Visa/residence content guardrails (must-read for speakers)

**Purpose:** This webinar is triage + orientation. It is not legal advice. Requirements can change by embassy/consulate, intake season, and individual circumstances.

### What we can do (safe)

- Explain the difference between entry permission (visa/entry permit) and post-arrival residence steps (residence permit/registration).
- Confirm what UNIC will issue (and by when): offer/acceptance letters, enrollment confirmation, invoices/receipts, housing confirmation once allocated.
- Help students build a timeline and identify the critical path (appointments, translations, proof-of-funds preparation).

### What we must not promise (risk)

- Visa approval, appointment availability, processing timelines, work rights, or that a student can enter first and “sort it out later”.
- That a document/checklist item is “not needed” unless the relevant embassy/consulate or immigration authority confirms it for that student.

### Minimum data to collect before/at Webinar #2 (doc triage)

- Campus (Nicosia / Athens) + intended intake (Fall/Spring) + target travel date
- Passport nationality(ies) (incl. dual nationality) + passport expiry date
- Current country of residence + legal status (citizen/resident/student/other)
- Where they plan to apply (embassy/consulate location)
- Whether they need housing support and by when

## Webinar #2 — Serbia-ready Q&A bank (visa, docs, housing)

Use these as prompts + safe answer frameworks. If the student is not in the default segment, route to 1:1 doc triage.

### Shared (both campuses)

1) **“I heard Serbians can travel without a visa for short visits — does that mean I can start studies without a student visa/entry permit?”**  
   Even if short-stay entry is possible for your passport, long-stay study/residence is a different pathway. We’ll confirm your route based on your passport + campus and point you to the embassy/consulate instructions you must follow.

2) **“When should I book my embassy/consulate appointment?”**  
   Treat appointments as the critical path. Book as soon as you have your offer and know your intended intake/campus. We’ll issue university documents as fast as possible, but appointment availability is outside UNIC control.

3) **“What’s the difference between an entry visa/permit and a residence permit/registration?”**  
   Entry permission gets you into the country for studies; residence steps keep you legal after arrival for the duration of your program. Both matter and have different documents and deadlines.

4) **“Which documents usually need translation/legalisation (apostille/notarisation)?”**  
   Requirements vary by destination and by the consulate handling your application. Assume education documents and some civil documents may need official translation and/or legalisation; follow the embassy/consulate checklist as the final authority.

5) **“What does ‘proof of funds’ mean, and can parents sponsor me?”**  
   Most study pathways require proof you can cover living costs/tuition. The accepted formats (bank statements, sponsor letters, etc.) depend on the authority processing your case. We’ll share examples, but your embassy/consulate’s instructions are definitive.

6) **“Do I need housing booked before I apply?”**  
   Many processes require an address or accommodation proof. If you use UNIC housing support, we can issue confirmation once allocated; if you choose private housing, you’ll usually need a lease/reservation that meets the authority’s requirements.

7) **“Should I buy flight tickets before my visa/entry permit is approved?”**  
   Avoid non-refundable tickets until you have confirmation from the issuing authority. If you must book, use flexible/cancellation-friendly fares.

8) **“What if I have dual nationality (e.g., EU passport)?”**  
   Your pathway can change materially. Bring both passports to triage, and we’ll route you to the correct campus-specific checklist and the relevant official guidance.

### UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus) — prompts

9) **“For Cyprus, do I need to do anything before travel (entry permit/visa), or is it all done after arrival?”**  
   Many non-EU students must complete a pre-arrival step via the Cyprus embassy/consulate (or an entry permit route) before travel, then complete post-arrival residence steps. We’ll confirm based on your passport and point you to the Cyprus Embassy in Belgrade guidance.

10) **“Are medical checks required for Cyprus, and when do they happen?”**  
   Medical requirements can be part of the student permit/residence process and can be time-sensitive. We’ll share what UNIC needs for onboarding, but you must follow the official immigration instructions for your permit.

### UNIC Athens (Greece) — prompts

11) **“For Greece, do I need a national long-stay student visa (type D) even if I can enter for tourism?”**  
   For non-EU students, studying long-term typically requires a national visa for studies before travel and then a residence permit after arrival. We’ll confirm your route and direct you to the consulate’s study visa instructions and the official residence-permit guidance.

12) **“After arriving in Greece, what’s the first residence-permit step and deadline?”**  
   There is usually a post-arrival residence-permit application process with deadlines (and fees) that can vary. We’ll provide a checklist and help you plan, but the official authorities determine the exact steps and timing.

## Operating notes

- **Cadence:** Send invites 72h before each session; same-day SMS reminder in Serbian; post-event drip (T+24h and T+5d) with recording + CTA.  
- **Segmentation:** Run separate Q&A rooms for Medicine vs other programs; for Webinar #2, run separate breakouts for **Nicosia vs Athens** and keep an “exceptions” room for non-default passport/residence cases.  
- **Language:** Slides bilingual (EN primary, Serbian secondary). Live Q&A can mix; provide translated FAQ doc.  
- **Platform:** Zoom with registration form that tags “Serbian Offerholder,” campus (Nicosia/Athens), program interest, agent (if any), and passport/residence segment. Enable auto-reminders + attendance export to CRM.  
- **KPIs:** Attendance rate >60% of registrants; deposit conversion uplift tracked vs non-attendees; time-to-deposit reduction measured per cohort.

## Risks and mitigations

- **Low attendance in July/August:** stack sessions early evening Belgrade time (18:30–19:30) and post recordings within 12h.  
- **Medicine seat anxiety:** include “seat caps + deposit timelines” slide; live escalation path for payment proof.  
- **Immigration misinformation risk:** lead with guardrails, use campus-specific checklists, and direct students to authoritative sources; route non-default cases to 1:1 doc triage.  
- **Athens vs Nicosia confusion:** open with comparison slide (tuition, housing, arrival docs) and provide campus-specific contact emails; route follow-ups within 24h.
