# International School of Belgrade (ISB)

## Snapshot
- City: Belgrade (Lower School in Senjak; Middle/High School in Banjička šuma).
- School type: private, non-profit, IB World School (PYP, MYP, DP); accredited by the Council of International Schools and the New England Association of Schools and Colleges.
- Enrollment: ~350–400 students across K–12 (check current ISB fact sheet for exact HS headcount).
- Programs: IB Diploma Programme in grades 11–12; strong English-medium instruction with elective depth in sciences, social sciences, arts; active Model UN and athletics.
- Counselor contact: University Counseling Office (contacts rotate yearly; request latest list via counseling@isb.rs).

## Academics and programs
- Tracks: IB DP with subject breadth; TOK/EE/Creativity-Activity-Service requirements in place; no national-state exam pathway.
- Strengths: High English proficiency, regular IBDP outcomes, solid extracurricular portfolio (MUN, robotics, arts).
- Language profile: English-dominant; Serbian offered to maintain local language development.

## Mobility and destinations
- Typical destinations: US, UK, Netherlands, Germany, Switzerland; rising interest in EU English-taught options closer to home (Austria, Italy, Greece/Cyprus).
- Fit cues: Families value small cohorts, global university pathways, and predictable counseling support; transparent net cost and recognition proof drive conversion.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens fit
- UNIC (Nicosia): Lead with CS/data, business, and communications programs; scholarship transparency is key for families comparing to Western Europe.
- UNIC Athens: Emphasise proximity, EU recognition, and English-medium degrees; pitch business/CS/psychology and selective Medicine for students seeking mainland EU.

## Recruiting history and access notes
- Past engagement: No UNIC visit logged; ISB hosts university visits and mini-fairs each semester—coordinate via the counseling calendar.
- Gatekeepers: University Counseling Office; align visits with scheduled college blocks to avoid classroom disruption.
- Event cadence: Fall and spring university visits; request slots 6–8 weeks in advance.
