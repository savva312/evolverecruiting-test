# Ministry of Interior – Directorate for Foreigners

last_checked: 2025-12-20

## Mandate
- National authority for visas, temporary residence permits, and ID cards for foreign nationals in Serbia.
- Through police directorates, registers foreigner addresses and issues biometric residence cards needed for longer study stays.

## Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Serbian nationals going abroad rely on this ministry mainly for passport issuance, but inbound students or visiting recruiters must comply with its visa and stay rules.
- Knowledge of visa categories (notably long-stay D visas) and residence-permit steps helps UNIC advise Serbian applicants on timelines for internships or exchanges in Serbia, and informs reciprocal expectations when explaining Cyprus visa processes.

## Key processes and timelines
- Long-stay (D) visas are issued by Serbian consulates based on Ministry of Foreign Affairs policy; once in Serbia, foreigners apply for temporary residence with the Ministry of Interior within 90 days of entry.
- Temporary residence for study is commonly granted for up to one year and renewable; decisions are generally issued within 30 days of a complete application at the local police directorate.
- Applicants must provide proof of enrollment/acceptance, accommodation registration, health insurance, financial means, and a valid passport; biometrics taken during the residence-card appointment.

## Contact channels
