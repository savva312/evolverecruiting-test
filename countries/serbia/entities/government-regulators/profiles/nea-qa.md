# National Entity for Accreditation and Quality Assurance in Higher Education (NEAQA)

last_checked: 2025-12-20

## Mandate
- Independent national body responsible for external quality assurance, institutional accreditation, and study program accreditation in Serbia.
- Conducts periodic evaluations, site visits, and publishes accreditation decisions; participates in ENQA/European QA frameworks.

## Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Serbian stakeholders may check NEAQA decisions to validate foreign partnerships or mobility pathways; demonstrating UNIC’s accredited status helps mitigate concerns about recognition when Serbian students return.
- NEAQA standards influence expectations for curriculum design, ECTS transparency, and QA documentation that UNIC should surface in outreach.

## Key processes and timelines
- Institutional and program accreditation cycles typically run on five-year validity; re-accreditation requires self-evaluation reports and external review.
- Evaluation timelines vary but usually span several months from application to decision, including peer-review visits and panel reports.
- Publishes public registers of accredited institutions/programs, which Serbian high schools and parents reference when vetting foreign options.
