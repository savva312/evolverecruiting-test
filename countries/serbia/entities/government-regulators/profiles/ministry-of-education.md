# Ministry of Education (Serbia)

last_checked: 2025-12-20

## Mandate
- National ministry setting policy for preschool, primary, secondary, and higher education; oversees public funding, quality norms, and legislation.
- Hosts Serbia’s ENIC/NARIC center for academic recognition of foreign higher-education qualifications.
- Coordinates scholarship schemes and bilateral education agreements impacting outbound mobility.

## Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Recognition of Cypriot degrees for further study or public-sector employment in Serbia flows through procedures under this ministry.
- Policy shifts on foreign-provider recognition, distance learning, or joint programs could shape marketing claims and partnership structures.
- Ministry data or approvals may be requested by Serbian high schools or municipal education offices when UNIC engages locally.

## Key processes and timelines
- Academic recognition (ENIC/NARIC Serbia): applicants submit legalized diplomas, transcripts, translations, and proof of program accreditation; decisions typically issued within ~60 days once a complete file is lodged (extensions possible for complex cases).
- Professional recognition for regulated professions is routed to sectoral bodies; the ministry sets the framework and issues referrals.
- Issues opinions on cross-border/distance education offers; institutions must evidence accreditation in the home country.
