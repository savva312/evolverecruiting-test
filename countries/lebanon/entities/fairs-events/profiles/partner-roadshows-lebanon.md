# Partner-branded roadshows (example: CedarEdu, Orient MENA)

## Overview
Some agents run their own “study abroad” branded tours across Lebanon and invite universities to join as anchor partners. These can combine weekend expos with weekday school visits. Treat this profile as the template for any specific partner package.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** Spring and fall windows; 2–4 city runs over 7–10 days.
- **Cities/venues:** Beirut hotel ballroom, Tripoli community centers, Saida/Tyre cultural venues; optional Zahle/Bekaa schools.
- **Format:** Booth expo + co-branded seminar; agent may bundle school visits and digital ads.

## Audience and expected reach
- 150–400 visitors per weekend expo; 50–120 students per school visit.
- Expected marketing-qualified leads: 60–150 per expo if badge scanning + QR forms are enforced.

## Pricing (indicative)
- €800–€1,600 per expo day; discounts for booking all cities.
- Add-ons: co-branded social ads, SMS blasts, and counselor dinner sponsorships.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Scholarships/merit clarity; medicine/health/business positioning; emphasize admissions speed and permit guidance.
- **UNIC Athens:** Urban campus appeal; business/psychology/ICT programs; proximity and Schengen mobility.

## Engagement plan
- Pre: request attendee acquisition plan and school list; insist on GDPR-compliant data transfer; prepare Arabic/French/English landing pages with UTM tags.
- During: staff scanning + short-form surveys; confirm school visit slots on the roadshow schedule; host mini-seminars that separate Nicosia vs Athens value props.
- Post: 24–48 hour follow-up segmented by city and program interest; share conversion summary with the organizing agent and agree next co-marketing step.

## Risks and notes
- Lead quality swings with the agent’s marketing; push for transparency on channels and historical turnout.
- Some organizers sell high-priced upsells with limited ROI; model expected cost-per-lead before committing.
