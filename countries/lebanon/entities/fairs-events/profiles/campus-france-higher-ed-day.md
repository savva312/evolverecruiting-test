# Institut français / Campus France Higher-Education Day – Beirut

## Overview
Annual/biannual showcase hosted by Institut français du Liban and Campus France. Focus is on francophone students considering France/Canada/Europe, but English-medium EU options are welcome. High counselor attendance makes it valuable for school relationship-building.

## Timing and format
- **Typical month:** November (one full day) with occasional spring mini-editions.
- **Location:** Beirut cultural center or partner hotel.
- **Format:** Booth exhibition with optional seminar slots; some editions include counselor breakfast briefings.

## Audience and expected reach
- 500–800 visitors across students, parents, and counselors.
- 150–200 marketing-qualified leads for EU English-medium programs if messaging highlights French-language support and recognition.

## Pricing (indicative)
- Often free for cultural partners; otherwise €0–€500 for booth/seminar.
- Printing/local collateral and interpreter costs may apply.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Present as EU-accredited, English-medium alternative with scholarships; emphasize medicine/health, business/fintech, and digital programs; include French-language support and visa documentation clarity.
- **UNIC Athens:** Stress mainland EU setting, Schengen travel ease, and business/psychology/ICT strengths; position as culturally close and quick to reach from Beirut.

## Engagement plan
- Pre: request booth + seminar; prepare French/Arabic/English one-pagers; collect list of attending schools/counselors; set QR landing pages with French copy and UTM codes.
- During: prioritize counselor conversations; log school needs; invite counselors to virtual briefings; scan/QR all student interactions with consent messaging.
- Post: 24–48 hour follow-up segmented by campus and language preference; share counselor toolkit and propose school visits during next Lebanon trip.

## Risks and notes
- Francophone bias may reduce volume for English-only programs; tailor messaging accordingly.
- High footfall can bottleneck scanning—assign a dedicated lead-capture role.
