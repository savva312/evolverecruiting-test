# Virtual education fairs (Lebanon/MENA)

## Overview
Online fairs run by regional organizers or agents that include Lebanese students and counselors. Useful for reaching audiences outside Beirut and for low-cost testing of messaging.

## Timing and format
- **Typical months:** May/June and November (1–2 days each) with evening peak hours.
- **Format:** Virtual booths with chat/video, webinar slots, and downloadable materials.

## Audience and expected reach
- 500–1,000 registrants depending on organizer; 150–250 engaged chats if staffed well.
- Mix of high-school students, parents, and counselors from Beirut, Tripoli, Saida, and diaspora audiences.

## Pricing (indicative)
- €500–€1,000 per virtual day; add-ons for featured placement or dedicated email blasts.

## UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning
- **UNIC Nicosia:** Emphasize medicine/health, business, and ICT with English-medium delivery and scholarship clarity.
- **UNIC Athens:** Position as mainland EU urban option with fast travel from Lebanon; highlight business/psychology/ICT pathways and residence steps.

## Pre/during/post actions
- Pre: set Arabic/French/English booth copy; upload campus photos/videos; schedule live staff coverage during evening peaks; prepare 20–30 minute webinar per campus.
- During: proactively invite visitors to chat; use quick-qualifying questions (intake, GPA, language scores, budget, destination preference).
- Post: export chat logs and registrations; send immediate thank-you email with booking link; retarget attendees via social/Google audiences.

## Risks and notes
- Engagement drops without proactive outreach; assign dedicated staff with scripts.
- Lead quality varies; avoid overcommitting budget without post-event conversion tracking.

## Organizer contacts

- CIS virtual events — https://www.cois.org/ (counselor-led virtual fairs).
