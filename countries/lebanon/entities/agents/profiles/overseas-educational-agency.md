# Overseas Educational Agency

- **Website**: https://overseas-leb.com/
- **Headquarters**: Beirut (Manhal/Beirut office per site); serves Beirut, Tripoli, and Bekaa via roadshows.
- **Ownership & affiliations**: Lebanese-owned agency; public site lists scholarships/offers but no ownership disclosure. Verify registration and any Ministry of Education license. No ICEF badge shown—assume unaccredited until confirmed.
- **Destinations & segments**: Mix of UK, Canada, Germany, Turkey, and CIS options; markets language prep and undergraduate admissions. Likely to target middle-income families seeking scholarship/discount offers.
- **UNIC Nicosia positioning**: Emphasize medicine foundation, business, computing as affordable EU alternatives to UK; promote campus housing to counter safety concerns.
- **UNIC Athens positioning**: Sell as Schengen capital experience; angle for hospitality, business, fintech/data masters; highlight Greek residence-permit path.
- **Service mix & conversion signals**: Application counseling, offer marketing (“Offers & Programs”), events/news, registration portal. Track conversions via registration form submissions and webinar attendance.
- **Payment/commission & FX handling**: Assume standard 15–20% commission; propose EUR invoices with USD settlement to avoid LBP risk. Ask if they charge students service fees; ensure receipt controls.
- **Compliance & risk notes**: Website suggests aggressive marketing; require transparent offer validation and ban unauthorized discount claims. Request student-consent language for data sharing; audit sub-agent use outside Beirut.
- **Offices & counsellors**: Beirut office with traveling counselors; request named counselors and volume history before onboarding.
