# Ministry of Foreign Affairs & Emigrants (MFAE) — Consular Legalization

- **Mandate:** Consular network that authenticates Lebanese public documents (e.g., diplomas, transcripts, affidavits) for use abroad by legalizing signatures and seals; coordinates with MEHE and General Directorate of Civil Status for upstream attestations.
- **Relevance to UNIC/UNIC Athens:** Outbound students often need legalized academic records for applications or visa files. Embassies (e.g., Washington, D.C.) legalize education documents and student IDs before submission to destination authorities.
- **Key processes:** Applicants submit original authenticated documents plus a copy, money order/cashier’s check, and contact details to the embassy’s Legalization Department; commercial documents require notarization wording on the signature page. Student files must include a copy of the student ID and phone number. 【https://www.lebanonembassyus.org/consular-services/legalization-of-documents-and-signature/】
- **Timelines / checkpoints:** Embassies do not publish SLAs; plan for mailing time plus several business days for processing and return (assumption—confirm with the specific mission).
- **Fees:** Embassy schedule lists $40–$60 per certificate (education/civil status) and $225 per commercial document, payable to the “Embassy of Lebanon”; requirements vary by document type. 【https://www.lebanonembassyus.org/consular-services/legalization-of-documents-and-signature/】
- **Contact channels:** consular@lebanonembassyus.org; embassy switchboard +1 202-939-6301 (Washington, D.C. mission). 【https://www.lebanonembassyus.org/consular-services/legalization-of-documents-and-signature/】
