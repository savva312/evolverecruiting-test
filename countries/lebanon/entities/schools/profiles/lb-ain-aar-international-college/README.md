**One-paragraph summary:**  
International College (Ain Aar) is the Metn campus of the International College network, serving nursery through Grade 9 with students transitioning to the Ras Beirut campus for upper secondary (IB DP/American/Lebanese Bac). The school is an independent, non-profit institution with strong accreditation signals (NEASC, CIS, AEFE, IB) and a high USD solidarity subsidy for secondary grades (\$9,280–\$10,847 for Grades 10–12, 2024–25). Affluent parent base, bilingual instruction, and a university-placement ethos make IC Ain Aar a priority feeder for UNIC Athens (cost-competitive EU option) and UNIC Nicosia (medicine/health/ICT).

**Top opportunities:**  
- Target bilingual cohorts and STEM/health interest groups that seek EU-recognized degrees with manageable tuition.  
- Leverage shared IC counselor relationships from Ras Beirut to extend access to Ain Aar families and feeder middle school streams.  
- Position UNIC Athens as an affordable alternative to US/UK pathways amid high USD solidarity fees.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Secondary counseling decisions may centralize at Ras Beirut; access depends on shared gatekeepers.  
- Currency volatility and solidarity subsidies may shift affordability perceptions year to year.  
- Need confirmation on which Ain Aar grades participate in university fairs vs. Ras Beirut-led events.
