# Anera (American Near East Refugee Aid) – Lebanon

## Overview
Anera is a humanitarian NGO serving Palestinian and Syrian refugees and vulnerable Lebanese communities. In Lebanon it delivers education, vocational training, and livelihood programs that prepare youth for employment and higher education opportunities.

## Programs and services
- **Education and remedial learning:** Non-formal education centers and catch-up classes to return out-of-school youth to formal pathways.
- **Scholarships and stipends:** Tuition/transport support for refugee youth enrolling in vocational institutes and select higher-education tracks funded by donors (e.g., UNICEF, UNHCR, Islamic Development Bank partners).
- **Workforce development:** Market-linked vocational training (ICT, healthcare support, construction trades) with job placement and entrepreneurship coaching.
- **Psycho-social and wraparound services:** Counseling and community outreach that improve student retention.

## Target students and reach
- Palestinian refugees in camps across Beirut, South Lebanon, and Bekaa.
- Syrian refugee youth and vulnerable Lebanese students needing financial and academic support.
- Operates through community centers and partner institutes, with growing digital training components.

## Partnership fit for UNIC/UNIC Athens
- Pipeline for refugee students who outgrow vocational tracks and seek affordable university degrees; UNIC can offer tailored scholarships and online/hybrid entry paths.
- Potential to co-design bridging courses (English, digital skills) that map to UNIC admission requirements.
- Joint impact reporting with Anera’s donors can justify scholarship co-funding and student support services.
