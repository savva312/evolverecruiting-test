# Lebanon addendum: school outreach & counselor engagement

Use `/skills/school-outreach-and-counselor-engagement/SKILL.md` for the global workflow. Apply these Lebanon-specific points when targeting and visiting schools.

## Data sources and routing
- Draw targets from `countries/lebanon/entities/schools/` and related CSVs in `countries/lebanon/data/entities/`.
- Tag contacts by city (Beirut, Mount Lebanon, Tripoli, Bekaa, South) and curriculum (Lebanese Baccalaureate vs international) to tailor messaging.
- Log interactions and consent in the country data model (`countries/lebanon/skills/lebanon-data-model-csvs-and-profiles/`).

## Language and etiquette
- Default to Arabic-first outreach; have French or English versions for schools that operate in those languages.
- Avoid in-school visits during Lebanese Baccalaureate exam periods (late spring/early summer) and near major holidays; offer virtual briefings instead.
- WhatsApp is common for quick coordination—capture opt-in before sending materials; some counselors prefer email for documents.

## Visit logistics
- Confirm visitor policies and security procedures, especially at private schools; share agenda and presenter names in advance.
- Bring concise UNIC vs UNIC Athens positioning plus scholarship summaries cleared by admissions; keep claims conservative amid currency volatility.

## Consent and data protection
- Obtain explicit permission before storing counselor or student contacts; note source/time of opt-in.
- Avoid recording sensitive data (IDs, grades) in notes; keep to contact info, program interest, and interaction history.

## Follow-up cadence
- Send Arabic (and French/English when relevant) recaps within 48 hours of visits/fairs with promised links and application checklists.
- Plan webinars shortly after exam results when families reassess options; tag event/visit name in all follow-up logs.
