# Education system (Lebanon)

Concise, source-backed map of Lebanon’s education system so recruiting teams can translate credentials into UNIC admissions pathways.

## Governance and oversight
- **Ministry of Education & Higher Education (MEHE)** sets policy and operates the public system through the Directorate General of Education (general/basic/secondary) and Directorate General of Vocational & Technical Education (DGVTE).
- **Center for Educational Research and Development (CERD)** develops curricula, examinations, and teacher training.
- Compulsory schooling covers **9 years of basic education (ages ~6–15) under Law 686/1998**, with Arabic as the language of instruction; English or French are common second-language mediums in private schools.

## Structure and age/grade bands
| Stage | Grades | Typical ages | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-school (optional) | KG1–KG3 | 3–5 | Lays foundation; not compulsory. |
| Basic education (Cycle I–III, compulsory) | 1–9 | 6–15 | Nine-year cycle leading to the **Brevet** (Certificate of Intermediate Education). |
| General secondary | 10–12 | 15–18 | Three years; specialization intensifies in grades 11–12. Culminates in **Baccalauréat Libanais** (General). |
| Technical/vocational secondary | BT1–BT3 (post-Brevet) | 15–18 | Three-year **Baccalauréat Technique (BT)** programs run by DGVTE; pathways continue to **Technicien Supérieur (TS)** and **Licence Technique (LT)**. |

## Curriculum, credentials, and streams
### Brevet (Certificate of Intermediate Education)
- National exam at end of grade 9; required for promotion into general or technical secondary.
- Subjects typically cover Arabic, foreign language, math, sciences, history, geography, civics.

### General secondary and Baccalauréat Libanais
- Three-year sequence with four streams: **General Sciences (SG)**, **Life Sciences (SV)**, **Socio-Economic (SE)**, and **Humanities (LH)**.
- National written exams in grade 12; results reported on a **0–20 scale**. **10/20** is the minimum pass; higher averages (e.g., 12, 14, 16) commonly used as merit bands for selective faculties.
- Language of instruction varies by school (Arabic + English or French); exams available in both language versions.

### Technical and vocational routes
- Post-Brevet students can enter **BT (Baccalauréat Technique)** programs (3 years) in applied fields (hospitality, business, IT, health).
- Progression options:
  - **TS (Technicien Supérieur):** typically 2 additional years after BT (grade 14 equivalent); prepares for supervised practice roles.
  - **LT (Licence Technique):** 1 extra year post-TS; positioned as a vocational bachelor-level qualification.
- Admissions teams should check for **official transcripts and DGVTE-issued certificates**; equivalence to the General Baccalauréat is granted through MEHE’s Equivalence Committee when continuing to university.

### International pathways in private schools
- High-fee private schools (especially Beirut/Tripoli/Saida) frequently offer **dual Lebanese + international tracks**:
  - **French Baccalauréat** (AEFE/mission laïque network) with MEHE equivalence for local recognition.
  - **IB Diploma Programme (IB DP)** with Arabic, civics, and philosophy hours added to secure MEHE equivalence.
  - **Cambridge IGCSE + A-Level/AS-Level** sequences; equivalence typically requires at least **two A-Levels + one AS** in academic subjects plus 12 years of schooling.
  - **US high school diploma** programs often bundle **SAT/ACT and AP** subjects; MEHE equivalence usually needs Grade 12 completion plus standardized tests (e.g., SAT + 3 APs) to match the Lebanese Baccalauréat.
- For UNIC admissions, treat these credentials as valid secondary completion **when backed by MEHE equivalence** or official attestations; request legalized transcripts where possible.

## Grading and assessment
- **National scale:** 0–20 across Brevet, Baccalauréat Libanais, and most public schools; **10/20** is passing.
- Private/international schools may issue **IB (1–7)** or **GPA (4.0)** scales; request school conversion tables when present. A rough heuristic for Lebanese grades: **12/20 ≈ “good”, 14/20 ≈ “very good”, 16/20+ ≈ “excellent.”**
- National exams are centralized (CERD + MEHE). School-based continuous assessment counts toward promotion but final credentials rely on the national exam result.

## University entry mechanics
- **Baseline:** Lebanese University and accredited private universities require the **Baccalauréat Libanais (any stream) or MEHE-recognized equivalent**.
- **Stream expectations:** Science/engineering/health programs generally expect **General Sciences or Life Sciences** streams (or science-heavy BT/TS/LT fields). Business/social sciences accept **Socio-Economic** or **Humanities** alongside math/foreign-language proficiency.
- **Selective faculties:** Medicine, pharmacy, engineering, and some business schools apply **institutional entrance exams or interviews** on top of the Baccalauréat score.
- **International credential holders** (IB, French Bac, A-Levels, US diploma + AP/SAT) should provide **MEHE equivalence certificates** to smooth visa/recognition; notarized translations into English or Greek can accelerate UNIC Athens/Nicosia processing.
- **Vocational graduates** with BT/TS/LT may access applied bachelor or bridging programs when equivalence confirms parity with the general Baccalauréat; document the technical specialization to align with UNIC programs.
