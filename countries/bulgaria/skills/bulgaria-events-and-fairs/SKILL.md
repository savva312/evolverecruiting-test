# Bulgaria addendum: events and fairs

Use `/skills/events-and-fairs/SKILL.md` for the core pre/during/post event workflow. Apply these Bulgaria-specific notes for lead capture and staffing.

## Organizer and venue patterns
- Anchor the calendar around Sofia/Plovdiv/Varna/Burgas fairs and school-hosted career days; confirm table/booth sizes early as space is often limited in public schools.
- Some organizers expect Bulgarian-language materials by default; bring EN versions for IB/international schools but keep signage bilingual.

## Lead capture and consent
- Collect counselor/student details only after verbal consent; add a short GDPR notice on paper forms and tablets.
- Avoid collecting birth dates or national IDs; capture name, role, email/phone, city, and program interest. Note the event name/date for import into the country data model.
- If scanning badges, verify the organizer’s consent language before importing data.

## Staffing and materials
- Prioritize Bulgarian-speaking staff at the booth; pair with an Athens vs Nicosia quick comparison handout and scholarship summary cleared by admissions.
- Test internet access at venues; bring offline QR codes and printed sign-up sheets to back up tablets.
- For school visits, request AV access and follow school security sign-in procedures.

## Post-event follow-up
- Send Bulgarian-language recaps within 48 hours with promised links; include English attachments for international tracks.
- Log leads in the country data model with event name/date, city cluster, and campus interest (Nicosia vs Athens) before launching sequences.
- Route hot leads to application workshops timed after exam results but before peak summer travel; keep follow-ups tagged to the originating event and city.
