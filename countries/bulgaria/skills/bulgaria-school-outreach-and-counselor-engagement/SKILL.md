# Bulgaria addendum: school outreach & counselor engagement

Use the global workflow at `/skills/school-outreach-and-counselor-engagement/SKILL.md` for targeting, sequences, and artifacts. Layer these Bulgaria-specific notes to stay aligned with local etiquette and data paths.

## Where to source and log data
- Pull priority schools and counselor contacts from `countries/bulgaria/entities/schools/` (profiles) and any CSVs under `countries/bulgaria/data/entities/`.
- Store visit notes and interaction logs in the country data model (see `countries/bulgaria/skills/bulgaria-data-model-csvs-and-profiles/`) before syncing to any CRM.
- Tag entries by city cluster (Sofia, Plovdiv, Varna, Burgas, regional) to keep travel plans efficient.

## Language and etiquette
- Default to Bulgarian for counselor emails and meeting materials; bring English copies for IB or international schools.
- Respect quiet periods around national exams (Matura in May–June) and winter breaks; avoid in-school visits during those windows unless invited.
- Viber and email are acceptable follow-up channels; confirm WhatsApp use before sending student data.

## Meeting/visit logistics
- Confirm if schools require visitor clearance or Ministry notifications; many public schools need a prior approval letter.
- Offer in-person visits for Sofia/Plovdiv/Varna/Burgas first; propose virtual briefings for regional schools to reduce travel time.
- Bring translated leave-behinds (Bulgarian) and a short Athens vs Nicosia positioning summary for counselors.

## Consent and data protection
- Treat counselor and student contact details under EU GDPR expectations. Capture opt-in (source + timestamp) and honor opt-out immediately.
- Avoid storing birth dates or national ID numbers in notes; keep logs to professional contact info and interaction history only.

## Follow-up timing
- After fairs or school visits, send Bulgarian-language recap emails within 48 hours and share links to application checklists.
- Schedule application workshops near intake deadlines but avoid weeks overlapping with exam grading and graduation ceremonies.
