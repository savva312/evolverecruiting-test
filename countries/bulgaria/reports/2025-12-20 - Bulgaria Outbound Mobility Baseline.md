# 1. Executive summary

## Snapshot: Bulgaria outbound degree mobility (what we can say with high confidence today)

- **Latest solid baseline (Eurostat, tertiary ED5–8, origin Bulgaria, reference year 2023): 15,236 mobile tertiary students**, summed across host countries reported in the dataset (excluding EU aggregates and Bulgaria as a host). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Outbound is highly concentrated**:
  - **Germany: 5,410 (35.5%)**
  - **Türkiye: 5,120 (33.6%)**
  - **Austria: 1,334 (8.8%)**
  - Top 2 destinations (Germany + Türkiye) account for **69.1%** of the Eurostat-reported total. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Direct relevance to UNIC Athens (Greece) and UNIC (Cyprus), 2023**:
  - **Greece (EL): 280 students, rank #8, 1.8% share**
  - **Cyprus (CY): 66 students, rank #16, 0.4% share** ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))
  - Within this reporting set, **Greece hosts about 4.2 times as many Bulgarian mobile tertiary students as Cyprus** (280 vs 66). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

## What this implies for UNIC and UNIC Athens (practical, near-term)

- **UNIC Athens: Bulgaria is already a “proven outbound-to-Greece” origin**, with Greece sitting inside the Top 10 hosts for Bulgarian mobile tertiary students in Eurostat 2023. This supports treating Bulgaria as a priority feeder market for Athens, especially for English-taught programs where Greece can compete on proximity, EU context, and total cost. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **UNIC Cyprus: Bulgaria-to-Cyprus is smaller but present**, and currently trails Bulgaria-to-Greece within the Eurostat reporting set. Cyprus positioning may work best when it offers a clearly differentiated value proposition relative to nearby options (admissions flexibility, English-taught portfolio, student experience, and career pathways) rather than relying on “default” regional pull. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Competitive reality**: the dominant destinations (Germany and Türkiye) suggest that Bulgaria’s outbound demand is shaped by a mix of opportunity-seeking (large systems, perceived quality) and strong cross-border ties. UNIC and UNIC Athens should assume they are competing against established pathways, and should emphasize program-level differentiation and outcomes.

# 2. Outbound volumes and destination structure (degree mobility baseline)

## Baseline source, scope, and definitions (what these numbers mean)

- **Primary baseline used for destinations and volumes in this section**: Eurostat learning mobility dataset **`educ_uoe_mobs02`** (“Mobile students from abroad enrolled by education level, sex and country of origin”), filtered to:
  - Origin (partner): **Bulgaria (BG)**
  - Level: **Tertiary education (ISCED 2011 levels 5–8, `ED5-8`)**
  - Sex: **Total (T)**
  - Unit: **Number (NR)**
  - Reference year (latest used here): **2023** ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Definition alignment (important for comparability)**:
  - In Eurostat UOE learning mobility tables, **“mobile students” refers to degree-mobile students**.
  - **Country of origin** should in principle be the **country of education prior to entering tertiary** (with possible national deviations documented in footnotes). ([webgate.ec.europa.eu](https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm?utm_source=openai))

- **Geographic reporting set (important for “total outbound” interpretation)**:
  - The UOE collection covers EU Member States, EFTA/EEA and candidate countries, plus additional non-European OECD members, although Eurostat dissemination is often focused on EU, EFTA/EEA and candidates. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm?utm_source=openai))
  - As a result, a “total outbound” computed from `educ_uoe_mobs02` should be treated as **within the Eurostat reporting set**, not necessarily worldwide.

## Data limitations to keep front-of-mind (applies to all figures above)

- **This is not a guaranteed global outbound total**:
  - The computed “15,236” total is **within the host geographies returned in the Eurostat slice** and excludes hosts with missing or confidential values in the response.   - EU aggregates (EU27_2020, EU28) appear alongside country rows; they are analytically useful for EU-host totals but must not be summed with country rows. # 3. Program and discipline preferences (and relevance to Cyprus and Greece)

## What is directly measurable today (and what is not)

- **Direct, Bulgaria-outbound field-of-study splits are not yet available in this baseline**:
  - Eurostat learning mobility statistics do support degree mobility reporting and, in principle, include **breakdowns by field of education (ISCED-F 2013)** for “mobile students” (degree mobile students) and related indicators. ([webgate.ec.europa.eu](https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm?utm_source=openai))
  - However, the current baseline extraction is strongest on **origin Bulgaria by host country** (Section 2), and does not yet include a reliable **origin Bulgaria by host by field** view.

- **Practical consequence for UNIC and UNIC Athens**: program prioritisation for Bulgaria must, for now, rely on (a) destination structure, (b) host-market characteristics (Greece vs Cyprus), and (c) disciplined testing, until outbound-by-field extraction is added.

## Host-market signals that matter for program strategy (Greece vs Cyprus)

These signals do not tell us “what Bulgarians study” directly, but they strongly affect what can scale in each destination.

- **Cyprus is structurally more internationalised than Greece in tertiary education**:
  - In 2023, Eurostat reports Cyprus among the EU countries with the highest shares of “students from abroad” (22.3%), while Greece is among the lowest (3.0%). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/products-eurostat-news/w/ddn-20250822-1?utm_source=openai))

- **Implications for program portfolio and delivery (Bulgaria-facing)**:
  - **Cyprus**: a higher international-student share typically aligns with a larger English-taught footprint and more mature international student services, which tends to support scaling in globally traded disciplines (business, ICT, selected health and social science programs).
  - **Greece**: a lower international-student share implies that scaling from Bulgaria is more likely to depend on a smaller set of high-fit programs and clear differentiators (campus location, cost, employability pathways, bilingual student support).

## Bulgaria-to-Greece and Bulgaria-to-Cyprus: program clusters to prioritise (evidence-led hypotheses)

Because outbound-by-field data is not yet populated, the items below are framed as **working hypotheses** designed to be validated via (1) application/enquiry data, (2) agent feedback, and (3) the next iteration of Eurostat/UIS field-of-study extraction.

### A. Most defensible “first-wave” clusters to test (Bulgaria recruitment)

- **Business, administration, and entrepreneurship**
  - Rationale: these programs are broadly portable, less constrained by regulated-profession recognition, and often available in English across many systems.
  - Best fit: both UNIC and UNIC Athens, with stronger scale potential where the host market is already international-student oriented (Cyprus signal). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/products-eurostat-news/w/ddn-20250822-1?utm_source=openai))

- **ICT and data-oriented programs (informatics, software, data analytics)**
  - Rationale: strong cross-border labour market demand and high willingness to study in English; attractive for students seeking international employability without profession-licensing barriers.
  - Best fit: both locations; consider clear employability framing and industry links.

- **Psychology, education, and related social sciences**
  - Rationale: common outbound interest areas when delivered in English and when pathways are clearly explained (degree recognition, postgraduate routes, practical placements).
  - Best fit: particularly relevant for Athens positioning if student experience, city brand, and practicum pathways are credible.

- **Health-related programs (selectively, with careful compliance)**
  - Rationale: health demand can be strong in outbound markets, but scaling depends on licensure, language of clinical training, and recognition in Bulgaria/EU.
  - Best fit: treat as “high-intent but high-friction”; lead only where UNIC/UNIC Athens can clearly document professional pathways and requirements.

### B. Second-wave clusters (test after initial conversion proof)

- **Engineering and built environment**: often attractive in large destination systems, but competition is intense and outcomes messaging must be specific.

- **Tourism, hospitality, and services**: can fit regional value propositions (Mediterranean location), but is sensitive to perceived labour market outcomes and program quality signals.

## Program packaging and channel implications (especially for Erasmus-linked behaviour)

- **Credit mobility context (useful for pipeline building)**:
  - EU reporting notes that, for some countries including Bulgaria, short study periods abroad tend to be organised largely under EU programmes (Erasmus and similar), which makes Erasmus-linked partnerships and visibility strategically important for upstream awareness, even when the primary revenue goal is degree mobility. ([op.europa.eu](https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/comparative-report/chapter-6.html?utm_source=openai))

- **Operational guidance for UNIC / UNIC Athens**:
  - Build Bulgaria visibility around a small set of program “hero offers” (3–6) per destination, then expand only after conversion proof.
  - Ensure each program has a Bulgaria-facing evidence pack: language of instruction, total cost of attendance, recognition notes (where relevant), internship/placement structure, and graduate outcomes narrative.

## Data gaps to close next (to convert hypotheses into a sourced Bulgaria outbound-by-field view)

- **Eurostat (UOE) field-of-education mobility cuts**: extract and publish Bulgaria-outbound field distribution (and, if feasible, Bulgaria-to-Greece/Cyprus by field), using the Eurostat learning mobility framework that explicitly supports field breakdowns. ([webgate.ec.europa.eu](https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm?utm_source=openai))

- **UNESCO UIS global flows**: complete UIS extraction for Bulgaria outbound totals and destinations, then seek any UIS-compatible field proxies or host-side national reporting where available (especially for Cyprus and Greece).

- **Institutional lead indicators (UNIC internal)**: applications, offers, enrolments, and enquiry topics by program and city, segmented by Bulgaria, to validate which clusters outperform the market baseline.

# 5. Data limitations, gaps, and how to use this baseline (UNIC and UNIC Athens)

## What this baseline is reliable for (and how to interpret it)

- **Reliable for**: ranking and sizing Bulgaria outbound degree mobility **within the Eurostat reporting set** (tertiary ED5–8), including clear Bulgaria-to-Greece and Bulgaria-to-Cyprus counts for 2020–2023 and a defensible 2023 destination ranking. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Not reliable for**: claiming a **global** outbound total or a worldwide destination ranking for Bulgaria, because UNESCO UIS global flows were not successfully extracted into this baseline yet. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/en/methodology/communication-and-information))

## Eurostat data limitations (material for totals, trends, and ranks)

- **Reporting-set coverage, not worldwide coverage**
  - The computed “total outbound” is the sum of host-country values present in the Eurostat extract, it should be described as **“Eurostat reporting-set total”**, not “all Bulgarian students abroad.” ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Missing host observations can understate totals and distort rankings**
  - The API explicitly lists host positions with no data for the Bulgaria-origin slice (for example: EU28, Netherlands, Liechtenstein, United Kingdom, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro in 2023). If a meaningful host is missing, the computed total and the destination rank order are affected. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Aggregates present, must be excluded from any summed totals**
  - Aggregates such as **EU27_2020** appear alongside country rows; summing them with country rows causes double counting. Aggregates are still useful as a separate EU-host indicator if kept separate. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Status flags signal comparability issues**
  - The Eurostat API includes status labels such as “definition differs,” “estimated,” “break in time series,” and “confidential.” These flags should be treated as cautions for year-over-year comparisons and for host-to-host comparisons. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Origin concept is not always citizenship**
  - Eurostat’s explanatory metadata indicates “country of origin” is intended to reflect **country of education prior to entering tertiary**, with documented national deviations. This can produce different results than citizenship-based counts, especially in countries with significant long-term resident foreign nationals. ([webgate.ec.europa.eu](https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm))

- **Year availability can be uneven by slice**
  - The dataset can be updated later than the “latest usable year” for a particular origin (Bulgaria), and earlier years may lack aggregates or have different coverage (for example: missing EU aggregates in the 2019 Bulgaria slice). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

## UNESCO UIS gap (what is missing, and why it matters)

- **Why UIS is needed**
  - UNESCO UIS “internationally mobile students” is the standard route to a **global** baseline (all destinations), and it provides definitions designed to avoid inflating mobility with long-term residents when citizenship data are used. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/en/methodology/communication-and-information))

- **What is missing in this ticket’s current baseline**
  - UIS outbound totals for Bulgaria (latest year and time series) and the global Top destinations list, including Bulgaria-to-Cyprus and Bulgaria-to-Greece counts and ranks. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/en/news/february-2025-uis-data-refresh-new-data-and-expanded-coverage-global-education-monitoring))

- **Data currency**
  - UIS has explicit “refresh” cycles, and recent publication narratives indicate that a 2025 refresh corresponds to reference year 2023 and prior years, which reinforces that “latest available” can be 2023 even when accessed in 2025. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/en/news/february-2025-uis-data-refresh-new-data-and-expanded-coverage-global-education-monitoring))

## Confidence levels (how to communicate results safely)

- **High confidence (use in external-facing positioning and internal targets)**
  - 2023 Bulgaria-to-Greece and Bulgaria-to-Cyprus counts and ranks within the Eurostat reporting set.
  - 2023 concentration picture within the Eurostat reporting set (Germany and Türkiye dominance). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Medium confidence (use with explicit caveats)**
  - Eurostat “total outbound” year-to-year movements, because missing hosts, definition differences, and breaks can affect totals. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Low confidence (do not use without UIS completion or host-country validation)**
  - Any claims about Bulgaria’s worldwide outbound total, or worldwide Top destinations.
  - Any claims about outbound field-of-study patterns for Bulgaria-to-Greece or Bulgaria-to-Cyprus (not extracted yet). ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/en/methodology/communication-and-information))

## How UNIC and UNIC Athens should use this baseline (practical decisions)

- **Market prioritisation**
  - Treat Bulgaria as a credible outbound origin for **Greece** (Greece is a Top 10 host within the Eurostat reporting set in 2023), and treat **Cyprus as smaller but present** (outside Top 15 in 2023, rank #16). ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Program strategy**
  - Until outbound-by-field is extracted, prioritise program clusters using a disciplined test approach (limited hero programs, clear employability and recognition messaging), and avoid over-committing to niche regulated pathways without Bulgaria-specific proof points. ([webgate.ec.europa.eu](https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm))

- **Performance management**
  - Use Eurostat to set a “floor” for market sizing and to benchmark Greece vs Cyprus share, then upgrade to a “global ceiling” once UIS totals and global destinations are integrated. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

## Recommended monitoring and next steps (to close the gaps)

- **Complete the global baseline**
  - Extract UNESCO UIS: Bulgaria outbound total and global destination ranking, then reconcile with Eurostat without merging totals into one number. ([uis.unesco.org](https://uis.unesco.org/en/methodology/communication-and-information))

- **Harden the Eurostat trend series**
  - Standardise a repeatable computation rule for totals: sum numeric country hosts only, exclude aggregates, exclude host BG, document missing hosts each year from “positions-with-no-data.” ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))

- **Add program evidence**
  - Extend extraction to mobility by field (ISCED-F) where available in UOE reporting, or use host-side official statistics for Greece and Cyprus by nationality and field where possible, then map results to UNIC and UNIC Athens program clusters. ([webgate.ec.europa.eu](https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/metadata/en/educ_uoe_enr_esms.htm))

- **Adopt a refresh cadence**
  - Annual refresh for degree mobility (Eurostat, UIS when available), plus a quarterly internal view using leading indicators (applications, enquiries, conversion by program and channel) to detect shifts earlier than official statistics can. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/api/dissemination/statistics/1.0/data/educ_uoe_mobs02?isced11=ED5-8&lang=EN&partner=BG&sex=T&time=2023&unit=NR))
