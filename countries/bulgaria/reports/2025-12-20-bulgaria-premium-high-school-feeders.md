# Bulgaria Premium High-School Feeders

## Produced by: UNIC Evolve, December 20, 2025

# 1) Executive Summary — where Bulgaria’s “UNIC-affording” secondary market concentrates

Families able to sustain **€9,000 – €15,000 per year** (≈ \$10,000 – \$16,000) for UNIC/UNIC Athens programs, or **€20,000 – €25,000 per year** for Medicine, are overwhelmingly concentrated in **international and high-fee private schools in Sofia**, with a thinner but still relevant layer of premium schools in **Plovdiv** and **Varna**. A small number of selective public gymnasiums with IB/DP or bilingual elite tracks can generate scholarship-eligible, outbound-oriented students even when tuition is low.

**Highest-yield priorities**

- **Sofia Tier 1 (high-fee international/IB schools):** Anglo-American School of Sofia (AAS), American College of Sofia (ACS), British School of Sofia (BSS), St. George International School, Deutsches Auslandsschule Sofia (DSB), Lycée Français de Sofia Victor Hugo, Zlatarski International School of Sofia (IB DP), and the Sofia Mathematical/Language elite schools with IB/bilingual streams. These institutions either publish **€10k–€25k annual tuition** or operate on fees-on-request international models that typically sit in the same band.
- **Tier 2 premium cities:** Plovdiv (Anglo-American School of Plovdiv, Plovdiv Math and Language private schools with premium bilingual programs, private gymnasiums such as Stoyanstroi), Varna (French School “Antoine de Saint-Exupéry”, Cambridge/IB-positioned private gymnasiums), Burgas (Geo Milev AEG as an international-intent magnet, plus emerging private bilingual schools). These markets are smaller and more mixed, so outreach should start with fee-evidenced or clearly international schools.
- **Selective public IB/bilingual magnets (scholarship/outbound intent):** National High School of Mathematics and Natural Sciences (NPMG) and Sofia Mathematical Gymnasium (SMG) in Sofia; First English Language School (1 AEG) and 9th French Language School (9 FEG) in Sofia; elite language/math gymnasiums in Plovdiv and Varna. These are **lower-fee but high-intent** feeders suited to scholarship and honors-track messaging.

**Implications for recruitment**

- Run **Medicine and premium non-Medicine outreach first** through Sofia’s Tier 1 fee-published/fee-on-request schools; these households already match or exceed UNIC tuition bands.
- Treat Plovdiv/Varna/Burgas as **selective expansions**: prioritize schools with international leaving qualifications (IB DP, Cambridge IGCSE/A-Levels) and confirmed premium fee structures; use counselor validation to avoid mid-market noise.
- Maintain a **structured scholarship track** for elite public gymnasiums to capture high-academic students with outbound intent, while keeping the core affordability focus on premium private/international schools.

# 3) Selection criteria and inclusion rubric (Bulgaria-specific)

- **Tier 1 (Medicine-capable households):** International/IB schools and private bilingual schools with **published or peer-benchmarked tuition ≥ €12k/year** or hard-currency schedules. Clear IB DP/Cambridge A-Level/French Bac/German Abitur pathways.
- **Tier 2 (strong non-Medicine + selective Medicine):** Premium private schools with **€7k–€11k/year** tuition, bilingual or Cambridge/IB-aligned programs. Include international schools with fees-on-request where peer benchmarks indicate premium pricing.
- **Tier 3 (scholarship/high-intent public):** Elite public math/language gymnasiums and IB DP public sections; low tuition but strong outbound intent. Use scholarship and honors-track messaging; verify English/French/German proficiency for placement.
- **Exclusions / deprioritized:** Mid-market private schools without international leaving qualifications, vocational secondary schools without strong outbound pathways, and schools lacking counselor infrastructure.

# 4) Sourcing and validation notes (how to keep the list defensible)

- **Fee confirmation cadence:** Capture official tuition PDFs or admissions statements each cycle (August–September) for AAS, BSS, ACS, St. George, DSB, Lycée, Zlatarski. For fees-on-request schools, log counselor-provided ranges and date-stamp them.
- **International-pathway verification:** Maintain a short register of IB DP authorization (Zlatarski, AAS), Cambridge/A-Level centers (BSS, ACS tracks), French AEFE alignment (Lycée, Varna AEFE school), and German abroad-school status (DSB). Refresh annually.
- **Outbound placement cues:** Ask counselors for prior-year destinations (UK, Netherlands, Germany, Greece/Cyprus). Track Medicine-specific placements separately to prioritize households that can sustain €20k–€25k.
- **Regional prioritization rule:** If a non-Sofia school cannot provide either (a) tuition ≥ €7k/year **or** (b) confirmed international leaving qualification with counselor contact, keep it on a watchlist rather than active outreach.

# 5) Recommended outreach plays (fastest path to leads)

1. **Sofia Tier 1 counselor circuit:** In-person or virtual counselor briefings at AAS, ACS, BSS, St. George, DSB, Lycée, Zlatarski. Package includes Medicine prerequisites, scholarship policy, and Athens vs Nicosia positioning.
2. **Parent evenings for Medicine-intent families:** Co-host with Sofia international schools; use UNIC Athens proximity and clinical placement messaging to counter UK/Netherlands competition.
3. **Selective city sprints (Plovdiv, Varna):** Two-week validation + outreach cycle: confirm fees, schedule 1–2 school visits or webinars, and capture counselor contacts. Proceed only with schools meeting Tier 1/2 rules.
4. **Scholarship track for elite public gymnasiums:** Target SMG, NPMG, 1 AEG, 9 FEG, and top math/language gymnasiums in Plovdiv/Varna. Offer honors workshops, Olympiad-linked sessions, and early-application coaching.
5. **Data hygiene:** After each outreach wave, update fee bands, counselor contacts, and outbound placement notes in the country data workspace to keep future cycles fast and defensible.

# 6) Risks and mitigations

- **Fee opacity or rapid updates:** Mitigate by date-stamping all fee confirmations and refreshing each admission cycle; keep a standing request with counselors for updated schedules.
- **Over-indexing on public elite schools (affordability mismatch):** Keep scholarship track separate from core premium outreach to avoid diluting Medicine/high-fee efforts.
- **Regional dilution:** Apply the Tier rules strictly; if a regional school cannot meet the fee/qualification bar, deprioritize until verified.
- **Competing destinations (UK/NL/DE):** Emphasize UNIC recognition, scholarship offers, and proximity for Athens; for German/French schools, highlight EU campus advantages and visa/work pathways.
