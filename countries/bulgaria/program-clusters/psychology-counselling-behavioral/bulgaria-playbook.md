## Goal (90-day)
Generate qualified Bulgaria pipeline for psychology/counselling interest, while keeping claims compliant:
- Convert “interest” leads (high-school and career-switchers) into **pathway conversations**.
- Convert practitioner leads (working counsellors/educators/NGO staff) into **master’s-level consultations** where supervised practice is central.

Primary success metrics (pick targets per intake capacity):
- **Webinar registrations:** 150–250 total (2–3 sessions)

## Messaging pillars (what to say) + proof points (what to show)

### Pillar 1 — Supervised practice is the credibility hinge (for clinical/counselling paths)
**Core message:** “If your goal is practice, ask every university the same question: what supervised practice do you actually get, and where?”  
**Proof points to show:**
- UNIC MSc Clinical Psychology programme page publishes practicum-hour structure (1000 hours + optional additional hours) and references EuroPsy framing.  
  - Source: https://www.unic.ac.cy/clinical-psychology-msc-3-years-or-6-semesters/
- UNIC MSc Counseling Psychology programme page lists practicum courses with hour designations.  
  - Source: https://www.unic.ac.cy/counseling-psychology-msc-2-5-years-or-5-semesters/
**What not to say:** do not promise “licensure” or “automatic right to practice” in Bulgaria/any country.

### Pillar 2 — Recognition planning: separate academic recognition vs right-to-practice
**Core message:** “We’ll help you plan recognition early, but requirements depend on the country and the role (psychologist vs clinical psychologist vs psychotherapist).”  
**Proof points to show:**
- NACID info page for foreign qualifications in Bulgaria.  
  - Source: https://nacid.bg/en/foreign_qualifications_in_bulgaria_b_m/info/
- EFPA EuroPsy baseline definitions (certificate, not a legal licence).  
  - Sources: https://www.efpa.eu/europsy and https://www.efpa.eu/supervised-practice

### Pillar 3 — Wellbeing is not marketing copy; it’s a safeguard and a student-support promise
**Core message:** “Psychology is serious; we treat ethics, supervision, and student support as part of the offer.”  
**Proof points to show:** student support services, counselling/wellbeing supports, safeguarding language (use official campus pages; avoid improvising).

### Pillar 4 — Affordability with integrity (no bespoke promises)
**Core message:** “We can explain published fees and published scholarships; anything special must go through admissions approvals.”  
**Proof points to show:**
- Bulgaria scholarship guardrails and routing: `../../skills/bulgaria-scholarships-and-discounts/SKILL.md`

## Messaging pillars (Bulgaria) + proof points

### Pillar 1 — “Wellbeing is real; we treat it seriously”
**Core message:** Families are not buying “a psychology degree” in the abstract; they are buying **support + structure** in a period where youth wellbeing is a visible concern.

**Proof points and talk-tracks**
- Bulgaria youth mental-health need is measurable; UNICEF Bulgaria reports **1 in 10** children aged 10–19 diagnosed with a mental disorder and high stigma around help-seeking (use as context, not fear).  
  Source: UNICEF Bulgaria (link in `cluster.md` sources list).
- Tie the offer to concrete student support services (campus-specific assets should be linked when available).

### Pillar 2 — “Clear pathway: degree ≠ licence, and that clarity is a benefit”
**Core message:** We win trust by **not** promising licensure outcomes; we explain the pathway and help families plan it.

**Proof points**
- EuroPsy framing: **300 ECTS + 1 year / 1,500 hours supervised practice** (use to explain the education + supervised practice logic).  
  Source: EuroPsy basic requirements (link in `cluster.md` sources list).
- Bulgaria recognition is via **NACID**; don’t improvise on legal requirements.  
  Source: NACID recognition page (link in `cluster.md` sources list).

### Pillar 3 — “Practical exposure (where documented)”
**Core message:** Students want to see how psychology is applied (schools, youth work, organisations), not only theory.

**Proof points**
- UNIC Nicosia Psychology BSc programme plan lists a **Practicum in Psychology (6 ECTS)** module (anchor for “applied learning” when discussing UNIC).  
  Source: UNIC programme page (link in `cluster.md` sources list).
- Competitors like UCLan Cyprus highlight a **Psychology Placement module**; students will ask what the equivalent looks like at UNIC/UNIC Athens — answer with specifics only when confirmed.  
  Source: UCLan Cyprus programme page (linked in `competitors.md`).

### Pillar 4 — “Value story vs Dutch publics: support + proximity + outcomes planning”
**Core message:** Netherlands publics often win on statutory EU tuition; we win on **proximity, support, and a clearer pathway plan**.

**Proof points**
- Netherlands statutory tuition fee is published at **€2,601** for 2025–2026 (EU/EEA).  
  Source: Government of the Netherlands (linked in `cluster.md` and `competitors.md`).

## Objections (Bulgaria) + recruiter-safe answers
- **“Can I become a psychologist in Bulgaria with this degree?”**  
  Answer: “A psychology degree is the education foundation. Professional practice requirements vary by role and country; we can explain the pathway and point you to NACID recognition and professional-body guidance. If you want clinical practice, plan for postgraduate specialisation and supervised practice.”
- **“How many supervised hours do I need?”**  
  Answer: “A common Europe-wide reference is EuroPsy’s 1,500 hours supervised practice after completing 300 ECTS; your exact requirement depends on the country/role.”
- **“Why not the Netherlands?”**  
  Answer: “NL is an excellent option for EU price points, but it can be selective and the student support model is different. Our offer competes on proximity, onboarding/support, and practical exposure with clearer planning for the post-degree pathway.”

## Operating guardrails (do not skip)
- Never promise licensure/registration outcomes; always separate **education** from **professional practice** requirements.
- For any clinical/counselling claim, cite the programme’s own published structure (practicum modules, placement rules, hours/ECTS).
- If a student’s goal is a protected clinical title, route them to a recognition specialist call early.
