# Psychology, Counselling, and Behavioral — competitor set (Bulgaria)

Last updated: **2025-12-22**

This file prioritises **real alternatives** Bulgarian students commonly consider when they ask for psychology/counselling paths in **English** (or locally in Bulgaria), and it frames how to differentiate against them without overclaiming on licensure.

## How to use this competitor sheet in recruiter calls
Ask these 5 questions early; most competitors fall apart on (3) and (4):
1) What is the **instruction language** end-to-end?
2) What is the **total cost** (tuition + living) and what is the realistic scholarship story?
3) Is there **supervised practice / practicum** and is it **quantified** (hours, supervision model, settings)?
4) What does the programme say about **recognition / accreditation** (BPS, NVAO/NEAA, EuroPsy framing)?
5) Where will the graduate realistically work (Bulgaria/Cyprus/Greece/NL/UK) and what is the **right-to-practice** plan?

## Competitor entries

### University of Central Lancashire Cyprus (UCLan Cyprus) — BSc (Hons) Psychology (Cyprus)
- **Why Bulgarians consider it:** English-taught Cyprus option with a UK-linked brand and clear “psychology” signalling.  
- **Tuition (published):** **€9,000/year (60 ECTS)** for undergraduate students after bursary discount (2025/2026 fee page).  
- **Practicum / placement (hours):** Programme page references a **Psychology Placement module**; public materials do **not** publish a fixed number of placement hours (confirm via course handbook).  
- **Accreditation / recognition:** Programme page states it is **accredited by the British Psychological Society (BPS)**.  
- **Differentiation vs UNIC / UNIC Athens:**  
  - If the prospect needs a **clinical/counselling pathway with explicitly stated supervised practicum-hour structures**, anchor them on UNIC’s MSc pages (clinical/counselling) and recognition planning.  
  - If the prospect is UK-pathway focused, treat BPS accreditation as a serious comparator and compete on **programme fit + supervised practice + student support + cost clarity** rather than dismissing it.  
- **Sources:**  
  - Programme page (BPS accreditation; placement module mention): https://www.uclancyprus.ac.cy/undergraduate-course/bsc-hons-psychology/  
  - Fees & scholarships (UG tuition): https://www.uclancyprus.ac.cy/study/fees-scholarships/  

### Maastricht University — Bachelor Psychology (Netherlands)

- **Tuition (published):** Statutory (EU/EEA) tuition for this programme is **€2,601**; institutional tuition is **€13,000** (programme tuition page).  
- **Practicum / placement (hours):** Bachelor programmes in NL are typically **research-and-theory heavy**; public pages do not advertise a quantified clinical practicum hour model at bachelor level (verify per track/electives).  
- **Accreditation / recognition:** Accredited programme decision available via **NVAO** (Dutch-Flemish Accreditation Organisation).  
- **Differentiation vs UNIC / UNIC Athens:**

  - Compete on **support + proximity**: NL can be a great academic choice but is farther and can feel less “guided” for families; Cyprus/Greece proximity and structured onboarding can win.  
- **Sources:**

# Psychology, counselling, and behavioral competitors (Bulgaria)

Last updated: 2025-12-22

This list prioritises institutions and programmes that Bulgarian applicants commonly compare when they consider psychology/counselling degrees in **Cyprus, Greece-proximate options, and “low-tuition EU” destinations**.

Notes:
- Many **BSc/BA psychology degrees are not licensure-qualifying** on their own; compare pathways (BSc/BA → MSc → supervised practice).
- Where programmes publish practice as **ECTS**, hours vary by institution; avoid promising an exact number unless it is explicitly stated.
- EuroPsy publishes a useful reference point for “pathway planning”: **300 ECTS** plus **1 year / 1,500 hours** supervised practice (individual certificate; not automatic).  
  Source: https://www.europsy.eu/quality-standards/basic-requirements

## Snapshot: how Bulgarian students tend to decide
- **Netherlands publics** win on price (EU statutory tuition) and brand, but are selective (often numerus fixus).
- **Cyprus options** compete on proximity, English delivery, and support structures; differences come down to placements, recognition guidance, and student experience.
- **CEE options** (e.g., Hungary) offer mid-priced English degrees with manageable distance; brand outcomes depend heavily on the student’s postgraduate plan.

## Competitor entries

- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** High brand, English track, strong research reputation, low statutory tuition for EU.  
- **Differentiation vs UNIC:** UNIC can win on proximity, onboarding/support, and clearer “what happens after graduation” advising; Dutch publics win on price and brand.

## Differentiation reminders vs UNIC / UNIC Athens (recruiter use)
- **Don’t oversell licensure:** Use EuroPsy as a pathway explanation (300 ECTS + 1,500 hours supervised practice), not as a guaranteed outcome.  
- **Win condition vs Dutch publics:** support + proximity + transparency (placements, recognition, total cost), not sticker price.  
- **Win condition vs Cyprus competitors:** be specific about practicum/placement structure, student services, and Bulgaria-facing recognition FAQs.
