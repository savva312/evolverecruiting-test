# Psychology, Counselling, and Behavioral cluster (Bulgaria)

Last updated: **2025-12-22** | Scope: **Bulgaria** | Primary audience: **Recruiters, counselor-facing teams**

## One-line positioning
For Bulgaria, this cluster wins when we lead with **credible supervised practice (where applicable)**, **ethics and wellbeing**, and **clear recognition planning** (academic recognition + country-specific right-to-practice), not generic “psychology is popular” messaging.

## What this cluster covers
- **Psychology foundation** (Bachelor-level) for students considering psychology as a modern career (research/UX/HR) or as a stepping-stone to postgraduate clinical/counselling tracks.
- **Counselling / clinical psychology pathways** (Master-level) where **practicum hours and supervision** are the decision hinge.
- Adjacent “behavioral” narratives used in recruiting:
  - **Organizational/HR psychology** (employee wellbeing, people analytics, coaching).
  - **Youth work / NGO psychosocial support** (trauma-informed practice).
  - **Applied behavioral approaches** (autism/ABA interest) — only when we can be precise about scope and supervision.

## Bulgaria demand drivers (evidence-led)

### 1) Wellbeing pressure is real — families want credible pathways, not vibes
- **Adult depressive symptoms (Bulgaria):** Eurostat EHIS reports **4.5%** “depressive symptoms” (2019, total population).  
  - Breakdown (2019): **2.1% major** depressive symptoms, **2.4% other** depressive symptoms.  
  - Implication: treat wellbeing as a **parent concern** and a **student concern**; position pastoral support and evidence-based practice.  
  - Source: Eurostat API (EHIS, BG, 2019).  
- **Suicide mortality trend:** Eurostat’s standardised death rate for suicide (BG) declines from **8.83 (2018)** → **6.35 (2022)** per 100,000 (standardised rate; interpret directionally).  
  - Implication: this is a credibility signal for the market — anchor messaging in **public-health seriousness**, avoid sensationalism.  
  - Source: Eurostat API (BG, 2018–2022).  

### 2) Demand is shifting from “interest” to “professional pathway”
Bulgarian prospects increasingly ask:
- “How many **supervised hours** do I get?”
- “Is this accepted for **EuroPsy** / UK pathways?”
- “What do I need to do to practice in **Bulgaria/Cyprus/Greece**?”

Implication for recruiters: the fastest path to trust is **documented programme facts** (language, ECTS, practicum structure) plus a clear “here’s what we can say vs what must be confirmed” recognition script.

### 3) NGOs and donor ecosystems shape inclusion + wellbeing priorities
For Bulgaria, NGO/donor conversations often sit adjacent to wellbeing and youth outcomes. Use the repo’s NGO/SGO index as a starting short-list and layer in mental-health organisations when building partnerships.
- Repo reference: `../../entities/ngos-sgos/index.md`

Implication for UNIC/UNIC Athens:

## Recognition / licensure dependencies — recruiter-safe guidance
> This section is for recruiting conversations, not legal advice. Always route applicants to competent authorities and confirm requirements for the target country and role.

### What to separate (always)
1) **Academic recognition** (degree recognition for continuing study/employment)  
2) **Professional right-to-practice** (licensing/registration; varies by country and by title)

### Practical guardrails for Bulgaria prospects
- If the student plans to **use a foreign degree in Bulgaria**, start with **NACID** information and the applicant’s intended use-case (further study, public-sector role, private employment).  
  - Source: NACID general information page: https://nacid.bg/en/foreign_qualifications_in_bulgaria_b_m/info/  
- In Europe, “psychologist”, “clinical psychologist”, and “psychotherapist” are **not interchangeable**; regulation varies by country and setting.  
- **EuroPsy** is a European certificate (not a legal licence) with a supervised practice component; use EFPA pages for the baseline definition in conversations.  
  - Sources: https://www.efpa.eu/europsy and https://www.efpa.eu/supervised-practice  

### Open research gaps to close (explicit)
- Bulgaria return-path mapping by role (school psychologist vs clinical psychologist vs counselling roles) with named Bulgarian authorities and a parent-ready FAQ (requires a dedicated recognition ticket).

- The European Commission frames mental health as a cross-sector priority (education, employment, digital life, health systems).  
  Source: European Commission “A comprehensive approach to mental health” (2023–2024 policy page).  

**Implications**

### 3) NGO/SGO ecosystem + inclusion priorities (pipeline + partners)

**Implications**
- Treat NGOs/SGOs as **co-marketing partners** (workshops, panels) and **placement multipliers** (projects, volunteer roles) where programme rules allow.

### 4) Study-abroad decision context: “low-tuition EU” competitors shape expectations

**Implications**

## Recognition, licensure, and “what this degree enables” (recruiter-safe notes)

### 1) Academic degree recognition (Bulgaria)
- Recognition of foreign higher-education diplomas for Bulgaria is handled via **NACID** (National Centre for Information and Documentation).  
  Source: NACID “Recognition of foreign higher education diplomas” (English site).

### 2) Regulated-profession check (Bulgaria)
- Bulgaria’s **NACID list of regulated professions** (for professional qualification recognition) is the first stop to confirm whether a title is regulated; “psychologist” is not obviously listed in the alphabetical index (verify during advising for the student’s intended job title/sector).  
  Source: NACID “List of regulated professions” (English site).

### 3) EuroPsy (Europe-wide framing recruiters can use)
- EuroPsy is an **individual certificate**, not an automatic degree accreditation. EFPA’s published basic requirements include **300 ECTS** (typically 3+2 years) plus **1 year / 1,500 hours** of supervised practice.  
  Source: EFPA / EuroPsy “Basic requirements”.

**How to use this in recruiting**
- Use EuroPsy to explain the **logic of the pathway** (education + supervised practice), not as a promise that “this programme gives EuroPsy”.
- When asked “How many practice hours do I need?”, anchor to the EuroPsy basic requirement (1,500 hours) and then state “country/role specifics vary”.

### 4) UK pathway (for students comparing BPS-aligned routes)
- If a student is comparing against a UK pathway, they may ask about **BPS-accredited degrees** (a typical route to Graduate Basis for Chartered Membership, then further training for protected practitioner roles).  
  Use this as a comparison point only; do not promise UK registration outcomes.
