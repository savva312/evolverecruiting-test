# Health, Pharmacy, and Allied Health cluster (Bulgaria)

Last updated: **2025-12-22** | Scope: **Bulgaria** | Primary audience: **Enrollment (recruiters + counselors + agent managers)**

## One-line positioning
In Bulgaria, this cluster wins when we lead with **clinical training + patient-contact readiness** and **recognition/licensure process clarity** (regulated professions), not generic “health careers are in demand” messaging.

## What this cluster covers (Bulgaria scope)
Primary (student-facing “hero” pathways):
- **Nursing (BSc)** — regulated profession in Bulgaria; recognition handled by the competent authority (see guardrails).
- **Physiotherapy (BSc)** — often treated as a regulated health profession in many EU countries; always confirm Bulgaria’s competent authority.
- **Pharmacy (MPharm / Integrated Master)** — regulated profession in Bulgaria; recognition handled by the competent authority (see guardrails).

Out of scope:
- **Medicine / MD** (separate cluster): `../medicine-md/cluster.md`

## Non-negotiable guardrails (regulated professions)
Do not promise licensure or “automatic” right-to-practice in Bulgaria.
- Use the Bulgaria recognition brief as the single counselor script for “return-to-Bulgaria”: `../../market/recognition-and-licensure.md`
- Start every regulated-profession counseling conversation with: **“Degree recognition and professional recognition/licensure are different processes; the competent authority decides.”**
- When asked for certainty: “We can outline the process and typical documents. The final decision is issued by the Bulgarian competent authority for that profession.”

Quick official starting point for Bulgaria regulated professions: https://nacid.bg/en/professio/

## Bulgaria segments (minimum 3) + how to win each

### Segment A — Nursing (BSc) candidates (regulated, family-driven)
**Typical profile:** strong “helper” identity; parent-led decision; price-sensitive vs Bulgarian public options; anxious about right-to-practice.  
**Primary competitors:** Bulgarian medical universities (public), plus Cyprus/Greece options for mobility.  
**Decision hinges:**
- Clinical placement structure and supervision (where/when/what kind of settings).
- Language reality for clinical practice (patient contact often requires local language).
- Clear plan for Bulgaria professional recognition (documents + timelines, not promises).
**How UNIC wins (safe, specific):**
- “We’ll map your pathway: study → clinical training → what documents you’ll need → which competent authority decides.”
- Use a **two-step close**: (1) “clinical fit” (shadowing-style webinar + student story), (2) “recognition plan” call with a checklist.

### Segment B — Pharmacy (MPharm) candidates (high friction, high intent)
**Typical profile:** academically strong; compares Bulgaria vs Greece vs Cyprus; wants clarity on integrated-master status and professional pathway.  
**Decision hinges:**
- Integrated-master structure, traineeship requirements, and patient-facing language.
- Whether they are Greece-oriented (Athens, Greek instruction) or want a bilingual environment (Nicosia published as English+Greek).
- Professional recognition plan for Bulgaria (competent authority + dossier).
**How UNIC wins:**
- Lead with a “competence story” (pharmaceutical care, medication safety, research literacy) + a documented traineeship plan.
- Run a **parent webinar** that is explicitly titled “Recognition & right-to-practice: Bulgaria return planning for Pharmacy” and links to `../../market/recognition-and-licensure.md`.

### Segment C — Physiotherapy / allied health “hands-on” candidates (outcomes-focused)
**Typical profile:** sports/rehab interest; often underestimates regulated-profession complexity; looks for practical training and employability.  
**Decision hinges:**
- Evidence of hands-on training (labs, clinical exposure) and realistic career roles.
- Recognition path clarity (do not assume non-regulated).
**How UNIC wins:**
- Position a credible *skills-to-job* map (MSK rehab, sports therapy ecosystems, wellness/rehab centres) plus a “Bulgaria return dossier” checklist.

## Fast triage script (use in the first 10 minutes)
Ask these in order:
1) **Where do you want to practise after graduation?** (Bulgaria / Greece / Cyprus / other EU)
2) **Which job title?** (“nurse”, “pharmacist”, “physiotherapist”, “clinical role vs research/industry”)
3) **Language readiness:** Are you willing/able to study and practise in **Greek** where required (patient contact, placements)?
4) **Academic readiness:** Bio/Chem/Math level; any prior healthcare exposure; health clearance comfort.
5) **Family constraint:** budget, timeline, and preference to stay close (Greece/Cyprus proximity).

If the student answers “Bulgaria + regulated job title”, immediately send:
- `../../market/recognition-and-licensure.md` and schedule a 15-minute “recognition plan” follow-up.

## What evidence to bring to a sales conversation (so we beat “cheap public” options)
Minimum proof points to request from the campus team (or pull from official pages) before you go to fairs:
- Published **language of instruction** and whether the programme offers parallel tracks.
- Clinical placement structure (types of sites, supervision model, when patient contact starts).
- Degree structure (duration/ECTS) and traineeship expectations (pharmacy/health fields).
- A counselor-safe “Bulgaria return dossier” checklist (see recognition brief).
