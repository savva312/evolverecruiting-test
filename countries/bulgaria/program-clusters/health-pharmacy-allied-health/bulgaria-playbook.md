# Bulgaria playbook — Health, Pharmacy, and Allied Health

Last updated: **2025-12-22** | Owner: **Bulgaria market lead / Enrollment**

This playbook turns the cluster into an **operating motion**: who to target, what to say (safely), how to qualify, and how to move candidates from “interest” → “application” → “enrolment”.

## 1) Outcomes (what “good” looks like)
Use these KPIs to run weekly standups with agents + counselors:
- **Lead → qualified lead (QL) rate:** target ≥35% (health leads are high-intent but “misinformed”; qualification lifts quality).
- **QL → application rate:** target ≥25% with a 14-day follow-up SLA.
- **Application → deposit:** target ≥20% after a counselor-led “recognition plan” call (regulated professions reduce ghosting when de-risked early).

## 2) Recognition/licensure caveats (must be in every counselor script)
For Bulgaria returns, Nursing and Pharmacy are regulated professions; “recognition” is a **process** handled by the **competent authority** (often Ministry of Health per NACID profession pages). Do not promise outcomes.

Use these two assets as your standard language:
- Bulgaria recognition brief (single source of truth): `../../market/recognition-and-licensure.md`
- Regulated professions index (official): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/

Minimum safe language (copy/paste):
- “We can explain the process and typical documents. The **final decision** is issued by the Bulgarian **competent authority** for your profession.”
- “A university degree does not automatically equal a licence to practise a regulated profession.”

## 3) Who to target (segments + routing)

### Segment A — Nursing (BSc)
**Best channels:** parent-led webinars; school counselor partnerships; Tier-1 agents with health pipeline.  
**Routing:** if the student wants to practise in Bulgaria → schedule a “recognition plan” call within 72 hours.

### Segment B — Physiotherapy (BSc)
**Best channels:** sports schools/clubs + science gymnasiums; rehab/wellness interest groups; influencer-style student content (day-in-the-life).  
**Routing:** always confirm whether the student is targeting a regulated clinical role in Bulgaria (job title matters).

### Segment C — Pharmacy (MPharm)
**Best channels:** top Bio/Chem cohorts + Greek-speaking communities; Greece/Cyprus intent signals; academic competitions.  
**Routing:** split early:
- **Athens** if the candidate is strong in **Greek** (programme published as Greek).
- **Nicosia** if they need an English+Greek environment (programme published as English and Greek) and want Cyprus positioning.

## 4) Messaging angles that convert (and what not to say)

### What to lead with (works in Bulgaria)
- **Clinical training structure** and “patient contact readiness” (where/when/what settings).
- **Language reality** for placements and patient communication (do not imply English-only patient contact).
- **Return-to-Bulgaria planning**: show the process + checklist, not guarantees.
- **Proximity + family access:** Cyprus/Greece are close enough for “supportive distance”.

### Do-not-say list (regulated professions)
- “Guaranteed licensure in Bulgaria”
- “Automatic recognition because it’s EU”
- “You’ll work as a pharmacist/nurse/physio in Bulgaria immediately after graduation”

## 5) Qualification workflow (what counselors do every time)

### Step 1 — 10-minute triage (first contact)
Ask in order:
1) Intended practice country after graduation (BG / GR / CY / other EU)
2) Intended job title (nurse/pharmacist/physio vs non-clinical roles)
3) Language readiness (Greek willingness/ability)
4) Academic readiness (Bio/Chem/Math; graduation type; English level)
5) Family constraints (budget, timeline, proximity)

### Step 2 — Build the “Return-to-Bulgaria dossier” (before application if possible)
Use the checklist in `../../market/recognition-and-licensure.md` and gather:
- Transcript + predicted grades + diploma type
- Clinical exposure evidence (if any)
- ID/passport + consent for agent data sharing (GDPR)

### Step 3 — The “recognition plan” call (15 minutes; parent included)
Deliverables at the end of the call:
- “Regulated vs non-regulated” clarification for the job title
- Which authority decides (point to NACID profession index)
- What documents to preserve during study (Diploma Supplement, clinical hours logs)
- Next action: apply + book a campus webinar (programme-specific)

## 7) Counselor-ready FAQ outline (safe answers)
Use this as an outline for a Bulgarian landing page, webinar Q&A, and agent scripts.

1) **“Will I be able to practise in Bulgaria after graduation?”**  
Answer outline: regulated-profession check → competent authority decides → typical document set → timelines (process-based, not promises). Link: `../../market/recognition-and-licensure.md`.

2) **“Is an EU degree automatically recognised in Bulgaria?”**  
Answer outline: academic recognition vs professional recognition; employer vs regulated roles; show NACID starting points.

3) **“Is the programme taught in English?”**  
Answer outline: quote the published language on the official programme page; explain patient-contact language realities; confirm cohort/track details per intake.

4) **“Where will I do clinical placements?”**  
Answer outline: describe the *types* of sites and supervision model (as published/confirmed by campus); avoid promising a specific hospital unless contracted and published.

5) **“Can I work while studying?”**  
Answer outline: depends on campus country rules and schedule intensity; health programmes are time-heavy due to placements; confirm with admissions and visa guidance.

6) **“What documents should I keep for recognition later?”**  
Answer outline: Diploma Supplement, transcripts, clinical hours logs, placement site letters; point to the “return-to-Bulgaria dossier” section in the recognition brief.

7) **“Do I need Bulgarian language / Greek language?”**  
Answer outline: Bulgarian for practising in Bulgaria; Greek often needed for patient interaction in Cyprus/Greece clinical settings; confirm by programme/placement.

## 8) Follow-up sequences (simple, high-performance)

### Sequence 1 — 48-hour “recognition clarity” follow-up (regulated titles)
Day 0: Send recognition brief link + ask 3 qualification questions (practice country, job title, language readiness).  
Day 2: Book “recognition plan” call; request transcript + predicted grades.  
Day 7: Programme webinar invite + deadline reminder; share competitor-compare checklist (clinical + language + support).

### Sequence 2 — School-origin lead (student + parent)
Day 0: Thank-you email + 60-second recap + next webinar slot.  
Day 3: Parent-focused WhatsApp/SMS (short): “We’ll explain the recognition process and what documents matter.”  
Day 10: Application CTA + agent/counselor booking link.
