# Medicine / MD cluster (Bulgaria)

Operational cluster pack to recruit Bulgarian students and families into Medicine/MD pathways for **UNIC Nicosia** and **UNIC Athens**.

This cluster is **high-stakes** (regulated profession). Messaging must be compliant:
- Do **not** promise licensure, registration, “automatic recognition”, residency placement, or employment outcomes.
- For “return to Bulgaria” questions, use the recognition brief: `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`.

## 1) Market reality (Bulgaria) — what you’re competing against
Bulgarian applicants commonly compare:
- **Bulgarian public medical universities** (price + staying in-country).
- **CEE English MD tracks** (Czechia/Hungary/Slovakia/Poland/Romania) that run entrance exams and have strong agent/test-prep ecosystems.
- **Greece** (proximity) and “Mediterranean” lifestyle factors.

Reference competitor set: `countries/bulgaria/program-clusters/medicine-md/competitors.md`.

## 2) Segment map (≥3 segments)

### Segment A — Premium private/bilingual schools (“international-track” families)
**Who they are:** IB/IGCSE/bilingual profiles; families pay for predictability and “international pathway” support.  
**What they optimize for:** reputation signal, English-medium delivery, structured student support, mobility options, clear clinical pathway narrative.  
**Primary fears:** “Will this be recognized back home?”, “Will my child get real clinical exposure?”, “Will English be enough in hospitals?”  
**Win condition:** tight, evidence-based story + fast response + credible academic touchpoints.

### Segment B — Public elites and science-focused gymnasiums (“achievement + value”)
**Who they are:** strong grades; often compare MU Sofia/Varna/Plovdiv and CEE publics.  
**What they optimize for:** price-to-quality, admissions odds, academic rigor, practical training, total cost (tuition + living).  
**Primary fears:** cost vs Bulgarian publics, administrative friction, “hidden requirements”.  
**Win condition:** transparent total-cost framing + clear admissions roadmap + proof points on support and clinical training *without overclaiming*.

### Segment C — Transfer/second-chance candidates (“rescue” segment)
**Who they are:** started medicine elsewhere (Bulgaria/CEE/Ukraine/other) or are re-applicants; want stability and a credible reset.  
**What they optimize for:** transfer policy clarity, credit recognition, continuity, lower risk of losing years.  
**Primary fears:** “Will my credits transfer?”, “Will I lose time?”, “Will my prior institution cause issues?”  
**Win condition:** disciplined pre-assessment process and expectation-setting (what can/can’t be recognized) + fast document triage.

### Segment D — Premed/foundation + gap-year planners (“readiness-builders”)
**Who they are:** not ready for direct entry (bio/chem gaps, English confidence, or interview readiness).  
**What they optimize for:** a structured pathway, predictable next steps, coaching, confidence.  
**Primary fears:** failing entrance tests elsewhere; wasting a year; unclear options.  
**Win condition:** “pathway with checkpoints” + weekly cadence + clear go/no-go.

## 3) What drives decisions (what Bulgarians actually ask)
These are the recurring “family decision drivers” to address early:
- **Recognition/licensure**: “Can my child practise in Bulgaria/EU?” (route + timelines + documents, not promises).
- **Clinical training reality**: where/when hands-on starts; how rotations work; what language is needed in patient settings.
- **Admissions predictability**: requirements, interview process, timeline, and how decisions are made.
- **Total cost & risk**: full cost of attendance, payment schedule, what happens if they fail a year, and refund rules.
- **Safety & support**: housing, student support, pastoral care, and escalation points.
- **Mobility narrative**: what’s possible after graduation (EU rules, pathways, exams) framed as “depends on jurisdiction”.

## 4) Positioning pillars (with “proof points to prepare”)
Use pillars as a messaging architecture; attach verifiable proof points before scaling claims.

1) **Regulated-profession transparency (trust pillar)**
   - Proof points to prepare: a one-page “return-to-Bulgaria” explainer based on `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`, and a standard script that says “we can’t guarantee outcomes; we can explain the process”.

2) **Clinical training clarity (competence pillar)**
   - Proof points to prepare: rotation structure overview, clinical sites list *where publishable*, and a plain-language explanation of language requirements in clinical settings.

3) **English-medium learning + preparedness (confidence pillar)**
   - Proof points to prepare: official language-of-instruction statements, assessment style overview, and sample week schedule (high level).

4) **Student support + operational excellence (service pillar)**
   - Proof points to prepare: response SLAs, advisor assignment model, admissions timeline, and “who does what” contacts (no personal emails in repo unless explicitly allowed).

## 5) Qualification: what counts as a “qualified MD lead”
Treat a lead as “qualified” when you can answer (or schedule to confirm) all of the below:
- Academic readiness (bio/chem + overall profile) and graduation timeline.
- English readiness (test scores if available; otherwise a structured assessment plan).
- Budget range and decision-maker alignment (student + parent/guardian).
- Campus preference (Nicosia vs Athens) and start term.
- Willingness to follow a regulated-profession pathway (recognition/licensure may take months and depends on authorities).
- Document availability timeline (transcripts, IDs, etc.).

## 6) Objection handling (compliant defaults)
- **“Is it recognized in Bulgaria?”**  
  “Recognition and licensure depend on the Bulgarian competent authority and your intended profession. We can explain the usual routes and documents using `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`, but we can’t promise an outcome.”
- **“Will my child be able to practise as a doctor in Bulgaria?”**  
  “Medicine is a regulated profession. The pathway is a professional qualification recognition process; timelines and requirements vary by case.”
- **“Is everything in English in the hospital?”**  
  “Academic instruction may be in English, but patient interaction often involves the local language. We’ll confirm the exact requirements and help you plan language readiness.”

## 7) “What families ask” — FAQ outline (use in events + counseling)
Use these headings to structure webinars, counselor decks, and 1:1 advising.

### Fees & affordability
- Total cost of attendance (tuition, housing, living, insurance, travel) and payment schedule
- Scholarships/discounts (eligibility, timeline, and renewal rules)
- Refund and deferral rules (what triggers each)

### Admissions & timelines
- Requirements (academic, English), admissions timeline, and interview expectations
- How competitiveness is evaluated (no “guarantees” language)
- Deposit deadlines and what happens after deposit

### Recognition / licensure / returning to Bulgaria
- Academic recognition vs regulated-profession licensure (what’s the difference?)
- Which Bulgarian authorities are involved and typical timelines
- What documents to collect before graduation (Diploma Supplement, transcripts, clinical logs)

### Clinical training
- When clinical exposure begins (high-level) and how rotations are structured
- What documentation students receive for rotations/clinical hours (if applicable)
- Language expectations in clinical settings

### Student life & risk management
- Housing options and support model
- Safety, wellbeing, and escalation points
- Failure/retake policies (high-level) and academic support

### Pathways after graduation (jurisdiction-dependent)
- EU mobility basics (framed as “depends on country + competent authority”)
- Exam preparation resources (if offered) and how support works
