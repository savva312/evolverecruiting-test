# Computer Science, Data, AI, and Cyber cluster

## Bulgaria 2025 market pulse
The domestic software industry remains Bulgaria’s fastest-growing export service: revenues expanded by **11.5% in 2024**, with 6,100 software firms, 1,197 net new jobs, and a projected **BGN 11 billion** in volume for 2025 according to BASSCOM’s latest barometer (BTA, 9 Dec 2024). Even with that momentum, only **35.5% of Bulgarians hold basic digital skills** and ICT specialists represent 4.6% of the workforce, underscoring the headroom for tech education providers (Digital Skills & Jobs Coalition snapshot, 2024). Bulgaria also sustains one of the EU’s most gender-inclusive ICT labor markets, with women making up **27% of ICT specialists in 2024**—a differentiator for inclusive messaging (Eurostat, 2024).

### Quantified demand signals
1. **Growth & scale:** Software exports are still 87% of sector revenue and forecast to top BGN 11 billion in 2025, keeping employer demand ahead of local graduate supply (BASSCOM Barometer 2024).  
2. **Hiring velocity:** DEV.BG now aggregates **30,000+ tech professionals, 1,200+ hiring companies, 1,000+ events, and 200,000+ applications**, making it the highest-signal job board for CS, data, and cyber roles (DEV.BG All in One 2025 fact sheet).  
3. **Talent density:** ICT specialists now make up **4.6% of Bulgaria’s workforce**, up from 4.3% in 2023, signalling steady expansion of tech employment beyond Sofia (Digital Skills & Jobs Coalition snapshot, 2024).  
4. **Outbound mobility:** Integral Education reports **27% of newly enrolled Bulgarian students choose the Netherlands** (with CS among the most requested majors), followed by Austria (12%) and Spain (11%), showing how often tech talent compares continental options (Integral Education “Study Abroad 2024” report).  
5. **Diversity benchmark:** Bulgaria’s **27% female ICT share** (vs. EU’s 19.5%) enables purpose-built women-in-tech campaigns and scholarships (Eurostat Women in ICT release, Jun 2024).

### Talent supply & pipeline levers
- **STEM high schools and olympiad pipelines** (e.g., Sofia Math School, AUBG STEM magnet) continue to send top achievers abroad; they expect early research exposure plus EU-recognised accreditation aligned with the Greek Panhellenic requirements.  
- **SoftUni and private academies** drive volume reskilling: SoftUni alone counts **500k+ cumulative learners, 155k paying students, and 145+ partner companies** across software, AI, and digital tracks—ideal for bridge programs and stackable credits (SoftUni impact report, 2024).  
- **University–industry hackathons** (AmCham Hackathon 2025, HackAUBG, ETHSofia) place 60–200 participants per event into AI/cyber problem statements, giving UNIC ready-made sponsorship platforms (AmCham Bulgaria, HackAUBG 5.0, ETHSofia).  
- **Digital-skill gap**: With only 35.5% basic digital proficiency and national coalitions still scaling, there’s a policy push for programs that blend CS fundamentals with applied AI/cybersecurity safety (Digital Skills & Jobs Coalition snapshot, 2024).

## Persona-specific needs & UNIC positioning

### 1. STEM high-school graduates / first-degree seekers
- **Needs:** English-taught CS/AI programs with EU-recognised degrees, affordable tuition, structured internships, and clear visa-free travel for Erasmus+/Greek market immersion.  
- **UNIC Athens edge:** Opening Fall 2025 inside The Ellinikon with **12,500 m² of labs, 43 specialised facilities, and proximity to HHG’s clinical/tech partners** provides a “living lab” near Bulgaria (80-minute flight) that still feels like an EU metro hub (UNIC Athens campus reveal, 2024).  
- **UNIC Nicosia complement:** Established Computer Science, Data Science, and Blockchain pathways (world’s first MSc in Blockchain & Digital Currency, €300k annual scholarships) plus Mediterranean campus life appeal to families who prioritise innovation credentials and on-campus safety (UNIC programme announcements, 2024).
- **Message:** “Graduate with an EU-recognised CS degree that plugs you into Greece’s largest smart-city redevelopment while retaining Cyprus’s blockchain leadership.”

### 2. Transfer students, academy graduates, and upskillers
- **Needs:** Credit recognition from Bulgarian universities or SoftUni modules, hybrid schedules, micro-credentials in AI/ML/cloud, and accelerated pathways to EU jobs.  
- **UNIC offer:** Online/hybrid MScs (Blockchain, Computer Science with blockchain concentration) and Athens-based Applied Data labs let students stack credentials while relocating only for capstones or residency weeks (UNIC online programme catalogue, 2024; UNIC Athens lab overview, 2024).  
- **Differentiator vs. Bulgarian incumbents:** UNIC pairs academic credits with HHG internships and Ellinikon smart-city datasets—advantages local universities rarely match.

### 3. Working professionals & employer-sponsored learners
- **Needs:** Skills refresh to move from outsourcing roles to AI product ownership, flexible tuition, and employer-recognised certifications.  
- **UNIC response:** Corporate cohorts can tap UNIC Athens’ proximity to Sofia-based multinationals for weekend residencies, while Nicosia’s Institute for the Future delivers remote-first executive training in AI governance, metaverse strategy, and cyber resilience (UNIC Institute for the Future briefs, 2024).
