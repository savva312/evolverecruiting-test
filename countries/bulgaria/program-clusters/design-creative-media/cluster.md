# Design & Creative Media cluster (Bulgaria recruiting pack)

This cluster is for Bulgarian applicants who want **design-led careers** (graphic/digital design, UX/UI, motion + 3D, digital art, content/creative strategy) and are comparing **portfolio-heavy** programmes across Bulgaria and Europe.

**Related files**
- Competitors: `./competitors.md`
- Bulgaria outreach playbook: `./bulgaria-playbook.md`

## What “Design & Creative Media” includes (in this repo)
- **Graphic + digital design:** branding, typography, layout, editorial, social-first design systems.
- **Product/UX/UI:** interaction design, prototyping, user research, design systems, accessibility.
- **Motion + 3D:** motion graphics, 3D modelling, rendering, basic animation pipelines.
- **Digital art:** illustration, concept art, creative coding (where relevant), portfolio practice.
- **Creative media practice:** content design and storytelling for digital channels (not “film school” unless the student is explicitly film-first).

### UNIC Athens (current reality check)
As of the current Athens programme catalogue in this repo (`UNIC/unicathens/programs.md`), **UNIC Athens does not list a design/creative media degree**. For “Athens-only” families:
- Qualify whether “Athens” is a hard constraint or a preference.
- If hard: redirect to **adjacent Athens programmes** (e.g., Computer Science for VR/game dev tracks, Business/Marketing for creative strategy) and be explicit that it is not a design degree.
- If preference: position **Nicosia** as the design campus and frame Athens as an optional future pathway (transfer/exchange/short visits) only if/until officially available.

## Bulgaria recruiting reality: who we win (segments) and why
Build campaigns and outreach around segments that are **portfolio-led** and **deadline-sensitive**.

### Segment 1: Art high schools and applied arts tracks (UG, “portfolio-first”)
**Who:** Students in specialised art/gymnasium tracks (Sofia + major cities) with strong drawing/design foundations.  
**Decision drivers:** portfolio feedback quality, studio culture, English delivery, affordability vs Western Europe, degree recognition, “can I get in without a risky one-shot exam?”  
**UNIC angle:** structured portfolio coaching + clear admissions checklist + fast decisions.

### Segment 2: NATFA/NAA “near-miss” applicants (UG, creative media alternative)
**Who:** Applicants targeting Bulgarian academies (fine arts / theatre & film) who often face high competition and exam risk.  
**Decision drivers:** credible alternative that still respects craft, portfolio acceptance, community, clear progression.  
**UNIC angle:** “keep building, don’t lose a year” — portfolio-based entry and a safer admissions path while staying in a creative ecosystem.

### Segment 3: Private academies & portfolio prep ecosystems (UG + bridge)
**Who:** Learners coming from paid portfolio preparation, game art/3D tracks, or creative short courses (common in Sofia).  
**Decision drivers:** turning skills into an accredited degree, credit/recognition conversations, quality of peer group, equipment/software access.  
**UNIC angle:** portfolio mapping to modules + clear “what you’ll build each semester” portfolio outcomes.

### Segment 4: Career switchers into UX/UI (PG or second-degree)
**Who:** Bulgarian professionals switching from marketing/content/IT support into design roles.  
**Decision drivers:** employable portfolio, pace/flexibility, industry critique, realistic outcomes, ability to keep working.  
**UNIC angle:** structured projects + critique cadence + clear portfolio requirements (avoid “learn UX in 4 weeks” promises).

### Segment 5: EU-bound applicants comparing Italy/Netherlands/UK (UG, “brand + value”)
**Who:** Students aiming abroad for reputation and creative networks.  
**Decision drivers:** cost vs outcomes, teaching language, mobility, campus/city preference, perceived brand, alumni visibility.  
**UNIC angle:** EU-campus experience + lower total cost than many Western options + tight portfolio coaching and student-support services.

## Objections you will hear (and how to answer)
- **“I need a famous art-school brand.”** Acknowledge, then pivot to: portfolio outcomes, studio culture, mentor access, and what the student will *actually produce* in year 1–2.
- **“Design doesn’t have jobs.”** Avoid guarantees; use a skills framing: design + digital tools + business/tech collaboration expands role options (product design, content design, motion, junior designer roles, etc.).
- **“I’m not ready with a portfolio.”** Convert with a portfolio pathway: 4–6 week plan + review slots + submission checklist.
- **“Cyprus feels far.”** Reframe with practicalities: short flights from Bulgaria, EU context, safety, and a focused campus environment (no visa claims; confirm per citizenship).

## What we need from admissions to sell this cluster well (asset checklist)
- Portfolio rubric (UG + PG): categories, minimum pieces, acceptable formats, “red flags”.
- 10–15 student work examples (permissioned): “what good looks like”.
- 2 live session formats:
  - 60-minute “Portfolio teardown + Q&A” (monthly, online)
  - 30-minute 1:1 portfolio review slots (2x per month)
- One “Studio + student life” visual pack (short clips, lab photos, student testimonials).

## KPIs (Bulgaria)
- Leads → portfolio-review booking rate (target: build this as the core conversion lever).
- Portfolio-review → application conversion rate.
- Application → offer rate (track portfolio readiness as a leading indicator).
- Offer → deposit/commitment rate (yield), segmented by: art high school vs career switcher vs EU-bound.
