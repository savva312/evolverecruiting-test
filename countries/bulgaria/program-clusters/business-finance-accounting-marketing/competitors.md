# Business, Finance, Accounting, and Marketing competitors

Use this page to brief reps, counselors, and agents on what Bulgarian students hear from the most visible business/finance providers. Every entry includes tuition (EU students), delivery language, intake cadence, notable partnerships or employer outcomes, and a linkable source for verification.

## American University in Bulgaria (AUBG) — Blagoevgrad
- **Tuition & aid:** €6,700 per semester plus fees for 2024/25 undergraduate study; AUBG states that 93% of students receive institutional scholarships, so families expect aggressive discounting even at a €13,400 sticker.  
- **Language & delivery:** Entire portfolio is English-taught and culminates in a dual U.S. & EU accredited diploma, mirroring the UNIC promise on recognition.  
- **Intake cadence:** Runs true bi-annual intakes with orientation in late August (Fall) and late January (Spring), so Bulgarian students comparing UNIC offers will be reminded of year-round start flexibility.  
- **Partnerships/outcomes:** The university markets 20+ years of collaboration with Postbank (Eurobank Bulgaria) for internships, placements, and scholarships—a signal that local finance employers already have embedded AUBG pipelines.  
- **Implication:** Lead with UNIC’s differentiated program mix (fintech, blockchain, Medicine pathways) and cost-of-attendance transparency; match AUBG’s scholarship narrative only where academically justified.

## Sofia University “St. Kliment Ohridski” — Faculty of Economics & Business Administration (FEBA)
- **Tuition & fees:** The official 2025/26 fee table lists Business Administration at **BGN 2,627 (~€1,344) per year** for EU students, and the English-language “International Trade & Investment” program at the same rate—ultra-low versus private options.  
- **Language & delivery:** FEBA operates Bulgarian-language and English-language tracks (International Trade & Investment, Business Administration) under the same faculty, so counselors pitch it as the most affordable English bachelor in Sofia.  
- **Intake cadence:** Primary admission round closes in August with occasional extra windows (e.g., Aug 23–Sep 13, 2025) when seats remain, reinforcing a single major intake per academic year.  
- **Partnerships/outcomes:** FEBA is the only Bulgarian business school in the Global Business School Network (GBSN) and partners with Euro Games Technology on dedicated labs, highlighting employer-backed facilities.  
- **Implication:** UNIC must offset the fee gap with proof of English-medium specialization, internships, and dual-campus mobility; anchor messaging in ACCA exemptions and the Athens proximity story.

## University of National and World Economy (UNWE) — Sofia
- **Tuition & fees:** EU/EEA students pay just **BGN 350 (~€179) per semester**, while non-EU English-track students pay **€3,800 per year**, creating a perception of elite training at state-subsidized prices.  
- **Language & delivery:** Offers multiple English-taught bachelor programs (International Economic Relations, International Business, Business Economics) alongside Bulgarian-language majors.  
- **Intake cadence:** Annual admission campaigns run May–July with published schedules (first stage, appeals, enrolment) and top-up rounds through late summer; students view UNWE as a once-per-year decision anchored to national exam timelines.  
- **Partnerships/outcomes:** Co-founder of the ENGAGE.EU alliance and hosts themed summer schools on the future of work and digital transformation, featuring multinational corporate partners—evidence of strong employer integration.  
- **Implication:** Compete by emphasizing UNIC’s smaller cohorts, English-first environment, and applied international internships; use scholarship grids plus agent SLAs to neutralize the headline price gap.

## New Bulgarian University (NBU) — Sofia
- **Tuition & fees:** Official pricing sets **BGN 2,300 (~€1,178) per semester for Bulgarian/EU students** and €1,500 per semester for other European Economic Area students, payable in two instalments, so parents benchmark a €2,356 annual spend.  
- **Language & delivery:** NBU mixes Bulgarian- and English-taught programs; business majors frequently advertise English tracks, and the campus positions itself as Bulgaria’s first liberal-arts-style private university.  
- **Intake cadence:** Fees are invoiced per semester with mandatory payments before each term, reinforcing the standard October/February academic rhythm.  
- **Partnerships/outcomes:** Strategic alliance with The Open University Business School (UK) since 2004 delivers joint training and executive programs, adding UK branding to their business degrees.  
- **Implication:** Stress UNIC’s higher accreditation stack (ACCA/CIMA exemptions) and the two-campus mobility story; package articulation pathways for NBU alumni who want English-language master’s degrees abroad.

## Varna University of Management (VUM)
- **Tuition & fees:** Charges **€1,500 per semester for EU/EEA students and €1,950 for non-EU/EEA** across English-taught bachelor programmes, plus €100 application and €450 status fees—families benchmark €3,000–€3,900 per year before scholarships.  
- **Language & delivery:** Business, hospitality, and culinary programmes are taught entirely in English with synchronized curricula that lead to VUM + UK degrees.  
- **Intake cadence:** Admissions content highlights October and February start dates (plus September campaigns), so agents pitch VUM as a multi-intake option similar to UNIC.  
- **Partnerships/outcomes:** Strategic alliance with Cardiff Metropolitan University and British Accreditation Council approval let students complete dual diplomas in Bulgaria while leveraging Erasmus+ placements across the UK and EU.  
- **Implication:** Position UNIC’s differentiators (larger programme catalogue, Cyprus/Greece employer networks, ACCA/CIMA status) while acknowledging VUM’s double-degree and Erasmus funding narrative so counselors can contrast recognition pathways.

## VUZF University (University of Finance, Business and Entrepreneurship) — Sofia
- **Tuition & fees:** English-language bachelor programs list **€1,950 per semester**, with Bulgarian-language options billed at BGN 2,350 (~€1,205) per semester; fees are due before each term, underscoring a two-semester cadence.  
- **Language & delivery:** Markets bilingual (Bulgarian + English) delivery for finance, insurance, and business information programs, catering to students who want local instruction with international terminology.  
- **Intake cadence:** Operates fall and spring admission windows aligned to the semester payments, commonly opening early-summer for October entry and winter for February entry.  
- **Partnerships/outcomes:** Publishes a corporate partnership roster spanning Allianz, AIG, PwC, Coca-Cola HBC, Experian, and Bulgaria’s Association of Certified Fraud Examiners, with co-branded scholarships and internships.  
- **Implication:** UNIC must counter the “stay-in-Sofia” affordability narrative by proving higher international mobility, ACCA/ACFE alignment, and dual-campus networking advantages while offering selective scholarship tiers for math/finance magnets.
