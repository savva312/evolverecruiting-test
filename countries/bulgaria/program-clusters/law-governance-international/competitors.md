# Bulgaria — Law, Governance & International competitors

Last updated: 2025-12-22  
Sources last checked: 2025-12-22

This list prioritizes programs Bulgarian applicants commonly consider alongside UNIC Nicosia / UNIC Athens for:
- Law (LLB/LLM intent)
- International Studies / Political Science / Public Policy / Governance

Notes for Bulgarian conversion:
- For “practice law in Bulgaria” intent, Bulgarian universities often look attractive because families assume **lower friction** for domestic practice. Our job is to qualify intent and avoid overpromising.
- For English-taught international careers, common competitor gravity is the **Netherlands** (English-taught bachelor options) plus strong local options like AUBG.
- Tuition changes frequently; refresh each cycle and keep “last verified” dates.

## Competitor entries (sourced)

### Sofia University “St. Kliment Ohridski” — Faculty of Law (Bulgaria)
- **Primary fit:** Bulgarian-law pathway; strong brand for domestic legal careers.
- **Language:** Primarily Bulgarian.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Prestige + domestic pathway; large alumni network.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Lower cost and local system fit; less English-medium international positioning vs an EU-based international pathway.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.uni-sofia.bg/index.php/bul/universitet_t/fakulteti/yuridicheski_fakultet

### University of National and World Economy (UNWE) — Faculty of Law (Bulgaria)
- **Primary fit:** Business-law-adjacent positioning; attractive for corporate/legal/compliance intent inside Bulgaria.
- **Language:** Primarily Bulgarian (English-taught options vary by cycle).
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Strong “business + law” ecosystem; Sofia location.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Very familiar local market brand; less international mobility storytelling than a Cyprus/Greece pathway (depends on track).
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://faculties.unwe.bg/law/en, https://www.unwe.bg/international/en/pages/3656/fees.html

### South-West University “Neofit Rilski” — Faculty of Law and History (Bulgaria)
- **Primary fit:** Bulgarian-law and public-sector adjacent careers; regional draw (Blagoevgrad).
- **Language:** Primarily Bulgarian.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Cost/value and location; pragmatic domestic option.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Local system alignment; less international exposure by default (depends on student’s own activities).
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.swu.bg/en/facultiesen/pifen

### Varna Free University “Chernorizets Hrabar” — Faculty of Law (Bulgaria)
- **Primary fit:** Private Bulgarian option; law + security/administration adjacency.
- **Language:** Bulgarian and/or track-dependent options.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Private-university flexibility; Varna region.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Convenient local option; international mobility positioning varies.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.vfu.bg/faculties/faculty-of-law-2.html, https://www.vfu.bg/students/tuition-fees-non-eu-citizens-2.html

### Burgas Free University — Law (Bulgaria)
- **Primary fit:** Private Bulgarian option; regional (Burgas) convenience.
- **Language:** Track-dependent.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Locality + private-institution structure.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Lower relocation friction; international brand/mobility story is typically weaker than a Cyprus/Greece pathway.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.bfu.bg/en/law

### American University in Bulgaria (AUBG) — Political Science & International Relations (Bulgaria)
- **Primary fit:** International careers (policy/IR/NGO/public affairs) with English-medium US-style liberal arts.
- **Language:** English.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** English-medium degree without leaving Bulgaria; strong international cohort + outcomes.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Very strong local “international” brand; can be more expensive than local publics and not a “law degree” if families want LLB specifically.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.aubg.edu/academics/bachelor-degrees/political-science-and-international-relations/, https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/tuition-and-fees/

### Maastricht University — European Law School (Netherlands)
- **Primary fit:** English-taught EU law foundation; international student pipelines.
- **Language:** English.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Netherlands brand + English delivery + EU career narrative.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Strong international signalling; higher living costs and longer distance vs Cyprus/Greece proximity.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.maastrichtuniversity.nl/education/bachelor/bachelor-european-law-school, https://www.maastrichtuniversity.nl/education/bachelor/programmes/european-law-school/tuition-fees

### University of Groningen — International and European Law (LLB) (Netherlands)
- **Primary fit:** English-taught international/EU law bachelor; strong pipeline to EU/international careers and further study.
- **Language:** English.
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Clear English-taught LLB; strong international cohort.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Strong Dutch brand; higher living costs and “farther from home” for some families.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.rug.nl/bachelors/international-and-european-law/, https://www.rug.nl/education/application-enrolment-tuition-fees/tuition-fee/

### Leiden University — International Studies (Netherlands)
- **Primary fit:** International careers (IR/policy/diplomacy/NGO); strong area studies and language tracks.
- **Language:** English (programme-specific structure applies).
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Top-tier international profile for IR/policy pathways.
- **Strengths/risks vs UNIC:** Strong signalling for IR; can be competitive and cost-heavy vs Cyprus/Greece proximity.
- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
- **Sources:** https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/en/education/study-programmes/bachelor/international-studies, https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/en/education/study-programmes/bachelor/international-studies/admission-and-application/tuition-fees

- **Primary fit:** Political science / governance pathways; strong international city context.
- **Language:** English (programme-specific structure applies).
- **Why Bulgarians choose it:** Amsterdam brand + international career story.

- **Last verified:** 2025-12-22
