# Bulgaria playbook — Law, Governance & International (UNIC Nicosia / UNIC Athens)

Last updated: 2025-12-22

This playbook is built for Bulgarian prospects who:
- want **law** but may be unclear on licensure realities, and/or
- want **international relations / policy / NGO / governance** careers.

Guardrail: Bulgaria “law practice” questions must be handled with compliance-safe language. Use:
- `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md` (law section) as the internal reference.

## Messaging pillars (what wins in Bulgaria)

1) **Pathway clarity (trust-builder)**
- “We will help you choose a pathway that matches *Bulgaria’s rules* and your career intent.”
- “A degree is not the same as a licence; regulated professions have competent authorities.”

2) **Law-adjacent outcomes (conversion driver)**
Position outcomes that do not require Bulgarian bar admission:
- compliance / AML / governance
- legal operations / contracts / corporate roles
- policy / EU project management / public affairs
- NGO programme roles / advocacy (non-advokat roles)

3) **International exposure with EU proximity**
- Cyprus/Greece are geographically close and culturally familiar, while still offering international contexts.
- Emphasize student support + practical transition (housing, onboarding, community).

4) **Language-fit routing**
- English-taught options are a major driver for Bulgaria.
- Athens opportunities are strongest for **Greek-speaking** students or those intentionally targeting Greece-linked outcomes.

## Channel plan (what to do, not just where to be)

Execution: sponsor 1–2 prize-driven events/semester (essay contest, policy memo competition) with scholarship hooks.

Agent enablement must include the objection-handling script below to prevent overpromising.

Always run two ad tracks separately:

- **Track B (International outcomes):** “Careers in EU policy / compliance / international relations” → webinar registration.

## Objection handling scripts (copy/paste ready)

### Objection: “Can I practice law in Bulgaria after UNIC?”
**Recommended script:**
1) “It depends on the **competent authority** in Bulgaria and your specific file. In Bulgaria, ‘Lawyer’ is a **regulated profession**.”
2) “A law degree is an important foundation, but it’s not automatically a licence to practice in Bulgaria.”
3) “What we *can* do is explain the process at a high level and point you to the official sources. What we *can’t* do is promise eligibility or timelines for your individual case.”
4) “If your non-negotiable goal is to practice in Bulgarian courts as soon as possible, we should compare the **lowest-friction** routes honestly — including Bulgarian universities.”
5) “If you’re open to international outcomes (EU/policy/compliance) and keeping Bulgarian practice as a longer-term option, we can explore which UNIC pathway fits best.”

**Always share official sources (do not paraphrase rules as guarantees):**
- NACID regulated professions database (Lawyer): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/?prf=2
- Supreme Bar Council (VAS): https://www.vas.bg/bg

### Objection: “Is the degree recognized in Bulgaria?”
**Safe answer:**
- “For many non-regulated roles, recognition is mostly an employer/university decision. For regulated professions, you follow the competent authority pathway.”
- Then route to the Bulgaria recognition brief for the correct authority links:
  - `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`

### Objection: “Why not just study law in Bulgaria?”
Use a “fit-first” frame:
- If their goal is Bulgarian advocacy/courts: Bulgarian schools can be **simpler for licensure**.
- If their goal is international careers + English-medium education + broader mobility: UNIC can be a stronger fit.

### Objection: “Do I need Greek for Athens?”
Answer directly:
- “Some Athens programmes (including Law) are taught in **Greek**. If you’re not comfortable studying in Greek, we should route you to an English-taught pathway.”

## Qualification checklist (so we don’t waste cycles)
Require answers to:
- Target country for work/practice (Bulgaria vs EU/global)
- “Bar-admission intent” (yes/no/maybe)
- Working languages (Bulgarian/English/Greek)
- Budget sensitivity (low/medium/high) + scholarship dependency
- Timeline (intake) + readiness to move

## Offerholder/yield plays (what to do after admit)
1) **1:1 licensure expectations call (15 minutes):** confirm career intent; document it.
2) **Parent call (Bulgarian/English):** “Here’s what we can promise / can’t promise” (reduces refunds).
3) **Peer proof:** alumni/student ambassador session focused on careers in compliance/policy/NGOs (not bar claims).

## Escalation rule (protect the brand)
If a prospect requests a definitive “yes/no” on Bulgarian bar eligibility:
- pause the sales conversation,
- share official links above,
- offer to connect them to admissions + an internal compliance/academic advisor for a general (non-personalized) explanation,
- do not provide legal advice.
