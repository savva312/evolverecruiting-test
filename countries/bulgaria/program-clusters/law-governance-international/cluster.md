# Bulgaria — Law, Governance & International cluster (UNIC Nicosia / UNIC Athens)

Last updated: 2025-12-22

This cluster is a **recruiting pack** for Bulgarian prospects interested in:
- **Law (LLB/LLM intent)**, including students who think they want to “practice law” after graduation.
- **International careers**: EU/international organizations, NGOs, policy, diplomacy, security, compliance.
- **Governance/public administration** (often “law-adjacent” careers).

Primary goal: **convert interest without mis-selling licensure**. In Bulgaria, “I want to be a lawyer” is frequently a *regulated-profession* pathway with **high friction**.

Internal reference for compliance-safe guidance:
- `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md` (law section + sources)

## Core reality check (Bulgaria): practicing law is a high-friction pathway

Use this language consistently:
- “A law degree is not the same as a licence to practice.”
- “In Bulgaria, ‘Lawyer’ is a regulated profession and the pathway depends on the competent authority’s requirements.”
- “We can explain the *process at a high level*, but we can’t confirm eligibility or timelines for your individual case.”

Always direct “practice in Bulgaria” prospects to official sources:
- NACID regulated professions database (Lawyer): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/?prf=2
- Supreme Bar Council (VAS): https://www.vas.bg/bg

## Bulgaria segment map (who we target + how we route)

### Segment A — “I want to practice in Bulgaria (advokat)”
**What usually drives it:** family influence, prestige, “safe profession,” and the belief that any EU law degree = Bulgarian practice.

**Positioning (safe):**
- UNIC can be a strong foundation for **legal thinking + EU/international exposure**, but Bulgarian practice is a **separate** regulated pathway.
- If the student’s end-goal is *Bulgarian court practice* and they want the “lowest-friction” route, Bulgarian universities often look attractive. Our role is to **qualify intent early** and avoid mismatch.

**Best-fit student profile:**
- Open to a broader set of outcomes: compliance, legal ops, policy, NGOs, corporate roles.
- Comfortable with “extra steps / extra time” if they still want Bulgarian practice later.

### Segment B — “EU/international career (not focused on Bulgarian courts)”
**Examples:** compliance/AML, privacy, corporate governance, EU institutions, human rights, public policy, diplomacy, security, think tanks.

**Positioning:**
- Emphasize EU context + international networks + transferable skills (research, argumentation, writing, analytical rigor).
- Make outcomes concrete: “corporate compliance analyst,” “policy analyst,” “NGO programme officer,” “public affairs associate,” “EU project coordinator,” “risk & governance.”

### Segment C — “International Relations / diplomacy / NGO impact”
**What to highlight:**
- Applied pathways: internships, simulations (Model UN/debate), policy writing portfolio, capstone projects.
- Clear “first job” outcomes and optional Master’s pathways.

### Segment D — “Greek-speaking student / Greece pathway”
UNIC Athens is relevant when:
- The student has **Greek language strength** (or family/education background), and
- They are intentionally pursuing Greece-linked outcomes (study environment, networks, possible Greek-regulated pathways).

Important: do not imply Greek-law licensure equals Bulgarian licensure; they are different competent-authority systems.

## First-call qualification questions (non-negotiable)
Ask these in the first conversation (or first email exchange):
1) “Where do you want to *work* after graduation (Bulgaria, Cyprus, Greece, elsewhere in EU)?”
2) “Do you want to *practice as a licensed lawyer* (court/advocacy), or are you open to compliance/policy/NGO careers?”
3) “Which languages can you study and work in (Bulgarian/English/Greek)?”
4) “What is your timeline and budget (and is family optimizing for ‘lowest sticker price’ or ‘best outcomes’?)”

## Differentiation messages that work in Bulgaria (test + measure)
- **Clarity, not hype:** “We’ll help you choose a pathway that matches Bulgaria’s rules and your career goal.”
- **Law-adjacent outcomes:** show concrete employer roles for governance/compliance/policy.
- **EU proximity + support:** practical student support + mobility story (without promising licensure).
