# Bulgaria — recognition & licensure (UNIC Nicosia / UNIC Athens)

Last updated: 2025-12-22  
Sources last checked: 2025-12-22

This brief is a **practical guide** for Bulgarian students (and counselors/recruiters) on how degrees from:
- **UNIC Nicosia** (Cyprus, EU), and
- **UNIC Athens** (Greece, EU),
are typically **recognized in Bulgaria**, and how **regulated-profession** pathways (licensure) work.

If your intended job in Bulgaria is regulated (medicine, nursing, pharmacy, law, etc.), do not rely on “degree recognition” alone: you must follow the **professional qualification** route with the **competent authority** for that profession.

## Quick decision guide (what do you need?)

1) **Private-sector job in Bulgaria (non-regulated role)**  
Often **no formal recognition** is legally required; the employer decides. If the employer asks for an official recognition certificate, use the **NACID academic recognition** route. (See “Academic recognition”.)  
Source note: NACID explicitly states that for **non-regulated professions**, the qualification is recognized by the **employer**. (NACID FAQ, checked 2025-12-22: https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/frequently-asked-questions/)

2) **Further study in Bulgaria (Master/PhD, transfer, admissions)**  
The **Bulgarian receiving university** is typically the competent authority for recognition for **access to further education**; however, an academic recognition certificate from NACID is commonly accepted as supporting evidence.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://www.euraxess.bg/bulgaria/information-assistance/diploma-recognition

3) **Regulated profession (e.g., doctor, nurse, pharmacist, lawyer)**  
You need **recognition of professional qualification** by the Bulgarian **competent authority** for that profession (often a ministry + professional chamber). EU rules apply (Directive 2005/36/EC). See “Regulated professions / licensure”.

## 2) First: determine whether your degree is Cyprus-awarded or Greece-awarded

Bulgaria’s procedures depend on the **awarding institution and awarding country shown on your documents** (diploma + diploma supplement), not just where you physically studied.

Practical check:
- If your diploma is awarded by the **University of Nicosia** (even if you studied in Athens), treat it as a **Cyprus (EU)** qualification.
- If your diploma is awarded by a **Greek** institution (UNIC Athens), treat it as a **Greece (EU)** qualification.

This matters most for **professional qualification** cases (health fields), where you may be asked for a **Certificate of Conformity** / proof your training meets EU minimum requirements — issued by the **competent authority in the awarding country**.

## 3) Academic recognition in Bulgaria (NACID) — step-by-step (Bachelor/Master)

### When you should do it
Typical triggers:
- Bulgarian employer (especially public sector) requests official recognition of your degree.
- You need a formal equivalence certificate for a regulated-profession file.
- A Bulgarian university asks for a formal recognition decision as part of admissions.

### Fee and timeline (important practical points)
- **Fee:** NACID states the procedure is **free of charge** for Bachelor/Master recognition. (Checked 2025-12-22: https://nacid.bg/en/foreign_qualifications_in_bulgaria_b_m/bm_page_faq/)  
- **Timeline:** statutory term **2 months**; “in practice” often about **1 month** if no additional data is required. (Checked 2025-12-22: https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-diplomas-in-bulgaria-bachelor-master/bm_page_time/)

### Documents (core pack)
Use NACID’s checklist as the source of truth; at minimum expect:
- Application form (NACID template; checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/tl_files/nacid/DocNBO/Prizn_VO_2017.pdf
- Diploma (copy) + required translation(s)
- Diploma Supplement / transcript (copy) + required translation(s)
- Identity document (copy)

Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-diplomas-in-bulgaria-bachelor-master/bm_page_documents/

### Legalisation / apostille (Cyprus & Greece cases)
For diplomas issued in **EU/EEA/Switzerland**, Bulgaria’s eGovernment guidance states **no legalisation/certification is required** — and explicitly lists **Cyprus** and **Greece** in the “no legalisation” group.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://egov.bg/wps/portal/egov/dostavchitsi+na+uslugi/ministerstva/ministerstvo+na+obrazovanieto+i+naukata/480

### Translation (what “sworn translation” means in practice)
For documents that are not in Bulgarian, eGovernment guidance indicates:
- The diploma and the diploma supplement should be translated into Bulgarian by a translator/company **contracted with the Bulgarian MFA**, and then certified.  
- eGovernment guidance also notes that the **European Diploma Supplement** does not require certification/translation; if your supplement is not the standard EU format (or an employer requests Bulgarian anyway), confirm with NACID what they will accept in your case.  
- The **MFA publishes the list** of contracted translators.  
Sources (checked 2025-12-22):  
- https://egov.bg/wps/portal/egov/dostavchitsi+na+uslugi/ministerstva/ministerstvo+na+obrazovanieto+i+naukata/480  
- https://www.mfa.bg/en/services/administrative-services/list-translators  

### Recommended “return-to-Bulgaria” workflow (UNIC Nicosia vs UNIC Athens)

**A) UNIC Nicosia (Cyprus-awarded degree)**  
1. Confirm your diploma is **Cyprus-awarded** (award section on diploma + Diploma Supplement).  
2. If you need academic recognition: prepare the NACID document pack + translations (no legalisation needed for Cyprus/EU diplomas) and submit to NACID.  
3. If you target a regulated profession: start the professional recognition route with the Bulgarian competent authority (often requires awarding-country certificates such as conformity/right-to-practise) and plan for Bulgarian-language requirements.

**B) UNIC Athens (Greece-awarded degree)**  
1. Confirm your diploma is **Greece-awarded** (award section on diploma + Diploma Supplement).  
2. If you need academic recognition: prepare the NACID document pack + translations (no legalisation needed for Greece/EU diplomas) and submit to NACID.  
3. If you target a regulated profession: start the professional recognition route with the Bulgarian competent authority; you may be asked for awarding-country certificates issued by Greek authorities (conformity/right-to-practise) and you must plan for Bulgarian-language requirements.

### How to submit (practical)
NACID lists multiple submission routes, including:
- Electronic submission (portal) when you have a qualified e-signature, and
- In-person / via an authorized person / by post/courier.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign_qualifications_in_bulgaria_b_m/bm_page_faq/

Practical portal starting points:
- e-services portal: https://portal.nacid.bg
- public registers (for verification/precedents): https://portal.nacid.bg/registry

## 4) Regulated professions / licensure (Bulgaria) — how it works

### What changes when a profession is regulated
If a profession is regulated, you need **professional rights** in Bulgaria (licence/registration), not just an academic degree.

NACID summarises the EU-rule timelines as:
- **Automatic recognition:** decision within **3 months** after all required documents are submitted
- **General system:** decision within **4 months** after all required documents are submitted  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/vocational-recognition-procedure/

### Typical document set (EU professional recognition)
Expect variations by profession, but NACID’s generic list commonly includes:
- Application
- Passport/ID and nationality evidence
- Diploma/certificate(s) for professional qualification
- Certificate(s) from the awarding country about rights to practise (e.g., good standing / no disciplinary sanctions), where applicable
- Translations and (if required) legalisation per authority instructions  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/vocational-recognition-procedure/

### Fees (professional recognition)
Professional recognition procedures can include an **administrative fee** (often described as a stamp duty) depending on the competent authority and profession; NACID’s generic document set includes proof of payment “when required.”  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/vocational-recognition-procedure/

### Bulgarian language (don’t miss this)
NACID notes that for regulated professions, applicants need to demonstrate **knowledge of Bulgarian** (the competent authority defines how).  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/frequently-asked-questions/

### “Is my target job regulated?”
Use NACID’s “Regulated professions in Bulgaria” list as the quickest check:
- https://nacid.bg/en/professio/ (checked 2025-12-22)

## 5) Field notes (UNIC-relevant regulated areas) — documents and steps

Below are **Bulgaria-focused** notes for common UNIC/UNIC Athens program areas. Always confirm the latest requirements with the competent authority before you plan internships or graduation timelines.

### Medicine (Doctor / MD)

**Bulgaria competent authority (per NACID regulated professions list):** Ministry of Health.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/?prf=1

**Recognition route:** EU “sectoral” health profession → often falls under **automatic recognition** if the qualification meets EU minimum training requirements (Directive 2005/36/EC). Use the 3‑month timeline as the planning baseline once the file is complete.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/vocational-recognition-procedure/

**Prepare early (before graduation):**
- Diploma + official transcript + Diploma Supplement
- Clinical placement documentation (rotations, hours) in case the competent authority asks for it
- Ask the awarding-country authority about a **Certificate of Conformity** and any “good standing” style documents if required in your case

**After recognition (practice readiness):**
- Practising doctors must typically be entered in the relevant professional register; the Bulgarian Medical Association notes that doctors practise if they meet Health Act requirements and are entered in the association’s register.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://blsbg.eu/en

### Nursing (Nurse)

**Bulgaria competent authority (per NACID regulated professions list):** Ministry of Health.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/?prf=47

**Recognition route:** commonly treated as an EU sectoral profession; plan for the **automatic recognition** timeline (3 months after a complete file) unless you are told otherwise.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/vocational-recognition-procedure/

**Prepare early:**
- Diploma + Diploma Supplement (course + clinical components)
- Evidence of clinical practice hours and sites
- Any awarding-country certificates proving conformity / right to practise

### Pharmacy (Pharmacist / MPharm)

**Bulgaria competent authority (per NACID regulated professions list):** Ministry of Health.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/?prf=70

**Recognition route:** EU sectoral profession → often **automatic recognition** if requirements are met.  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/foreign-qualifications-in-bulgaria/vocational-recognition-procedure/

**Prepare early:**
- Diploma + Diploma Supplement + transcript
- Evidence of traineeships/placements (hours, sites)
- If requested: awarding-country certificates proving EU conformity / right to practise

### Law (Lawyer / “advokat”)

**Bulgaria competent authority (per NACID regulated professions list):** Supreme Bar Council (Vissh Advokatski Savet).  
Source (checked 2025-12-22): https://nacid.bg/en/professio/?prf=2

**Reality check (important):**
- “Recognition” is usually not the whole pathway in law: admission to practise in Bulgaria is governed by bar rules and national law, and may involve additional requirements beyond a foreign law degree.
- Treat law as a **high-friction** pathway: advise candidates to contact the Supreme Bar Council early and plan for longer lead times.

## 6) Common questions (and what we can/can’t confirm yet)

### Psychology / counselling
As of the NACID “regulated professions” listing checked on 2025-12-22, **“psychologist” does not appear as a regulated profession entry**. That suggests many psychology/counselling roles may be handled as **non-regulated employment** (employer requirements + ethics standards), but some roles (healthcare, schools, courts) may have additional, role-specific requirements.

Action: confirm with the specific employer/sector (hospital, school, private clinic) what proof is required, and whether a professional qualification recognition procedure applies in your case.

### Teaching
“Teacher” is not shown as a generic regulated profession in NACID’s list (checked 2025-12-22). However, teaching jobs in Bulgaria (especially in the public system) can have strict **employer/MoES rules** (degree level, pedagogy modules, Bulgarian language, clean record, etc.). Plan for academic recognition + employer requirements unless a sector-specific regulated-profession route is identified.

## 7) Recruiter/counselor playbook (reduce student friction)

1) **Set expectations early**
- For most business/ICT degrees: “recognition” is mainly a paperwork step and often optional for private jobs.
- For medicine/nursing/pharmacy/law: “recognition” is a **process**, not a checkbox — plan months, not weeks.

2) **Build a “return-to-Bulgaria dossier” before graduation**
- Diploma Supplement (preferably the EU/European DS where available)
- Transcript + course descriptions/syllabi for borderline cases
- Clinical placement logs for health degrees
- Contact details for the faculty administration (for later verification requests)

3) **Use official channels**
- Academic recognition: NACID (https://nacid.bg; https://portal.nacid.bg)
- Professional recognition: competent authority for the target profession (start from https://nacid.bg/en/professio/)
- Translations: MFA list of contracted translators (https://www.mfa.bg/en/services/administrative-services/list-translators)
