## Greece and Cyprus focus (implications for UNIC/UNIC Athens)
- **UNIC Athens:** Bulgaria is already a Top 10 source for Greece within the Eurostat set; Athens can position Bulgaria as a priority feeder market (proximity, EU context, cost) with English-taught “hero” offers.
- **UNIC Cyprus:** Present but smaller (rank #16); growth depends on clear differentiation versus nearby options (admissions flexibility, English portfolio, student experience, career pathways) rather than default regional pull.
- **Competitive reality:** Germany and Türkiye dominance signals competition with established pathways; Bulgaria-facing messaging must stress program-level differentiation and outcomes.

## Data reliability and gaps
- Totals are **Eurostat reporting-set totals**, not guaranteed global outbound counts; several hosts show missing values.
- **Exclude aggregates** (EU27_2020, EU28) and host BG from sums to avoid double counting; aggregates are useful only as separate EU-host indicators.
- **Origin concept** is “country of prior education” (not necessarily citizenship), with national deviations.
- **Dataset currency:** Bulgaria-origin slice confirmed for 2023 even though dataset latest period is 2024; refresh when 2024 values land.
- **UIS gap:** UNESCO UIS global outbound totals and worldwide destination ranks are not yet integrated; needed for a global ceiling and for Bulgaria→Cyprus/Greece validation outside the Eurostat set.
- **Field-of-education gap:** outbound-by-field for Bulgaria (and bilateral Greece/Cyprus splits) still needs extraction; treat current program guidance as hypotheses to be validated.
- **Confidence levels:** high — 2023 Bulgaria→Greece/Cyprus counts/ranks and concentration; medium — Eurostat total movements; low — any claims about worldwide totals or field-of-study patterns until UIS/field data arrive.

## How to use this baseline (UNIC / UNIC Athens)
- **Market prioritisation:** Treat Bulgaria as a proven feeder to Greece (Top 10 in 2023) and a smaller but viable origin for Cyprus (rank #16).
- **Program strategy:** Test with limited hero offers per destination; emphasise employability, recognition, and value-for-money vs dominant destinations; avoid over-committing to regulated niches without Bulgaria-specific proof.
- **Performance management:** Use Eurostat as the **floor** for sizing and Greece/Cyprus share benchmarks; add UIS for the **ceiling** once extracted.
- **Next steps:** complete UIS extraction; standardise Eurostat total computations (sum numeric country hosts only, note missing hosts); extract field-of-education cuts or host-side stats; maintain annual official refresh plus quarterly internal lead-indicator reviews.
