# Bulgaria — education system overview

## Governance and structure
- The Ministry of Education and Science (MES) sets policy, curricula, standards, and assessment rules; regional education directorates oversee compliance at provincial level.
- School types: municipal public schools (majority), state schools with national status (often language/gifted), and private schools licensed under MES standards.
- Compulsory education spans ages 5–16 (pre-primary year through Grade 10).

## Stages, ages, and progression points
- **Pre-primary (compulsory preparatory year):** Age 5–6.
- **Primary (Grades 1–4, ages 7–10):** Core literacy/numeracy; end-of-stage diagnostic tests.
- **Lower secondary (Grades 5–7, ages 11–13):** General curriculum; **National External Assessment (NEA) Grade 7** in Bulgarian language & literature (BLL) and Mathematics determines entry to selective schools.
- **Upper secondary pathways (Grades 8–12/13, ages 14–18/19):**
  - **General education (comprehensive or language/gifted schools):** Academic curriculum, often with intensive first/second foreign language in Grades 8–9.
  - **Vocational education and training (VET):** Integrated general + professional modules leading to professional qualification. Duration 4 or 5 years depending on specialty; some start at Grade 8, others at Grade 9/10 for post–Grade 7 entrants.
- Education remains compulsory through Grade 10, even in VET.

## Curricula and tracks
- **General schools:** Follow MES curriculum across BLL, Mathematics, sciences, social sciences, foreign languages, ICT, and physical education. Language schools add intensive language years (8th grade “zero year” or extended hours).
- **VET schools/parallel classes:** Combine MES general education with sectoral standards defined by MES + Ministry of Economy/branch chambers; include dual training options (school + employer-based).
- **International curricula:** IB World Schools and Cambridge/A-level providers operate under MES licensing; graduates also take Bulgarian state matriculation to receive national diploma.

## Credentials and leaving certificates
- **Primary completion certificate (Grade 4)** and **lower secondary completion certificate (Grade 7)** mark stage exits.
- **Secondary Education Diploma (Diploma za Sredno Obrazovanie, DSO):** Awarded after successful completion of Grades 11–12 (or 13 in some VET programs) plus mandatory state matriculation exams.
- **Professional qualification certificate:** For VET students who pass state qualification exams (SQE) in their specialty; may accompany the DSO.

## Grading scales
- **Numerical scale 2–6**: 6 Excellent, 5 Very good, 4 Good, 3 Satisfactory, 2 Fail. Teachers may use decimal refinement (e.g., 5.50).
- Annual grades derive from term marks, coursework, and end-of-year exams per MES ordinance.

## National exams and assessments
- **National External Assessment (NEA) Grade 4:** Low-stakes diagnostics (Bulgarian language, Mathematics, Man and Society/Science) for system monitoring; not used for placement.
- **NEA Grade 7:** High-stakes for admission ranking into language/gifted and some VET schools; subjects: BLL and Mathematics (optional modern language in some years).
- **State matriculation (DZI) Grade 12:** Two mandatory exams: (1) Bulgarian language & literature; (2) elective subject (Mathematics, foreign language, History, Geography, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Philosophy, specialized VET theory). Additional elective exams possible for university admission advantages. Scores reported on a 2–6 scale with points conversion (max 100 points).
- **State qualification exams (SQE) for VET:** Practical + theoretical components; required for issuing professional qualification levels 2–4.

## Pathways into higher education
- Universities primarily use **DZI results** for competitive ranking; many weight the Bulgarian language exam and a subject relevant to the program (e.g., Mathematics for engineering, Biology/Chemistry for health sciences).
- Some faculties administer **institutional entrance exams or tests** (e.g., architecture/design drawing, language proficiency) or accept **Olympiad/competition results** as bonuses.
- **VET graduates** with DSO + professional certificate are eligible for university; some institutions grant credit/recognition for aligned specialties.
- **International curricula graduates** (IB, A-levels, AP) typically need DSO or recognized equivalent; universities may convert foreign grades to the 2–6 scale and still require DZI or an institutional exam unless exempted by regulation.

## Recognition and quality assurance
- All schools must follow MES licensing/accreditation; private schools sign a compliance contract to issue state-recognized diplomas.
- National Inspectorate for Education conducts school evaluations on teaching quality, leadership, and outcomes.
