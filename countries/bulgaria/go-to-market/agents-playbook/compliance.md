# Bulgaria agent compliance playbook (UNIC Nicosia + UNIC Athens)

**Purpose:** protect students, protect UNIC, and keep agent operations audit-ready.  
**Applies to:** any Bulgaria-based education agent/counselor/partner (and any sub-agents) sourcing for **UNIC Nicosia** and/or **UNIC Athens**.  
**Non-legal note:** this is an operational playbook, not legal advice. When in doubt, escalate to UNIC Legal/Compliance.

---

## 0) Non-negotiables (deal-breakers)

If any of the following occurs, **stop onboarding / stop new referrals immediately** and escalate:

1) **Cashback / rebates / fee manipulation**
   - No “cashback,” “refund of deposit,” “we will cover your fees,” “we’ll return part of the commission,” or similar inducements.
   - No splitting/discounting tuition or deposits outside official UNIC offers.

2) **Unapproved marketing claims**
   - Any claim about recognition/licensure, scholarships, employment outcomes, visa outcomes, or timelines that is **not in the approved UNIC messaging**.

3) **Data privacy breaches or unsafe handling**
   - Collecting/sharing passport scans, national ID numbers, DOB, health data, or other sensitive data via email/WhatsApp without UNIC-approved process.
   - Transferring leads to UNIC without valid consent and traceable proof.

4) **Undisclosed sub-agents**
   - Any sub-agent used without prior written disclosure + approval + flow-down obligations.

5) **Corruption / bribery risk**
   - Any request for “off-book” payments, gifts to decision-makers, or unethical facilitation.

## 1) Due diligence checklist (before contracting)

### A. Entity verification (must collect/verify)
- Legal entity name (BG + EN), registration number, address, VAT number (if applicable).
- Ownership: beneficial owners and controlling persons (high-level only; do not store IDs in-repo).
- Proof of authority to sign (e.g., board resolution / power of attorney where applicable).
- Bank details on official letterhead; beneficiary name must match the contracting entity.
- Website + primary domains + official social handles used for recruitment.

### B. Operating model (must understand)
- Primary lead sources (schools, fairs, web, social, referrals) + evidence they are lawful/permission-based.
- Whether they charge students counseling/service fees (yes/no + published price list).
- Whether they use sub-agents (names, geographies, channels, volume share).
- Team structure: named compliance owner + escalation contact.

### C. Compliance controls (must confirm)
- Written privacy notice for prospects (Bulgarian + English if used), including lead transfer to universities.
- Consent capture method and proof retention (CRM logs, form submissions, timestamped exports).
- Data retention policy (time-bound; deletion process).
- Incident reporting process (timeline, who notifies whom).
- Marketing approval workflow (who signs off on claims and creative).

### D. Reputation & integrity (must check)
- References from at least 2 universities/partners (names + contact emails).
- Public controversy scan (news, regulator actions, court actions) and any prior sanctions/complaints.
- Prior contract terminations and the reasons (self-declared + reference-checked).

### E. Commercial transparency (must document)
- Fee/commission model is written, simple, and maps to UNIC’s published tuition (no hidden rebates).
- Student-facing fees (if any) are clearly disclosed and not tied to admission outcomes.

**Onboarding gate:** the above is complete and reviewed by UNIC, and an agent contract (including compliance clauses) is signed **before** sharing UNIC application links or accepting leads.

## 2) GDPR / data handling rules (operational)

### 2.1 What data may be collected for a “lead”
Agents may collect and share **only what is necessary** to support inquiry and admissions steps, typically:
- Full name
- Email, phone
- City, country
- Intended program/level/intake
- Basic education background (school name, graduation year) as needed for advising

### 2.2 What data must NOT be collected or stored by the agent for marketing/lead transfer
Unless UNIC explicitly instructs otherwise via a secure admissions process, agents must not collect, transmit, or store:
- Passport/ID numbers, scans of passports/IDs
- Date of birth (unless required at the formal application stage via UNIC process)
- Health data, disability information, religion, ethnicity, political opinions
- Criminal records
- Payment card data

**Repo rule (UNIC internal):** do not store any student PII in this repository (no names, IDs, documents, emails, phone numbers). This repo is for playbooks only.

### 2.3 Lawful basis and consent (lead transfer must be permission-based)
Agents must obtain **explicit, informed consent** before transferring a lead to UNIC (Nicosia/Athens). Consent must be:
- specific (UNIC named; campus identified if possible)
- informed (what data, why, and how it will be used)
- freely given (no coercion)
- recorded (timestamp, source, text shown, opt-in checkbox)

**Minimum consent language (high-level; adapt to the agent’s form):**
> “I agree that [Agent Name] may share my contact details and study preferences with the University of Nicosia (UNIC) and/or UNIC Athens so they can contact me about programs, admissions, and scholarships. I understand I can withdraw my consent at any time.”

**Proof requirement:** agents must be able to produce consent proof within **5 business days** upon request.

### 2.4 Data transfer and security (how leads are shared)
- Leads are shared only via UNIC-approved channels (preferred: CRM/portal export or secure upload).
- No sending application documents by WhatsApp/Viber/Telegram; no open Google Drive links.
- Email is acceptable only for low-risk lead fields (name + contact + program interest) and only if encrypted/approved by UNIC.

### 2.5 Retention (default guardrail)
- Marketing leads: retain for a defined period (default expectation: **12 months**) then delete/anonymize unless the student re-consents or becomes an applicant.
- Applicants: follow UNIC admissions instructions; agent should not keep extra copies once submitted.

### 2.6 Data subject requests and incident response (must-have behavior)
- If a student asks to delete/access/correct their data, agent must acknowledge within **2 business days** and notify UNIC within **2 business days** if UNIC also holds the data.
- If a breach is suspected (lost device, mis-sent email, hacked account), agent must notify UNIC within **24 hours** with: what happened, what data, how many records, and immediate mitigation steps.

## 3) Marketing claim control (what agents can and cannot say)

### 3.1 General rule
Agents may only use:
- UNIC-approved fact sheets, program pages, and official fee/scholarship tables; and
- UNIC-provided “Bulgaria recognition & licensure” guidance: `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`

If it is not in approved materials, it is **not claimable**.

### 3.2 Scholarships and discounts (strict)
Allowed (when true and current):
- Scholarship names, amounts, eligibility, deadlines **exactly as published by UNIC**

Required qualifiers:
- “Subject to eligibility and availability”
- “UNIC confirms final award after review”

Forbidden:
- “Guaranteed scholarship”
- “Scholarship for everyone”
- Any private “agent scholarship” funded by fee manipulation or commission rebates

### 3.3 Recognition / licensure (high-risk category)
Agents must not promise licensure, guaranteed recognition, or a specific authority outcome. Use only:
- “Recognition depends on the profession and the competent authority in Bulgaria.”
- For regulated professions (medicine, nursing, pharmacy, law): “You must follow the professional qualification route with the Bulgarian competent authority.”

Use this repo guidance for details and links:
- `countries/bulgaria/market/recognition-and-licensure.md`

### 3.4 Job outcomes / salaries / employability (strict evidence rule)
Allowed:
- Verbatim, current statements from official UNIC outcomes pages if provided and approved

Forbidden unless UNIC gives written approval and evidence:
- “Guaranteed job”
- salary claims
- placement rates not sourced from UNIC-approved reporting

### 3.5 Visa, residency, timelines (avoid promises)
Allowed:
- General process explanations with clear uncertainty (e.g., “processing times vary”)

Forbidden:
- “Guaranteed visa”
- guaranteed timing commitments

### 3.6 Brand and creative controls
- Agents may not create “UNIC-branded” websites, pages, or paid ads without written approval.
- All event flyers, ad copy, landing pages, and webinar scripts must be reviewed/approved by UNIC before launch.

## 4) Anti-rebate / pricing integrity policy (Bulgaria)

**Operational rule:** all commissions are calculated on **published tuition** (net of UNIC-approved scholarships/discounts only). Agents must not:
- refund any portion of counseling fees conditioned on admission
- offer cash or gifts conditioned on enrollment
- “bundle” tuition with travel/insurance at inflated or hidden margins
- collect deposits on behalf of UNIC unless explicitly authorized

**Payment hygiene:**
- UNIC pays only the contracted legal entity to the contracted bank account.
- No third-party payment instructions, split invoices, or offshore reroutes.

---

## 5) Sub-agent controls (risk containment)

### 5.1 Disclosure and approval
- Agent must disclose any sub-agents before they are used (names, locations, channels, expected volumes).
- UNIC must approve sub-agents in writing.

### 5.2 Flow-down obligations (must be contractually enforced)
Sub-agents must follow the same rules on:
- GDPR/consent and secure transfer
- claims control (use only approved materials)
- anti-rebate policy
- audit cooperation

### 5.3 Accountability
Primary agent is fully accountable for sub-agent conduct, quality, and compliance.

---

## 6) Red flags and “stop” conditions (quick triage)

Stop and escalate if you see:
- “We can guarantee recognition/licensure” (especially health fields)
- “We can guarantee a visa” or “we have a fast-track arrangement”
- refusal to share consent capture method or proof
- refusal to name sub-agents or “our network is confidential”
- unusually high lead volume with low intent (likely bought lists)
- insistence on WhatsApp-only document handling
- student complaints about undisclosed fees, pressure selling, or cashback promises

## 7) Quarterly audit cadence (what UNIC reviews)

**Cadence:** quarterly for active partners; ad-hoc audit anytime after a complaint or anomaly.

### Audit inputs (agent must provide on request)
- Consent proof samples for a small lead sample (e.g., 10 leads): screenshots/exports showing opt-in text + timestamp (redacted where appropriate).
- Marketing inventory used in the quarter: ads, landing pages, webinar decks, email templates.
- Sub-agent roster (current) and changes since last quarter.
- Complaints log and resolution notes (high-level; no student PII).
- Commission/invoicing reconciliation summary (volumes, intakes, cancellations).

### Audit checks (UNIC performs)
- Consent integrity: opt-in exists and matches minimum language; no purchased lists.
- Claims control: scholarships, recognition/licensure, job/visa claims are compliant.
- Data minimization and secure transfer behavior is followed.
- Anti-rebate: no cashback signals; no off-book payment patterns.
- Sub-agent governance: disclosure, approval, and flow-down compliance.

### Outcomes (enforcement)
- Pass (continue)
- Conditional pass (30-day remediation plan; increased monitoring)
- Fail (freeze referrals; contract review/termination)

---

## 8) Operational enforcement (how we run this)

**Before first lead:** agent must complete due diligence + sign contract + complete UNIC compliance briefing.  
**During operation:** non-negotiables apply; quarterly audits; random spot checks; immediate escalation on red flags.  
**Termination triggers:** any non-negotiable breach, repeated claim violations, privacy breach, undisclosed sub-agents, or integrity concerns.
