**One-paragraph summary:**  
NPMG is Bulgaria’s flagship public STEM gymnasium, located in Sofia’s Lozenets district and serving highly selective cohorts in grades 8–12. The school is known for mathematics, physics, and informatics Olympiad performance, laboratory resources, and competitive culture. English proficiency is strong for STEM contexts, though instruction is primarily in Bulgarian. Families are price-sensitive because tuition is free; scholarships and affordable EU options resonate. UNIC and UNIC Athens can position CS, cybersecurity, fintech, and data programs as competitive, EU-recognized alternatives to Germany/Austria/Netherlands pathways.

**Top opportunities:**  
- Engage Olympiad and robotics communities with technical workshops and hackathons.  
- Position UNIC scholarships and cost-of-attendance advantages versus Western Europe.  
- Leverage alumni abroad (Germany/Netherlands/UK) for peer validation and parent reassurance.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Heavy competition from EU technical universities and scholarship programs.  
- No visible dedicated counseling office; approvals likely run through leadership and STEM department heads.  
- Peak academic/competition calendar limits visit windows (especially Feb–Mar).
