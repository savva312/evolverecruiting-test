**One-paragraph summary:**  
Elite state mathematics and informatics gymnasium in central Sofia, founded in 1971 and consistently ranked among Bulgaria’s top secondary schools for STEM competitions and Olympiad results. Serves ~1,300 students across grades 5–12, admitting via competitive exams and math contest performance. Strong English exposure through competition prep and intensive language tracks makes SMG a prime STEM/CS prospect pool for UNIC Nicosia and UNIC Athens, though medicine prospects require careful affordability screening and scholarship positioning.

**Top opportunities:**  
- Target STEM/CS aspirants and Olympiad-track students for UNIC CS, Data Science, and Engineering programs.  
- Leverage value messaging (EU campus, English-medium instruction, scholarships) versus UK/US options.  
- Build relationships with competition coaches and parent association to reach pay-capable families.

**Top risks or constraints:**  
- Public school affordability mix; medicine candidates may need scholarship/aid to convert.  
- High counselor and student time pressure around national exams and Olympiad calendar; outreach windows narrow.
