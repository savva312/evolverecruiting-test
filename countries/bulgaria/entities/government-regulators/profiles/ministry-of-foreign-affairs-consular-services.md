# Ministry of Foreign Affairs – Consular Affairs Directorate
last_checked: 2025-12-20

## Mandate
- Issues Bulgarian passports and provides consular protection for citizens abroad, including student travelers.
- Publishes visa and entry guidance for destinations where Bulgaria lacks local representation, directing applicants to partner EU consulates where applicable.【https://www.mfa.bg/en/consular-services】
- Coordinates legalization of public documents for use abroad (apostille/legalisation), working alongside the apostille portal for electronic applications.【https://apostille.bg/】

## Relevance to UNIC / UNIC Athens
- Sets expectations for travel documentation and safety registration for Bulgarian students heading to Cyprus or Greece (even when visas are not required for EU/EEA travel).
- Provides advisories on destinations that may affect elective clinical placements or internships tied to UNIC programmes.
- Legalisation guidance impacts how students prepare Bulgarian-issued documents (birth certificates, police certificates) for enrollment or residency processes abroad.

## Key processes and timelines
- Consular information and visa instructions are published on mfa.bg; updates occur when partner-country rules change—monitor before recruitment cycles.
- Apostille applications for public documents can be initiated through https://apostille.bg; processing times vary by document type and responsible ministry.
- Crisis registration (e.g., “Travel to…” portal) recommended for outbound students in higher-risk destinations; not mandatory for EU travel but used during disruptions.

## Contact points
- Consular services portal: https://www.mfa.bg/en/consular-services (guidance + contact forms).
- Apostille portal for document legalization: https://apostille.bg/.
- Emergency consular hotline published on the portal; verify latest number before campaigns (subject to change).
