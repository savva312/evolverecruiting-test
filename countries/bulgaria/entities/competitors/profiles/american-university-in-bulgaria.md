# American University in Bulgaria (AUBG)
- **last_verified:** 2025-12-22
- **location:** Blagoevgrad, Bulgaria
- **website:** https://www.aubg.edu/
- **summary:** Private, English-medium university positioned as a U.S.-style liberal arts experience in Bulgaria, with both Bulgarian and U.S. accreditation and a strong “study-in-English + international outcomes” pitch. (Note: AUBG’s website uses a region selector; if you hit it, select your region once and continue.) [Sources](https://www.aubg.edu/about-us/) · [Accreditation](https://www.aubg.edu/accreditation/)

## Tuition & admissions snapshot (recruiter-usable)
- **Tuition (2025–2026):** €6,700 per semester. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/)
- **Typical required fees (2025–2026):** student activity fee €320/semester; meal plan €150/semester. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/)
- **Housing (on-campus, indicative range):** €900–€1,500 per semester depending on room type. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/)
- **Application deadlines (as published):** Priority Admission **January 15**; Early Admission **March 15**; Regular Admission **May 15**. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/how-to-apply/)
- **How applicants apply:** online application portal (AUBG apply). [Source](https://apply.aubg.edu/apply/)
- **English proficiency:** AUBG publishes specific English proficiency requirements and accepted tests/scores. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/how-to-apply/english-proficiency-requirements/)

## Target segments and commercial posture
- **Internationally minded Bulgarian students seeking English-language study** but wanting to stay in-country (or close to home) versus relocating to Cyprus/Greece.
- **Price/aid posture:** Private-tuition model paired with structured scholarships/financial support, enabling discounting/aid-led conversion for top candidates. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/)
- **High-touch admissions:** Multiple deadline “rounds” (priority/early/regular) and a dedicated application portal support earlier pipeline capture. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/how-to-apply/)

## Overlap with UNIC/UNIC Athens
- **Business and computing pathways:** AUBG explicitly markets bachelor’s degrees in business-facing and computing-adjacent areas (review current bachelor portfolio for overlap with UNIC business/CS/data). [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/)
- **English-medium “international outcomes” framing:** Competes directly with UNIC/UNIC Athens when applicants prioritize English delivery, cross-border recognition, and a “global campus” story. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/about-us/)
- **Cost comparison in the funnel:** AUBG’s published tuition/fees provide an easy side-by-side benchmark for families versus Cyprus/Greece options. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/)

## Channels, partnerships, and tactics
- **Apply-portal conversion:** Dedicated application portal and deadline-driven urgency create clear “next steps” in outreach. [Source](https://apply.aubg.edu/apply/)
- **Scholarship storytelling:** AUBG publishes scholarship/aid pathways as part of admissions conversion. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/)
- **Entrepreneurship framing:** AUBG markets an on-campus accelerator as part of student-life value. [Source](https://www.aubg.edu/student-life/aubg-elevate-accelerator/)

## Risks and opportunities for UNIC
- **Risk:** English-language, U.S.-accredited degree with published tuition/aid options can anchor expectations and win “stay in Bulgaria” candidates versus relocating to Cyprus/Greece; AUBG’s Executive MBA offering also competes for postgraduate business-minded applicants. [Sources](https://www.aubg.edu/accreditation/) · [Cost/Aid](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/bachelors/cost-aid/) · [EMBA](https://www.aubg.edu/admissions/emba/)
- **Opportunity:** Position UNIC’s **blockchain/fintech leadership** and **medical/nursing** options—areas AUBG does not offer; highlight Cyprus/Greece clinical pathways and ACCA-aligned business degrees plus Mediterranean placements as differentiated outcomes.
