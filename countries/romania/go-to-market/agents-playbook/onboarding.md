# Romania agent onboarding SOP

Build every Romanian agent from first touch to productive partner with a single, auditable flow. The steps below cover target validation, due diligence, contracting, training, activation, and the “first 10 leads” quality gate so we can scale only the partners that protect UNIC Nicosia and UNIC Athens brands.

## Step-by-step onboarding flow

### 1. Target validation (Days 0–2)
- Pull candidate from `countries/romania/entities/agents/feeder-candidates.md`; confirm tier, city coverage, and program focus.  
- Log the opportunity in CRM + `templates/agent-onboarding-checklist.md` with owner, expected launch intake, and risk notes.  
- Pre-screen for campus fit (Nicosia vs Athens) and minimum funnel expectations (≥30 qualified inquiries/semester unless boutique).  
- Exit when Partnerships Director confirms pursuit via email/CRM note.

### 2. Due diligence & compliance pack (Days 2–7)
- Send diligence request list within 24h of pursuit approval and schedule a compliance interview.  
- Run checklist items below; document evidence links (PDFs, screenshots, URLs) in the template.

#### Romania-specific due diligence checklist
1. **Legal entity & ownership** — Collect ONRC *certificat constatator* (≤30 days old) plus VAT certificate; confirm CAEN codes cover education counseling. Verify beneficial owners vs. sanction lists; flag any offshore ownership.  
2. **Financial health** — Capture last two fiscal statements or accountant attestation; align invoicing currency, bank account (IBAN), and tax residence.  
3. **Sub-agent governance** — Request written sub-agent policy, named list, and proof of oversight (contracts or SLAs). Reject undisclosed sub-agents.  
4. **GDPR & data handling** — Review privacy notice and consent form; confirm data stored/processed in EU/adequate jurisdictions, DPA ready for signature, and explicit consent language for sharing leads with UNIC.  
5. **Marketing claims** — Require them to use the approved program facts (`UNIC/unicnicosia/programs.md`, `UNIC/unicathens/programs.md`) and cost/deadline references (`countries/romania/go-to-market/offerholder-and-yield/deposit-deadlines.md`). Ban scholarship “guarantee” language and claims not documented.  
6. **Operational readiness** — Check staffing (at least one certified counselor), CRM stack, and meeting notes showing how they will capture and store consent and Bac/SAT score data.  
7. **Compliance red flags** — Search for complaints, regulator actions, or prior partner issues; log outcomes + mitigation plan.

- Escalate “High” risks to Legal before continuing; medium risks need mitigation documented in checklist.  
- Exit when Compliance, Legal, and Finance sign the checklist and store artifacts in the shared drive.

### 3. Contracting & commercial approvals (Days 7–12)
- Populate latest contract template, including commission tiers by program (Medicine vs non-Medicine) and marketing cost-sharing rules.  
- Align payment triggers with deposit schedule: Medicine commission payable only after first semester instalment confirmation; other programs after Day 14 deposit.  
- Ensure clauses cover: GDPR (joint/independent controller roles), sub-agent disclosure, audit rights, and marketing content approval.  
- Capture Finance approval for any co-funded fairs/events and note them in the checklist.  
- Exit when DocuSign (or wet signature) is fully executed, counter-signed copy stored, and vendor is in finance system.

### 4. Training & enablement sprint (Days 12–19)
- **Module 1 – Product & positioning (2h)**: Walk through `UNIC/unicnicosia/programs.md` and `UNIC/unicathens/programs.md`, highlight differentiators (medicine labs, HAHE accreditation, blockchain leadership).  
- **Module 2 – Cycle & compliance (1.5h)**: Review `countries/romania/market/exams-and-calendar.md` and deposit timing; outline Bac, IB, and A-Level milestones plus visa nuances for EU vs. third-country passport holders.  
- **Module 3 – Counselor toolkit deployment (1h)**: Demonstrate how to personalize assets inside `countries/romania/go-to-market/schools-playbook/counselor-toolkit.md`; require agent to show school-ready email template.  
- **Module 4 – Digital & lead capture (1.5h)**: Train on `countries/romania/go-to-market/digital-playbook/channel-strategy.md`, GDPR-compliant lead forms, and required UTMs/lead-status fields.  
- **Systems check (30 min)**: Provide CRM credentials, tagging conventions (Agent Name + `RO-AGT`), and reporting cadence.  
- Record attendance, quiz scores (≥80%), and note any remedial actions in the checklist.  
- Exit when agent signs the enablement receipt (email is fine) and asset kit delivery is confirmed.

### 5. Activation & campaign launch (Days 19–22)
- Approve initial campaign brief (channels, budget, copy) referencing the digital playbook guardrails.  
- Load agent-branded landing pages or lead forms; verify GDPR consent text and storage path.  
- Configure CRM automation: auto-assign leads to Admissions RO queue, create “Agent QA – First 10” view, and ensure notifications fire to Agent Success + Admissions.  
- Schedule first co-hosted webinar or school visit, aligning with counselor toolkit assets.  
- Exit when first live activity (event, paid campaign, or counselor session) has a dated plan and CRM tag.

### 6. First 10 leads QA loop (Days 22–30)
- Admissions logs every lead within 4 hours; QA review happens within 72 hours of each batch of 5.  
- Quality bar per lead: full contact info, intended intake, program, Bac/IB/A-Level status, consent proof, and advisor notes aligned to campus positioning.  
- Host twice-weekly huddles (30 min) with Admissions + agent to score leads: **Green** (ready for application), **Amber** (needs info), **Red** (non-compliant).  
- Require corrective action within 48 hours for any Amber/Red patterns (e.g., missing consent, inflated scholarship promises).  
- Exit when ≥8 of 10 leads are Green and no critical compliance findings remain. Escalate to Partnerships Director if fail rate persists for two huddles.

### 7. Scale / no-go decision (Day 30+)
- Compile performance memo (volumes, conversion %, QA history, compliance issues, marketing spend) and submit to Director for sign-off.  
- **Scale**: set quarterly targets, add to partner SLA dashboard, and unlock co-funding or campus visits.  
- **Slow burn**: keep pilot limits (≤15 leads/month) and schedule remedial coaching.  
- **No-go**: pause campaigns, notify agent formally, document reasons, and update roster file.  
- Update `countries/romania/go-to-market/agents-playbook/partner-slas.md` with agreed SLAs once scaling.

## Escalation & documentation rules
- Use `countries/romania/go-to-market/agents-playbook/templates/agent-onboarding-checklist.md` as the single log; no checklist = no onboarding.  
- Store all diligence files and signed documents in the shared secure drive, linked inside the checklist or CRM.  
- Record every escalation (compliance, finance, marketing) in the checklist and copy to `executions/` run notes if needed.  
- Review this SOP quarterly or when Romania regulations change; proposals for edits must go through a new ticket.
