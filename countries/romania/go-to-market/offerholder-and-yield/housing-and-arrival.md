# Housing and arrival

- **Last updated:** 2025-12-20  
- **Owner:** Housing Lead (campus) + Admissions Ops; Support: Country Recruiter Romania

## Assumptions (visa/residence)

- Romanian/EU passport holders: visa-free entry to Cyprus and Greece; must complete residence/registration after arrival if staying >90 days.  
- Non-EU residents in Romania: need admission letter, proof of funds, housing proof, and insurance for student visa; processing may take 3–5 weeks (plan buffers).  
- Insurance: require EU EHIC or private policy accepted locally; university policy available at arrival.  
- If assumptions change, update checklists and webinar slides within 24h.

## Housing playbook

- **UNIC Nicosia:** University-managed dorms + vetted private flats. Send housing offer within 48h of deposit; hold room 10 days pending contract signature.  
- **UNIC Athens:** Private housing partners + student hubs; provide neighborhood guide (Ampelokipi, Pangrati, Neos Kosmos) with price bands and commute times.  
- **Process owners:** Housing Lead issues contracts; Finance confirms deposit; Recruiter keeps students warm with bilingual FAQs.

## Travel windows

- **Fall intake:** Arrivals recommended 10–14 days before classes; Medicine arrivals 14–21 days to finish medical checks.  
- **Spring intake:** Arrivals 7–10 days prior; schedule campus SIM/bank booth for first weekend.  
- Avoid booking until housing allocation + acceptance letter issued; share cancellation-friendly airline options.

## Pre-departure checklist (Romanian offerholders)

- Passport/ID valid 6+ months; copies uploaded to portal.  
- Deposit paid + receipt saved (owner: Admissions Ops).  
- Housing contract signed; first rent/security paid (owner: Housing Lead).  
- Insurance proof (EHIC or private) uploaded; if not, opt into university policy.  
- Flights booked inside recommended window; arrival form submitted with flight details (owner: student; verified by Recruiter).  
- Academic docs translated if needed (diploma, transcripts); originals packed.  
- Join campus WhatsApp/Discord; RSVP to airport pickup (if offered).

## Arrival-week tasks

- **Day 0–1:** Airport pickup or taxi instructions; check-in to housing; Wi-Fi + SIM guidance.  
- **Day 2–3:** Campus ID issuance; tuition payment confirmation; schedule orientation.  
- **Day 4–7:** Residence registration (EU): proof of address, bank statement, enrollment letter; book appointment slots via Housing desk. Non-EU: finalize biometric/permit steps per consulate checklist.  
- **Week 2:** Health insurance validation; open bank account (if needed); attend club fair; register with local embassy (optional).

## Residence/permit workflows (state assumptions)

- **Cyprus (Nicosia):** EU students file for residence certificate (“yellow slip”) within 4–6 weeks of arrival; documents: passport/ID, enrollment letter, lease, 2 photos, bank statement. Non-EU: temporary residence permit after D-visa entry; biometrics scheduled by Admissions Ops.  
- **Greece (Athens):** EU students can remain without entry visa; for stays >90 days, apply for registration certificate via local Aliens Bureau with lease + enrollment + insurance. Non-EU: student national visa before travel; residence permit appointment booked within 30 days of arrival.  
- Add “Assumption check” note on each confirmation email to verify current municipal requirements.

## Risks and mitigations

- **Late housing allocation:** keep 10% buffer rooms; publish weekly availability dashboard; redirect to vetted private listings if under 14 days to arrival.  
- **Non-EU visa delays:** identify non-EU residents at offer stage; fast-track document issuance within 48h; provide consulate booking script.  
- **Athens vs Nicosia confusion:** include campus tag on every email subject; distinct arrival forms; separate WhatsApp groups per campus.
