# Business, Finance, Accounting, and Marketing competitors

Document universities and private schools recruiting Romanians into business, finance, accounting, and marketing programs. Every entry highlights the positioning hook, price signals, and how UNIC should respond.

## How to use this list
- **Brief counselors quickly:** each snapshot is built for enrollment one-pagers or counselor webinars.
- **Set scholarship guardrails:** “Price & cadence” bullets anchor the budget asks you will face in market.
- **Prep value stories:** use the “Implication for UNIC” lines verbatim in messaging guides.

## Competitor dossiers

### 1. Bucharest University of Economic Studies (ASE) — FABIZ (Bucharest)
- **Offer & signal:** Romania’s flagship economics university runs full English/French/German business tracks (Accounting, Finance, Marketing, International Business) and markets dual accreditation plus a downtown campus.  
- **Price & cadence:** EU citizens see a 7,000 lei (€1,400) sticker in FABIZ while non‑EU students are quoted €3,000/year; 482 seats (52 state-funded) keep counselors laser-focused on early applications.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Lead with selective scholarships tied to English-medium specialization (fintech, blockchain) rather than blanket discounts, and pre-wire agents on UNIC’s mobility story so FABIZ’s “stay in Bucharest” narrative does not win by default.

### 2. Babeș-Bolyai University (FSEGA) — Cluj-Napoca
- **Offer & signal:** Multilingual BBA/BSc portfolio (Romanian, Hungarian, English, German, French) plus an in-house investment club makes FSEGA the “polyglot” option for Transylvania’s top achievers.  
- **Price & cadence:** Tuition is 4,900 lei (~€985) per year across business majors, and admission runs on a transparent waitlist posted mid-September, keeping price-sensitive families patient.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Package Cluj-sourced leads with cost-of-living comparisons (Nicosia/Athens vs. Cluj), and upsell paid internships to counter the perception that local multilingual programs guarantee the best ROI.

### 3. West University of Timișoara (FEAA)
- **Offer & signal:** FEAA highlights English Finance & Banking plus German/French bilingual options and Erasmus-heavy itineraries, appealing to Banat students who still want international exposure.  
- **Price & cadence:** EU tuition hovers around €800/year, with non‑EU fees at €1,980; admissions emphasize rolling online campaigns and Erasmus pathways published each May.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Treat Banat counselors to mobility masterclasses—show how UNIC secures placements beyond Erasmus (e.g., Cyprus fintech, Athens shipping) to justify a higher net price.

### 4. Alexandru Ioan Cuza University (FEAA Iași)
- **Offer & signal:** FEAA runs Business Administration and Economics & Finance entirely in English, backed by international coursebooks and English-language competency tests during admission.  
- **Price & cadence:** 4,500 lei (~€905) per year for the English track, with July English exams and 100% reliance on Bac averages; language exemptions are granted for recognized IELTS/CAE scores.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Own the “no extra language exam” message and fast-offer timelines for Moldova and Iași applicants who dread July testing bottlenecks.

### 5. Transilvania University of Brașov (UNITBV)
- **Offer & signal:** UNITBV’s Faculty of Economic Sciences and Business Administration positions itself as the mountain-campus alt with Romanian- and English-taught “Business Strategies” bachelor tracks.  
- **Price & cadence:** EU tuition is 3,850–4,800 lei (€770–€960) depending on the English stream, with fees due in full before the first exam session; non‑EU Economics majors pay €3,000/year.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Counter with total cost-of-attendance calculators that bundle accommodation and part-time work options to neutralize the “pay everything upfront” advantage UNITBV claims.

### 6. Romanian-American University (RAU) — Bucharest
- **Offer & signal:** Fully English Business, Marketing, Tourism, and Aviation programs plus dual-degree pathways with U.S. partners (St. Francis College, Northwood University) resonate with families chasing American branding.  
- **Price & cadence:** Bachelor tuition lists at €3,400/year (with early-bird €2,700 offers before April 30) and master’s from €3,600–€4,700, all payable in EUR with staged deposits.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Promote UNIC’s own North America partnerships (semester abroad, ACCA/CFA links) and highlight residence-permit support to beat RAU’s “U.S. diploma at home” pitch.

### 7. SNSPA — Faculty of Management (Bucharest)
- **Offer & signal:** Romania’s policy school markets Management BSc as a competitive-entry public option with an entirely online application window (4–14 July) and government-funded seats.  
- **Price & cadence:** Admission is based 100% on Bac averages; candidates pay a 100 lei registration fee once regardless of program count, and results drop July 18 with same-week enrollment.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Run SNSPA-shadow campaigns that open earlier (May/June) and dangle conditional offers so high-achievers aren’t tempted to “wait and see” for the state lists.

### 8. The Entrepreneurship Academy (EA) — Bucharest
- **Offer & signal:** Four-year, English-taught, venture-building BBA modeled on Team Academy Netherlands, complete with paid learning journeys (Porto Year 1, Amsterdam Year 4) and mandatory Bootday selection.  
- **Price & cadence:** Tuition is €9,000/year (moving to €9,500 from Nov 1) plus €2,600–€3,500 travel budgets per journey; financing blends scholarships for entrepreneurs with early-bird discounts.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Position UNIC Enterprise or Erasmus for Young Entrepreneurs as the “Mediterranean hub” alternative—same entrepreneurial rigor, lower ancillary travel spend, and optional double campus between Nicosia and Athens.

### 9. Bucharest Business School (ASE) — INDE & Education Leadership MBAs
- **Offer & signal:** Co-branded Franco-Romanian (INDE) and sectoral MBAs (Economics of Education & Leadership) mix ASE faculty with international partners (University of West London, francophone schools).  
- **Price & cadence:** INDE MBA fees stand at €7,000 (four €875 installments per semester), while the Education Leadership MBA sits at €6,000 with staged €1,500 payments; intake each September.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** When pitching graduate top-ups, stress AMBA/EQUIS recognitions plus UNIC’s regional employer labs to justify premium pricing over ASE’s value-focused MBAs.

### 10. WU Vienna — BSc Business & Economics
- **Offer & signal:** Austria’s WU runs a fully English BSc with 180 ECTS, an annual selection exam, and Vienna’s “world’s most liveable city” narrative—popular with Romanian IB and German-language grads.  
- **Price & cadence:** EU/EEA students pay the statutory €363.36 per semester (tuition-free within standard duration), while non‑EU peers are charged €726.72; application opens each March for October start.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Use side-by-side comparisons on faculty-student ratios and guaranteed English delivery; also highlight easier visa processing and campus housing to reduce WU’s quality-of-life edge.

### 11. Tilburg University — BSc International Business Administration
- **Offer & signal:** AACSB-accredited Dutch program emphasises AI-supported matching interviews, compulsory programming modules, and a 50-nationality cohort.  
- **Price & cadence:** Statutory tuition for EU students is €2,601 (2025/26) with institutional fees €10,200 for others; admissions demand English proficiency proof plus a chatbot interview before conditional offers.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Reinforce that UNIC’s admissions stay human-led (no bot), offer earlier scholarship decisions, and guarantee internships in Cyprus/Greece to compete with Tilburg’s analytics-first brand.

### 12. Athens University of Economics & Business (AUEB)
- **Offer & signal:** AUEB lists nine English-taught master’s (Analytics, Marketing, International Shipping, etc.) and markets new English BSc International Business & Technology with mandatory placements.  
- **Price & cadence:** Flagship MSc Marketing & Communication charges €6,900 with four installments; multiple programs tie scholarships to corporate sponsors (e.g., National Bank of Greece).  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Pitch dual-campus Athens access plus UNIC’s own professional exemption pathways (ACCA/CFA) so Romanian applicants see equal Greek exposure without the intense AUEB competition ratios.

### 13. Prague University of Economics and Business (VŠE)
- **Offer & signal:** English BBA/BSc/MSc programs at VŠE tout CEE affordability, Prague lifestyle, and tight intakes capped by February 28 deadlines—attractive for Romanians eyeing Schengen mobility.  
- **Price & cadence:** Tuition is €5,000 per academic year (payable in two €2,500 installments); visa-seeking students are urged to apply in the first intake, with a €50 application fee.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Offer rapid visa coaching for Cyprus/Greece plus paid internships so Prague’s “value + Schengen” proposition doesn’t dominate the consultancies advising Romanian families.

### 14. ALBA Graduate Business School (American College of Greece)
- **Offer & signal:** ALBA positions its €14,950 MBA (and €12,500 MScs) as AMBA-accredited, modular, and scholarship-heavy (corporate discounts up to 30%, Leventis full scholarships).  
- **Price & cadence:** Fees are identical for EU/non-EU students, payable in installments; corporate scholarship schemes kick in when two or more employees enroll, and living cost guidance runs €300–€400 per month.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** Highlight comparative total price (UNIC MBA undercuts €14.9k), and push Mediterranean employer labs/internships so candidates feel they can build the same Athens network via UNIC.

### 15. University of Piraeus — MBA International (i-MBA)
- **Offer & signal:** AMBA-accredited i‑MBA sells a “highest value-for-money” story with sustainable finance partners, small 50-student cohorts, and living-cost guidance baked into brochures.  
- **Price & cadence:** Full-time tuition is €9,700 (four staged payments) while part-time rises to €9,900; living expenses are budgeted at €500–€800/month with cafeteria subsidies.  
- **Implication for UNIC:** To win Greek-facing Romanians, emphasize UNIC’s two-campus access plus English delivery across all modules, and package guaranteed capstones so the incremental € difference feels justified.
