# Romania addendum: school outreach & counselor engagement

Use `/skills/school-outreach-and-counselor-engagement/SKILL.md` for the core workflow. Apply these Romania-specific details when planning outreach.

## Data sources and routing
- Source school and counselor targets from `countries/romania/entities/schools/` and related CSVs in `countries/romania/data/entities/`.
- Tag interactions by region (Bucharest, Cluj, Iași, Timișoara, Constanța, regional) and curriculum (Romanian Baccalaureate vs international) for sequencing and travel planning.
- Log all touches and consent in the country data model (`countries/romania/skills/romania-data-model-csvs-and-profiles/`).

## Language and etiquette
- Default to Romanian for outreach and visit materials; keep English versions for international/IB schools.
- Avoid in-person visits during Baccalaureate exam and results periods (June–July) and major holidays; propose virtual briefings instead.
- Email and phone are standard; WhatsApp is acceptable with opt-in, especially for quick scheduling.

## Visit logistics
- Some public schools require advance approval from leadership; share agenda and presenter names in Romanian.
- Bring concise UNIC vs UNIC Athens comparisons and admissions timelines; avoid speculative scholarship claims.

## Consent and data protection
- Apply GDPR expectations: capture opt-in source/time for any contact data and honor opt-outs immediately.
- Do not store national ID numbers or grades in notes; limit records to contact info, program interest, and interaction history.

## Follow-up cadence
- Send Romanian-language recaps within 48 hours with promised links; attach English copies for international schools when relevant.
- Time application workshops after exam results but before late-summer travel to secure commitments.
