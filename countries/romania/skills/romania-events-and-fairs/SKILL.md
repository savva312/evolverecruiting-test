# Romania addendum: events and fairs

Use `/skills/events-and-fairs/SKILL.md` for the standard workflow. Apply these Romania-specific notes.

## Organizer norms and assets
- Prioritize fairs and school days in Bucharest, Cluj, Iași, Timișoara, and Constanța; confirm booth power/Wi‑Fi and carry printed QR codes as backup.
- Provide Romanian-language signage and handouts; include English versions for international schools.

## Lead capture and consent
- Display a GDPR-compliant consent statement on tablets/paper forms; log opt-in source and date.
- Collect minimal fields (name, role, school, email/phone, city, program interest). Avoid birth dates or grades; only collect WhatsApp if opt-in is recorded.
- If using organizer badge scans, confirm consent language before importing data into `countries/romania/data/`.

## Staffing and follow-up
- Staff at least one Romanian-speaking rep; keep an English-speaking backup for international tracks and carry a concise UNIC vs UNIC Athens comparison sheet.
- Send Romanian recaps within 48 hours, tagging event and city, and include English attachments when relevant.
- Log leads in the country data model with event name/date, city, and campus interest (Nicosia vs Athens) before launching sequences.
- Route hot leads to webinars/application workshops scheduled after Baccalaureate results; tag event names and campus preference in follow-up logs.
