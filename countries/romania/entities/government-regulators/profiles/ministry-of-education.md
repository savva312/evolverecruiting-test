# Ministry of Education (Ministerul Educației)

- last_checked: 2025-12-20
- website: https://www.edu.ro/
- contacts: Secretariat General (via https://www.edu.ro/contact); public information / petitions handled through GOV.RO portal

## Mandate

Sets national education policy, oversees higher education legislation, and supervises agencies such as CNRED (recognition) and ARACIS (quality assurance). It negotiates bilateral education agreements, sets scholarship schemes, and issues orders governing credential formats and quality standards.

## Relevance to UNIC / UNIC Athens

- Ministry orders determine how foreign university degrees are recognized domestically and what supporting documents Romanian students must provide when returning with UNIC credentials.  
- Policy signals on outbound mobility, state scholarship portability, and student protection shape demand for foreign study options.

## Key processes and timelines

- Issues ministerial orders regulating recognition/equivalence and ensures CNRED implements them; updates typically align with EU directives and Bologna processes.  
- Bilateral or institutional cooperation agreements are reviewed by the International Relations directorate; approvals can span several months depending on inter-ministerial review.

## Contact and engagement channels

- Public-facing contact form and petition channels at https://www.edu.ro/contact.  
- International Relations inquiries are routed via the Ministry’s contact portal; meetings often require formal letters in Romanian plus a short English brief.  
- Official gazette (Monitorul Oficial) publishes new orders; monitoring is necessary for any changes that could affect UNIC degree recognition.
