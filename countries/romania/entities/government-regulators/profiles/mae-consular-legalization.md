# Ministry of Foreign Affairs – Consular Department (Legalization Office)

- last_checked: 2025-12-20

## Mandate

The Consular Department legalizes Romanian public documents for use in states that are not party to the Hague Apostille Convention and provides guidance on apostille procedures handled by county prefectures (under the Ministry of Internal Affairs). It also issues travel advisories and consular assistance guidance for Romanians studying abroad.

## Relevance to UNIC / UNIC Athens

- Students may need legalized or apostilled diplomas/transcripts when applying to UNIC; MAE publishes the correct pathway (apostille vs. consular legalization) and the list of documents that require prior authentication.  
- Advisories and consular support influence parental confidence in study destinations and contingency planning.

## Key processes and timelines

- Consular legalization requires prior authentication by the issuing authority and typically involves in-person submission or notarized proxy; processing windows vary by diplomatic mission but are often same-day to a few working days.  
- Apostille requests for education documents are directed to the relevant county prefecture; MAE maintains guidance and country lists clarifying when apostille suffices versus full legalization.

## Contact and engagement channels

- Email: legalizari@mae.ro for legalization-specific questions; consular call center numbers are published on the MAE site for appointment scheduling.  
- Public guidance on apostille/legalization steps is maintained on MAE’s consular portal; updates should be monitored before each recruitment cycle.
