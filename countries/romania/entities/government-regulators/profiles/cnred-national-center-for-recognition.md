# CNRED (National Center for Recognition and Equivalence of Diplomas)

- last_checked: 2025-12-20
- website: https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en
- contacts: cnred@edu.gov.ro; +40 21 405 6300

## Mandate

CNRED, under the Ministry of Education, evaluates and recognizes degrees earned abroad for employment and further study in Romania. It issues equivalence/recognition decisions, maintains guidelines for EU vs. non-EU credentials, and liaises with the ENIC-NARIC network.

## Relevance to UNIC / UNIC Athens

- Romanian graduates of UNIC programs need CNRED recognition for regulated professions or admission to postgraduate study in Romania.  
- CNRED guidance shapes what documentation UNIC should supply (transcripts, diploma supplements, proof of accreditation).

## Key processes and timelines

- Standard recognition applications (employment/continuing study) are filed online or via county school inspectorates; CNRED aims to decide within 30 days of receiving a complete file.  
- EU/EEA diplomas typically follow a simplified route; non-EU awards may require comparability checks and supplementary documents.  
- Applicants must provide legalized/apostilled diplomas and official translations where applicable.
