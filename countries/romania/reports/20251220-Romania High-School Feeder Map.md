# Romania High-School Feeder Map

## Produced by: UNIC Evolve, December 20, 2025

# 1. Executive Summary: Where Romania’s Highest-Value High-School Feeder Pools Are for \$10,000 – \$27,000/Year University Tuition

Romania’s public high schools (liceu) are tuition-free, so “expensive enough” is rarely a property of the public system itself. For identifying families who can plausibly pay **\$10,000/year to \$15,000/year** for UNIC programs (and **\$22,000 – \$27,000/year** for medicine), the most decision-relevant signal is enrollment in a **fee-charging private or international high school**, plus a second signal: participation in an **international upper-secondary pathway** (IB Diploma Programme, Cambridge IGCSE plus AS/A Levels, French lycée to Terminale, German Abitur track, or a US Grades 9 – 12 model). Elite public “colegiu național” high schools can be used as a **separate proxy** for international mobility, but they are not “expensive,” so they should be treated differently in targeting and messaging.

## What “expensive enough” means in practice (Romania-specific segmentation)

For this report, schools are segmented into three practical categories aligned to ability to pay international university tuition:

1. **Premium fee-charging liceu providers (direct affordability signal)**  
   These are private or international schools that operate a liceu (Grades IX – XII or equivalent) and charge meaningful annual tuition. In Bucharest-area examples, published or widely documented fees for upper secondary can reach levels comparable to, or higher than, many international university tuition points. These schools are the most direct feeder base for families able to budget \$10,000 – \$27,000/year for university.

2. **International upper-secondary pathways (international orientation plus affordability signal)**  
   Schools delivering **IB DP** or **Cambridge Upper Secondary plus Cambridge Advanced** (IGCSE plus AS/A Levels) are strong predictors of English-taught university intent, overseas application behavior, and willingness to pay for education. Even when tuition is not publicly posted, the program infrastructure and positioning are commonly associated with higher household education spend.

3. **Public elite proxies (mobility signal, not an “expensive high school” signal)**  
   Select state schools (for example an IB DP state college) can produce many outbound students, but household ability to pay varies widely, and students often rely on scholarships or low-cost EU pathways. These are best used for brand building and academic outreach, not as the core “ability-to-pay” acquisition list.

## City prioritization: where the paying capacity and international pathways concentrate

### Tier 1: Highest-yield, must-cover geographies
- **Bucharest + Ilfov (including Voluntari, Pipera, Băneasa, Otopeni corridor)**  
  This is Romania’s highest-density market for premium private liceu and international schools, including multiple IB DP providers and several large fee-charging institutions. For recruitment outcomes per hour of outreach, this geography dominates.

### Tier 2: High-value regional hubs with credible premium/international upper secondary
- **Cluj-Napoca (Cluj County)**  
  A strong private and international school ecosystem relative to city size, including Cambridge-oriented offerings and multiple fee-charging liceu units. Cluj is a consistent “international education” market with visible premium segments.
- **Iași (Iași County)**  
  A smaller premium high-school universe than Bucharest or Cluj, but still contains multiple private fee-charging liceu units, including international-branded providers.
- **Timișoara (Timiș County)**  
  The standout outside the Bucharest and Cluj ecosystems for a clear premium international upper-secondary offering (including IB DP and Cambridge-related pathways), with published high upper-secondary fees.

### Tier 3: Include selectively (single-school cities and partial signals)
- **Constanța (Constanța County)**  
  Evidence supports Cambridge IGCSE delivery and a high-school offering, but upper-secondary completeness through Years 12 – 13 (AS/A Levels) requires careful confirmation before treating it as a full Cambridge-to-graduation pipeline.
- **Brașov area (Brașov County)**  
  A fee-charging liceu exists with international positioning, but published fee levels appear far below the “premium” segment seen in Bucharest and top international schools. This city is best treated as a supplementary, lower-yield pool.
- **Oradea (Bihor County)**  
  An international-school brand presence is visible, but the critical question is whether upper-secondary delivery (GCSE/A Levels) is actually provided onsite versus referenced as a future pathway. Treat as “verify then include.”

## Diminishing returns and the stopping rule (when to stop adding cities)

Because Romania’s premium private and international upper-secondary supply is highly concentrated, the report will stop adding cities once incremental yield becomes small. The operational cutoff used is:

Include a city if it has at least one school that meets any of the following:
- **Confirmed private/international IB Diploma Programme provider**, or
- **Confirmed Cambridge Upper Secondary plus Cambridge Advanced delivery** (IGCSE plus explicit AS/A Level, or explicit Years 12 – 13 delivery), or
- **A private liceu with published premium tuition at or above €6,000/year equivalent** (or clearly comparable high-fee positioning), even without IB/Cambridge.

Cities that do not meet one of these tests typically add many hours of validation work for a small number of additional, often lower-fee units, which does not align with the user’s goal of identifying families plausibly able to pay \$10,000 – \$27,000/year for university tuition.

## Bottom line for UNIC recruitment targeting

- **Core acquisition market**: Bucharest + Ilfov, then Cluj, Iași, and Timișoara.  
- **High-probability payer profile**: students already paying substantial private/international high-school tuition and students enrolled in IB DP or Cambridge-to-A Level style pathways.  
- **Long-tail approach**: add only the small number of verified “single-school cities” with true international upper-secondary delivery; deprioritize the rest unless there is a clearly documented premium tuition signal.

# 3. Methodology and Controls: Inclusion Criteria, Evidence Standards, and Known Gaps

## 3.1 How “expensive enough” was operationalized in Romania (and why)
Romania’s public high schools are tuition-free, so the report treats “expensive enough” as a **household education-spend and international-orientation signal**, not as a characteristic of the public system. The list therefore prioritizes schools that meet at least one of the following tests:

1. **Direct expense signal (highest priority)**: a private or international unit that operates **liceu level (Grades IX – XII or equivalent)** and is fee-charging, ideally with a published fee schedule.
2. **International upper-secondary pathway signal**: verified delivery of an internationally recognized upper-secondary pathway (for example IB Diploma Programme, Cambridge IGCSE plus explicit Years 12 – 13 delivery such as AS/A Levels, French lycée cycle through Terminale, German Abitur track, US Grades 9 – 12 model). These programs correlate strongly with outward mobility and willingness to pay for education, even when fees are not publicly posted.
3. **Public elite proxy (handled cautiously)**: top state schools can be strong outbound academic pipelines, but they are not “expensive high schools,” and family ability-to-pay varies. For this reason, public proxies were generally excluded from the “expensive enough” core list and should only be used as a separate academic outreach layer if needed.

## 3.2 Evidence hierarchy and inclusion controls (what counted as “confirmed”)
To make the list usable for targeting and defensible for internal review, evidence was handled in a clear hierarchy:

### A. Legal existence and liceu authorization (primary control)
- **ARACIP unit records and registers** were treated as the backbone to confirm that an institution is a recognized education unit in Romania and to check whether liceu level is authorized or accredited.
- Where a brand operates multiple campuses or legal units, records were treated **at the campus or legal-unit level** whenever possible, since liceu authorization can differ by site.

### B. Upper-secondary grade coverage (required for inclusion)
A school was included only if upper-secondary coverage was supported by at least one of:
- a registry record indicating liceu level, or
- the school’s own official pages stating Grades IX – XII, Years 10 – 13, Grade 12 completion, Terminale, Oberstufe, or equivalent.

If a school is high-profile but grade coverage was not explicitly confirmed in the available evidence, it was kept as a **candidate requiring verification**, and clearly labeled as such in Section 2.

### C. Tuition and affordability signal (no guessing rule)
- Tuition was recorded only when **published by the school** (fee page, admissions schedule, PDF image, or equivalent), or when a clearly identified published schedule was otherwise available.
- Where fees were not publicly posted, the entry was labeled **Not published** rather than estimated.
- Press-reported fee figures were treated as **secondary** and were not used as the sole basis for a “premium” classification without a corroborating primary source.

## 3.3 How cities were selected, and the stopping rule
The report is city-organized, but it intentionally stops short of listing every Romanian city because the premium private and international upper-secondary supply is highly concentrated.

A city was included when it met at least one of these thresholds:
- at least one **confirmed private or international IB DP provider**, or
- at least one school with **confirmed Cambridge upper-secondary through graduation** (IGCSE plus explicit Years 12 – 13 delivery), or
- at least one private liceu with **published premium tuition** broadly comparable to €6,000/year or higher.

Cities without at least one of these signals typically add limited incremental value for identifying families able to pay \$10,000 – \$27,000/year, relative to the validation work required.

## 3.4 Known gaps, edge cases, and data-quality risks (what to verify before outreach)
Several recurring issues appear in the Romanian market and should be treated as standard diligence items:

1. **Split authorizations across legal units or campuses**  
   Some brands operate multiple legal entities, and liceu authorization may sit on a different unit record than lower levels. This is especially common for fast-growing operators and international brands.

2. **“Pathway referenced” versus “pathway delivered”**  
   Some schools market GCSE, A Levels, or “international progression,” but do not clearly state that Years 12 – 13 are delivered onsite. These should be treated as **Needs verification** until the school confirms delivery through graduation.

3. **Fee opacity (fees on request)**  
   Many premium schools require inquiry for fee schedules. These can still be high-likelihood affluence pipelines, but segmentation requires direct confirmation.

4. **Program marker ambiguity**  
   Language-prep references (for example Cambridge English exams) are not the same as Cambridge International Upper Secondary or A Level delivery. Program tags should be assigned only when the school explicitly evidences the academic pathway.

5. **Out-of-scope public international programs**  
   State schools that run IB DP or Cambridge approvals can be important academic pipelines but are not reliable “ability-to-pay” screens. They should be handled separately if the objective is purely fee-based recruitment.

## 3.5 Practical verification checklist (recommended before launching recruitment)
Before prioritizing outreach lists or allocating budget by city, apply the following checklist for each target school:

1. **Confirm grade span**: does the school deliver Grades IX – XII (or Years 10 – 13) through graduation onsite?
2. **Confirm pathway** (if international): IB DP authorization, Cambridge IGCSE and explicit AS/A Level delivery, or equivalent national curriculum pathway (French Terminale, German Abitur, US Grade 12).
3. **Confirm latest fee schedule**: obtain the current academic year’s liceu fees, plus key add-ons (enrollment fee, bus, meals, exam fees).
4. **Confirm campus that actually hosts liceu**: for multi-campus brands, confirm the address where upper secondary is delivered.
5. **Capture admissions timing and counseling capacity**: identify whether the school has university counseling, standardized test preparation, and an international applications calendar, since these correlate with higher conversion to fee-based programs abroad.

If these steps are applied consistently, the list in Section 2 becomes a reliable, action-ready pipeline map for identifying Romanian families most likely to afford UNIC Nicosia and UNIC Athens tuition levels, including the higher-cost medicine programs.
