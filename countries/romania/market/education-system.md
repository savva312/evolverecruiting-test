# Romania education system overview

## Governance and oversight
- **National:** Ministry of Education sets curricula, assessment rules, and credential standards (Bacalaureat, vocational qualifications). National Centre for Technical and Vocational Education and Training Development (CNDIPT) oversees VET frameworks and qualifications.
- **Local:** 42 county school inspectorates implement policy, allocate places, and supervise schools; local councils fund infrastructure and transport.
- **Quality assurance:** Romanian Agency for Quality Assurance in Pre-University Education (ARACIP) inspects schools; ARACIS oversees higher education.

## Structure and age/grade bands
- **Early childhood:** kindergarten (grădiniță) for ages 3–6; the **preparatory grade (clasa pregătitoare)** at age ~6 is compulsory and counted as the first year of primary.
- **Primary:** grades 0–4 (ages ~6–10), common core curriculum.
- **Lower secondary (gymnaziu):** grades 5–8 (ages ~10–14), general curriculum; ends with the **National Evaluation** exams used for upper-secondary placement.
- **Upper secondary (liceu):** grades 9–12 (or 13 in some vocational routes), ages ~14–18/19, with differentiated pathways (theoretical, technological, vocational/arts/pedagogical, or dual VET).
- **Post-secondary non-tertiary:** **Şcoli postliceale** (foreman/nursing/technician colleges, 1–3 years) that do not require a Bac diploma but often require a professional qualification.
- **Higher education:** Bologna-aligned; Bachelor’s 3–4 years (medicine/dentistry 6 years), Master’s 1–2 years, Doctorate 3+ years.

## Tracks and curriculum pathways
- **Theoretical liceu (4 years):** sciences (real) or humanities (uman), preparing for academic Bac subjects.
- **Technological liceu (4 years):** technology, services, natural resources/environment; combines academic study with technical training and typically leads to the technological Bac profile.
- **Vocational/arts/pedagogical liceu (4–5 years):** arts, sports, theology, military, teacher-training; culminates in Bac with profile-specific exams.
- **Professional/VET school (3 years) + optional continuation:** dual or school-based VET delivering **level 3 professional qualification**; graduates can enter the labor market or enroll in a **2-year complementary route (grades 11–12 of technological liceu)** to sit the Bac later.

## Key transition points and exams
- **End of grade 8:** National Evaluation (Romanian, Mathematics, plus mother-tongue for minority students) determines high-school placement alongside GPA.
- **Throughout liceu:** competency tests in digital skills and modern language (CEFR A1–B2) and oral Romanian/mother tongue before Bac written exams.
- **Bacalaureat (National Baccalaureate):** taken after grade 12/13. Written exams typically include Romanian language and literature, a profile-compulsory subject (e.g., Mathematics for sciences/technology, History for humanities), and a profile/elective subject (e.g., Physics/Biology/Geography). Passing requires **minimum 5/10 on each written exam and overall average ≥6/10**. Grants access to universities.
- **Vocational credentials:** Professional qualification certificates (levels 3–4) issued after dual/school-based VET and post-secondary schools; regulated by the National Qualifications Framework.

## Grading scales
- **Pre-university:** numeric **1–10 scale**, with **5 as the minimum passing grade** per subject. Behavior is reported separately. Annual GPA (media generală) informs placement and scholarship decisions.
- **Bac results:** reported on the same 1–10 scale; averages convert directly into university admission score formulas.
- **Higher education:** also uses 1–10 with 5 as pass; some institutions display ECTS A–F alongside local grades.

## University entry mechanics
- **Baseline requirement:** Bacalaureat diploma (or equivalent EU/EEA-recognized credential) is mandatory for degree enrollment.
- **Admission models:** public universities commonly use weighted formulas combining Bac average and specific Bac subject marks; competitive programs (medicine, computer science, military, arts) add **institution-set entrance exams or portfolio/audition**. Post-liceal qualifications alone do **not** grant university access without Bac.
- **Language expectations:** programs in Romanian require language competence; English/French-language tracks often require CEFR B2 certificates or institutional tests. International students with foreign diplomas submit recognition to the National Centre for Recognition and Equivalence of Diplomas (CNRED).

## Implications for UNIC/UNIC Athens outreach
- Prioritize **Bac candidates from theoretical and technological liceu** profiles for direct undergraduate entry; confirm subject alignment (e.g., Math/Physics for STEM, Biology/Chemistry for health sciences).
- For **VET graduates without Bac**, highlight pathways to complete the 2-year continuation and Bac, or consider foundation/bridging offers.
- Engage **county inspectorates and top liceu tracks** near exam season for conversion; provide clear guidance on **minimum Bac averages and subject prerequisites** and accept CEFR B2 certificates for English-medium programs.
