# Recognition and licensure — Romania (updated 22 December 2025)

Romanian students returning from UNIC Nicosia or UNIC Athens will interact with two layers of validation:

1. **Academic recognition by CNRED (National Center for Recognition and Equivalence of Diplomas)** — establishes the degree’s comparability so graduates can enroll in Romanian programs or present their diploma to employers.
2. **Professional licensure by the competent body for each regulated field** — confirms the right to practice (medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, psychology, law, etc.).

Counselors must help families distinguish between these steps, prepare complete dossiers, and avoid promising automatic practice rights.

---

## Executive summary
- CNRED states that complete higher-ed recognition files are processed within **30 calendar days** once a fully apostilled, translated dossier is validated, but the clock pauses whenever sectoral regulators (medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, general care nursing, midwifery, veterinary medicine, architecture) must confirm professional details ([CNRED – recognition for employment](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/recognition-studies-performed-abroad-for-employment)).
- Diplomas issued in Cyprus or Greece **must** carry the Hague Apostille, and sworn Romanian translations are required if documents are not in RO/EN/FR/ES/IT ([CNRED document requirements](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/recognition-studies-performed-abroad-for-employment)).
- Applications for regulated professions are lodged via Romania’s Electronic Single Point of Contact (PCUe) using ROeID/eIDAS credentials; CNRED only issues the statement of recognition after the relevant professional body confirms that sectoral conditions are met ([CNRED – regulated professions](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/professions-regulated-specially)).
- Each professional order imposes its own membership, malpractice, CPD, language, and aptitude-test requirements. Recent fee updates—such as the **€1,000** Colegiul Medicilor din România (CMR) specialist evaluation fee and the **2,500 RON** Colegiul Medicilor Stomatologi din România (CMSR) temporary practice review—materially change student cost models ([CMR Decision 15/2025](https://www.cmr.ro/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Decizia-15_2025-fiscalizare-proceduri-operațiuni-financiare-ale-CMR-1-1-1.pdf); [CMSR Hotărârea AP 29/2025](https://cmsr.ro/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Hot-AP-29-2025.pdf)).
- Safe messaging: UNIC can guarantee accredited instruction and complete documentation, **not** Romanian licensing outcomes; every student must engage directly with CNRED plus the Romanian regulator for their field.

## CNRED recognition essentials

### When CNRED recognition is required
- Any Romanian graduate presenting a foreign bachelor’s, master’s, or doctoral diploma for employment or further study.
- Regulated professions (medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, general care nursing, midwifery, veterinary medicine, architecture) **always** require a CNRED Statement of Recognition before the professional order will register them ([CNRED – regulated professions](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/professions-regulated-specially)).

### Submission pathways
- **PCUe (Electronic Single Point of Contact):** fastest route; requires ROeID/eIDAS/ghiseul.ro credentials and allows e-delivery of decisions.
- **Physical filing:** personal/authorized representative delivery at CNRED (12 Spiru Haret St., Bucharest) or via courier/post; still common when families lack qualified digital IDs ([CNRED contact guidance](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/contact)).

### Baseline dossier checklist
- Application form (CNRED template, physical or PCUe).
- Diploma (copy in RO/EN/FR/ES/IT; otherwise sworn translation).
- Transcript/diploma supplement (especially if specialization needs clarification).
- Passport/ID copy + proof of any name changes.
- Proof of establishment in Romania for sectoral professions (employment contract, residence proof, etc.).
- Proof of payment (100 RON) — payable via CNRED cashier, ghiseul.ro, or international bank transfer.
- **Authentication:** Hague Apostille for Cyprus/Greece-issued diplomas; alternative legalization chain for non-Apostille states.
- Additional field-specific pieces (syllabi, clinical logs, authenticity certificates) when CNRED requests deeper comparability checks ([CNRED – document list](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/recognition-studies-performed-abroad-for-employment)).

### Timelines and predictable bottlenecks
- CNRED targets **30 calendar days** once a complete file is registered; incomplete files or pending verification with sectoral regulators pause the clock.
- Peak congestion: late summer (post-graduation) and January (post-fall ceremonies).
- Delays most often stem from: missing apostilles, uncertified translations, or inability to access PCUe (families without RO/UE eIDs).
- Status tracking is available via CNRED’s online “File status check” so counselors can keep families informed ([CNRED status checker](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/status-file)).

### Safe language for recruiters
- **Say:** “CNRED typically issues decisions within ~30 days once it has apostilled, translated documents. UNIC will support you with diploma supplements and proof of accreditation.”
- **Add:** “Regulated professions also need clearance from Romanian professional bodies; we’ll outline their checklists, but they make the final call.”
- **Avoid:** “Your Romanian license is automatic” or “UNIC can obtain CNRED approval on your behalf.” Students (or authorized relatives) must hold the CNRED mandate.

## Regulated fields checklist

### Medicine (doctors + specialists)
- **Regulator:** Colegiul Medicilor din România (CMR) / Ministry of Health.
- **What to verify with the student:**
  - CNRED recognition certificate for the MD (mandatory for foreign degrees) — Romanian county colleges explicitly request it during enrollment ([Colegiul Medicilor Galați – acte necesare înscriere](https://cmgl.ro/acte-necesare-inscrierea-in-cmgl/)).
  - Proof of residency slot or employer in Romania; malpractice insurance; A5 medical certificate; criminal-record check ([Colegiul Medicilor Brașov – înscriere rezidenți](https://www.cmmbrasov.ro/inscrierea-in-cmmb-medici-rezidenti/)).
  - Language proficiency: CMR requires foreign citizens to prove Romanian-language ability for patient interactions ([Colegiul Medicilor Brașov – înscriere rezidenți](https://www.cmmbrasov.ro/inscrierea-in-cmmb-medici-rezidenti/)).
- **Cost & timing signals:**
  - Professional evaluation fee for third-country specialist titles is **€1,000** (rising to €2,000 if an aptitude test is imposed) per CMR Decision 15/2025 (effective 16 June 2025) ([CMR Decision 15/2025](https://www.cmr.ro/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Decizia-15_2025-fiscalizare-proceduri-operațiuni-financiare-ale-CMR-1-1-1.pdf)).
  - Transfers between territorial colleges can add 4–6 weeks, especially if good-standing certificates or EMC credits are pending.
- **Counselor-safe phrasing:** “UNIC provides detailed curricula and clinical logs so CMR can benchmark your training; CMR alone grants the right to practise and may require an aptitude test.”
- **Key links:** [CMR recognition guidance](https://www.cmr.ro/recunoastere-profesionala), [CNRED regulated professions list](https://www.cnred.edu.ro/en/professions-regulated-specially).

### Dentistry
- **Regulator:** Colegiul Medicilor Stomatologi din România (CMSR).
- **What to verify:**
  - CNRED-recognized DDS/DMD plus any specialty certificates.
  - CMSR membership application (malpractice insurance, EMC evidence, Romanian language capability).
- **Recent changes (Nov 2025):**
  - Temporary/occasional practice review fee for foreign dentists increased to **2,500 RON**.
  - New **500 RON** fee for evaluating foreign citizens’ permanent practice requests, **5,000 RON** for aptitude exams, and **500 RON** for Romanian language testing (CMSR Hotărârea AP 29/2025 effective 28 November 2025) ([CMSR Hotărârea AP 29/2025](https://cmsr.ro/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Hot-AP-29-2025.pdf)).
- **Messaging tips:** highlight the need to budget for CMSR fees and potential aptitude/language exams; do not promise expedited slots — CMSR calendars fill quickly after safety-driven policy updates.
- **Key link:** [cmsr.ro](https://cmsr.ro/).

### Pharmacy
- **Regulator:** Colegiul Farmaciștilor din România (CFR) + county branches (e.g., CFB, CF Iași).
- **Minimum dossier:**
  - CNRED recognition of the MPharm/BPharm.
  - Membership certificate + annual “aviz de liberă practică,” which requires malpractice insurance covering the calendar year, proof of **40 EFC credits** (with 8 hours of RBPF/legislation), paid cotization of **300 RON**, valid employment contract, and for pensioners an additional medical certificate ([CF București – aviz anual 2025](https://www.cfbucuresti.ro/2024/12/12/avizul-anual-de-libera-practica/); [CF București – condiții avizare 2026](https://www.cfbucuresti.ro/2025/10/20/cotizatie-si-avizare-2026/)).
- **Operational notes:**
  - Aviz renewals open every December–January; missing the upload window delays the ability to work legally (county branches such as CF Iași lock the portal after 31 December for the prior year’s credits) ([CF Iași – Info Aviz Anual 2025](https://iasi.colegfarm.ro/noutati/Info-AVIZUL-ANUAL-2025---CF-Iasi)).
  - Pharmacies must also renew RBPF (Good Pharmacy Practice) annexes annually; students targeting ownership roles should plan for the extra 200 RON evaluation and facility documentation.
- **Safe phrasing:** “UNIC supplies transcripts and syllabi for CNRED; CFR still verifies malpractice cover, CPD, and Romanian-language compliance before activating your right to dispense.”
- **Key link:** [cfbucuresti.ro](https://www.cfbucuresti.ro/).

### Psychology (including counseling & psychotherapy)
- **Regulator:** Colegiul Psihologilor din România (CPR).
- **Obligations:**
  - Practising in Romania is legal **only** with a CPR-issued “atestat de liberă practică”; CPR alone recognizes foreign qualifications under Law 213/2004 and the implementing norms published in Monitorul Oficial ([Law 213/2004 norms](https://legislatie.just.ro/Public/DetaliiDocument/215768)).
  - Applicants must document accredited psychology degrees (or equivalent specialties defined by CPR), supervised practice, and portfolios aligned with the competency annexes updated via Hotărârea nr. 4/2025 (effective 20 March 2025) ([Hotărârea CPR nr. 4/2025](https://legislatie.just.ro/Public/DetaliiDocument/309236)).
- **Risk cues:** CPR may impose adaptation periods, additional supervised hours, or deny titles if the foreign curriculum lacks required credits (e.g., 500+ hours for psychotherapy specializations).
- **Safe phrasing:** “UNIC can confirm your course content and practicum hours; CPR will still evaluate your portfolio and may require extra supervised practice before issuing the atestat.”
- **Key link:** [copsi.ro](https://www.copsi.ro/).

### Law (admission to the Romanian Bar)
- **Regulator:** Uniunea Națională a Barourilor din România (UNBR) + the target county bar.
- **Pathways for UNIC law graduates:**
  - **Full admission:** sit the UNBR national bar exam (organized via INPPA) — requires CNRED recognition plus success in civil, criminal, and procedural subjects (see UNBR exam notices and local bar circulars).
  - **EU/EEA lawyers seeking Romanian practice rights:** must pass UNBR’s exam on Romanian law and Romanian language; Decizia Comisiei Permanente nr. 258/23.01.2025 schedules the 8–9 May 2025 sitting, while Decizia nr. 323/28.05.2025 adds an 11–12 September 2025 session ([UNBR Decision 258/2025](https://www.unbr.ro/decizia-comisiei-permanente-a-unbr-nr-258-din-23-ianuarie-2025-privind-organizarea-examenului-de-verificare-a-cunostintelor-de-drept-romanesc-si-de-limba-romana-pentru-avocatii-straini-in-vederea-dob/); [Baroul Alba summary of Decision 323/2025](https://www.barou-alba.ro/decizia-comisiei-permanente-a-unbr-nr-323-din-28-mai-2025-privind-organizarea-examenului-de-verificare-a-cunostintelor-de-drept-romanesc-si-de-limba-romana-pentru-avocatii-straini-sesiunea-septembri/)).
- **Documentation:** proof of good standing from the home bar, Romanian language proficiency, CNRED recognition, and any materials demanded by the target bar.
- **Safe phrasing:** “UNIC ensures your academic documentation meets CNRED standards; only UNBR can authorize bar admission, and it routinely requires a Romanian-law exam even for EU-qualified lawyers.”
- **Key link:** [unbr.ro](https://www.unbr.ro/).

## Counselor workflow
1. **Pre-final year:** brief Romanian nationals on apostille requirements and encourage them to request UNIC transcripts/diploma supplements early.
2. **At graduation:** host clinics on CNRED file assembly; share PCUe registration guides for families lacking Romanian eIDs.
3. **3–6 months pre-return:** pre-screen regulated field students against the relevant checklist above; flag language upskilling needs and cash outlays (fees, malpractice, CPD).
4. **Post-return:** track CNRED file numbers, remind students to monitor the online status page, and schedule follow-ups with professional orders.
