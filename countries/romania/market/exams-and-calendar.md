# Exams and academic calendar

Romania’s secondary exit landscape centers on the **Bacalaureat (Bac)**, with international alternatives used by premium private schools. The calendar below emphasizes timing, registration windows, and outreach levers for UNIC and UNIC Athens.

## Bacalaureat (national pathway)
- **Administered by:** Ministry of Education via county school inspectorates; syllabi and calendars are published annually on [edu.ro](https://www.edu.ro/).
- **Profiles:** theoretical (real/sciences, human/humanities), technological, and vocational/arts/pedagogical. Exam subjects vary by profile (e.g., Math is compulsory for sciences/technology; History for humanities; an elective aligned to profile such as Physics, Biology, Geography, Logic, or an advanced language).
- **Structure:** three written exams (Romanian language and literature; profile-compulsory; profile/elective), plus oral/competency tests in Romanian, mother tongue (for minorities), modern language (mapped to CEFR A1–B2), and digital skills (ICT).
- **Passing rules:** minimum **5/10 per written** and **overall average ≥6/10**. A Bac diploma is required for university admission; certificate of competencies alone is insufficient.

### Vocational and dual nuances
- **Technological liceu graduates** sit the same Bac structure but with technology-oriented compulsory subjects (e.g., Math/Technological). Scores remain fully valid for university entry.
- **Dual/VET graduates (3-year route)** earn a **level 3 professional qualification** but need the **2-year continuation (grades 11–12 of technological liceu)** to attempt the Bac; highlight UNIC foundation/pathway options for those still completing continuation.
- **Retake options:** a **June–July main session** and an **August–September resit** allow candidates to improve marks; outreach should encourage applicants to secure unconditional offers contingent on final Bac scores and to book resits early if needed.

## International alternatives (common in premium schools)
- **IB Diploma Programme:** Offered by select international schools in Bucharest/Cluj. Exam sessions **May and November**; results released **early July (May session)** and **early January (Nov session)** via the International Baccalaureate Organization. Recognition is strong for English-medium degrees; ensure subject combination covers math/science where relevant.
- **A-Levels / Cambridge International / Pearson Edexcel:** Centres in Bucharest/Constanța/Timișoara typically sit **May/June** papers; some offer **Oct/Nov** sessions (and **Jan** for select Pearson subjects). Results generally arrive **Aug (May/June)** and **Jan/Feb (Oct/Nov)**. Seek predicted grades by **March–April** for conditional offers.
- **SAT (digital):** College Board runs international dates in **Aug, Oct, Nov, Dec, Mar, May, Jun** (occasional Jan). Scores release about **2–3 weeks post-test**; align with US/English-medium program marketing. SAT is optional for Romanian universities but valued for UNIC/UNIC Athens merit positioning.

## Month-by-month timing (2024–2026 cycles)
> Dates reflect Ministry orders for recent sessions; the Ministry may shift by ±1 week. Confirm annually on edu.ro and county inspectorate notices.

- **January–February**
  - University winter exams; **high-school semester break** varies by county (1 week in February).
  - IB/A-Level predicted grade meetings; SAT (March) registration deadline typically **early Feb**.
  - Outreach: nurture juniors (grade 11) while seniors await mock Bac results.
- **March**
  - Digital SAT administration; A-Level/IB coursework deadlines.
  - Schools finalize **Bac exam registrations** and competency test schedules.
  - Outreach: request updated transcripts/predicted grades; push early application/offer pipelines.
- **April**
  - **Spring/Easter break** (~1–2 weeks around Orthodox Easter).
  - Last window for June SAT registration; finalize Bac oral exam logistics.
  - Outreach: host virtual sessions while students are off campus; promote UNIC offer turnaround before Bac season.
- **May**
  - **IB and A-Level May/June exams** begin; SAT (May) test date.
  - High-school instruction winds down; practical exams/projects conclude.
  - Outreach: secure **conditional offers** before Bac written exams; highlight housing/visa timelines.
- **June**
  - **Bac competency tests** (Romanian, digital, modern language) early/mid-June.
  - **Bac written exams late June** (e.g., Romanian Mon; compulsory subject Wed; elective Fri).
  - **SAT June** test; school year ends mid/late June; summer break starts.
  - Outreach: keep offerholders warm; remind about transcript attestation and CNRED recognition steps.
- **July**
  - **Preliminary Bac results early July**; **appeals mid-July**; **final results mid/late July**.
  - **IB May session results early July**; A-Level results pending.
  - University summer exams/retakes; admission portals open; housing deposits due.
  - Outreach: convert on Bac outcomes; fast-track scholarship/discount decisions; invite for August orientation.
- **August**
  - **Bac resit session:** registrations late July; competencies mid-Aug; written exams **late Aug**; results **late Aug/early Sept**.
  - SAT is usually **not offered** (check yearly); A-Level Oct/Nov entry deadlines mid-Aug/early Sept.
  - Outreach: recovery campaigns for near-miss Bac candidates; promote foundation/transfer options.
- **September**
  - **Final Bac resit results early Sept**; university admission confirmations close.
  - **School year restarts early Sept**; universities begin **late Sept/early Oct**.
  - Outreach: engage new grade 12 cohort; school visits; counselor briefings with prior-year outcomes.
- **October–November**
  - **University autumn exam/retake period** and **A-Level Oct/Nov exams**.
  - **SAT Oct/Nov**; IB **Nov session**.
  - Outreach: application launch for next intake; collect predicted grades; schedule fall school fairs.
- **December**
  - Winter break **late Dec–early Jan**; SAT Dec test date.
  - Outreach: send pre-holiday nudges; promote early-bird scholarship deadlines (Jan/Feb).

## Outreach and offer timing guidance
- **Anchor around Bac milestones:** push conditional offers before written Bac (May–June) and reconfirm after **early-July** results; keep resit pathways open through August.
- **Counselor alignment:** share UNIC entry criteria and Bac subject expectations with county inspectorates and high-performing liceu; emphasize acceptance of CEFR B2 certificates from Bac language competencies.
- **International curricula:** request **predicted grades by March–April** (IB/A-Level) for scholarship assessment; set score submission deadlines aligned to **IB July** and **A-Level Aug** release cycles.
- **Seasonality:** avoid major outreach during **spring/Easter** and **winter** breaks; prioritize **September–November** school visits and **February–April** virtual events when seniors are available.
