# Culinary Arts Lab — Naming and signage plan

**Former name:** Europa Building New Teaching Kitchen  
**Source evidence:** 2025-12-20 photo set in `photos/` (see `photos/photoindex.md`)  
**Goal:** Apply the “Culinary Arts Lab” branding and tighten wayfinding/safety signage using observed conditions.

## Branding and material standards
- **Primary type:** Gotham (or UNIC’s approved sans-serif). Use Medium weight for headers, Book for body copy. Minimum cap height 35 mm on primary signage to preserve readability from 4–6 m.  
- **Color palette:** UNIC Red `#c10230`, Anthracite `#2b2b2b`, support neutral `#f5f5f5`. Ensure ≥70% contrast per EMEA accessibility guidance. Accent red strip (25 mm) anchors the left edge of every plaque/blade to cue UNIC brand continuity.  
- **Bilingual layout:** Reserve the second line for Greek if required; mirror hierarchy (English on top, Greek below) with 70% font size ratio.  
- **Substrate:** Satin-brushed anodized aluminum (4–6 mm) for plaques/blades; 6 mm clear acrylic with rear-applied vinyl for glass decals; 3 mm PVC or ACM for temporary inserts.  
- **Mounting:** Use 3M VHB 5952 tape + concealed anchors on drywall, stainless stand-offs on glass or masonry, magnetic rails for changeable content on equipment rooms. All fixings must be food-safe and resistant to steam/grease.  
- **Finish:** Anti-glare matte laminate; radius corners (5 mm) to avoid snags; apply NSF-certified cleaning compatibility labels on reverse.  
- **Digital assets:** Produce vector artboards (600 × 300 mm baseline) plus SVG icons for PPE, accessibility, and recycling. Store master files under `Infrastructure/.../artwork/` once created.

## Naming guidance
- Use **“Culinary Arts Lab”** on all door placards, corridor wayfinding, digital schedules, and scheduling systems. Include a secondary line “Europa Building — Level ? (confirm)” once level is verified.
- When referencing legacy materials, pair the new name with the former reference on first mention: “Culinary Arts Lab (formerly Europa Building New Teaching Kitchen).”
- Stamp a short-form code (`CAL-EU-01`) on every physical sign + drawing set so maintenance can track revisions.
- Add QR codes that land on a digital SOP packet (`UNIC.edu/culinary-lab/rules`) to reduce poster clutter.

### Glass observation rail
- Apply a frosted lower band (1,100 mm AFF, 150 mm tall) with negative-cut “Culinary Arts Lab” wordmark every 1.5 m to control glare yet maintain visibility.  
- Install removable acrylic caption rails (50 × 300 mm) under each pane to highlight active modules, e.g., “Module 3 — Mediterranean Cuisine.”

## Implementation checklist
- Confirm floor level for corridor wayfinding text and update art files before fabrication. Capture as-built dimensions for entry wall width and ceiling height to validate plaque and blade proportions.  
- Issue one consolidated drawing pack (PDF + CAD) showing mounting heights (AFF), clearance from hood edges, and relation to glazing mullions.  
- Procure all aluminum components from the same fabricator to guarantee finish match; specify RAL color chips in PO.  
- Pre-drill pilot holes with debris capture to avoid metal filings entering kitchen surfaces; schedule work outside of service hours with lab deep clean afterwards.  
- Integrate station IDs into scheduling, lesson plans, and AV overlays simultaneously so instructors reference the new schema on day one.  
- Photograph installed signage (repeat of each photo reference) and append images + captions to `photos/photoindex.md` for traceability.  
- Schedule annual audit each August to wipe, re-laminate, and verify QR destinations; note updates in Infrastructure change log.
