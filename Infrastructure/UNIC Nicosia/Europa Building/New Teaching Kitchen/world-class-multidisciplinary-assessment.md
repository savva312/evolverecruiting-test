# UNIC Nicosia — Europa Building New Teaching Kitchen  
*Multi-perspective assessment for the incoming Culinary Program Director*  
**Date:** 2025-12-22 | **Source set:** 20251220 photos (corridor and lab walkthrough)

## 1) Executive summary
- The new teaching kitchen presents a professional-grade lab with generous glazing for observation, strong ventilation coverage, and diversified production zones (hot line, bakery, pastry, equipment bay, warewashing, cold/dry storage, instructor office).
- Spatial flow is generally linear (corridor → observation → entry → stations → storage/warewashing). Station density is high but seems workable; aisle widths and height clearances should be verified against code and accessibility standards.
- Mechanical and safety fundamentals (gas manifold with E-stop, distributed hoods, recycling and waste segregation) are present; documentation and labeling require tightening to support operations and teaching safety.
- The facility is immediately usable for production-style classes, but to reach “world-class” teaching quality it needs: (a) explicit station numbering and AV overlays, (b) calibrated SOPs for gas/electrical and hood use, (c) measurement-backed layout map, and (d) a clear food-safety + allergen control playbook with equipment-specific training modules.

## 2) Architectural & space planning perspective
- **Visibility & supervision:** Full-height corridor glazing (photo: `20251220 - teaching kitchen overview from corridor window.jpeg`) gives strong passive supervision and audience visibility for tours. Add low-reflectance film or internal blinds for glare control during demos.
- **Circulation:** Linear bench/range layout with parallel hoods (photos: `front cookline...`, `main cookline...`, `mid-lab cook stations...`). Confirm aisle widths (target ≥1.2 m main, ≥0.9 m secondary). Consider one designated ADA-compatible workstation (knee clearance, height-adjustable table).
- **Zoning:** Clear differentiation of hot line, bakery deck/combi, pastry/sheeter corner, dry store, cold room doors, and warewashing. Recommend a wall graphic or overhead banner map for students to internalize zones quickly.
- **Observation & pedagogy surfaces:** Instructor-facing island with hanging utensils and monitors (photos: `front cookline...`, `instruction line...`). Evaluate adding a mobile demo cart or camera rail for close-up captures of pastry/butchery.
- **Adjacency quality:** Offices and recycling bins flank the corridor (`instructor office view...`, `service corridor...`), which supports quick staff access. Ensure odor and noise transfer is controlled—check door seals and potential need for acoustic panels.
- **Egress & wayfinding:** Emergency exit doors in corridor present; ensure illuminated signage and floor-direction arrows from all student stations to exits. Add tactile/visual markers for fire blanket/extinguisher points.

## 3) Engineering & systems perspective
- **Ventilation & extraction:** Multiple canopy hoods over hot line, fryer/grill, combi/deck ovens. Action: capture model numbers, airflow specs, and maintenance schedule; confirm make-up air balance and hood zoning relative to gas solenoid valves.
- **Gas distribution:** Visible manifold with regulator, gauge, and emergency shutoff panel (`gas supply manifold...`). Ensure: (a) E-stop signage at each entry, (b) station-level isolation (if available), (c) weekly test log, (d) integration with fire suppression interlocks.
- **Electrical:** Mixer-heavy pastry/bakery benching suggests multiple 230V circuits. Action: map outlets per station with load limits; label panels; provide surge-protected power for AV monitors.
- **Fire suppression:** Hood systems appear present but labeling is not visible. Action: verify last service date, nozzle coverage for fryers/griddle, and placement of Class K and ABC extinguishers; add QR-coded inspection logs.
- **Plumbing & drainage:** Multiple paired sinks along cookline and warewashing corner (`warewashing corner...`). Action: check air gaps, trap priming, slope to floor drains, and anti-slip treatment near wet zones.
- **Cold & dry storage:** Walk-in cold doors with posted schedules (`cold storage doors...`) and dry store shelving. Action: confirm temp monitoring (data-logged), door alarms, and pest-proofing; ensure allergen-segregated shelving.
- **Lighting & acoustics:** Uniform LED grid; review lux levels at bench height (target 500–750 lux for prep). Add task lighting over pastry sheeter/mixers and dampening where metal-on-metal noise is high.
- **Waste & recycling:** Color-coded stations in corridor and pastry corner. Action: add organics/compost stream if program includes sustainability; ensure chemical storage is segregated with spill kit nearby.

## 4) Culinary operations perspective
- **Line readiness:** Hot line equipped with fryers, grill, bain marie, and multiple gas hobs; combi oven bay and deck oven for baking; pastry corner with sheeter and mixers; slicer and Thermomix units in equipment bay.
- **Station standardization:** Stations appear similar but lack visible numbering. Action: assign numeric/alpha codes (e.g., A1–A6) with laminated station inventories (utensils, smallwares, thermometer, sanitizing kit).
- **Production flow:** Logical sequence—ingredient storage → cold/dry → prep benches → hot line → pass/demo → warewashing. Add a defined “clean landing” zone for finished plates and a “QC/temps check” point with probe thermometers.
- **Food safety:** Need labeled raw/ready-to-eat separation and allergen protocols. Add color-coded boards/knives per station and sanitizer test strip caddies; mount HACCP charts near entry.
- **Equipment commissioning:** Capture and post quick-start guides with model/serial numbers for combi ovens, fryers, mixers, sheeter, slicer, Thermomix, deck oven; include preheat and cooldown SOPs and oil management for fryers.
- **Receiving & storage:** Not visible; confirm receiving path and scales for yield tests. Dry store shelving should include sealed bins and FIFO labels.

## 5) Culinary pedagogy perspective
- **Instructor control line:** Overhead monitors and hanging utensils support demos. Action: map AV inputs (HDMI/USB-C) and add a ceiling/rail camera for hands-in-bowl views; provide wireless mic/lapel for narration.
- **Student visibility:** Corridor glazing enables external observation; ensure in-class sightlines by staggering monitor angles and keeping tall equipment at perimeter.
- **Assessment & feedback:** Add a visible station rubric (mise-en-place, hygiene, technique, timing) and QR code linking to digital checklists. Use station numbering to log temps and progress.
- **Differentiated learning:** Set up at least one inclusive station (lower height/clear knee space) and one “advanced” station with specialty tools (sous-vide, induction, blast chiller if available).
- **Safety teaching moments:** Post a “first 5 minutes” checklist: hood on, gas check, sanitizer mixed and tested, knives inspected, hair restraints, allergen briefing. Use the gas manifold and E-stop signage as live teaching props.
- **Portfolio/recording:** Encourage recording of demos; store clips with recipes and HACCP notes. Add wall-mounted whiteboard (seen in combi bay) as the “daily plan” zone with learning outcomes.

## 7) Risk and gap register (evidence-based)
- **Labeling gaps:** No visible station IDs, hood IDs, extinguisher labels, or circuit/gas isolation labels. Risk: slower emergency response; inconsistent setup.
- **Data gaps:** No measured aisle widths, counter heights, CFM specs, lux levels, or load ratings; limits ability to certify accessibility and capacity.
- **Process gaps:** No visible SOPs for oil handling, allergen control, or equipment startup/shutdown. Risk: safety incidents and equipment damage.
- **AV uncertainty:** Monitor inputs/cabling not visible; risk of teaching friction if adapters missing.
- **Inventory mapping:** Cabinet/drawer contents not documented; risk of class setup delays.

## 8) Recommendations (prioritized punch list)
**P0 – Safety and compliance (immediate)**
- Label and test gas E-stop, hood suppression, extinguishers; post inspection QR codes and service dates (photos: gas manifold and corridor doors).
- Number stations and hoods; post egress arrows and extinguisher/blanket locations to match corridor exits.
- Create laminated safety startup/shutdown cards at each station (gas, hood, sanitizer mix, PPE), anchored to hood/gas interlock steps.

**P1 – Operational readiness**
- Map electrical/gas outlets and max loads per station; document equipment model/serial numbers and create 1-page quick-starts (fryers, combi/deck ovens, mixers, sheeter, slicer, Thermomix).
- Build HACCP/allergen SOPs: color-coded tools, raw/RTE zoning, temp logging kit at QC point.
- Inventory all drawers/cabinets; issue station kits with check-in/out sheets.

**P1 – Pedagogy & AV**
- Add ceiling/rail camera for demo close-ups; standardize monitor inputs (HDMI/USB-C) with labeled cables and adapters; secure cable routing near instructor island to avoid trip hazards.
- Publish station rubrics and QR-coded checklists; set one inclusive workstation with height accommodation and one “advanced tools” bay (sous-vide/induction if available).
- Add wall/overhead map of zones and station numbering for onboarding, visible from entry glazing.

**P2 – Experience & resilience**
- Measure and publish floor plan with aisles, counter heights, hood capture areas, and accessibility notes.
- Enhance lighting over pastry/sheeter; add anti-fatigue mats at wet zones where safe.
- Expand waste program (organics/compost) and chemical storage segregation with spill kit.

## 9) Next measurements/artifacts to collect
- Scaled floor plan with station numbers, aisle widths, and door swing/egress paths.
- MEP schedule: hood CFM and make-up air, gas line sizing/pressure, circuit loads, water supply/return, drain locations.
- Asset register: model/serial numbers, commissioning dates, warranty contacts for all major equipment.
- AV map: monitor models, inputs, cable runs, potential camera mounting points.
- Lighting survey (lux at bench) and acoustic check during peak load.
