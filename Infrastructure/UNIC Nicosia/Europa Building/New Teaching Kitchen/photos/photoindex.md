# New Teaching Kitchen (Europa Building, UNIC Nicosia) — photo index — 2025-12-20

- **Location:** UNIC Nicosia — Europa Building — New Teaching Kitchen
- **Photos:** 26 (`.jpeg`)
- **Evidence style:** “Observed” stays literal as much as possible; stakeholder notes explain how each photo can be used to make decisions.

## Scope of this set

Interior and corridor documentation covering lab layout, equipment placement, safety/utilities, access control, storage, and teaching/observation conditions.

## How to use this index

- Use the linked filenames to open images; the index is ordered to follow a plausible walking/arrival path where possible.
- Treat photos as supporting evidence; confirm code-critical dimensions (accessibility, egress, clearances) with measurements/as-builts.
- When something is unclear, the safest next step is a targeted re-shoot: one wide establishing shot + one close-up of labels/controls/details.

## Stakeholder perspectives (what to look for)

### Architect / designer
- Read the lab as a workflow: entry → storage → prep → hot line → warewashing; confirm zoning supports teaching flow.
- Use window/corridor shots to assess visibility and the observation/teaching interface.

### Engineer (MEP / safety systems)
- Gas and extraction photos are safety-critical: capture shutoffs, hood labeling, make-up air, and electrical loads in follow-ups.
- Use equipment and washdown shots to confirm drainage, ventilation, and heat management assumptions.

### Facilities / operations
- Use the set to define cleaning zones, chemical storage, waste segregation, and maintenance access to equipment.
- Ensure posters/signage don’t replace durable labels; plan a formal signage pack where needed.

### Marketing / admissions
- Hero content should show the lab “ready to teach”: wide clean overviews, quality equipment, and visible safety culture.
- Capture people-in-space (with consent) and student output to avoid a sterile feel.

### Educator / teaching delivery
- Use these photos to design station numbering, lesson logistics, and safety onboarding (PPE, allergens, hot surfaces).
- Tie AV/monitor shots to teaching modes (demo, observation, team-based work).

- Overhead shot or floor plan showing full station layout and clear numbering scheme for benches.

- Detailed views of electrical and gas outlets per station, including any labeling for maximum load.

- HVAC/extraction details: hood labeling, filter access points, and make-up air vents.
- Accessibility considerations: knee clearance at sinks, counter heights, and aisle width measurements.
- Any AV setup details: display inputs, camera positions, microphone/speaker placements for demos.
- External wayfinding: directional signage from main entrance to the teaching kitchen.
