# RTB area (UNIC Nicosia) — photo index — 2025-12-22

- **Location:** UNIC Nicosia — RTB / Medical Centre area (exterior context)
- **Photos:** 20 (`.jpeg`)
- **Evidence style:** “Observed” stays literal as much as possible; stakeholder notes explain how each photo can be used to make decisions.

## Scope of this set

Exterior context around the RTB area, emphasizing rear parking/service conditions and (based on visible signage) the Medical Centre frontage and canopy.

## How to use this index

- Use the linked filenames to open images; the index is ordered to follow a plausible walking/arrival path where possible.
- Treat photos as supporting evidence; confirm code-critical dimensions (accessibility, egress, clearances) with measurements/as-builts.
- When something is unclear, the safest next step is a targeted re-shoot: one wide establishing shot + one close-up of labels/controls/details.

## Stakeholder perspectives (what to look for)

### Architect / designer
- Use the set to contrast rear parking/service character with the Medical Centre’s canopy and signage; identify where identity breaks down.
- Look for opportunities to define pedestrian routes and screen waste/plant so the campus edge reads intentionally.

### Engineer (MEP / structural / civil)
- Use pavement/cracking shots as lifecycle evidence; confirm drainage falls and maintenance needs.
- For louvered enclosures and rooftop condensers, verify clearances, drainage, and fall protection; capture nameplates in a follow-up set.
- Check vehicle/pedestrian separation under the canopy and along service lanes.

### Facilities / operations
- Waste and recycling photos indicate where pickup is informal; prioritize a signed, screened enclosure and consistent contractor access.
- Use edge/low-activity corner shots to plan lighting, CCTV, and housekeeping routines.
- Roof plant photos should trigger a safety check (guardrails/anchors, drainage, standing water).

### Marketing / admissions
- Prefer the Medical Centre frontage/canopy/flags for public comms; avoid rear waste/industrial-adjacency angles as hero visuals.
- Keep rear-condition shots for internal improvement narratives or sustainability transparency once addressed.

### Educator / student experience
- Use for orientation (where to park/enter) and safety briefings for staff/students navigating service-heavy edges.
- For a “research/technology” story, plan a future interior set (labs, teaching spaces, student activity) with permissions.
