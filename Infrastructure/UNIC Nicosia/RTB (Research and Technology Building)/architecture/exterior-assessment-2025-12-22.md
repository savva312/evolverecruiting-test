# RTB (Research and Technology Building) area (UNIC Nicosia) — exterior assessment (photo-based) — 2025-12-22

- **Area/building:** UNIC Nicosia — RTB (Research and Technology Building) area  
  - **Important limitation:** The current photo set includes **rear parking/service conditions** and a **Medical Centre–signed frontage** under a blue canopy. It does not clearly show a single, unambiguous “RTB main entrance” from primary pedestrian routes.
- **Assessment date:** 2025-12-22
- **Photo set used:** `architecture/photos/` (see `architecture/photos/photoindex.md`)
- **Evidence note:** Photo-based screening only. Confirm code-critical items (accessibility, guards/rails, drainage falls) via measurement and as-builts.

## 1) Scope of this assessment (what “outside” includes here)

Based on the available photos, “outside” coverage includes:
- Rear parking lot, perimeter edge/fencing, and adjacent industrial context.
- Service lanes and rear entries, including louvered plant enclosures.
- Waste and recycling staging points at the parking perimeter.
- Rooftop mechanical plant visible in photos.
- Frontage sequence for a glass-and-canopy building signed as a Medical Centre (within the RTB area context).

## 2) Stakeholder perspectives (what to look for)

### Architect / designer
- Read the arrival sequence and how transparency/opacity (curtain wall vs translucent panels) signals public vs studio/service zones.
- Treat courtyards, benches, decks, and the sculptural plinth as place-making moments; note where comfort, shade, and accessibility could be improved.
- Track material transitions (pavers → thresholds; metal rails; panel joints) as likely durability/maintenance hotspots.
- Use the set as a baseline for any future campus-edge upgrades (signage hierarchy, lighting, landscape buffers).

### Engineer (MEP / structural / civil)
- Use drainage-related photos to flag falls, trench drains, ponding, and water management at thresholds and stairs.
- For external plant and utilities, capture labels, isolation points, clearances, and weatherproofing; photos should trigger a follow-up nameplate set.
- For stairs/guardrails, treat photos as a screening tool; confirm compliance via measured checks (guard heights, handrail continuity, slip resistance).
- For access-control hardware (boom gates, fences), confirm impact protection and safe maintenance access.

### Facilities / operations
- Map service routes, waste staging, and boundary conditions; prioritize lighting/CCTV coverage in long alleys and perimeter corridors.
- Watch for slip/trip risks (wet pavers, leaf litter, loose furniture) and keep egress widths clear.
- Use condition shots to plan maintenance cycles (cleaning glazing, repainting rails, fixing paver settlement, graffiti removal).

### Marketing / admissions
- Use the entrance, glazed corner, and courtyards as hero story beats; pair with student activity (consent) and interior studio shots in a future set.
- Avoid “service-only” images in recruitment pages; keep them for behind-the-scenes operations or sustainability narratives.
- Use wayfinding/signage photos to improve the visitor journey before filming virtual tours.

### Educator / student experience
- Use arrival and circulation shots to build orientation maps (where to enter, where to gather, where to find studios).
- Treat the building as a living lab: discuss daylight, envelope choices, circulation, and how services are integrated (or exposed).
- Note safety cues (assembly point, egress routes) for onboarding and drills.

## 4) Key condition and risk observations (grounded in photos)

### 4.1 Pavement condition and drainage cues

**Observed:**
- Cracked asphalt is visible in multiple parking/drive lane areas. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-parking-cracked-asphalt-tree-buffer.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-parking-cracked-asphalt-tree-buffer.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-side-drive.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-side-drive.jpeg>))
- Rooftop mechanical area shows a puddled/wet zone adjacent to equipment (possible ponding) in at least one image. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-rooftop-mechanical-condensers.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-rooftop-mechanical-condensers.jpeg>))

**Implications (to confirm by survey):**
- Cracking can indicate aging surface, sub-base issues, or drainage problems; confirm falls and locate inlets/outfalls. Roof ponding (if present) should be checked against roof design falls and drain maintenance.

### 4.2 Pedestrian/vehicle separation and glazing protection

**Observed:**
- Vehicles can park or pass directly along the glazed frontage under the canopy. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entrance-flags.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entrance-flags.jpeg>))

**Questions to confirm:**
- Whether bollards/kerbs/planters protect the glazing from bumper strikes; whether a clearly marked pedestrian path exists separate from vehicle movement under the canopy.

### 4.3 Waste presentation and hygiene controls

**Observed:**
- Waste equipment is visible and appears unscreened; litter is visible near the compactor area. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg>))

**Implications (neutral):**
- Unscreened waste near visible edges is a common perception and hygiene risk (odour, pests, windblown litter). It does not necessarily mean poor operations overall, but it suggests the need for a formal, signed, screened enclosure with a hardstand and washdown/drainage provisions if volumes justify it.

## 5) Stakeholder analysis (detailed)

### 5.1 Architect / designer lens

**Identity and arrival:**
- The canopy façade provides a strong institutional identity: deep overhang, repetitive structural rhythm, and large-scale glazing. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-frontage-blue-canopy.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-frontage-blue-canopy.jpeg>))
- Entry signage appears integrated into a metal-clad portal with timber lining; this is a clear “front door” detail and can be a strong first-impression asset. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entry-signage-closeup.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entry-signage-closeup.jpeg>))

**Where the story breaks down (from photos):**
- The rear environment is visually utilitarian (asphalt, fences, industrial backdrop) and may feel disconnected from the public identity unless visitor routing is controlled. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-parking-rows-industrial-backdrop.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-parking-rows-industrial-backdrop.jpeg>))
- Waste elements are prominent at edges; this competes with brand messaging (UNIC banners) and can undermine the perceived quality of the campus edge. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg>))

**Upgrade focus suggested by this set:**
- Define **pedestrian routes** from parking to doors (markings, paving cues, lighting).
- Screen/relocate waste to reduce visual impact and improve hygiene.
- Use landscape buffers strategically to soften industrial adjacencies without creating blind spots.

### 5.2 Engineer (MEP / structural / civil) lens

**Pavement and site drainage:**
- Use cracking as lifecycle evidence; confirm whether the lot needs regrading, patching, or full resurfacing, and locate drains/inlets/outfalls. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-parking-cracked-asphalt-tree-buffer.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-parking-cracked-asphalt-tree-buffer.jpeg>))

**External plant / louvered enclosures:**
- Louvered panels and enclosures likely hide mechanical/electrical infrastructure; confirm access clearances, weatherproofing, and whether equipment nameplates and isolation points are documented and reachable. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-service-lane-louvered-panels.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-service-lane-louvered-panels.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-rear-entry-ramp-louvered-enclosure.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-rear-entry-ramp-louvered-enclosure.jpeg>))

**Roof plant:**
- Rooftop condensers are visible; assess fall protection, safe access, drainage (standing water), and condensate routing. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-rooftop-mechanical-condensers.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-rooftop-mechanical-condensers.jpeg>))

### 5.3 Facilities / operations lens

**Waste and recycling operations:**
- Current evidence suggests waste/recycling points are informal and distributed (compactor, recycling cage, wheelie bins). Formalizing a dedicated enclosure with consistent pickup route and signage can reduce litter and improve contractor reliability. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-recycling-cage-cardboard.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-recycling-cage-cardboard.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-wheelie-bin-hedge.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-wheelie-bin-hedge.jpeg>))

**Security/low-activity corners:**
- Perimeter fences and far parking bays should be treated as higher priority for lighting and CCTV due to low passive surveillance. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-perimeter-fence-rear-parking.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-perimeter-fence-rear-parking.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-parking-far-bays-cypress-bin.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-parking-far-bays-cypress-bin.jpeg>))

**Housekeeping and perception:**
- Litter at the waste perimeter and visible cracking in paving are high-leverage “quick wins” for perception: even small cleanups and targeted repairs can shift first impressions for staff/students arriving by car. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-parking-cracked-asphalt-tree-buffer.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-parking-cracked-asphalt-tree-buffer.jpeg>))

### 5.4 Marketing / admissions lens

**What works for public-facing visuals:**
- The canopy façade, entry portal, and flags provide a strong institutional frame suitable for public comms (health/clinical context). (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-frontage-blue-canopy.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-frontage-blue-canopy.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entrance-flags.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entrance-flags.jpeg>))

**What to keep internal (or re-shoot after improvements):**
- Waste compactor, industrial adjacency, and rear parking condition shots are valuable for internal planning and “before/after” improvement tracking, but are generally poor hero images. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-industrial-yard-across-fence.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-industrial-yard-across-fence.jpeg>))

**Wayfinding gap implied by the set:**
- The photo set does not conclusively show an RTB-branded main public entrance; for marketing and visitor navigation, a clear “RTB entrance” establishing shot and map would reduce ambiguity.

### 5.5 Educator / student experience lens

**Orientation and safety:**
- Rear parking dominates the arrival experience for anyone arriving by car; without clearly marked pedestrian routes, students/staff may rely on informal desire lines. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-rear-parking-wide-to-buildings.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-rear-parking-wide-to-buildings.jpeg>))
- The frontage canopy provides weather protection, but vehicle presence along the glazed edge suggests the need for clear pedestrian guidance during peak times. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entrance-flags.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-entrance-flags.jpeg>))

**Living-lab opportunities:**
- Use the contrast between public-facing architecture (canopy/glazing) and back-of-house realities (waste, plant screening, parking maintenance) to teach about campus operations, lifecycle costs, and how design decisions play out over time. (Evidence: [20251222-rtb-medical-centre-frontage-blue-canopy.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-medical-centre-frontage-blue-canopy.jpeg>), [20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg](<./photos/20251222-rtb-waste-compactor-unic-banner.jpeg>))

## 6) Targeted follow-ups (what the photos do not resolve)

- **RTB entrance clarity:** capture establishing shots of RTB main entrance(s) and signage from primary pedestrian approaches.
- **Accessibility checks:** confirm curb ramps, tactile paving, accessible stalls, ramp slopes/landings, and door thresholds at main entries.
- **Pedestrian routes from parking:** document and (if needed) design marked walking routes, crossings, and lighting.
- **Waste enclosure design:** document intended waste streams, pickup route, and propose a screened hardstand with washdown/drainage if appropriate.
- **MEP documentation:** capture nameplates and isolation points for louvered enclosures and rooftop equipment; confirm safe maintenance access.
- **Night lighting/CCTV:** day/night comparison for rear parking and perimeter edges; confirm camera coverage of far bays and waste areas.
